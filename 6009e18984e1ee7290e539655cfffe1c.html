Peduto: City's health plans will cover in vitro fertilization in 2021

Mayor Bill Peduto’s office announced Wednesday that the city will offer fertility coverage for the first time to its employees.

Starting in 2021, the city will offer two cycles of in vitro fertilization for employees covered under its health plans.

According to the announcement, the city will pay the healthcare costs in full, and employee monthly insurance contributions for those using the benefit will not increase.

The benefit will be offered to all employee groups that are part of the Municipal Healthcare Group, according to a statement.

“The City of Pittsburgh is a welcoming place to work, where we are always looking to improve the quality of life for our employees and their families,” Mayor Peduto said in the release.

The latest announcement comes nearly three years after the city began covering employees seeking gender affirmation surgery.

Although there isn’t yet an estimate on number of requests for either item, Tim McNulty, the mayor’s spokesman, said both have been asked for by employees.

“We're constantly in talks with our many unions about employee healthcare needs.” Mr. McNulty said.

According to the announcement, the new policy means that Pittsburgh will be one of only a few cities in the country that offers coverage for both fertility treatments and gender affirmation surgery.
