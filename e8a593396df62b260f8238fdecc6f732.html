Pittsburgh Public Schools opens tech support centers for students

Pittsburgh Public Schools is offering in-person tech support for students and families experiencing problems with their district-issued devices.

District officials said families can make an appointment over the next two weeks to bring the devices to one of five Technology Resource Centers located at:

▪ Pittsburgh CAPA School -- 111 9th St., Downtown.

▪ Pittsburgh Carrick -- 125 Parkfield St., Carrick.

▪ Pittsburgh King -- 50 Montgomery Place, North Side.

▪ Pittsburgh Langley -- 2940 Sheraden Blvd., Sheraden

▪ Pittsburgh Obama -- 515 N. Highland Ave., East Liberty

The Technology Resource Centers will be open from 8 a.m. to 2 p.m., weekdays through Oct. 2.

“We want all PPS families to have secure, functional devices for E-Learning,” said Mark Stuckey, the district’s interim chief technology officer.

Families can schedule an appointment by visiting this PPS district webpage. Parents are asked to arrive 10 minutes before their appointment, and to bring their district-issued device, charger, and student log in information to the appointment. Some walk-ins will be allowed, district officials said.

The district, which launched its school year fully online, said remote learning has gone more smoothly after some first-day internet bottlenecks. In one instance, PPS blocked student access to a website, SafeYouTube.net, after users in the district and elsewhere around the country found video links that had been hacked, leading to inappropriate sites.
