Up to 100 people now allowed at outdoor events in Allegheny County

Allegheny County Health Department Director Dr. Deborah Bogen announced Friday that the department will amend its July order aiming to mitigate the spread of COVID-19 in order to increase the amount of people allowed at outdoor events in the county to 100, up from the previous cap of 50. 

All other requirements of the order — which went into effect July 17 — will remain in place, according to the announcement. 

The amendment was made due to the decrease in the county’s virus positivity rate and rolling daily average case counts over the past month, the announcement said, and will take effect at 12:01 a.m. Aug. 29.
