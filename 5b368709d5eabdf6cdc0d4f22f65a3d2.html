Turtle Creek felon pleads guilty to fentanyl, heroin conspiracy with his sisters

A Turtle Creek man indicted in 2017 in a heroin and cocaine conspiracy with his two sisters admitted his guilt Thursday in federal court and is facing many years in federal prison.

Jerry Coker, 28, pleaded guilty to a count of conspiracy to distribute and possess with intent to distribute fentanyl and heroin.

Coker admitted that on Sept. 11, 2016, his sister Marleka Robinson obtained a large amount of fentanyl from a dealer in Knoxville at his request. She came back to the house she shared with him and another sister, Monique Robinson.

Law officers investigating Coker then executed a search warrant on the home the next day and seized more than 285 grams of fentanyl and smaller amounts of heroin, cocaine and marijuana.

Prosecutors said the house was strewn with materials for drug packaging and distributing along with two guns and nearly $9,000 in cash. Federal agents and police seized all of it.

A grand jury indicted Coker and his sisters in November 2017.

The cases against the women are pending.

U.S. District Judge Cathy Bissoon said she'd sentence Coker on May 18. Because of a prior felony conviction, he faces at least a decade in prison and could get life.
