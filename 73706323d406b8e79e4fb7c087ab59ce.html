Fourth Port Authority bus driver tests positive for COVID-19

Port Authority on Friday confirmed a fourth employee tested positive for COVID-19.

The employee was a bus driver who worked at the East Liberty garage, and the driver has not been at work since Thursday, authority officials said. The employee had been on “light duty” since Feb.12.

No other information about the driver would be given to “protect the employee’s identity,” a release from the authority said.

When the employee reported the positive test, Port Authority conducted a deep cleaning of the East Liberty garage and any buses the driver would have come into contact with.

Buses have been disinfected on a daily basis since March 14.

The case was “confidentially” reported to the Allegheny County Health Department, a release said.

All four Port Authority employees who tested positive for the novel coronavirus have been bus drivers. Bus operators make up around 1,300 of the authority’s 2,600 employees.
