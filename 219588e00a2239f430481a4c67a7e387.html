Stick taps, chirps and observations from Penguins-Canadiens Game 1

In a game that featured two penalty shots, an overtime period and a standout performance by Montreal Canadiens goalie Carey Price, the Penguins dropped Game 1 of their best-of-five qualifying-round series, 3-2.

It was over when: With just over six minutes left in overtime, Montreal defenseman Jeff Petry scooped a loose puck, side stepped Brandon Tanev’s block attempt and ripped a shot past Matt Murray.

Turning point: The Penguins led considerably in shot attempts but trailed 2-0 on the scoreboard midway through the second period. It looked like Price would steal Game 1, but then Sidney Crosby tossed a puck on net from below the goal line and in. It didn’t change the result, but it changed the momentum.
