About 12,000 remain without power in Pittsburgh region

About 12,000 utility customers remain without power Thursday morning, more than 24 hours after severe storms and strong winds hit Western Pennsylvania and spawned a small tornado near Tarentum.

Duquesne Light was reporting about 10,000 of its customers were still without power in Allegheny and Beaver counties as of 4:30 a.m. West Penn Power reported 2,000 outages in Pittsburgh and the surrounding counties. 

The utility companies restored power to thousands since an early morning Wednesday storm knocked out power to more than 50,000 at its peak.

Winds gusted up to a record 75 mph at Pittsburgh International Airport, according to the National Weather Service. The Weather Service confirmed a tornado touched down near Tarentum around 1:19 a.m Wednesday.

The storm tore off part of the roof at St. Mary of Czestochowa Church in New Kensington and lifted a hangar off the ground at the Arnold Palmer Regional Airport in Latrobe.
