Catch a park program Thursday on PA's state insect, the firefly

One of the traditional thrills of childhood is getting tougher every year. Catching lightning bugs was once a summer evening pastime that formed a glowing connection between kids and nature. Today, most of the planet’s 2,000 firefly species are waning in population due to habitat loss, pesticide use and light pollution.

Spaces are still available for an educational and participatory firefly program Thursday evening at Moraine State Park in Butler County, part of a state Department of Conservation and Natural Resources celebration of the park’s 50th year.

“We call it a family event, but the kids get most excited about it,” said Mike Shaffer, one of the park’s environmental education specialists. “They’re interesting creatures and it’s a chance to get outside at night.”

An annual firefly festival in Forest County celebrating Pennsylvania’s state insect was canceled this year. Moraine’s event will be held from 7:30 to 9 p.m. Thursday at Pavilion 7 near McDanel’s Boat Launch on the North Shore of Lake Arthur. It will start with a PowerPoint presentation explaining the biology and conservation of fireflies and segue to a free-for-all catch ’em if you can.

“Any program that gets you out at night using other senses is very, very special,” said Mr. Shaffer.

Fireflies are technically soft-bodied beetles that produce a biochemical “cold light.” With no infrared or ultraviolet frequencies, there is no increase in temperature. The bugs mate from May to September at Pittsburgh’s latitude.  Males blink patterns that vary by area, signaling their availability to females. The females turn on patterned flashes in reply.

Many of the events scheduled to celebrate Moraine State Park’s semi-centennial were canceled.

As a COVID-19 precaution, participation in the firefly program will be limited to 25 pre-registered visitors. Register by calling the park office at 724-368-8811. Visitors will be required to wear a mask or face covering and social distancing will be in effect.
