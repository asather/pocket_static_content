BNY Mellon appoints new CFO

Bank of New York Mellon Tuesday announced that effective immediately, Emily Portney will serve as the New York-based company’s new chief financial officer, succeeding Mike Santomassimo, who is leaving to join Wells Fargo in the same position in the fall.

Ms. Portney, 48, had been leading the client management, sales and service teams for BNY Mellon’s asset servicing business globally. She also oversaw the Americas region of the asset servicing business.

She reports to CEO Todd Gibbons.

Previously, Ms. Portney was CFO at Barclays International and spent 22 years at JPMorgan Chase, where she served as global head of clearing and collateral management as well as in other roles.

Mr. Santomassimo will report to his former boss, Charlie Scharf, who left BNY Mellon for San Francisco-based Wells Fargo last year.
