Commuters passing through Painters Run Road to expect more traffic changes

Commuters passing through Painters Run Road will experience a new set of changes as The Department of Public Works announced Wednesday that part of Painters Run Road has reopened – and another part has ‘unexpectedly’ closed. 

Part of the 1800 block of Painters Run Road in Scott Township and Upper St. Clair has reopened to traffic after completion of repairing Bridge No. 9 that started on July 20. 

Another part of Painters Run Road – just west of the intersection with McMillan Road in Upper St. Clair – has closed “unexpectedly” after crews discovering an unmarked underground mine void that would “affect the road’s stability” while originally completing drainage work there.

Public Works said in a press release that they will keep that section of road closed, “until it can ensure that it is safe for drivers,” with “no time table” for when the road will reopen. Representatives of the PA Department of Environmental Protection’s Bureau of Abandoned Mine Reclamation are also being brought in to “evaluate the mine void and potential remediation options.”

In the meantime, traffic will be redirected to use Bower Hill Road, Kelso Road and Rob Hollow Road.
