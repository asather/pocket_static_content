Pirates spring training: Derek Holland finds a groove in starter's role

PORT CHARLOTTE, Fla. — Derek Holland had a nearly perfect pitching performance Thursday — other than about five pitches to former Pirate Austin Meadows. The All-Star outfielder took the first pitch he saw from Holland and deposited it into the Rays bullpen for a home run to lead off the bottom half of the first inning.

The quick home run by Meadows put a slight crimp in Holland's plans for working on his craft against a fellow lefty.

"I'm not going to worry about that. It was the first pitch of the ballgame," Holland said. "We were supposed to go fastball away and curveball and slider. I want to work on those things, especially against a lefy hitter like that. He didn't even let me get that far."

Yandy Diaz followed with a fly ball that prospect Jared Oliva quickly caught in center field. The left-hander then got soft contact from Yoshi Tsutsugo and Jose Martinez to end the frame.

The Pirates lost the game 2-1 to take their Grapefruit League record to 2-11-1. Results during spring training don't count, but seeing Holland go four innings and only giving up one hit with two walks is encouraging.

"We did exactly what we wanted to do today," Holland said. "I felt with my fastball. My off-speed use was great today. The curveball and changeup were throwing guys off. We did a good job of making hitters uncomfortable."

Holland credited the work with catcher Luke Maile for making the game flow smoothly.

"We had a good plan today," Holland said. "Other than the home run, which a pitch down the middle, we did some good work against a strong lineup."

Remember those other four pitches that Holland didn't like as much? It was a walk against Meadows in the second inning.

"I'm more upset about the walks than anything," Holland said. "I want to make sure that I'm making my pitches and getting a feel for them.

In the field

Holland walked Meadows with one out after striking out Willy Adames on a changeup in the third inning. Then Erik Gonzalez, in his first game back from foot surgery, fielded a ball from Diaz to start a double play. Gonzalez showed the type of range and arm strength that makes him a valuable defender.

On the mound

Chad Kuhl, Kyle Crick and Nick Burdi each threw shutout innings. Burdi had two strikeouts and his fastball was in the high 90s. Burdi has seven strikeouts in 3 2/3 innings this spring.

Kuhl looked composed and threw his pitches effectively.

Crick joined Kuhl and Burdi with three perfect frames. Each pitcher is returning from injuries, so seeing them get on the mound and get off healthy is a good sign with three weeks until opening day.

At the plate

Josh Bell went 2-for-2 with two singles as the designated hitter. Bell has found a groove with four hits in his last seven at-bats and has reached base safely in five straight games.

Jason Martin showed a lot of speed on Thursday. He legged out a triple with ease in the fifth inning and later scored the only run of the game for the Pirates. Martin also stole a base.
