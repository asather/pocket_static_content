Mars school board terminates money plan

The Mars Area school board has terminated a 2014 interest rate management plan, saving more than $1 million and divesting themselves of a risky investment.

Interest rate management plans, also known as interest rate swaps or swaptions, were undertaken by school districts beginning in 2005 by a company that is no longer doing business in Pennsylvania.

“They are investments, in my opinion, that school districts should never get involved in again,” district Solicitor Tom King said at the March 3 board meeting. “This is a rare opportunity to get rid of these dogs while they are not barking.”

Mr. King added that the Butler Area School District had to pay $5,000 to get out of their swaptions.

Jill Swaney, business manager, said the savings will be applied to the district’s debt service annually. If the savings are $1 million, $80,000 a year will be applied to debt.

Financial advisers had told board members last month that they could save more than $1 million by terminating the swap this year but could save double that if they wait until next year.

Resident Bill Sommers told members that they should wait.

“It doesn’t make sense to not look at more savings,” he said. “There are ways to lock in a better savings.”

In other business, Superintendent Wesley Shipley said the superintendents in Butler County are working with the county health department on responses to COVID-19 should it spread to the county.

A letter, signed by all the superintendents, will be sent to families this week outlining procedures and including advice from school nurses and the Centers for Disease Control.

Mars Area has a pandemic response protocol in its emergency operations plan, he said.

“Use best judgment. Don’t come to school if you are sick,” he said.
