NYSE to begin process of delisting GNC shares

The New York Stock Exchange has notified Pittsburgh-based GNC Holdings, Inc. that it will begin the process of delisting the health and wellness retailer’s common stock from its exchange.

In a filing Tuesday, GNC said the NYSE told the company it is no longer suitable for listing now that it has filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy.

Stock exchange officials had earlier warned GNC of a possible delisting based on its average share price falling below $1 as well as insufficient capitalization.

GNC officials said in Tuesday’s filing that they do not intend to appeal the delisting and that it expects trading of its stock “will transition to the OTC [over-the-counter] Bulletin Board or “pink sheets” market shortly.
