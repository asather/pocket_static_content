Diocese of Pittsburgh cancels Masses ‘until further notice’

The Catholic Diocese of Pittsburgh has canceled all Masses in the diocese effective Monday amid concerns about the COVID-19 pandemic.

In a press release Sunday evening, the diocese said all Masses would be canceled “until further notice.”

“Although in many ways it saddens me to make this decision, given the best advice from health experts on federal, state and local levels, I feel it is a necessary one at this time,” Bishop David Zubick wrote in the release. “I’m counting on the understanding and cooperation of all in the Diocese of Pittsburgh as we pray for each other.”

Arrangements are being made to live stream future Masses, the release said. More information on how to proceed with sacraments like baptisms and confession would be announced Monday.

The cancellation comes during the Lenten season. The six-week period, which ends on Good Friday, is one of the most significant in the Catholic Church’s calendar.
