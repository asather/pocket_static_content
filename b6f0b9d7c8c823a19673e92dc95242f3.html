Allegheny County updates guidelines for likely arrival of COVID-19

The Allegheny County Health Department on Wednesday updated its guidelines and recommendations it hopes will minimize the spread of COVID-19.

So far, cases of the disease have yet to appear in the region, but the new measures are made "in light of the probability that the county will have presumptive positive cases."

The county is encouraging the most vulnerable groups — the elderly, those with underlying medical conditions, and those that are currently sick — to follow these steps to prevent the spread of the virus once it inevitably arrives in Western Pennsylvania:

● Avoid large events and mass gatherings, as well as other settings where you would have close contact (within six feet) with a large group of people.

● Continue proactive hygiene measures such as washing your hands frequently, covering coughs and sneezes with a tissue, and frequently cleaning highly-touched surfaces.

● Stay home if you are sick and call your primary care provider to discuss next steps.

The health department said it "will continue its surveillance and containment measures, which include issuing orders to separate potentially sick individuals from non-sick individuals as necessary."

For businesses in the region, the health department issued the following reminders:

● Encourage sick employees to stay home

● Emphasize cough and sneeze etiquette and hand hygiene

● Perform routine cleaning of frequently-touched surfaces and objects

● Provide disposable wipes for employees to use on frequently-touched surfaces.

Once a positive case of COVID-19 has been confirmed, the county said there will be a coordinated effort with state and federal health agencies "to implement procedures to mitigate additional exposure."

Those procesures may include additional social distancing tactics such as school closures and postponing large gatherings, the county said.

As of 2:30 p.m. Wednesday, there were 15 cases of COVID-19 in Pennsylvania. Thirteen are presumed, two are confirmed.

For more information on COVID-19, go to www.alleghenycounty.us/coronavirus.
