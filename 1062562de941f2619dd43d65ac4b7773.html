Four seriously hurt in hit-and-run crash involving SUV, Amish buggy in Crawford County

SPARTA, Pa.— A hit-and-run crash involving an SUV and a horse-drawn Amish buggy left two adults and two young children in the buggy seriously injured, Pennsylvania State Police said.

The accident in Sparta occurred shortly before 7:30 p.m. Sunday.

The SUV struck the buggy from behind and then fled the scene, authorities said. The SUV was later located, authorities said Monday, but further details were not disclosed.

Four people in the buggy — a 24-year-old man, a 23-year-old woman, a year-old boy and a newborn — were all being treated at hospitals for undisclosed injuries. Their names have not been released.

The cause of the crash remains under investigation. It wasn’t clear if any charges would be filed.
