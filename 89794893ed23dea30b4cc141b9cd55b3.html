Cranberry native David Testa and his short film, 'Balloon' take home Emmy awards from virtual ceremony

Cranberry native David Testa won a Television Academy Foundation award Saturday for co-writing a short film, “Balloon.” The Seneca Valley High School graduate, who also attended Duquesne University, was a winner with co-author/director Jeremy Merrifield and Christina Cha.

“Balloon,” which tells the story of a bullied teenager, won its Emmy in the Drama Series category. The three filmmakers submitted it as a senior thesis at the American Film Institute.

In addition, the Seymour Bricker Humanitarian Award, a $4,000 cash prize given to a College Television Award-winning project that best highlights a humanitarian concern, was awarded to Testa, Cha and Merrifield for “Balloon.” This award was created over two decades ago by the late entertainment attorney Seymour Bricker.

The awards were originally to be presented by celebrities in a red carpet gala, but were postponed until June due to COVID-19. It was then apparent the awards would be held virtually for the first time in the Academy’s 40-year history.

Jimmy Fallon, Reese Witherspoon, Andre Holland and Amy Poehler were among those involved in the presentation.
