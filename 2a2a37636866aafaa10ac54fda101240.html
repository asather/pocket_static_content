Online ticket scammer in Indiana, Pa., to pay refunds

An area company that scammed consumers by selling them fake tickets to events and concerts will provide $10,765 in refunds under a settlement announced Thursday with Pennsylvania’s attorney general.

The settlement with Ticket Victory, based in Indiana, Pa., also permanently shuts down the company and prohibits its owner, Brian Plowcha, from operating any online ticket business.

“Selling fake tickets online is unethical and illegal, and businesses [that] engage in these practices will be prosecuted by my office,” attorney general Josh Shapiro said in a statement.

The deal covers all known victims of Ticket Victory, which operated from 2015 to 2018.

It also provides for a 60-day window for any additional claims by unknown customers who paid for tickets and did not receive them, or for tickets that were not valid.

Those consumers should file a complaint — by Sept. 21 — at www.attorneygeneral.gov or by calling 800-441-2555.
