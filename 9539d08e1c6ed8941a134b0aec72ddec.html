Pitt will begin its 2020 ACC schedule without an important piece of its rotation.

Forward Au’Diese Toney will miss the Panthers’ game Saturday against Wake Forest after injuring his left elbow in a practice earlier in the week.

After a slow start to the season, Toney has been strong in the past five games, resembling the tenacious, aggressive player he was last season much more than he did in the first eight games. In that time, Toney averaged 8.8 points and five rebounds per game while playing no fewer than 25 minutes in one of those contests. He has started eight of Pitt’s 13 games this season.

For the season, the 6-foot-6 sophomore is the team’s second-leading rebounder (at 4.7 per game) and is third in offensive rebounding percentage (getting 8.8 percent of his team’s missed shots while he’s on the court).

A statement from the program didn’t specify how long he is expected to be out or the exact nature of the injury.
