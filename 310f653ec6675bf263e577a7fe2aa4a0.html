Ambridge interim police Chief Mark Romutis dead

Mark Romutis, Ambridge’s interim chief of police, died Sunday evening, officials said.

Beaver County officials said Chief Romutis was diagnosed with COVID-19  but had reportedly been cleared to return to duty, according to Post-Gazette news partner KDKA-TV. He returned to work on Thursday.

Chief Romutis’ cause of death was not immediately clear.

“We are saddened to hear the unexpected passing of Ambridge Borough's Interim Police Chief Mark Romutis,” a joint statement from the Beaver County Commissioners read. “We extend our condolences to his family, friends, co-workers, and the Ambridge community in general.”

Another Beaver County police chief, Aliquippa’s Rob Sealock in, died on Saturday.

“It's been a very sad and difficult few days for us in Beaver County, losing two dedicated police chiefs and even better people,” county Commissioner Jack Manning said on Sunday. “Ambridge Chief Romutis and Aliquippa Chief Sealock will be greatly missed.”

“This is a difficult time for all Beaver County's law enforcement agencies, and with the loss of two leaders in such a short period of time, we extend our sympathy and continued support to all of them as well,” the commissioners’ statement read.
