Art Rooney II: Steelers still have work left to get under cap

Steelers president Art Rooney II said his team is done releasing players for now, but a few more contracts have to be restructured in order to be in compliance with the salary cap by 4 p.m. Wednesday.

The Steelers have already shaved approximately $35 million off the salary cap by restructuring five contracts, releasing three players and with the retirement of guard Ramon Foster.

While the Steelers are not yet under the cap of $198.2 million, Rooney said they will be in compliance by Wednesday and could still be position to sign outside players in free agency.

“I think we have one or two more things to fall into place,” Rooney said Tuesday in an interview with the Post-Gazette. “There will be another move or two that we’ll have to talk about in the next 24 hours.”

One of the ways the Steelers could do that is by signing defensive end Cam Heyward to a contract extension that would lessen his cap hit of $13,251,250 in 2020. But any deal with Heyward is not expected to come until after Wednesday. They could also restructure the deals of high-priced veterans David DeCastro, Maurkice Pouncey and Stephon Tuitt.

So far, the Steelers have converted the 2020 base salaries of five players — Ben Roethlisberger, Vance McDonald, Joe Haden, Steven Nelson and Chris Boswell — into signing bonuses, pushing their cap money into future seasons.

“We expect we will be there,” Rooney said. “I think we’ll be fine by the required time.”

With the loss of Foster and departure of nose tackle Javon Hargrave, who signed with the Philadelphia Eagles, the Steelers could be in position to sign an offensive lineman and/or defensive lineman in free agency. Restructuring McDonald’s contract is a sign they are not interested in a top-tier tight end in free agency.

“Obviously we have our own players we’re still interested in,” Rooney said. “We’ll be looking at a combination that if we can’t sign one of our players, then we’ll look outside. But I do think we’ll be in position to sign a few players, whether they’re ours or coming from another team.”

Rooney, though, said the Steelers accomplished their No. 1 goal of the offseason — franchise tagging outside linebacker Bud Dupree — even though it came at the expense of losing Hargrave and having to cut inside linebacker Mark Barron.

“We’re glad we were able to do that,” Rooney said. “That was an important piece of the puzzle — keep Bud in the fold and keep that defense together as much as we can. That was our Number 1 priority.

“Every year you have to make decisions like that. We would’ve loved to keep Javon, but with the contracts we have with Cam and Tuitt, Javon would have been almost a luxury. Not many teams are paying three guys at that level on a defensive line. It’s disappointing we lost him but, under the circumstances, not unexpected.”
