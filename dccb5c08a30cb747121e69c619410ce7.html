Man acquitted in Wilkinsburg massacre ordered detained on federal gun charge

A federal magistrate judge on Monday ordered Cheron Shelton, recently acquitted in the killing of five people in Wilkinsburg, to be detained pending trial on a gun charge that is likely to send him to a U.S. prison.

U.S. Magistrate Judge Lisa Pupo Lenihan said Shelton will remain locked up as a danger to the community and as a flight risk.

Shelton, 33, is under indictment for possession of a rifle and ammunition by a felon. Prosecutors on Monday filed images of the weapon and bullets as court exhibits.

An Allegheny County Common Pleas jury acquitted him on Feb. 14 in the mass shooting at a Wilkinsburg cookout in 2016 that left five dead and three injured. Charges against his co-defendant, Robert Thomas, were dismissed.
