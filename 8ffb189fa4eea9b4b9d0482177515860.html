Allegheny County reports 68 new cases of COVID-19, no new deaths

Allegheny County is reporting 68 new cases of COVID-19 out of 910 tests for Saturday, a 7.4 percent positivity rate. The county is not reporting any new deaths.

Of Saturday’s cases, which come from tests taken between August 26 and Sept. 4, 55 are confirmed and 13 are probable.

Ages of those testing positive in the last 24 hours range from 6 to 87, with a median age of 27.

With the addition of Saturday’s new cases, Allegheny County has seen 10,709 cases of COVID-19, 1,026 hospitalizations and 349 deaths.

Statewide, Pennsylvania is reporting 963 new positive cases, bringing the state total to 138,625. Of the newest cases, 172 are in Western Pennsylvania. Allegheny County reported the highest increase for Saturday, followed by Butler with 31 new cases.

Pennsylvania reported 18 additional deaths — six of which were in Western Pennsylvania — bringing the state death toll to 7,760. Beaver county had three of area’s deaths, Armstrong county had two deaths and Lawrence county had one death.

Currently, there are 66 people in the county hospitalized for COVID-19 and 505 people across Pennsylvania, based on the latest figures from the state.

In total, Allegheny County has reported 1,000 people have been hospitalized for the virus since the pandemic began.
