Mayor Peduto to create permanent LGBT Commission

Pittsburgh Mayor Bill Peduto said Tuesday that he will ask City Council to form a permanent commission to advise the city on gay, lesbian and other related issues.

The commission will replace the LGBTQIA+ Advisory Council that the mayor created in 2016.

The mayor’s office said the new commission would have 11 members, meet monthly and provide policy recommendations to city government on ways to address challenges that the Pittsburgh LGBTQIA+ community faces.

The group’s acronym stands for lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer or questioning, intersex and asexual. The “+” symbol typically signals further inclusiveness.

“The City of Pittsburgh has long been a leader in protecting and preserving LGBTQIA+ rights, but our city, state and country still have a long way to go. Placing this commission into City Code will re-emphasize our commitment to fighting for the human rights deserved by all,” Mr. Peduto said.

The commission’s role would include regularly engaging with Pittsburgh’s LGBTQIA+ community and developing outreach and advocacy programming.

The group would also assist in the creation of cultural competency training opportunities for city departments.

If the commission is approved by the council, the mayor will ask residents who are interested in joining the commission to contact his office.
