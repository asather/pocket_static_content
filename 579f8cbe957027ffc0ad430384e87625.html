Rage Against The Machine to play Pittsburgh on July 31

Rage Against The Machine will bring their politically charged brand of hard rock to Pittsburgh on July 31 at the PPG Paints Arena, the band announced Monday afternoon.

The band reunited late last year, after a nine-year hiatus, and subsequently announced dates at major 2020 festivals including Coachella and Firefly. The band will play 40-dates on the “Public Service Announcement“ tour in North America and Europe, with hip-hop super duo Run The Jewels opening. Tickets will go on sale Thursday, according to the band’s website.

The seven-month tour begins March 26 in El Paso, Texas, and concludes Sept. 12 in Vienna. It is the first time the iconic Los Angeles band has toured together since 2011.
