Pittsburgh police officers self isolating after potential exposure to virus

An undisclosed number of Pittsburgh police officers from Zone 2 are self isolating after exposure to someone with symptoms similar to COVID-19.

Police were potentially exposed to the virus while responding Monday afternoon to a minor car crash involving two vehicles in Market Square Downtown, city police spokesman Chris Togneri said. 

One of the drivers involved in the collision showed symptoms resembling COVID-19, police said.

The driver was taken to a local hospital for testing.

“Out of an abundance of caution, officers who were at the crash scene were sent home to self-isolate until the matter is further evaluated,” Mr. Togneri said in a police report. 

None of the officers are showing symptoms, police said.
