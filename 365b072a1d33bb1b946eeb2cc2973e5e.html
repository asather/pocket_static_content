Latest on COVID-19: Number of confirmed cases rises in the region, Pa.

Allegheny County health officials reported Monday that the confirmed number of COVID-19 cases has risen to 642, up by 37 from the preceding day — one of the smallest increases in the past five days for the Pittsburgh area.

Health officials said that of those 642 cases of the viral disease, a total of 92 people have needed hospitalization, past or present. That was an increase of five hospitalizations from Sunday’s reported numbers.

Statewide, the Department of Health said Monday that the number of confirmed COVID-19 cases was nearing the 13,000 mark at 12,980, rising in one day by 1,470 positive cases. Reported deaths in the state increased by 12 to 162, according to state health officials.

In Western Pennsylvania, 4 deaths have been reported in Allegheny County, 6 in Beaver County, 2 in Butler County, 2 in Lawrence County and 1 in Fayette County, according to the latest numbers.

“The continued rise in cases combined with our increasing deaths from COVID-19 reflects the seriousness of this situation,” state Secretary of Health Dr. Rachel Levine said this past weekend. “We need everyone to listen to the orders in place and to stay calm, stay home and stay safe.

“We know that these prolonged mitigation effects have been difficult for everyone, but it is essential that everyone follows these orders and does not go out unless they absolutely must.”

Philadelphia County leads the state in the number of cases with 3,611 and in deaths at 28.

Throughout the rest of Western Pennsylvania: Armstrong County has 13 cases; Beaver, 86; Butler, 91; Cambria, 7; Clarion, 6; Clearfield, 7; Crawford, 9; Erie, 20; Fayette, 29; Forest, 4; Greene, 12; Indiana, 17; Lawrence, 24; Mercer, 18; Somerset, 6; Venango, 5; Washington, 53; and Westmoreland, 157. Jefferson County has no cases.
