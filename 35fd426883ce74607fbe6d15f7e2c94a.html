More sports events suspended amid COVID-19 pandemic

Numerous high school, college and professional sports leagues joined the NBA in halting their activities because of the coronavirus pandemic Thursday. 

• In Pennsylvania, the PIAA announced it will postpone its winter sports championships by two weeks.

• The NHL announced Thursday afternoon that its season is paused and that teams are advised not to hold morning skates, practices and team meetings. 

• Multiple major college conferences — including the Big Ten, Big 12, Southeastern, American Athletic, Duquesne’s Atlantic 10 and ACC — announced that their basketball tournaments have been canceled.  

• Major League Soccer followed with an announcement shortly thereafter that its season is suspended for the next 30 days. 

• The Riverhounds’ USL Championship has since announced it will join its upper division counterpart. 

• The PGA has said its events will continue without fans through the Valero Texas Open. 

• MLB haa not yet announced whether it will join MLS, the NHL and the NBA in suspending its season. The NBA made its announcement Wednesday night after a Utah Jazz player tested positive for the coronavirus.
