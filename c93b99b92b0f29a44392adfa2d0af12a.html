Firefighters from Beaver, Allegheny counties battle fire at Sukup Steel Structures in Ambridge

A major fire at a steel plant in Beaver County has been raging for hours Saturday morning as firefighters battle to control the blaze.

The fire at Sukup Steel Structures on 14th Street in Ambridge was reported around 4:50 a.m. and remained active nearly six hours later, according to a Beaver County 911 dispatcher. It led to a general alarm and drew multiple fire departments.

No injuries have been reported.

Sukup is a subsidiary of Iowa-based Sukup Manufacturing Co.

“As a family-owned business, first and foremost we are relieved that no employees were injured in this fire,” the company said in a statement. “We will be communicating instructions directly with employees and assessing damage to the property.”

Firefighters from Allegheny and Beaver counties were on scene. The factory is near the Ohio River and the border between the counties.

Sukup Steel Structures designs, engineers, and manufactures systems steel construction buildings.
