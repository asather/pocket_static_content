Roof collapses at dilapidated church in Brighton Heights

The roof of a Brighton Heights church collapsed Monday, according to KDKA-TV, the Pittsburgh Post-Gazette’s news partner.

No injuries have been reported, but the collapse sent debris onto McClure Avenue.

It’s unclear if the structure is still in use. Signage indicates it was once used as Christian Fellowship Center and the Church of God in Christ.

McClure Street has been closed off while the structure’s stability is assessed, KDKA said.

The cause of the collapse is undetermined.
