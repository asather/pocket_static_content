WPIAL 2A-1A roundup: Springdale remains undefeated

Springdale has a legend in the making as the surprising Dynamos remain unbeaten in the Class 1A Eastern Conference. Springdale (4-0,4-0) continued its winning ways by outsourcing host Greensburg Central Catholic, 45-22, Friday night.

Dynamos quarterback Legend Ausk completed 10-of-15 passes for 197 yards and two touchdowns. Ausk sealed the victory with a 13-yard touchdown run in the fourth quarter. Dimitri Fritch was Ausk’s top receiver with four catches for 107 yards and one touchdown.

Logan Dexter contributed to a consistent Springdale ground game with 91 yards on 17 carries, good for two touchdowns.

Unranked Springdale faces a huge challenge on Oct. 9 when it play host to No. 2-ranked Jeannette.

More Class 1A

Rochester 47, Our Lady Sacred Heart 21: The No. 5 Rams (4-0, 4-0) won their showdown against the visiting No. 3 Chargers (3-1, 3-1) in the Big Seven Conference. Rashawn Redi led Rochester with 166 yards rushing on nine carries. Reid scored six touchdowns, including one that went for 75 yards. Reid also scored on a 25-yarder with 43 seconds left in the first half to give the Rams a commanding 35-14 lead.

Shenango 48, Burgettstown 7: The No. 4 Wildcats (4-0, 4-0) kept pace with Rochester in the Big Seven as they routed visiting Burgettstown (1-3, 1-3). Reis Watkins, the leading rusher in the WPIAL, scored on runs of 54, 15 and 11 yards. Tino Campoli threw three touchdown passes, completing six of nine attempts for 143 yards.

Washington 41, Beth-Center 6: Mike Allen led a balanced Little Prexie attack as No. 3 Washington (3-1, 3-1) routed the Bulldogs (1-2, 1-3) in the Century Conference. Washington held a 13-6 halftime lead before scoring two touchdowns in each of the final two quarters. Six different players scored touchdowns for Washington.

California 51, Avella 16: Jaeden Zuzak rushed for 268 yards and scored five touchdowns as the Trojans (3-0, 3-0) defeated the host Eagles (3-1, 3-1) in a showdown for first place in the Tri-County South Conference. Quarterback Damani Stafford added 114 yards rushing and two touchdowns. Nathan O’Savage also caught a touchdown pass from Stafford, who completed 3 of 4 passes for 58 yards.

Fort Cherry 38, Northgate 8: The Rangers (1-3, 1-3) routed the host Flames (0-4, 0-4) in the Big Seven Conference. Maddox Truschel completed 10 of 12 passes for 246 yards and two touchdowns for Fort Cherry.

Mapletown 30, Monessen 6: The Maples (1-3, 1-3) downed the Greyhounds (0-4, 0-4) in the Tri-County South Conference as Landan Stevenson rushed for 201 yards on 21 carries while scoring a touchdown for Mapletown. The Maples’ Max Vanata also completed three of five pass attempts for 140 yards and two touchdowns.

Class 2A

Frazier 40, Waynesburg 14: Kenny Fine scored on runs of 70, 35, and 35 yards to lead the Commodores (2-1, 2-2) to an impressive home win against the Raiders (0-4, 0-4) in the Century Conference. Frazier outgained Waynesburg, 382 to 251, in total offensive yardage.

Freedom 14, Laurel 7: Reiker Welling caught a pair of scoring passes from Cole Beck as the visiting Bulldogs (2-2, 2-2) downed the Spartans (2-2, 2-2) in the Midwestern Conference. Welling caught scoring passes covering 72 and 19 yards as Freedom rallied from a 7-0 deficit.

Brentwood 40, Carlynton 21: The Spartans (2-0, 3-1) easily won despite Aiden Wardzinski, the second-leading rusher in the WPIAL, not playing in the offensive backfield. Freshman O’Malley Daly picked up the slack, rushing for 187 yards and three touchdowns during a road win against the Cougars (1-2, 1-3) in the Three Rivers Conference. Amire Spencer contributed 87 yards rushing and two touchdowns for Brentwood.

Sto-Rox 23, South Side Beaver 15: The host Vikings (1-1, 3-1) were led by Josh Jenkins who completed 18 of 29 passes for 227 yards and two touchdowns as Sto-Rox topped the Rams (1-2, 1-3) in the Three Rivers Conference.

Chartiers-Houston 48, Charleroi 13: Jimmy Sadler rushed for 220 yards on nine carries while scoring four touchdowns to lead the Buccaneers (2-1,2-2) to victory at Charleroi (1-3, 1-3) in the Century Conference.
