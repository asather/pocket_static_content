Pirates hire Mike Rabelo as assistant hitting coach

The Pirates announced on Tuesday that they’ve hired Mike Rabelo as an assistant hitting coach under Rick Eckstein.

Rabelo, 39, spent this past season as the manager of Double-A Erie in the Tigers organization, leading the SeaWolves to a 77-61 record.

Rabelo has managed six seasons at the minor league level, going 358-274 and posting winning records in all but one season. All of those came with Detroit.

In 2017, Rabelo was named the Midwest League’s Manager of the Year after his West Michigan Whitecaps won a league-best 91 games.

Rabelo played professionally for 10 seasons. Detroit drafted him in the fourth round in 2001. He played parts of three seasons in the big leagues as a catcher with Detroit (2006-07) and Florida (2008).
