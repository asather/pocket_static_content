Allegheny County reaches 50 deaths

Allegheny County has reached 50 deaths — 46 confirmed and four probable — as it continued to battle the COVID-19 pandemic, according to the latest numbers released Sunday.

The number of deaths is three more than Saturday’s figures. The county now has 1,035 cases — 1,019 confirmed and 16 probable of COVID-19. That total is 26 more than Saturday’s 1,009.

Statewide, the Pennsylvania Dept. of Health reported an additional 1,628 cases, bringing the total to 31,069.

The state also reported 80 new deaths Saturday, a steep increase from the 49 deaths reported Friday. The state has recorded a total of 836 deaths.

The county is also including probable cases in total counts to better assess the level of community spread. A case is probable when the person displays symptoms of COVID-19 and has had close contact with a confirmed positive case but has not had a test themselves.

On Saturday, Allegheny County added eight additional nursing and personal care homes to a list tracking the number of cases among residents and employees. The total facilities jumped from 19 Friday to 27 Saturday and the number of cases among residents jumped from 134 to 159.

In Beaver County, the number of cases among residents in three facilities increased, from 67 Friday to 166 Saturday.

In Pennsylvania, the total number of facilities being considered jumped 26, from 321 Friday to 347 Saturday. There were 4,185 cases reported among residents, an increase of 469, and 459 deaths, an increase of 61.

On Sunday, the county reported two new hospitalizations, bringing the total to 180 past or present hospitalizations. Of those 180, 67 patients have been admitted to the intensive care unit and 42 required medical ventilation.

Since the first case was confirmed on March 14, Allegheny County has administered more than 12,195 tests.
