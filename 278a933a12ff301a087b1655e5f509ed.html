At National Cemetery of the Alleghenies, an even more somber salute to U.S. heroes

A brief ceremony was held Sunday at the National Cemetery of the Alleghenies in Cecil to honor the men and women who gave their lives serving their country.

The Memorial Day event didn’t see the number of visitors it usually has for the annual occasion due to the ongoing pandemic. 

After a brief speech, there was a wreath-laying ceremony, a moment of silence and the playing of “Taps.” Only cemetery staff participated in the ceremony. Visitors were allowed to peruse the grounds of the 292-acre cemetery, but were held to strict social distancing guidelines and mask wearing.
