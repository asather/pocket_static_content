Jury for Wilkinsburg slaying remains at six people so far

No jurors were seated Thursday in the case against two men accused of killing five people and an unborn child at a Wilkinsburg cookout in 2016.

Twelve potential panelists were interviewed Thursday afternoon, but none were selected to sit on the capital case against Cheron Shelton, 32, of Lincoln-Lemington and Robert Thomas, 31, of Homewood, who face charges of criminal homicide, conspiracy and related counts, stemming from the March 9, 2016, shooting.

Jury selection in the case, to be heard by Allegheny County Common Pleas Judge Edward J. Borkowski began on Monday, when two jurors were chosen. Between Tuesday and Wednesday, four more people were selected.

The breakdown thus far includes three men and three women.

Testimony in the case is expected to begin the week of Feb. 3. The jurors will not be sequestered during the pendency of the trial.

Police said that Thomas used a handgun and Shelton used an assault-style rifle to herd the victims, who were enjoying an unseasonably warm evening, toward the back of the Franklin Avenue home. A total of 49 shots were fired.

Killed were siblings Jerry Michael Shelton, 35, Brittany Powell, 27, and Chanetta Powell, 25, who was eight months pregnant; and their cousins Tina Shelton, 37, and Shada Mahone, 26. Three others were wounded including a man who was paralyzed.

If a jury finds the defendants guilty of first-degree murder, the case will move into a penalty phase during which the panel will have to decide between the death penalty or a mandatory prison term of life without parole.

Thus far, both the prosecution and defense have used four peremptory strikes each, in which they can eliminate a potential juror for any reason without explanation.

The prosecution has 16 strikes remaining, as do both defendants.
