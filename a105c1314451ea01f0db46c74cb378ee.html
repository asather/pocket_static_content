Executive says Nova Chemicals not for sale

A top executive of the investment firm that controls Nova Chemicals said the company is not for sale at this time.

Reports surfaced last year that Nova’s owner, Mubadala Investment of the United Arab Emirates, was exploring its options including a sale.

In a video interview with Bloomberg, Musabbeh Al-Kaabi, chief executive of petroleum and refining for Mubadala, said the firm doesn’t think it’s the right time to sell.

Its owners are “very proud” of Nova, which has delivered “excellent operational performance” and is “enjoying a leading petrochemicals position in North America,” he said.

Nova, based in Calgary, Canada, has about 250 employees at an executive center in Moon, and 200 at a plant and technology center in Monaca, Beaver County.

It makes plastics used in packaging, industrial molding and other applications; and foams used in construction materials, coffee cups, automobile components and other products.

Sales totaled $4.5 billion in 2018.

In the Bloomberg interview, Mr. Al-Kaabi acknowledged Nova is currently making a “massive investment” in capital expenditures. Among its projects is a $2.2 billion facility expansion in Sarnia, Ontario.

Last week, Nova said it’s selling its 50% stake in a joint venture in the Texas Gulf Coast to venture partner Borealis, an Austrian business.

Terms weren’t disclosed.

Mubadala Investment is a government holding firm based in Abu Dhabi.

In June, Reuters reported that Chevron Phillips Chemical offered $15 billion for Nova. The companies at the time did not comment on that report.
