Nursing homes restricting visitors following federal mandate

Nursing homes around the region are halting visitations following a mandate from the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services.

The mandate came as President Donald Trump declared the COVID-19 pandemic a national emergency on Friday. Seema Verna, CMS administrator, announced Friday that CMS would issue guidance to  nursing homes to restrict all visitors and non-essential personnel to slow the spread of the virus. Local nursing homes are now following suit. 

HCR ManorCare and Concordia Lutheran Ministries, each of which have multiple locations in the Pittsburgh region, have announced they are halting visitors with few exceptions, such as end-of-life situations.

Nursing homes also announced they are taking measures to screen visitors for symptoms, recent travel history and potential contact with people who may have COVID-19.

For some homes, the restrictions have gone into effect immediately. Others are being rolled out over the weekend.
