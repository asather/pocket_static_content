State: 2 new COVID-19 deaths in Western Pa. nursing homes; County says 3 deaths at Kane Glen Hazel home

The state reported 543 new cases of COVID-19 and 24 deaths, including two in Western Pennsylvania, according to the latest figures released Monday by the health department.

Both deaths — one in Allegheny County and one in Armstrong County — occurred in nursing/long-term care homes, according to the state’s data. However, the county indicated Monday that the Kane Community Living Centers’ Glen Hazel facility has had 16 deaths, which is three more than it reported on Friday.

Allegheny County reported eight new cases of COVID-19, giving it a total of 1,511 cases — 1,439 confirmed and 72 probable — since the first case was reported March 14.

A case is probable “when the person displays symptoms of COVID-19 and has had close contact with a confirmed positive case but has not had a positive test themselves,” according to the county.

There are now 123 deaths blamed on COVID-19 in Allegheny County — 113 confirmed and 10 probable. Ninety-four of those deaths have occurred at nursing or long-term care homes.

The county said 274 people past or present have required hospitalization. There have been 116 people admitted to an intensive care unit, and 66 of them have required mechanical ventilation. So far, there have been 21,115 tests given.

No information has been provided about the latest Armstrong County death, but according to state data, all three nursing home deaths have occurred at one facility. In total, Armstrong County has had five deaths.

On Monday, there were 543 new cases of people infected with the novel coronavirus, bringing the commonwealth's reported total to 57,154. The state also reported 24 new deaths, bringing its total to 3,731.
