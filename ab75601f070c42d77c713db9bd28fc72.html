15 local students vie in August Wilson Monologue contest Monday

The Pittsburgh Regional August Wilson Monologue Competition has a new venue in its 12th year, heading to Lawrenceville.

On Monday, 15 students will perform monologues of 1 to 3 minutes from the works the late Pulitzer Prize-winning Pittsburgh playwright. There are cash awards of $500, $250 and $100 for the top three in the contest, and the Point Park University Conservatory of Performing Arts will offer scholarships of $10,000, $7,500 and $5,000.

Presented by Bill Nunn Theatre Outreach Project, Pittsburgh’s competition is 7 p.m. Monday, March 9, at Row House Cinema, 4115 Butler St. Lawrenceville.

Winners are eligible to attend the national program, May 6 at Broadway’s August Wilson Theatre and produced by Kenny Leon’s True Colors Theatre Company and Jujamcyn Theaters. Along with Wilson’s hometown of Pittsburgh, participating cities are Atlanta, Boston, Buffalo, Chicago, Dallas, Greensboro, N.C., Los Angeles, New Haven, Conn., New York, Norfolk, Va., Portland, Ore., San Diego and Seattle.
