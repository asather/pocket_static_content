Gov. Wolf revises list of which businesses qualify as "life-sustaining," delays enforcement

Gov. Tom Wolf on Friday updated a list of which businesses must close their physical operations to stop the spread of COVID-19 and delayed enforcement of the closures.

The governor’s order, which went into effect Thursday evening and now begins enforcement Monday at 8 a.m., says all “non-life-sustaining” businesses should stop their physical operations and work remotely if possible.

Businesses in accounting, payroll administration and tax preparation are now permitted to keep physical locations operating, as are insurance-related businesses, according to a new list from of the governor.

Insurance agencies and brokers are not to conduct in-person sales, however.

All natural resources and mining industries are also now to remain in operation. The original list released on Thursday stated those involved in forestry, logging and mining were to shut down their in-person facilities.

The announcement was met with confusion from workers and business owners as to what qualifies as life-sustaining when many businesses are part of a complex supply chain.

If business who have been told to cease physical operation believe they are life-sustaining, they can fill out a waiver arguing that they must remain in operation.

The waiver from the Department of Community & Economic Development asks questions about how businesses meet the definition of life-sustaining, if they have a plan to “meet CDC recommended guidelines to maintain employee safety” and how many employees must be on-location to provide critical work.

The new list was updated following “conversations with businesses, stakeholders and individuals,” according to a release from the governor’s office.

Enforcement of the closures was originally supposed to begin on Saturday, but was delayed due to a high volume of waiver requests, a release from the governor’s office said.
