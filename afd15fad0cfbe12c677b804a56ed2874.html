Pittsburgh Cultural Trust shuts down performances, exhibitions through April 6

The Pittsburgh Cultural Trust is shutting down all planned performances, exhibitions, films, and events through April 6, due to concerns over COVID-19.

In an announcement at 3 p.m. Friday, the Trust, which owns or operates many venues in the Pittsburgh Cultural District, Downtown, said it would cancel or postpone the following events to ensure the safety of guests, staff and artists, while they continue “to follow the leadership of our public health and government officials.

The following events, including the Pittsburgh Humanities Festival (March 21-22) and Bookish in the ‘Burgh (March 27-28) are among those affected. Visit trustarts.org for ticketing policies and further information:

• “The Band’s Visit,” March 13-15

• “The Queen of Cardistry” (Liberty Magic), March 13-29

• Nathaniel Rateliff, March 16

• “Keller Williams’ “Grateful Grass,” March 19

• Sh!!faced Shakespeare, March 20

• “An Evening with Ira Glass,” March 21

• “Diary of a Wombat, “March 22-29

• Blair Imani, March 22

• Rocío Molina, March 25

• Siegfried Tieber in “Sixty-Seven Keys,” April 1-6

• “Pss Pss,” April 3-4

• Banff Mountain Film Festival, April 3-5

Among the films at the Trust’s Harris Theater that fall into the time span are "Punk the Capital" (March 14-15), "Extra Ordinary" (March 20-23) and "Corpus Christi" ((March 27-April 2). Current exhibitions are "Voids, etc," 707 Gallery; "This is Not Conclusive / Ovo nije konačna," 937 Gallery, "Trumpet of the Tulips," SPACE, and "Oscillations and Complex Meshes," Wood Street Galleries. Greer Cabaret programs and Backstage Bar operations are also suspended.
