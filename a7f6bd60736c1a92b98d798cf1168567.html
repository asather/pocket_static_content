COVID-19 Update: Pa. reports 561 new cases, Pittsburgh region 126

The Pennsylvania health department on Tuesday reported 561 new cases of COVID-19, with the Pittsburgh region accounting for 126 of those latest infections.

Allegheny County reported 58 cases, bringing its total to nearly 10,000 — 9,990 cases — of the virus since the pandemic began in mid-March. The specific breakdown of data on the county’s newest cases have not yet been released. With the addition of the latest infections, the state now has 130,035 total cases.

The state health department has also reported an increase of 26 deaths for Tuesday, bringing the state death toll to 7,605.

Western Pennsylvania had four of those reported deaths, with Washington County reporting two, and Beaver and Erie counties reporting one each.

Figures on any latest deaths due to COVID-19 has not yet been released, but as of Monday, the county had 306 total deaths as a result of the virus. 

Based on the most recent data from the state, there are currently 543 people hospitalized for COVID-19 — 93 of which are in Allegheny County.
