Tram's Kitchen is not closed. It's in the movies

Rest easy, East Enders: Beloved Vietnamese restaurant Tram’s Kitchen is not closed. It’s just getting its close-up.

A Reddit post Tuesday morning caused an online panic when it stated that Tram’s had apparently closed and been replaced with a physical therapy and wellness center. The evidence? A large new sign.

Turns out the new sign was part of a faux facade (faux-cade?) put up for Tuesday night filming of “Sweetgirl,” a Netflix movie starring Jason Momoa and Marisa Tomei.

Tram’s was open for lunch business as usual on Tuesday afternoon. A sandwich board sign out front made it clear: “TRAM’S IS OPEN.”

Le Thao and his wife, Truong Bac Cuc, came to Pittsburgh in 1993 and opened Tram’s in 1997 at 4039 Penn Ave. in Lawrenceville. The little restaurant and its authentic Vietnamese food quickly built a cult following.

The restaurant is named for the couple’s daughter, Le Dinh Ngoc Tram, who had to remain behind in Vietnam when they first emigrated to the United States in 1992. An April 2000 Post-Gazette story chronicled the family’s effort to reunite.

The rest of the family went first to California to live with relatives there who sponsored them. They moved to Pittsburgh in 1993 because their younger son, Thien, needed a kidney transplant.

Finally, in April 2000, Tram and her family succeeded in untangling a ball of red tape between the two countries and were reunited here in Pittsburgh.
