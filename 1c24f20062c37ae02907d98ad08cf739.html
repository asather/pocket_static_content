People on the move: 5/18/2020

Associations & Organizations

Jay N. Silberblatt of Silberblatt Mermelstein PC was named vice president of the Pennsylvania Bar Association. Mr. Silberblatt is the vice chair of the PBA Review and Certifying Board. He serves on the PBA Minority Bar Committee Executive Council and is co-chair of the committee’s Diversity Summit Subcommittee.

Education

Duquesne University announced new hires for its Small Business Development Center: Cynthia Oliver, business consultant; Gary Plourde, business consultant/technology commercialization; and Lisa Walker, administrative assistant. 

Health

Allegheny Health Network said Louise Urban, president of AHN’s Jefferson and Canonsburg hospitals, has been promoted to senior vice president of operations for the network. Other leadership changes at AHN include expanded roles for Krista Bragg, chief operations officer for Forbes Hospital, and Jason Roeback, COO at Saint Vincent Hospital. Ms. Bragg will assume COO responsibilities for Allegheny Valley Hospital, and Mr. Roeback will do the same for Grove City Medical Center.

Law

Leech Tishman Fuscaldo & Lampl LLC said Michael H. Sampson joined as a partner its litigation practice group and will serve as chair of its insurance coverage group and co-chair of the cannabis group. Mr. Sampson focuses his practice on assisting policyholders across various industries to address and resolve complicated insurance coverage matters relating to commercial general liability policies, property insurance, management liability coverages and other types of insurance, such as cyber liability, hull and marine liability, specie insurance, and pollution liability insurance.

Rothman Gordon partnered with Mark Vuono and Louise Vuono, members of Vuono and Gray, who have been retained as special estate counsel to Rothman Gordon.

Awards and Honors

Nancy Murray, senior vice president of Achieva and president of The Arc of Greater Pittsburgh, was honored for her impact in the service of people with intellectual and developmental disabilities by the National Historic Recognition Project: 2000-2020. The project is an effort by a dozen national and regional organizations to recognize key individuals who have contributed to the transformation of the field of intellectual and developmental disabilities in the United States over the past 20 years.

Jason Shoemaker, an assistant professor of chemical engineering at the University of Pittsburgh, received a National Science Foundation Career Award for $547,000 for his work modeling the immune system response to viral lung infections. The predictive computational model will show how the human body will react to a viral lung infection and will flag biomarkers present for people whose immune systems react with excessive inflammation. Although modeled on the influenza virus, it will be applicable to other viral lung infections, like COVID-19.

PPG said Shanti Swarup, PPG associate fellow, research and development, received the 2020 Polymer Fellow Award and Division of Polymeric Materials: Science and Engineering Fellow Award from the American Chemical Society. Mr. Swarup was also recognized by the Pittsburgh Intellectual Property Law Association as the 2020 Inventor of the Year. 

The winners of the 14th annual Bob Fryer Memorial Scholarship and the Press Club of Western Pennsylvania Scholarship are: Sarah Donaldson, of Cranberry, for the $5,000 Bob Fryer Memorial Scholarship and Jordyn Hronec, of Ross, for the $2,500 Press Club Scholarship. 

Pittsburgh author Mary Grace Musuneggi’s book “A Man Is Not a Plan” was announced as a 2020 TAZ Award winner. The awards celebrate accomplished authors in the genres of poetry, fiction, nonfiction, young readers and drama.

North Hills Health and Rehabilitation Center was recognized as a Blue Ribbon District recipient by SavaSeniorCare Administrative Services LLC. The program is designed to drive superb performance and motivates center teams to deliver excellence in key areas of the business, providing awards to those that excel.
