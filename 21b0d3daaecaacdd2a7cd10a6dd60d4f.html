Peduto, public safety officials will read bedtime stories on Facebook Live

Have a child who’s growing restless from pandemic life? Or perhaps you need a spark of inspiration?

You’re in luck because Mayor Bill Peduto will be reading “The Little Engine That Could” live on Facebook Monday evening.

The story time event is part of the Grant Street Reading Series in which Mr. Peduto, on Mondays and Fridays, and Department of Public Safety personnel, on Wednesdays, will read children’s books provided by the Dolly Parton Imagination Library.

The city last year partnered with Ms. Parton’s Imagination Library program that provides free children’s books each month to families who have children under the age of 5. Families can register at imaginationlibrary.com/usa/affiliate/PAPITTSBURGH or by emailing tiffini.simoneaux@pittsburghpa.gov.

Story time begins at 7 p.m. and will be live on the mayor’s office Facebook page at facebook.com/city.of.pittsburgh. The Wednesday story time will be live at facebook.com/PghPublicSafety. Videos will be archived on the city’s YouTube channel.
