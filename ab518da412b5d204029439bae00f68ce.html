Local health clinics among those set to receive federal money for COVID-19 screening and testing

The U.S. Department of Health and Human Services has awarded $2.8 million to 43 health centers in Pennsylvania to battle coronavirus.

The money is to be used for screening and testing, acquiring medical supplies and boosting telehealth capacity.

The funds stem from the Coronavirus Preparedness and Response Supplemental Appropriations Act passed earlier this month to provide emergency money to combat the outbreak, including $100 million for health centers funded by the Health Resources and Services Administration.

HHS said the HRSA-funded centers provide primary care for 28 million people in the U.S.

The awarded money includes $61,000 for East Liberty Family Health Care Center, $53,000 to Community Health Clinic in New Kensington, $53,000 for North Side Christian Health Center, $64,000 for Primary Care Health Services in Pittsburgh, $59,000 for Squirrel Hill Health Center and $53,000 for Sto-Rox Neighborhood Health Council in McKees Rocks.
