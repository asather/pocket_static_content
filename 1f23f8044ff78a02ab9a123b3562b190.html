Officials announce Beaver County’s 1st confirmed COVID-19 case

Beaver County commissioners announced Monday night that a resident had tested positive for the novel coronavirus.

The county was notified of the case by the Pennsylvania Emergency Management Agency at 7 p.m., officials said at the press briefing. The new case brings the total number of confirmed positive cases in Pennsylvania to 77.

No other details of the case have been released at this time.

A state of emergency was declared in Beaver County after the announcement. 

The COVID-19 case is the first recorded in Beaver County. Six confirmed cases are in Allegheny County, while one other is in Washington County.
