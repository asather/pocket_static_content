Death of man disabled in 2006 Penn Hills shooting ruled homicide

A Pittsburgh man’s death has been ruled a homicide, nearly 14 years after he was permanently disabled in a shooting in Penn Hills, the Allegheny County medical examiner’s office reported Thursday afternoon. 

Mark McCaa, 45, died of pneumonia due to quadriplegia on May 29 at ManorCare nursing home in Shadyside, according to the medical examiner’s office.

He had been living at long-term care facilities since the shooting, county officials said. 

Mr. McCaa was shot in the head on Aug. 16, 2006, in the 7300 block of Schley Street in Penn Hills.

A motive for the shooting is unknown, and no charges have been filed in the case, according to Allegheny County police Lt. Venerando Costa. 

More information was not immediately available.
