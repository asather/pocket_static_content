133 new cases, no new deaths from COVID-19 in Allegheny County; 800 new cases in Pa.

Allegheny County reported 133 new cases of COVID-19 and no new deaths on Sunday, the health department said. 

The new cases were out of 1,750 tests performed between July 7 and 25 and ranged in age from 2 years old to 95, with 53 being the median.

Since March 14, there have been 7,294 cases of COVID-19 in the county.

The second-highest increase in Western Pennsylvania was reported by Westmoreland County, which had 31 new positive cases. The state also is reporting 43 deaths in Westmoreland County from the viral disease. That figure hasn’t changed from the previous day, although the county coroner’s office is reporting 38 deaths, which is an increase of three. The coroner’s office said its latest figure is through July 25.

 

 

The Allegheny County death total stands at 222. With four new hospitalizations past or present, the county’s total increased to 581.

Statewide, the Pennsylvania Health Department reported Sunday there were 800 additional positive cases of COVID-19, bringing the statewide total to 107,425. All 67 counties in Pennsylvania have cases of COVID-19. .

There were four new deaths reported in Pennsylvania, bringing the total to 7,118 deaths attributed to COVID-19, according to the state health department.
