Fishing Report: As waters warm, bass and walleye fishing improve

Fish for free in Pennsylvania on July 4. No licenses are required by anglers of any age, resident or nonresident, on all state waterways. All other fishing regulations and private property laws apply.

Lake Erie (Erie County) Walleye fishing was picking up, particularly off Walnut Creek, at 35-50 feet in 70 feet of water. In nearshore areas as deep as 30 feet, ’eyes were caught while trolling crankbaits or crawler harnesses.

Pymatuning Reservoir (Crawford County) Walleye were taken by anglers trolling crawler harnesses and crankbaits, but few limits were caught. Yellow perch were caught off the lake’s southern shores. The fish of 1,000 casts? One angler claimed to catch 10 muskies in a single day while trolling. A catfish in excess of 39 inches was caught.

Lake Wilhelm (Mercer County) Crappies were taken off the causeway.

Shenango Reservoir (Mercer County) Hybrid striped bass took live minnows at dusk.

Shenango River (Mercer County) Brendan Vasil of Glenshaw caught a 30-inch muskie on a crankbait below Pymatuning Reservoir.

Allegheny Reservoir (Warren County) Smallmouth bass went for soft plastics, jigs and crankbaits in waters up to 20 feet deep. Good walleye were taken on crawler harnesses, spoons and spinners in 6-20 feet.

Raccoon Lake (Beaver County) A few bass were taken and panfish were active at the edges of weed beds at dusk.

Pine Creek (Allegheny County) Trout were caught and released on caddis imitations in the Delayed Harvest Artificial Lures Only stretch in the hours before dark.

Conemaugh River Lake (Westmoreland County) Largemouths, smallmouths, yellow perch and crappies were caught using small live minnows by boaters launching at Aultman’s Run.

Loyalhanna Creek (Westmoreland County) Big trout in the trophy range were taken in the Delayed Harvest Artificial Lures Only section. Harvest is legal through Labor Day in waters enrolled in the Keystone Select program, in which large numbers of trophy-size trout were stocked. Nymphs were hot, with some caddis action near dusk.

Quemahoning Reservoir (Somerset County) Anglers reported catching Northern pike, largemouths, smallmouths and a few walleye.

High Point Lake (Somerset County) Largemouth bass were caught as well as yellow perch and bluegill.

Youghiogheny River Lake (Somerset, Fayette counties) Channel catfish and walleye were taken.
