Duquesne earns school-record 11th Atlantic 10 win with overtime victory against VCU

RICHMOND, VA. — Duquesne, behind 23 points from Tavian Dunn-Martin, rallied from 12 down with just over eight minutes left in regulation to pull out an 80-77 overtime win over VCU at the Siegel Center.

The Dukes, who were down 61-49 with 8:14 to go, tied it on a Lamar Norman Jr. drive with 5.6 seconds left.

Baylee Steele and Dunn-Martin hit big 3-pointers in overtime to give the Dukes (21-8, 11-6) their first win over the Rams (18-12, 8-9) in the eight-game series. The win also broke Duquesne’s school record for Atlantic 10 wins in a single season with the team’s 11th conference victory.

Dunn-Martin's 3-pointer was his fifth of the game, all coming in the second half and overtime.

Marcus Weathers and Michael Hughes added 16 and 14 points respectively for the Dukes, who shot 47.4%.

Marcus Santos-Silva led VCU with a game-high 24.

Duquesne turned it over six times early to fuel a 12-2 run that gave VCU a 25-15 lead. The Dukes — despite nine turnovers — cut the Ram’ lead to 30-26 at the half.

VCU took a brief 10-point lead early in the second half, before Dunn-Martin drilled three straight 3-pointers to make it 40-37.

VCU answered with six straight points after the under 16:00 timeout to take a nine-point lead.

The Rams, who hit 10 of their first 14 shots of the half, led 54-43 with 11:02 to go.

VCU upped the lead to 61-49 on a Jarren McAllister bucket.

Duquesne then put together an 11-2 run to make it 63-61 on a Norman Jr. 3-pointer with just under five minutes left.

Weathers was assessed a technical foul after a contested layup attempt and VCU hit both free throws. The Rams added two more free throws on the ensuing possession to make it 67-61 with 3:27 remaining.

From there, the Dukes outscored the Rams 9-3 to force the overtime.

Duquesne returns home to face Richmond in the Atlantic 10 regular season finale on Friday, March 6, at 7:00 p.m. at PPG Paints Arena
