Pittsburgh adopts hiring freeze, leaves 64 positions vacant

The City of Pittsburgh will leave more than 60 positions unfilled as a cost-savings measure against the pandemic-induced fiscal crisis.

The city estimates that the hiring freeze for 64 positions across 17 department and bureaus will save $3 million in salaries.

City department directors are also being asked to cut non-personnel spending by 10% citywide, according to the Monday announcement. No other budget cuts have been announced.

“The dedication and perseverance of city workers, especially those working on the front lines, has been on full display during this pandemic, and on behalf of all city residents I can’t thank them enough for their public service,” said Mayor Bill Peduto in a press release. “We have to put on hold these new hires, however, to help fill the ever-widening holes showing up in our budget due to this crisis.”

Among the departments and bureaus to leave positions unfilled are police, parks and recreation, mobility and infrastructure, and city planning.

Forty-three percent of the city’s $608 million operating budget for 2020 is allocated to salaries and wages. Another 33.5% is budgeted for employee benefits.

The city had just over 3,500 full-time employees at the end of 2019, according to employment records. 

The administration forecasts that the city could lose $127 million in revenue in 2020, dropping from a projected $608 million to $481 million. The administration further estimates a $239 million loss over the next five years.

During his annual budget review, City Controller Michael Lamb on Thursday advocated for a hiring freeze and said those predictions are in the “ballpark” of what’s to come during a likely “fiscal crisis.”

Mr. Peduto in March joined other mayors urging Congress to provide emergency relief for local governments amid the COVID-19 crisis. Two weeks ago he appealed directly to President Donald Trump on the same issue.
