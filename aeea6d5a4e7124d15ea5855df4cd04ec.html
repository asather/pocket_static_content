Kennywood's longtime GM steps down

The general manager of Pittsburgh’s Kennywood amusement park has decided to leave the position after decades of service, according to a park spokesman.

Longtime general manager Jerome Gibas’ departure became effective Feb. 5, company spokesman Nick Paradise said Monday. Rick Stammel, Palace Entertainment’s Theme Parks vice president, will serve as interim general manager while a nationwide search ensues.

“We would like to thank Jerome for his more than 40 years of commitment to providing the finest in family fun and entertainment at Kennywood and its sister park, Idlewild & SoakZone,” John Reilly, chief operating officer of Palace Entertainment said in a statement. 

“Jerome’s service and leadership during his time at each park are greatly valued. We wish him the best going forward,” Mr. Reilly added.

Reasons behind Mr. Gibas’ choice to step down were not immediately clear.
