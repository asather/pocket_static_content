Obama to address recent protests in Wednesday town hall

Former President Barack Obama is scheduled to address the recent nationwide protests against police violence in a Town Hall meeting Wednesday afternoon, according to his website.

Mr. Obama’s remarks will be livestreamed beginning at 5 p.m. ET via his website www.obama.org.

This will be the first time the former president is making on-camera remarks about the protests that were ignited by the Memorial Day death of George Floyd, a 46-year-old black man who died while in police custody in Minneapolis.

Mr. Obama will be joined by police reform activists and other panelists, including his former Attorney General Eric Holder.

The address is part of the Obama Foundation’s My Brother’s Keeper Alliance Town Hall series.
