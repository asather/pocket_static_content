COVID-19 Update: Pa. reports highest spike in new July infections

Pennsylvania health officials on Tuesday reported an increase of 1,120 new cases, the highest the state has seen this month, while Allegheny County is showing its second highest spike in hospitalizations since the pandemic began.

Western Pennsylvania accounts for 292 of those cases, with Allegheny County having the most — with 119 new infections — followed by Beaver County with 37 new cases.

Statewide, there have now been 109,384 cases of COVID-19.

The latest figures bring Allegheny’s total to 7,593 cases. The 119 additional diagnoses come from 1,804 tests that were administered between July 10 and July 27, according to health officials. They range in age from 10 months to 97 years old, with the median age being 50.

The county reported 20 new hospitalizations as a result of the coronavirus. In total the county has now had 603 patients hospitalized as a result of the virus, since the pandemic began in mid-March. The largest increase in hospitalizations occurred on July 24, with 22 new hospitalizations.

Statewide there were 24 new deaths, 11 of which occurred in Western Pennsylvania. Beaver County reported 3, Westmoreland County reported 1 and Allegheny reported 7 of the newest deaths.

The seven new deaths reported by the Allegheny County Health Department span from July 23 to July 26, and were people ranging from 75 to 95. The health department said that “all appear to be related to long-term care facilities.” These new deaths come after two days of no newly reported deaths for the county. The death toll is now 229 in Allegheny County.
