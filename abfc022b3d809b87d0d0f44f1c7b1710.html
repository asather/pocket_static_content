Volunteer at Pittsburgh Milliones being tested for COVID-19

A school volunteer who helped distribute instructional items to 12th-graders at Pittsburgh Milliones 6-12 school in the city’s Hill District neighborhood has fallen ill and was tested for COVID-19, the Pittsburgh Public Schools said.

The district said in a message to families Sunday that the volunteer had helped pass out laptops and instructional packets on April 16, 20 and 21. 

The district said it was sharing the information “out of an abundance of caution.”

“As a reminder, the symptoms of COVID-19 include a fever, cough and shortness of breath,” the district said. “If you or a member of your family experience any of the symptoms above, we encourage you to please follow-up with your primary care physician.”
