Allegheny County reports 6 new COVID-19 deaths, state indicates all are nursing home residents

This story was updated at 12:30 p.m.

Allegheny County reported six new deaths on Monday, and the state’s figures, which were released an hour later, said the county’s nursing/long-term care homes accounted for the six deaths.

With the exception of Westmoreland County, where its coroner reported three new deaths, there were no other fatalities blamed on COVID-19 in Western Pennsylvania.

Allegheny also reported 13 new cases of COVID-19.

Allegheny County, which reported zero deaths on Sunday, now has 79. The number of cases now totals 1,224 since the first instance was reported on March 14

Allegheny County’s total cases to date include 1,190 confirmed and 34 probable, according to the county health department. A case is probable “when the person displays symptoms of COVID-19 and has had close contact with a confirmed positive case but has not had a positive test themselves,” according to a statement accompanying the numbers.

Of the 1,224 cases, 169 of them — or 14% of the total number of cases — have been healthcare workers, the county said. There are 213 past or present hospitalizations.

Among those who have died, 72 are confirmed (had a positive test) and seven were probable. “A probable death is one where COVID-19 is listed on the deceased’s death certificate, but the person never had a positive test for the virus,” the county said.

Allegheny County’s totals are the highest in Western Pennsylvania. Philadelphia County has the highest totals in the state with 11,361 cases and 274 deaths, according to the state.

On Sunday, Pennsylvania reported 13 new deaths and 1,096 new cases, totaling 1,550 and 41,165, respectively.
