Pittsburgh Mills’ Cinemark movie theater to permanently close

Movie theaters throughout Pennsylvania have been closed for almost two months now as the COVID-19 pandemic continues to make public gatherings in those spaces unsafe. Even when they eventually are deemed eligible for opening, though, one local theater will remain shuttered.

The Cinemark 18 theater in Frazer Township’s Galleria at Pittsburgh Mills shopping center announced Monday that it will be closing for good soon, a company spokesman confirmed to the Post-Gazette. The national movie theater chain said that this decision wasn’t spurred by COVID-19 closures affecting the company’s economic prospects.

“This closure is normal course of business and the result of the careful and ongoing review of our theatre fleet,” read an emailed statement from the company.

There is no official closing date yet for Cinemark 18, though the company also indicated in its statement that the Pittsburgh Mills location’s lease is close to expiring.

The expected closure of the location in northern Allegheny County will leave four Cinemark theaters in Western Pennsylvania: Cinemark Monroeville Mall; Cinemark Robinson Township; Cinemark North Hills in McCandless; and Cinemark Center Township Marketplace in Beaver County.

The movie theater industry as a whole has been hit hard by the forced closures of the last two months. Cinemark is the country’s third-largest movie theater franchise, and its finances have been in such disarray that it opted to delay reporting them for the quarter that ended in March until June 11 at the earliest, Deadline reported.

Pennsylvania Gov. Tom Wolf is slowly allowing some businesses throughout the state to open. Allegheny County will move from a “red” designation to “yellow” starting on Friday, which will allow certain businesses — like drive-in movie theaters — to begin generating revenue again.

Standard movie theaters, however, will remain closed in both red and yellow counties for the foreseeable future.

The theater chain is owned by Cinemark Holdings, which is based in Plano, Texas.
