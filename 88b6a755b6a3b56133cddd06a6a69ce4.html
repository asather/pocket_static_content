City adds another $500K in grant money to go toward blue recycling bins

Pittsburgh’s city government is a half a million dollars closer to distributing blue recycling bins to residents in an effort to keep blue bags — which are a nuisance at recycling sorting facilities — off the curb.

City Council on Tuesday unanimously approved legislation from the mayor’s office to accept a $500,000 grant from the Falls Church, Va.-based nonprofit Recycling Partnership to purchase the bins and conduct public outreach. 

The number of bins and when and where they will be distributed is still under review, according to the mayor’s office.

The grant is in addition to the $538,888 in city money and $350,000 in state grant money included in the 2020 capital budget for blue bins. 

City residents can sign up at pittsburghpa.gov/blue-bin-info to receive updates on when blue bins will be available.
