PSO to release album of Tchaikovsky and Leshnoff on Friday

Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra recordings have won a Grammy Award, multiple Grammy nominations and other national and international accolades in recent years. On Friday, the orchestra will release “Tchaikovsky Symphony No. 4 & Leshnoff Double concerto for Clarinet and Bassoon.” 

The album, recorded under the baton of music director Manfred Honeck on the Reference Recordings label, is part of the “Pittsburgh Live!” series, which uses live concert recordings and a patch session to capture the energy and spontaneity of a live performance.

Tchaikovsky’s fourth symphony is part of the classical canon and is performed often. The second work features PSO principal clarinetist Michael Rusinek and principal bassoonist Nancy Goeres as soloists in a PSO-commissioned work that the orchestra premiered in 2019. Jonathan Leshnoff, an acclaimed Baltimore-based composer, introduces juxtapositions of tonality and key to give his music a bright, colorful feel.

The album will be available Friday for $20 at pittsburghsymphony.org as well as on streaming sites including Spotify.
