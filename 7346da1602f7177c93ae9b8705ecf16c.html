Bob Petcash is new boys basketball coach at Pine-Richland

A Petcash brother has been in the Pine-Richland basketball team’s starting lineup for the past seven seasons. Next season, their father will be the Rams’ head coach.

Bob Petcash was hired Monday night as Pine-Richland’s new coach. He is the father of Andrew, Dan and Joey Petcash, who all played for the Rams.

“They’re all happy for me,” Bob Petcash said. “It could always be fun for maybe one of them to come back someday and be an assistant coach.”

Bob Petcash was a Pine-Richland assistant coach the past six seasons under Jeff Ackermann, who left to become the coach at Baldwin. Petcash, 54, spent 11 other seasons as a coach for Pine-Richland at the freshman and junior-high levels.

Over the past five seasons, Pine-Richland has been one of the most successful programs in the WPIAL. The Rams won WPIAL championships in 2016-17 and lost in the 2018 title game. Bob Petcash, who makes a living as an oral surgeon, went through three interviews with Pine-Richland over the past few weeks before the school board officially hired him.

“Pine-Richland has great tradition and a lot of success in all sports, so this is exciting for me,” Petcash said. “I’m just looking forward to putting a good staff together and build on the tradition that’s already here.”

As for Bob Petcash’s sons, Andrew and Dan were both Post-Gazette Fabulous 5 selections and are playing college basketball. Andrew Petcash, Pine-Richland’s all-time leading scorer, will be a senior at Boston University while Dan will be a sophomore at Binghamton. Andrew Petcash started on the two WPIAL championship teams. Youngest brother, Joe, will be a senior guard at Pine-Richland.

Bob Petcash said he has hoped to be Pine-Richland’s head coach for a number of years.

“I’ve heard the comment from people that ‘Bob Petcash will only be the coach for a year or two because he wants to see his sons play in college,’” Bob Petcash said. “I would never do that to a program. How do people know what I want to do? I wanted this job. I’ve thought about it for a while.”
