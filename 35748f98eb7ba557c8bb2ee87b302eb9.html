Pirates add reliever Jandel Gustave after Henderson Alvarez deal falls through

Henderson Alvarez won’t become a Pittsburgh Pirate after all.

Meanwhile, Jandel Gustave is the latest pitcher the Pirates are hoping to add to help their pitching depth.

Alvarez, who threw a no-hitter in 2013 and was an All-Star with the Marlins in 2014, tweeted late Sunday night that his deal with the Pirates fell through.

It stops what could have potentially been a really neat story, the 30-year-old right-hander working his way back from the ground up, and the reasons why it happened differ.

Alvarez said it was because the Pirates signed another player. The Pirates dispute that, with one front-office source saying the two pitchers had nothing to do with one another. The source emphasized that the Pirates would never agree to terms with a player contingent on a deal with a different player.

The Pirates reached a tentative agreement with Alvarez on Aug. 9, plucking him from the Milwaukee Milkmen of the American Association of Independent Professional Baseball.

Gustave, who has pitched for the Astros (2016-17) and Giants (2019), offers an interesting consolation prize. The 6-foot-3, 220-pound right-hander has a 3.43 career ERA in 43 appearances, including a 2.96 ERA in 23 appearances last season.

The Dominican-born righty has a fairly good career strikeout rate (17.4%) but has also struggled with his control (10.9% walk rate).

San Francisco designated Gustave for assignment on Aug. 2. He has three pitches that he uses a decent amount: a four-seamer, slider and sinker. The four-seamer and sinker both averaged 96.1 mph in 2019, while opponents hit just .167 off Gustave's slider.

It’s likely that Gustave will report to the Pirates’ alternate-site camp in Altoona. Milwaukee-based freelancer Robert Murray first reported the Gustave agreement.
