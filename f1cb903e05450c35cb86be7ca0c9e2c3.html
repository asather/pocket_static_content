Penguins sign Sam Lafferty to new 2-year deal

The Penguins have rewarded Sam Lafferty after an encouraging rookie year.

The team announced Thursday that Lafferty, a restricted free agent who hails from Hollidaysburg, had signed a new two-year contract. The one-way deal has an average annual value of $750,000. He’s under contract through 2022-23.

“We have been very pleased with Sam’s development,” Pittsburgh general manager Jim Rutherford said Thursday in a statement. “His size and speed are big advantages in today’s game, and his style of play complements our team well.”

The 25-year-old made his NHL debut last October and played 50 games in 2019-20. His six goals were tied with John Marino for the most among Penguins rookies. He added seven assists and finished the regular season with a plus-5 rating.

Lafferty appeared in just one playoff game. It did not go well. After a rocky night against the Montreal Canadiens in Game 3, he was again a healthy scratch.

Still, Lafferty showed promise in his first NHL season and with his combination of size and speed, he can become a bottom-six mainstay if he further develops. The 6-foot-1, 195-pound forward saw time at both center and right wing as a rookie.

The Penguins drafted Lafferty in the fourth round in 2014. He spent four years at Brown, twice being named an All-Ivy League performer. After turning pro in 2018, he had 55 points in 84 games at the American Hockey League level.

With Lafferty now under contract, fellow forward Anthony Angello is the only remaining RFA among last season’s Penguins. He will likely sign a similar deal.
