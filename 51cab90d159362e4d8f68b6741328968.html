Bloomfield utility pole breaks, injuring worker in bucket truck

A utility worker was injured when a utility pole fell onto the bucket truck in which he was working around 4:30 p.m. at Liberty Avenue and South Mathilda Street in Bloomfield, according to a statement from Cara Cruz, deputy public information officer with the Pittsburgh Department of Public Safety.

The wooden utility pole snapped and collapsed onto the bucket, causing the bucket itself to break in half.

The worker, a Duquesne Light contractor with J.W. Didado Electric, was left dangling from the arm of the truck, Post-Gazette news partner KDKA reported. A coworker in a separate bucket truck rescued the worker. Police and emergency services responded to the scene, and the worker was taken to nearby West Penn Hospital.

The worker sustained only minor injuries to his rib cage, Ms. Cruz said. 

Live wires at the scene forced the closure of South Mathilda Street from Liberty to Friendship avenues. Duquesne Light arrived to assess damage and cleanup.
