City, union reach 5-year agreement for Citiparks employees, park rangers

The city of Pittsburgh has reached a five-year agreement with its unionized parks employees, including its park rangers that work within the Department of Public Safety.

The roughly 60 employees, represented by the Service Employees International Union Local 668, will now receive paid parental leave; paid “safe” leave in the case of domestic violence; the ability to donate paid time off to co-workers for medical reasons; and a salary increase “in line with compensation offered by peer cities that support family sustaining wages for the city's workforce,” according to the city’s Monday announcement.

The Citiparks employees include senior and community recreation center directors, program coordinators, the Schenley Rink leader, recreation leaders and park rangers.

The contract, signed with the union, “changed all gendered pronouns (he/him/his, she/her/hers) to gender neutral pronouns (they/them/theirs) in an effort to be more inclusive for all gender identities,” according to the city.

The agreement term is from January 2020 through December 2024.

SEIU Local 668 could not be immediately reached for comment.
