Pittsburgh's Entercom radio stations will raise funds next week for community food bank

Pittsburgh Entercom radio stations will participate in a “Feed the Need Radiothon” April 14 from 7 a.m.-5 p.m. Live broadcasts from News Radio KDKA-AM (1020), The Fan (KDKA-FM 93.7), Star (WBZZ-FM 100.7) and Y108 (WDSY-FM) are part of a nationwide effort to raise funds for 100,000 meals to be distributed through the Greater Pittsburgh Community Food Bank 

The meals are earmarked for displaced workers who are utilizing emergency drive-up distribution sites, as well as food pantries around the Pittsburgh region. It is part of an initiative from Philadelphia-based Entercom called Stay Connected.

“We are excited to be able to help the Food Bank and Pittsburgh,” said Michael Spacciapolli, senior vice president and market manager, Entercom Pittsburgh.

The live broadcasts will include hourly interviews and updates focusing on fundraising efforts. Listeners will have the opportunity to online or through phone or text message.

More information is at KDKARadio.com/feedtheneed.
