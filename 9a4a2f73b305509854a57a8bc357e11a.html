Pa. National Guard to arrive in Westmoreland nursing home Sunday morning

The Pennsylvania National Guard is scheduled to arrive at the Westmoreland Manor nursing home early Sunday morning after the county requested assistance earlier this week to stem a COVID-19 outbreak at its facility. 

Six members of the guard will arrive at 6 a.m. Sunday to assist with the county-owned nursing home to help administer COVID-19 tests to staff and residents, according to a press release sent on Saturday by the Office of Westmoreland County Commissioners. 

Earlier this week, a letter was posted onto the facility’s website by administrator Angela Knauff detailing the need for more assistance from the guard.

“The Pennsylvania National Guard will be able to perform testing that provides a faster turnaround time. This will also allow the managers at the Manor to focus on infection control and resident care,” the letter read.

The nursing home also has requested assistance with personal protective equipment supplies. The Manor currently uses 8,400 gowns per day, Saturday’s statement said.

The nursing home’s latest testing revealed no additional positive residents, the statement read. Two additional employees and an additional contracted staff member tested positive, the statement said. 

All positive employees are quarantining at home.

In addition to the National Guard’s assistance, the Westmoreland Manor has paused face-to-face visitation, as part of its phased reopening plan. Window visits will be permitted on a case-by-case basis.
