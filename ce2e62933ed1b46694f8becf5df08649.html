Allegheny County reports 142 new cases of COVID-19, no new deaths

After issues downloading the COVID-19 dataset from the Pennsylvania NEDSS database on Sunday, the Allegheny County Health Department is reporting an additional 142 cases of the virus and zero related deaths from the last 48 hours.

These 142 cases cover the results from 1,491 tests that were taken between Sept. 6 and Sept. 13.

Those who have recently tested positive for the virus range in age from 11 months to 92 years old, with a median age of 27.

With no new deaths reported in the last two days, the death toll for the county remains at 361.

Based on the most recent data from the state health department, there are currently 472 people hospitalized for COVID-19 across the commonwealth, 47 of which are in Allegheny County. The county has had a total of 1,057 people hospitalized for the virus since mid-March.

With the combination of data from Sunday and Monday, the county has now had 11,268 cases of COVID-19 since the beginning of the pandemic.

Statewide, there was an increase of 1,258 cases since Saturday —638 on Sunday and 620 on Monday. Western Pennsylvania reported 293 of the state's total number of positive cases. The state health department’s website outlining Pennsylvania’s data on the novel coronavirus was not updated on Sunday.

Totals from Philadelphia County were not included in Monday’s update due to a delay in reporting, according to the health department. A two-day total for that county will be included in Tuesday’s report.

With the addition of the data from Sunday and Monday, Pennsylvania has had a total of 145,063 cases of COVID-19. Of the total cases across the state, 10,117 are among healthcare workers. 

The state health department also reported zero new deaths for Sunday, and seven new deaths for Monday, five of which were in the Pittsburgh region. Beaver County reported three new deaths and Cambria and Crawford counties reported one each.

The state death toll is now 7,869.
