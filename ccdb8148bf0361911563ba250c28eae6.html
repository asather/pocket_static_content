Dozens more temporary election staffers set to be hired by Allegheny County

Funded by a $2 million private grant it was awarded this week, Allegheny County plans to hire 76 more temporary election workers to process and canvass the influx of mail-in ballots cast in this November’s presidential election. 

The funds — administered by the Center for Tech and Civil Life, a nonprofit group that focuses on modernizing elections and informing America’s democracy — will go toward addressing the “significant challenges” Allegheny County faces in executing the Nov. 3 election, the county wrote in its application for the grant.

The county had requested $1.24 million to hire dozens of additional staffers, who will help with processing mail-in and absentee ballot applications, verifying ballots as eligible and completing any post-count canvassing tasks. Currently, the county has six staff members to tabulate the ballots, the county wrote in its application.

It also intends to spend a little over $800,000 on ballot printing and mailing automation, noting that it’s “enlisted the services of a printer/mail house collaboration to assist with the automation of printing and mailing ballots to voters based on request.”

“Typically the ballots would have been printed, followed by a manual insert by staff and mailed through our in-house mail operation,” the county wrote. “However, due to the dramatic increase in volume, we can no longer handle this process in house.”

Upon signing the grant agreement, the county will agree to use the funds only for “planning and operationalizing safe and secure election administration” this year, and only for additional spending — not to reduce its budget or supplant previously appropriated funds. The money must be spent by the end of the calendar year.

The nonprofit said it would give $250 million to local election jurisdictions across the U.S. to help them fund staffing, training and equipment, re-gifted from a large donation by Facebook founder Mark Zuckerberg and his wife, philanthropist Priscilla Chan.
