Emergency crews responding to 4-alarm fire at Bellevue home

Emergency officials worked Friday morning battling a four-alarm fire that erupted at a residence in Bellevue, according to Allegheny County dispatch.

Firefighters responded around 8:20 a.m. to the 100 block of Grant Avenue for the blaze that had risen to two alarms within 10 minutes of the initial fire call before eventually going to a fourth alarm within an hour for extra manpower.

County officials said all of the occupants made it out of the house.

As of 10 a.m., firefighters were still on the scene.

Further information was not immediately available.
