First COVID-19 death reported at Concordia facility in Butler

Concordia Lutheran Ministries reported on Friday afternoon its first COVID-19 related death of one of its residents.

A spokesperson for the assisted living organization said that the death occurred Thursday night at their Concordia at the Orchard facility in Butler.

Concordia Lutheran Ministries also has reported it has three other confirmed cases of the virus at its Butler facility and one confirmed case of a staff member at its Concordia at Villa St. Joseph location in Baden.

The death comes days after several assisted living facilities and nursing homes in Western Pennsylvania began reporting a spike in confirmed COVID-19 cases and deaths.

St. Barnabas Nursing Home in Richland announced on April 7 that two of its residents had died of the virus, as well as an additional 18 confirmed cases.

Country Meadows Retirement Communities has reported confirmed cases at locations in other parts of the state but none at it South Fayette facility.

Others, such as Vincentian Home in McCandless, have reported zero cases so far.

As of Friday, Allegheny Health Network reported 26 current hospitalizations and two previous deaths across its various locations.

Heritage Valley Health System reported 13 confirmed cases across its three hospitals at Heritage Valley Beaver, Heritage Valley Kennedy and Heritage Valley Sewickley, as well as 18 suspected cases and two previous deaths.

Excela Health has 18 confirmed cases across its three hospitals in Westmoreland County, as well as 13 suspected cases and three deaths.

Washington Health System provides most of the advanced health care services in Washington and Greene counties. According to the Department of Health, there have been 66 cases in Washington and 21 cases in Greene. But no deaths have been reported in either county.
