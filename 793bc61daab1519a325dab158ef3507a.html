Police: Boy, 14, killed in rollover crash in Indiana County after car runs stop sign

A 14-year-old passenger in a pickup truck was killed in a rollover crash when the driver ran a stop sign and collided with another vehicle in Indiana County.

Pennsylvania State Police said the accident occurred around 5 p.m. Monday on Cherry Tree Road in Montgomery.

Police said the 18-year-old male driver of the first vehicle was driving on Cherry Tree Road when he failed to stop at the posted stop sign and struck a vehicle at the Acadia Road intersection. The collision caused the first vehicle to overturn.

A 14-year-old boy from Greene, Indiana County, who was a rear passenger in the truck, was pronounced dead at the scene; he was not wearing a seat belt, state police said. Another passenger, a 19-year-old woman, was also in the vehicle. It was unclear whether she or the driver suffered any serious injuries.

The driver of the other vehicle, a 32-year-old woman from Cherry Tree suffered minor injuries. She was the only passenger in that car.
