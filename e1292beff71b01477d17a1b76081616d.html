Ben Roethlisberger 'definitely sore,' but his arm feels OK after first game

It is not easy for teams who open the season on a Monday night to have a short week for the next opponent, especially when there were no preseason games for players to get their bodies acclimated to actual game contact.

That is especially true for a quarterback who hadn’t played in a game in nearly a year.

Which is why Ben Roethlisberger said his body felt as though he was involved in something more than a game after Monday night’s season-opening victory against the New York Giants.

“I said today I feel like I was just in a car accident; yesterday I felt like I was in a train wreck,” Roethlisberger said on Wednesday. “Hopefully tomorrow it will feel like I fell off a bike. I’m definitely sore. The good news is my arm is the only thing that doesn’t hurt. That’s good.”

Roethlisberger’s return from elbow surgery got off to a rousing start. He passed for 229 yards and three touchdowns without an interception and spread the ball to nine different receivers. His touchdown to JuJu Smith-Schuster in the second quarter was his first since the season-ending game of the 2018 season.

However, Roethlisberger admitted to having some rust with pocket awareness against the Giants, something that comes from not having any contact during the preseason. He gets a chance to knock off some more of the rustiness on Sunday in the home opener against the Denver Broncos.

“It’s not like I played perfect,” Roethlisberger said. “Sometimes I got up into pocket too quick. I didn’t have the same feeling I’m used to having. I think that comes from not getting hit even in practice.

“Defenders don’t hit you in practice so I think you lose some of that feel of where you can slide, where you can step to. I think that will come back with the more reps and plays you get into.”
