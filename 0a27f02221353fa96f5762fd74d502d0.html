COVID-19 Update: Allegheny County reports 204 new cases, zero deaths

The number of reported COVID-19 cases continues to rise in Allegheny County.

In the last 48 hours, 204 new cases of COVID-19 were reported to the Allegheny County Health Department, officials said Sunday. There were no new deaths.

The latest cases bring the county’s case total to 12,731 since the beginning of the pandemic in mid-March. There have been 1,227 hospitalizations. To date, a total of 386 people have died in Allegheny County as a result of the virus.

Those recently diagnosed range in age from a 1-month-old to 95, with a median age of 35. Of those, 104 were female and 100 were male.

The new cases were from tests taken between Sept. 12 and Oct. 3. Only one test is from Sept. 12. The remaining tests are from the last week. Of the 204 new cases, 180 are confirmed cases from PCR tests. A total of 24 cases are probable. Of the probable cases, 13 are from positive antigen tests.

Pennsylvania has had a total of 158,001 cases of the virus and 8,216 virus-related deaths, based on the state’s most recent figures.
