People on the move: 1/26/2020

People on the Move

Accounting

Goff Backa Alfera & Co. LLC hired Suhao Zhang as a consultant in its auditing and accounting department.

Associations & Organizations

Tony J. Thompson was named legal counsel for NAIOP Pittsburgh, the regional association of developers, owners, investors and professionals of commercial real estate. Mr. Thompson is a partner at Meyer, Unkovic & Scott. He focuses his practice on complex commercial litigation with an emphasis on real estate disputes, including ownership, boundary disputes, land use and zoning hearings, and commercial evictions.

Paul M. Palevsky was named president-elect of the National Kidney Foundation’s board. He is professor of medicine and clinical and translational science in the renal-electrolyte division at the University of Pittsburgh School of Medicine and is chief of the renal section at the VA Pittsburgh Healthcare System.

The Pennsylvania Chamber of Business and Industry announced that Sam Denisco, vice president of government affairs, was elected to serve as chairman of the Pennsylvania Health Care Cost Containment Council.

Ira Weiss, founding partner at Weiss Burkardt Kramer LLC, was named to the Pennsylvania School Boards Association Tax Reform Task Force. He serves as solicitor to the School District of Pittsburgh and several other school districts in Western Pennsylvania.

Consulting

Pharmacy Healthcare Solutions LLC promoted Ann Johnson to president and Melissa Krause to vice president.

Education

Matthew Sterne was named vice chancellor for business services within business and operations at the University of Pittsburgh.

Health

Net Health appointed Josh Pickus CEO and Anthony Sanzo executive chairman.

Insurance

Henderson Brothers hired Benjamin Wasson to HB Retirement as a client services associate; Andrew Cooper to HB retirement as senior plan consultant; and April Ginsburg to Henderson Brothers as a health strategy consultant, employee benefits practice.

Law

Babst Calland announced the addition of veteran environmental attorney Richard S. Wiedman, who joined as a shareholder.

Dominic Rupprecht was promoted to partner at Reed Smith.

Burns White LLC announced that Mary-Jo Rebelo was elected chief operating officer, Western Region.

Museum

Carnegie Museum of Natural History said Travis Olds joined as assistant curator of the minerals collection.

Nonprofit

Goodwill of Southwestern Pennsylvania hired McCrae M. Martino as its vice president for human services. She succeeds Ella Holsinger, who retired after 33 years of employment at Goodwill SWPA.

Real Estate

Keller Williams Bethel Park office hired new agents. They are Waldo Avoila, Patricia Dowiak, Jamie Huss and Tammy Streator.

Business notes

Gatesman added USG, Carestream Dental and CFA Institute to Client Roster.

LarsonO’Brien announces an official name change to BLD Marketing Inc. effective Jan. 1. The name, pronounced “Build”, reflects the company’s commitment to building client business through marketing solutions tailored to building product manufacturers.

CBD USA GROWN, a global CBD original formulation and distribution company, opened its fourth franchise store at 158 Tri-County Lane, Suite 2, Belle Vernon. The store is owned by Susan Mazanetz, a health science student at California University of Pennsylvania.

TTG Healthcare LLC, changed its name to TTG Imaging Solutions LLC. Additionally, TTG Diagnostic Solutions, Cardiologic LLC, and Omni Diagnostics have merged to form Cardiac Imaging Solutions (CIS). The company will continue to operate as a provider of diagnostic imaging services. 

Awards and Honors

Beyond Spots & Dots was recognized by the 7th Annual Education Digital Marketing Awards for its work with client Dixon University Center. Beyond Spots & Dots received a gold award for online advertising campaigns and a bronze award for website development. Dixon University Center is an academic and governance center that offers college and university-level programs from a consortium of schools in the Pennsylvania State System of Higher Education.
