Buying Here: A rehabbed house in Lincoln Place for under $90,000

When Danielle Condon went house hunting three years ago, she looked at five houses before buying the one at 5606 Rodgers St. Ext. in Lincoln Place.

“For under $100,000 it was a done deal,” she said. “The house was completely renovated and move-in ready.”

The seller who rehabbed the 103-year-old cottage had updated everything, including new siding, roof and mechanicals. He also removed walls and opened up the one-bedroom, 1½-bath house in this South Hills city neighborhood.

The light-filled spaces were a selling point for Ms. Condron, who is moving to a larger home and has put this on the market for $89,900 (MLS No. 1442140) with Heather Knepper of Your Town Realty (www.yourtownpittsburgh.com or 412-716-5536). It is unavailable for tours due to COVID-19 restrictions.

There is a vacant side lot next to the home that is available through the city’s Side Lot Program. It gives a buyer the opportunity to expand the property.

The house’s hardwood floors have been refinished and stained in a clear oak. The original staircase features the original walnut-stained banisters.

The open-concept living room has room for a large sofa and additional seating. The updated kitchen includes new cabinets, granite counter tops and stainless-steel appliances, including an electric range, large double bowl sink and dishwasher. There is lots of room for a dining table.

“We moved into a house that needs renovated,” Ms. Condon said. “I miss my granite countertops. Everything is really new and fresh there.”

The rear of the home has a converted mud room with a ceramic plank tile floor and a new powder room with pocket door. A door leads to a large deck and a fenced-in backyard that could accommodate a dog.

Upstairs, the two bedrooms and full bath have been done over.

“I just replaced all of the upstairs carpet and repainted the entire house,” Ms. Condon said.

The house has new central air-conditioning, a home warranty and a laundry in the basement. The Allegheny County Assessed value is $13,600. Two houses have sold in the 5600 block of Rodgers Street Extension over the last three years, one for $45,000 in December 2016 and this house for $78,000 in May 2017. (www2.alleghenycounty.us/RealEstate/Default.aspx).

The home is on a back street and has little traffic, Ms. Condon said. “My son could ride a bike. It was nice.”

It’s less than a 10-minute drive to shopping at The Waterfront in Homestead and Century III Mall in West Mifflin. “There is a Shop ‘n Save close to the house and a Dollar General in walking distance,” Ms. Condon said.

“That house was great and really saved a lot of money.”
