More banks sign on to program pledging breaks to customers

Three more banks with local operations have joined a state aid initiative, pledging to offer certain breaks to customers struggling financially during the pandemic.

Pittsburgh-based Dollar Bank, Indiana, Pa.-based First Commonwealth Bank, and Rhode Island-based Citizens Bank have committed to the program, called the “PA CARE Package” initiative. Pittsburgh-based PNC Bank, Pennsylvania’s biggest bank, was the first to support the program when it was launched a week ago.

Under the program, financial institutions must offer certain assistance to Pennsylvanians facing financial hardship. That includes:

• Expanded loan availability for small and medium-sized businesses.

• A 90-day grace period on mortgage payments and on other consumer loans such as auto loans.

• Relief from fees and charges, such as late and overdraft fees, for 90 days.

• A 60-day moratorium on foreclosures, evictions and vehicle repossession.

• No adverse credit reporting for taking relief on consumer loans.

For more information on the program, visit www.attorneygeneral.gov/COVID19.
