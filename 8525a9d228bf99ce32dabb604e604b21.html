Buying Here: Lake Erie house offers cozy charm and views for $695,000

If you’re feeling isolated and housebound during the pandemic, a house with a lake view may be just what the doctor ordered.

This stately, 3,841-square-foot house at 3862 Montrose Ave. in Millcreek, Erie County, has six bedrooms, three bathrooms, three powder rooms and sits on 2½ acres overlooking Lake Erie. It’s listed for $695,000 with agent Nancy Placidi of Howard Hanna Real Estate Services (814-882-1303 or www.howardhanna.com).

“It’s really been an amazing 24 years here,” said Dr. Lauren Mozdy, the owner. “It’s nearly 100 years old.”

Built in 1929, the center hall Colonial (MLS No. 152334) retains its original charm and architectural details.

“She has done numerous updates throughout while keeping the original character of the home intact,” said Ms. Placidi.

Dr. Mozdy, an anesthesiologist, noted that she is only the second owner of the property.

“The original owner, Ted Nagle, owned a boiler company and used it as a summer house,” she said. “He must have done very well since he built the home during the Depression.”

The property has a panoramic view of the lake because shrubs and trees have been regularly pruned and kept low. 

“When you are in the house, you get a view of the lake from all the back rooms,” said Dr. Mozdy. “You can’t find that anymore on the lake. Most homes now have a narrow view.”

A path leads to the large beach.

“It’s a very stable property because the slope to the lake is gradual and it is not being eroded,” she said.

Hand-cast plaster molding, wood paneling and tall baseboards are just a few of the indicators of the attention to detail. Dr. Mozdy, who lived here year-round, said she enjoyed spending time in the enclosed back porch. With so many full-length windows, “the views really are just gorgeous,” she said.

There is a curved staircase off the entry and a back staircase used by servants.

“Mr. Nagle had a maid and a butler,” said Dr. Mozdy.

An original stone fireplace dominates the paneled living room, which has built-in shelves and a coffered ceiling.

“The fireplace works perfectly and the way the mason built it was symmetrical,” Dr, Mozdy said.

“The built-ins and the stone fireplace are fabulous,” Ms. Placidi said. “I love the charm of this entire home.”

The dining room features built-in curio cabinets on either side of a large bay window that overlooks the back lawn and lake.

“The previous owner loved the ship room,” Dr. Mozdy said, adding that it’s now used as an office. She kept the original nautical wallpaper.

“There are two narrow — like a ship — entrances to the room and there is a 1920s map of the tri-state area on one wall.”

The house has a long driveway and sits back from the road, giving it a large front lawn and lots of privacy.

“You can’t see the house from the road,” Dr. Mozdy noted.

A garage that blocked part of the lake view was taken down and turned into a brick patio. There is also a smaller patio off the living room.

Annual property taxes are $14,959.

Dr. Mozdy said her twins, a boy and a girl, grew up here.  The children have left for college and she has remarried. Though she’s ready to move, she hates to sell her beautiful lake house.

“We are going to move to my husband’s house, but it is breaking my heart,” she said.
