Protesters show up at Wilkinsburg mayor's house demanding justice for police shooting victim

Protesters Tuesday evening gathered in front of the home of Wilkinsburg Mayor Marita Garrett to call for justice for a man who was killed by police in December.

Post-Gazette news partner KDKA-TV reported that demonstrators demanded justice for Romir Talley, who was shot and killed by Wilkinsburg police after a foot pursuit.

Allegheny County police said after the shooting on Dec. 22 that Talley turned around and fired one shot at two Wilkinsburg officers. An officer returned fire, striking Talley seven times, police said.

Ms. Garrett was escorted to her car by police during the protest, KDKA reported.

Talley’s family has since sought legal representation to demand transparency in the investigation of Talley’s death. 

Ms. Garrett told the Post-Gazette Wednesday morning that she plans on addressing the events later today.
