Pa. confirms 2 new presumptive cases of COVID-19 in Pennsylvania, bringing state total to 6

The Pennsylvania Department of Health on Sunday afternoon confirmed two new presumptive positive cases of COVID-19 in the state, making a total of six cases to date.

The two new cases are in Montgomery County, the health department said, bringing the total number of presumptive positive cases of COVID-19 in Montgomery County to four after two cases were announced on Saturday.

The health department said Sunday the new cases are adults who have mild symptoms and are isolated at home, and they tested “presumptive positive after international exposure.”

Gov. Tom Wolf announced on Friday the first cases in Pennsylvania were in Delaware County and Wayne County. 

Mr. Wolf announced Saturday he and Senate Republican leaders were working toward a bipartisan legislative package that “would provide resources to ensure we are aggressively mitigating the spread of COVID-19,” the governor wrote in a statement. 

To date, there have been over 109,000 cases confirmed worldwide, with at least 3,802 deaths. In the U.S., there have so far been over 500 confirmed cases and 21 deaths.
