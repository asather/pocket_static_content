Things to do in Pittsburgh this weekend, March 5-8

ALL WEEKEND

Goin’ Home

It’s that time of year when fans of both homes and gardens flock Downtown to see them live in person at the Duquesne Light Pittsburgh Home & Garden Show.

There will be hundreds of products and services spread over 10 acres in the David L. Lawrence Convention Center.

Special features this year include a Lakeside Cottage and a Cabin in the Woods. The Dream Home Innovation Center showcases the latest ideas and products for the home. Back again is the Farm To Table area, where small local growers and food producers sell and provide samples of produce, meats, cheeses, honey, wines and spirits.

For little kids, there are the activities and games in the Children’s Village and dozens of model trains chugging through miniature towns and villages.

On the Garden Stage, garden writer Doug Oster and others will offer presentations and the Pittsburgh Bonsai Society will exhibit miniature trees and demonstrate the ancient art.

Other returning features include antiques expert Dr. Lori and jazz musicians from Pittsburgh CAPA performing every day in the Crawford Grill.

It runs through March 15. Hours are 10 a.m. to 10 p.m. Fridays and Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Sundays and 4 to 10 p.m. Monday through Thursday. Admission is $10, $4 for children ages 6-12 and free for those under 6. Buy tickets at the convention center box office or online at www.PghHome.com. Park at Heinz Field on the North Side for $8 and ride the free continuous shuttle back and forth to the show.

At the Improv

Tony Roberts, a comedian from Detroit now part of the Bay Area’s KBLX Morning Dream Team, works the Pittsburgh Improv at the Waterfront in Homestead this weekend.

He’s been seen in a series of Burger King commercials, as part of The Shaquille O’Neal All Star Comedy Jam Tour, on HBO’s Def Comedy Jam, and as CY on “Uncle Buck.”

Times are 8 p.m. Thursday; 7:30 and 9:45 p.m. Friday; 7 and 9:30 p.m. Saturday; and 7 p.m. Sunday. Tickets are $20; ticketweb.com.

‘The Sound’ Downtown

Pittsburgh Musical Theater returns to the Byham Theater with “The Sound of Music," featuring Lara Hayhurst as Maria, Rob Jessup as Captain von Trapp Caroline Nicolian as Mother Abbess, Whitney Noelle as Elsa Schraeder and, as Max, Pittsburgh newcomer Thomas Conroy, a previously New York-based actor, director and educator.

Hayhurst will soon be seen among the just-announced cast of the new CLO Cabaret musical “Untitled: A New Musical Comedy About Serious Drama,” opening April 3. The Von Trapp children, from PMT's Richard Rauh Conservatory, are double cast and will alternate shows.

The Rodgers & Hammerstein musical about the real-life singing Von Trapp family, and set during the Nazi invasion of Austria, runs 7:30 p.m. Thursday-Saturday and 2 p.m. Sunday this weekend, then 7:30 p.m. March 13, 2 and 7:30 p.m. March 14 and 2 p.m. March 15. Tickets are $18.25-$55.25; pittsburghmusicals.com or 412-456-6666.

THURSDAY

One Night Standards

Ashley McBryde, the reigning CMA New Artist of the Year and ACM New Female Artist of the Year, stops at Mr. Smalls, Millvale, on her One Night Standards Tour.

It comes in advance of the April 3 release of her sophomore album, “Never Will,” the follow-up to her debut album “Girl Going Nowhere.” The first single, “One Night Standards,” is another taste of what the Washington Post called her “blue-collar storytelling and straightforward singing.”

It begins at 8 p.m. Tickets are $23; mrsmalls.com.

FRIDAY

Party on Mars

Phipps Conservatory and Botanical Gardens pays tribute “the space oddity himself” at a Party in the Tropics event themed Party on Mars.

Yes, Phipps asks that you “don your best Starman attire” while they spin David Bowie in the Oakland greenhouse for the final weekend of the space-themed Orchid and Bonsai Show: Out of This World. There will be dancing, food and drinks.

It runs from 7 to 10 p.m. Admission is $19.95; 21 and over. Advance registration is recommended at phipps.conservatory.org

FRIDAY

Got soul

The Soul Sessions series at the August Wilson Center African American Culture Center with Leela James, a soul singer more than capable of rockin’ the place.

The singer, who was called “Baby Etta,” after Etta James, when growing up in Los Angeles, started her career touring with the Black Eyed Peas and Macy Gray and sang on the posthumous Ray Charles album “Genius & Friends” before she even released a debut album.

That came in 2005 with “A Change Is Gonna Come,” an album that harked back to the R&B/soul of the ‘60s and ‘70s. In 2012 she honored her biggest influence with “Loving You More…In the Spirit of Etta James.”

Her latest project, “Are You Ready?” from Leela James & The Truth, finds her delving into Betty Davis territory of psychedelic soul.

The show is at 8 p.m. Tickets are $28.75; awc.culturaldistrict.org.

FRIDAY-SATURDAY

Echoes of Floyd

Rogers Waters returns to Pittsburgh in July, but you don’t need to wait till then to get your Pink Floyd fix.

Brit Floyd, the clear favorite among Pink Floyd tribute bands, returns for two nights at the Benedum. The band, formed in 2011 by former Australian Pink Floyd member Damian Darlington, will play songs from the entire catalog along with a 23-minute “note-for-note” performance “Echoes,” which appeared on the 1971 album “Meddle.”

They come with a multi-million dollar light show, the iconic circle screen, lasers, inflatables and theatrics.

It’s at 8 p.m. Details at trustarts.org.

FRIDAY-SUNDAY

The hoarder

Off the Wall Productions presents a new play and collaboration — Lissa Brennan’s “Hoard” starts at Carnegie Stage and then will move on to The Cell Theatre in New York City.

In “Hoard,” Viv (played by Off the Wall artistic director Virginia Wall Gruenert) has become a hoarder, alienating her adult children. Her daughter hires Claire (Erica Cuenca) to clean up her mom’s house, and both women begin to uncover underlying traumas.

Off the Wall, 25 W. Main St., Carnegie. 8 p.m. Friday and Saturday and 3 p.m. Sunday this weekend, then 8 p.m. Thursday-Saturday and 3 p.m. Sunday through March 15. Tickets: $5-$35, www.insideoffthewall.com/hoard.

FRIDAY AND SUNDAY

Sibelius symphony

There’s an incredibly delicate, whisper-like clarinet solo at the beginning of Sibelius’ Symphony No. 1 that gives way to sweeping string melodies and churning brass.

This Friday and Sunday the Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra performs Sibelius first Friday and Sunday in a program at Heinz Hall that also features Grieg’s Piano Concerto with pianist Yulianna Avdeeva and music by Berlioz. Sir Mark Elder conducts.

Times are 8 p.m. Friday and 2:30 p.m. Sunday. Tickets are $20-$98 at pittsburghsymphony.org.

SATURDAY

Dashboard 20

Dashboard Confessional is on the road celebrating 20 years since “The Swiss Army Romance,” the record that started it all for the Florida emo band led by Chris Carrabba.

The tour also coincides with the release of “The Best Ones Of The Best Ones,” a career-spanning compilation that follows 2018’s “Crooked Shadows.”

​They do a sold-out show at Stage AE with The Get Up Kids. Doors at 6:30 p.m.

Also, Saturday, Athens, Ga., indie-rock band of Montreal turns up at Mr. Smalls, touring behind “UR FUN,” the 16th album (since 1997) from Kevin Barnes and company. It begins at 8 p.m. with Lily and Horn Horse and Chariot Fade. $20; mrsmalls.com.
