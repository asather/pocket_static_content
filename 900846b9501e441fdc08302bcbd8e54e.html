AIU selects new executive director

The board of the Allegheny Intermediate Unit on Monday named a new executive director.

Robert Scherrer, the superintendent of the North Allegheny School District, will take over the regional public education agency on July 20.

Mr. Scherrer also has served as North Allegheny’s acting superintendent and assistant superintendent for K-12 education.

“I am ready to open the next chapter in my career and lead an organization with the reputation, influence and expertise of the Allegheny Intermediate Unit,” Mr. Scherrer said in a statement. “Through this process, I have had the opportunity to interact with the board and members of the executive leadership team and I see their passion around supporting all students in our region. I am excited and prepared to work closely with the entire AIU community as we create a compelling future for the organization.” 

The AIU is a taxpayer-funded agency that provides specialized services to the county's 42 suburban school districts as well as non-public, charter and vocational-technical schools.

The agency said Mr. Scherrer will begin a “comprehensive entry plan” when he takes office that will allow him to “become immersed in the organization, and position himself as the catalyst to developing a thoughtful, ambitious course of continued growth and improvement at the AIU.”

Before going to North Allegheny, Mr. Scherrer was the principal at Pittsburgh Science and Technology Academy and Taylor Allderdice High School in the Pittsburgh Public Schools. 

Mr. Scherrer also was an assistant principal and co-principal for curriculum in the Woodland Hills School District and an assistant principal at Peters Township High School. He began his career in education as a secondary educator at Baldwin High School. 

Mr. Scherrer will take over for Rosanne Javorsky, who had been serving as interim executive director since July 2018. Before that, Linda Hippert was the AIU’s executive director from 2009 to 2018.

Ms. Javorsky will remain as interim director until Mr. Scherrer takes over. She will then return to her previous position as assistant executive director for teaching and learning.

Mr. Scherrer will have an annual salary of $205,000.

“The board is thrilled to have landed Dr. Scherrer, and we are very excited to bring him into the organization,” AIU board president Debbie Beale said in a statement. “With his broad and diverse leadership and educational experiences, a demonstrated passion for students and families, and a thorough understanding of our region, we believe Dr. Scherrer is an excellent fit for the AIU. We also recognize that Allegheny County has a vibrant educational culture that presents Dr. Scherrer with an opportunity to thrive by working closely with other educational leaders, policy experts, and thought leaders throughout the state and the nation.”
