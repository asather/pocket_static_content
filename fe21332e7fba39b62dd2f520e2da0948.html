7 red-carpet fashion trends from the 2020 Golden Globes

The Golden Globe Awards’ red carpet produced a cornucopia of fashion trends as the lead-in to Hollywood awards season, with the always unpredictable Grammys  (Jan. 26) and the Oscars (Feb. 9) following in the Globes’ glamorous heels.

Here’s a list of trends from the night that saw many a grand entrance, including Billy Porter’s regal white tux with a feather train, Jason Momoa rocking a velvet blazer and tank top and Jennifer Lopez, wrapped in white and green and tied together with a giant gold bow.

Skin: Plunging necklines, thigh-high slits and overflowing bodices — or none at all — ruled the red carpet. Presenter Kerry Washington wore a jeweled harness with a black blazer and silk skirt that left little to the imagination; Saoirse Ronan (“Little Women”) wore a glittery Celine slip gown with lots of leg; presenter Gwyneth Paltrow showed off her figure in a sheer, tiered gown by Fendi.

Monochrome: Tiffany Haddish in a pink dress by Galia Lahav, Scarlett Johansson in a scarlet Vera Wang ballgown with a plunging neckline, Jodie Comer (“Killing Eve”) swathed in emerald green (complete with references to the internet’s adorable favorite Baby Yoda); Nicole Kidman in a red strapless sheath and Reese Witherspoon (“Big Little Lies”) all in white; and best actress winner Renee Zellweger (“Judy”) in a baby blue strapless gown by Armani Privé, 

Puffy sleeves: Comer and Cate Blanchett (“Where’d You Go, Bernadette”) in Mary Katrantzou, along with best actress in a TV drama winner Olivia Colman (“The Crown”), Dakota Fanning; Beyonce — with golden wings and lots of skin — and many more.

Black and white: Cynthia Erivo (“Harriet”) in a body-hugging beaded Thom Browne gown, plus a $3 million Bulgari necklace; director Greta Gerwig (“Little Women”) in off-the-shoulder Proenza Schouler; Zoe Kravitz in polka dots large and small, by Saint Laurent; Margot Robbie, whose top included another trend: intricate embellishments, which apparently took 4,750 sequins and 225 hours to construct.

Works of art: Joey King (“The Act”) in an Iris van Herpen creation that seemed to ripple and flow in a graphic optical illusion, and Blanchett in a rippling yellow gown with a daring woven diamond bodice by Mary Katrantzou.

Men in velvet: So many and all ages, from Lifetime Achievement honoree Tom Hanks to nominee Eddie Murphy, from presenters Ansel Elgort and Chris Evans to youngest nominee Roman Griffin Davis (“JoJo Rabbit”).

Women in suits: Phoebe Waller-Bridge (“Fleabag”), who won best actress in a TV comedy; presenter Kate McKinnon; honoree Ellen DeGeneres and wife Portia de Rossi.
