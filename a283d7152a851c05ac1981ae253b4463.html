You can wash your hands. But can you wash your smartphone?

Your smartphone is disgusting. And now, you can do a little bit more to make it less so.

That first point isn’t new. An oft-cited 2012 study by the University of Arizona — yep, we’re citing it too — revealed that a typical smartphone carries 12 times the bacteria than a common toilet seat. That’s not as awful as it sounds, though — toilet seats generally are cleaned frequently, while it’s tough to thoroughly clean electronic devices. And most of the bacteria found on your iThing comes from you, and is generally harmless.

But but but, you ask — what about COVID-19 germs?

Great question. And until recently, Apple — the manufacturer of our iThings — didn’t have an especially satisfying answer. The company’s cleaning advice in the past has suggested using a soft, lint-free cloth; of course, that will successfully clean fingerprints and other grime from your phone, but it won’t do much to disinfect it.

For the first time, though, Apple has offered a suggestion for doing more to make sure your phone is more than just fingerprint-free. On Monday, Apple released new guidelines that say using 70% isopropyl alcohol wipe or Clorox Disinfecting Wipes on the hard, exterior surfaces of your devices is OK — as long as you’re careful.

Note: No bleach. Don’t submerge your phone in any kind of liquid. And if you get moisture inside your device, it’s probably best to make a service call.

Also note that Apple’s new cleaning guidelines offered no advice about where consumers might find Clorox Disinfecting Wipes.

There is one other option for the hardcore gadget person. Popular Mechanics says that we can purchase UV-C devices that bathe your beloved iThing in ultraviolet light, which kills 99 % of germs instantly. They cost about $80, and unlike disinfectant wipes, they’re actually in stock. For now, anyway.
