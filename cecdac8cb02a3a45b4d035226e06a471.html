Arconic ready to split into two companies on April 1

Arconic Inc. is poised to officially split into two companies on April 1, CEO John Plant said Monday as the Pittsburgh-based aluminum parts producer for aircraft and cars reported higher profits for the fourth quarter on slightly lower revenue.

Revenue was $3.4 billion for the quarter, down 2% from $3.47 billion a year earlier.

Net income was $309 million, or 70 cents per share, up from $218 million, or 44 cents, in the fourth quarter of 2018.

Excluding special items such as restructuring costs, per-share profits were 53 cents vs. 33 cents. That was a notch lower than the consensus estimate on Wall Street of 54 cents per share.

The breakup of Arconic was announced in February 2019 in the wake of the company’s failed sale to a private equity firm. The new operations will be called Howmet Aerospace — which will include the engineered products and forgings segment — and Arconic Corp., which will be formed from the rolled products segment.

Arconic Corp. will be run by current group president of global rolled products Timothy Myers. A CEO for Howmet Aerospace hasn’t been announced, but Mr. Plant has said he intends to remain at the helm “through separation and beyond.”

For the full year, Arconic Inc. posted profits of $470 million, or $1.03 a share, down from $642 million, or $1.30, in 2018.

On Monday, the company issued earnings guidance for 2020 of $2.22 to $2.42 a share, excluding special items, on revenue of $13.9 billion to $14.2 billion.

The guidance was at the low end of what analysts were expecting, reflecting the uncertainty over when Boeing will restart production of the grounded 737 Max airliner.

Arconic was formed in 2016 when Pittsburgh’s Alcoa split in two.
