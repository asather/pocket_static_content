Pirates sign fifth-round draft pick Logan Hofmann

The Pirates announced the signing of fifth-round draft selection Logan Hofmann on Saturday.

Hofmann, a right-handed pitcher out of Northwestern State in Louisiana, is now the second of the Pirates six draft picks that has signed his name on the dotted line, following just a couple of days after first-round pick Nick Gonzales signed his own contract.

A second-team All-American in 2020, according to Collegiate Baseball, Hofmann began his college career at Colby Community College in Kansas, where he set the conference record for career strikeouts and was a third-team All-American in 2019.

When he was drafted at 138th overall, Hofmann, a Canadian native, became the highest-drafted player to ever come out of Saskatchewan.

“We are excited to add Logan to the Pirates organization,” said Pirates General Manager Ben Cherington in a press release. “Logan has performed well at every level of amateur baseball and has a strong combination of stuff, control, feel for pitching and competitiveness. We look forward to seeing Logan in a Pirates uniform.”
