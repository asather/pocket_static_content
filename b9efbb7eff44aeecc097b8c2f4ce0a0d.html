19 cats and kittens saved from dangerous conditions in White Oak home

19 cats including several kittens were rescued Friday morning from “deplorable conditions” in a White Oak home.

After receiving a report of cats running loose in the home, a Humane Officer of the Humane Animal Rescue obtained a warrant to enter the home and seize the animals, according to a release from the Humane Animal Rescue.

Members of the Humane Animal Rescue’s Humane Investigations team found a mother with three kittens, just shy of a one-week-old, on the first floor of the home. One kitten was found dead. The rest of the cats were located in the basement.

The animals were transported to the Humane Animal Rescue’s East End Campus where they were found to have health problems including being underweight and dehydrated, and they were treated for flea dermatitis, ear mites and gingivitis.

The Humane Animal Rescue said the cats and kittens will receive treatment until they are cleared for adoption.
