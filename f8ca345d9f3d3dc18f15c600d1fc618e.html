Federal judge schedules hearing on Teamsters contract fight with Post-Gazette

A federal judge said Thursday that he will hold an evidentiary hearing March 6 on a motion by the Teamsters union to hold the Pittsburgh Post-Gazette in contempt for not honoring the judge's order blocking the paper from laying off employees and reducing the hours of others in violation of the union's contract.

U.S. District Judge J. Nicholas Ranjan in November granted a request for a preliminary injunction filed by Teamsters Local 211, ordering that the company maintain the workers' status quo while contract negotiations continue. He reissued another order clarifying the injunction on Dec. 27.

Since then, the union has asked the judge for the reinstatement of 24 Teamsters who were terminated on Nov. 10 and 47 others who were reduced to part-time.

The judge indicated during a status conference Thursday that he was leaning toward holding the Post-Gazette in contempt and said he has broad authority to impose fines or even prison time, but he also said he needs to honor the company's right to due process.

The judge also ordered the parties into mediation with Jed Melnick of New York to try to reach a global resolution of all issues by the end of February.
