State reports no new COVID-19 deaths; Allegheny County reports 68 new cases

For a third straight day, Allegheny County’s daily increase of COVID-19 cases fell below 100, and the state on Monday reported no new deaths from the virus.

It marked the first time since March 22 that Pennsylvania hasn’t had a COVID-related death.

With 68 new cases, Allegheny County has had 8,310 since its first case in mid-March.

The new cases result from 1,104 tests that were administered between July 9 and August 2. Those who tested positive range in age from 8 to 96 years old, with a median age of 37.

The county also is reporting one new hospitalization, the lowest it has been since July 18, when the county reported zero new hospitalizations.

The total number of Allegheny County residents hospitalized as a result of COVID-19 is 686.

Statewide, there were 565 newly reported cases of the virus, with Western Pennsylvania making up 162 of them. Westmoreland County had 19 new cases.

The state total is now 114,155. The death toll remains at 7,209.
