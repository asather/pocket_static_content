Pa. sees highest single-day increase of COVID-19 cases since early May

The Pennsylvania Department of Health reported 995 new cases of COVID-19, marking the highest single-day increase since May 10, when the state saw an increase of over 1,000 cases. 

The latest data brings the statewide total to 91,299 cases. 

Allegheny County reported six new deaths as a result of COVID-19, the largest single-day increase in deaths since May 22 when the county was still in the yellow phase. This increase includes deaths from April 5 through June 13 and were among people 65 or older. 

With the new deaths, the county death toll is at 194. The state is reporting an additional 33 deaths, bringing its total to 6,787. Westmoreland County, with one new death, was the only other county in Western Pennsylvania to report a virus related death.

Allegheny County also reported its third day of more than 200 new cases in the last week. With 204 more people testing positive for the virus, the county total comes to 3,979. Of these, 3,798 are confirmed and 181 are probable.

Western Pennsylvania saw an increase of 347 new cases, with the majority in Allegheny County. Westmoreland County had the second-highest increase with 35 new cases. Beaver County also saw a significant increase with 28 new cases of the virus.

The increase in Allegheny County’s cases was the result of 2,404 tests given, according to the county’s health department. The tests were administered between June 21 and July 6. The number of positive cases reflects individuals ranging in age from 1 month old to 98 years old, with the median age being 30.

There also were seven additional hospitalizations as a result of the virus, bringing the total to 423 past or present hospitalizations. Of those, 78 required mechanical ventilation and 163 were admitted to intensive care units.

According to data released by the county, the positivity rate for the month is currently at 9.4%, though the county notes that it is incomplete, as they continue to receive more information.

County officials have scheduled a briefing at 4 p.m. Tuesday to address the pandemic.
