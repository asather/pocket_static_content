Carlynton declared forfeit winner over Sto-Rox after late game cancellation

Three hours before his school was set to kick off the football season with a home game against Sto-Rox, Carlynton athletic director Nate Milsom got an email from Sto-Rox coach/athletic director LaRoi Johnson saying they were postponing the game.

The reason for the late news has not been made public, but it does not have to do with COVID-19. Because of that, Carlynton was credited with a season-opening win via forfeit.

“The cancellation was not COVID-related, so it would be treated like any other game that was canceled three hours before kickoff and be counted as a forfeit win for Carlynton,” WPIAL executive director Amy Scheuneman said.

After receiving the email from Johnson at 4:01 p.m., Milsom said he was unsuccessful in multiple attempts trying to reach Johnson. Milsom said the two finally spoke around 6:30. When reached for comment earlier, Milsom said he had no answer for why Sto-Rox was choosing not to play.

“That would be a Sto-Rox question,” Milsom said. “Myself nor the WPIAL have not been able to get a hold of anyone there. I’m looking at 32 Carlynton kids dressed ready to go on the field. They’re heartbroken right now.”

The scheduled WPIAL Class 2A Three Rivers Conference contest was the season opener for both Sto-Rox and Carlynton. Sto-Rox went 10-2 last season when it reached the WPIAL Class 1A championship game. Carlynton finished 3-7 a season ago.

Five WPIAL football teams were forced to postpone their season openers, all because of COVID-19 issues: Mt. Lebanon, Seneca Valley, Peters Township, Kiski Area and Serra Catholic.
