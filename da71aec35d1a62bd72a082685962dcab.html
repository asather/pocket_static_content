Perry South man found guilty of raping child at mother's daycare

A Perry South man was convicted Friday of raping a child and other sexual assault charges.

A jury found David Tyus, 20, guilty of carrying out the assaults at his mother’s in-home day care on the North Side.

Two of the sexual assaults involved victims who were children at the day care; the third involved the rape of a 17-year-old girl in the house when there were no small children present.

Mr. Tyus is accused of raping, fondling and abusing the children while they were attending Friendships Play House daycare on Maple Street.

Allegheny County Common Pleas Judge Jill E. Rangos will sentenced Mr. Tyus in June.

Mr. Tyus’s mother, Victoria Tyus, pleaded guilty in July to one count of endangering the welfare of children for leaving the children alone with her son when he did not have proper clearances.

She is on probation.
