Whitehall man charged with vandalizing police car during Downtown protest

A 26-year-old man is in jail after he being charged with vandalizing a Pittsburgh police cruiser that was set on fire May 30 in Downtown Pittsburgh as a planned demonstration against police brutality turned destructive.

Police have charged Shaheed Hatch of Whitehall with rioting, theft and vandalism, among other charges.

Mr. Hatch was arrested, authorities say, after multiple tips led to a photo on Instagram that showed him — after a large-scale protest following the death of George Floyd, a black man in Minneapolis who died in police custody — holding a stop sign on Washington Place while wearing a Pittsburgh police vest backwards as an unmarked cruiser is ablaze behind him.

The Downtown demonstration turned destructive with the vandalism and burning of a marked Pittsburgh police car, authorities say, during which Mr. Hatch was observed standing nearby. He then leaves the scene, grabs a construction sign and uses its metal pole to smash an unmarked police car's windows, according to a criminal complaint filed Wednesday in the case.

Mr. Hatch is then seen grabbing another man's container of yellow liquid and pouring the substance onto a rag near the cruiser's tailpipe, according to police.

After other people add more flammable objects, the police vehicle is aflame — causing multiple small explosions "that could have endangered innocent civilians or [e]mergency responders," according to the complaint — and everyone involved leaves the scene.

Police also say multiple videos show Mr. Hatch pick up a police life vest and put it on.

Four days later, Pittsburgh narcotics detectives working a Pleasant Hills demonstration say Mr. Hatch spoke with them about recent events in Pittsburgh. The detectives say they were able to identify Mr. Hatch as a man from the May 30 rioting.

Mr. Hatch is in jail on $25,000 bond.

“When I saw the video of George Floyd being murdered it sent a shockwave through my entire soul,” he said in his Instagram post. “So I went to protest peacefully.

“Once things got out of hand due to a select few … people and cops I saw this vest and stop sign on the ground and picked it up. The reverse side of the stop sign reads slow. I wanted the message to be clear for people to stop and slow down to think about why things are happening.”
