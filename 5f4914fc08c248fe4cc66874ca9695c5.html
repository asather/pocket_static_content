Pirates stand pat at MLB trade deadline

This is a developing story and will be updated:

MILWAUKEE — The Pirates made a bit of a curious move Monday for the MLB trade deadline.

They did nothing.

Their lone move, according to a source, will remain sending Jarrod Dyson to the Chicago White Sox for $243,300 of international bonus pool money for the 2019-20 signing period, which was extended through Oct. 15.

Why did the Pirates not do anything?

Good question.

And one we’ll likely need general manager Ben Cherington to fully answer. Cherington is scheduled to meet with the media at 4:30 p.m. EST.

The Pirates came into Monday’s deadline with the belief that they had at least a few assets to peddle. Perhaps Keone Kela and Adam Frazier would go. Even high-leverage reliever Richard Rodriguez.

None of that came to fruition, as the Pirates were one of only a few teams that stood pat.

There are a couple reasons, though. For one, Chris Archer is out for the season after having surgery to alleviate symptoms of thoracic outlet syndrome. He would’ve been one of their biggest pieces.

Then, Keone Kela went down with right forearm tightness, continuing a run of throwing-arm issues he’s experienced since the start of the 2019 season.

Other players — Frazier is a good example — simply struggled early, potentially hurting their trade value.

Moran back

The Pirates on Monday announced that they've reinstated Colin Moran from the seven-day concussion list. To clear space, Will Craig has been optioned to their alternate training site in Altoona.
