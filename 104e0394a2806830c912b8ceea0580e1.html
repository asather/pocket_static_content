GOING LIVE: A list of concerts and performances in the virtual world

While we cope with the social distancing due to the coronavirus, performers near and far are stepping into the virtual realm to entertain us and bring us closer together.

Here’s a selection of performances you can find streaming online. We’re just starting, so this list will be updated regularly:

WEDNESDAY, MARCH 18

Music

Zach Schmidt (5 p.m.): Baldwin native and Americana singer-songwriter Zach Schmidt, now based in Nashville, will go live with his Quarantine Show tonight with fiance band member Jackie Berkley. He writes: “Since SXSW was canceled and we were not able to attend, we thought a streaming show in our living room seemed like a good idea. We are taking the suggestion to stay socially distanced during COVID-19 very seriously but still want to be able to provide music for people. Pay what you want to get in to the show but there is a tip jar if you are inclined and able… We will play some old songs, some new and some very very new.” 

Mike Medved (8:30 p.m.): Pittsburgh country-rocker Mike Medved will do a live concert from his home studio Wednesday night and Friday morning at 9:30 a.m. RSVP to the event to receive a FB notification when he goes live. He writes, “please add your song requests to the event comments to help me make a setlist!”

Alone/Together/Streaming (7 p.m.): Hear Corp and 25 Carrick continue their Live at 25 series with a conversation with councilperson Deb Gross, musical guest Jacquea Mae, house band Andre Kristofer Buccini Costello, Jake Miclot (True Believer) and Attack Theater’s Peter Kope and Michelle De La Reza.

Rhett Miller (9 p.m.): Frontman for alt-country band The Old ’97s.

Ben Gibbard: Live from Home (4 p.m.): Frontman for Deathcab for Cutie.

THURSDAY, MARCH 19

Music

Indigo Girls (6 p.m.): Free livestream show and Q&A with the folk-rock duo.

Theater

Pittsburgh Public Theater’s PlayTime (7 p.m.): New online reading series debuts live on Thursday and continues Friday with excerpts from Shakespeare’s “Henry V.” Hosted live by artistic director Marya Sea Kaminski, PlayTime continues March 26 and 27, with Oscar Wilde’s “The Importance of Being Earnest. (The URL currently takes you to Zoom and offers prompts). The PlayTime series is free, but donations are welcome.

FRIDAY, MARCH 20

Dance

Isol-art (8 p.m.): Watch artists from around the world — including a couple from Pittsburgh — perform in their bedrooms while audiences watch them online from theirs. Pittsburgh-based multidisciplinary performance artists Taylor Knight and Anna Thompson of slowdanger will be among the approximately two dozen people slated to participate in the livestream. The event also is a fundraiser for artists impacted by COVID-19.

SATURDAY, MARCH 21

Comedy

Arcade Comedy Theater (9 p.m.): “Knights of the Arcade: Online Adventure” finds the Downtown venue’s “merry band of Dungeons & Dragons improvisers” taking their show online for the first time, in place of their canceled stage show. They will be broadcasting a free show on Twitch.tv (and collecting donations to support the Arcade).

WEDNESDAY, MARCH 25

Trivia

Trivia night (7-9 p.m.): Zelienople brewpub ShuBrew -- temporarily closed -- takes its trivia night online, led by co-owner Zachary Shumaker. The event’s Facebook page wryly notes that it is BYOB. “There will be lots of prizes and laughs.. and more than likely some technical difficulties, too. We haven't done this before, but we are figuring it out and it will be a night to remember. If nothing else, we would love you to join us just so that we can interact with our community during this hard time.” 

SATURDAY, APRIL 11

Festival

In House Art Festival: An online arts festival created by Carnegie Mellon School of Drama juniors Rachel Kolb & Adira Rosen. They are asking artists to form online groups, "make some art -- a play, a dance, a painting, truly anything as long as it can be viewed on our website. Get creative and innovate!" Upload by April 9 at 11:59 p.m.; rules and how-to's are listed there. The festival will premiere at 8 p.m. April 11.

ARCHIVED

Music

Code Orange: Pittsburgh metal band’s March 20 release rolling out new album “Underneath.” 

Bastard Bearded Irishmen: St. Patrick’s Day living room show from the Pittsburgh Celtic punks.

The Metropolitan Opera: If you’ve ever wanted to hear the Met but haven’t felt like schlepping to New York City, the Metropolitan Opera is streaming some of opera’s greatest shows from its archives for free. A new show goes up at 7:30 p.m. each evening and remains for about 20 hours. “La Boheme” is currently available, and it’ll be followed by Verdi’s “Il Trovatore” and “La Traviata,” Donizetti’s “La Fille du Regiment” and more.  to stream.

Chamber Music Society of Lincoln Center: The Chamber Music Society is publishing recordings from its archives for free. From string quartets to to solo instrument recitals and more, the archive features some of the greatest artists in the world.

ONGOING

Stars in the House: A daily YouTube series of live performances, at 2 and 8 p.m., hosted by SiriusXM radio host Seth Rudetsky, with Tony-winning Broadway stars, to benefit The Actors Fund.
