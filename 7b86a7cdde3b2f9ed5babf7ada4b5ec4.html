'Incomprehensible': War memorial in Lawrenceville vandalized on holiday

Police are investigating after a war memorial in Pittsburgh’s Lawrenceville neighborhood was vandalized overnight on Memorial Day.

The statue of the World War I Doughboy, located at Penn and Butler streets where it meets 34th Street, was covered in red paint. A message was spray-painted on the base of the memorial, reading “JUNE 19, 1986, GLORY TO THE DAY OF HEROISM.”

"Vandalizing a memorial on any day is wrong, but it is incomprehensible to vandalize this memorial on a day in which we honor those who served and gave their lives for the freedoms we enjoy today,” said city Public Safety Director Wendell Hissrich. “Pittsburgh Police will vigorously investigate this crime.”

Police spokeswoman Cara Cruz said Monday morning that police are reviewing “all available video footage” from the area. She also said that arrangements are being made to clean the memorial.

Police ask that anyone with information contact them at (412) 323-7800.

It’s unclear around what time the memorial was vandalized.

The Doughboy statue was erected in 1921.

Ken Diulus, a resident of Lawrenceville who served in the U.S. Navy from 1982 to 1985, walked past the statue Monday and couldn’t believe his eyes.

"I've lived my whole life here, with the exception of when I was in the service," Mr. Diulus said, "and I have never seen anything like this."
