Former Pirate Starling Marte’s wife dies of heart attack

Former Pirates outfielder Starling Marte shared on social media Monday night that his wife, Noelia, has died of a heart attack.

“It is a moment of indescribable pain,” Marte wrote. “On behalf of my family, I am grateful for the expressions of esteem and solidarity in this difficult time.”

Starling and Noelia Marte had two kids, Starling Jr. (born in September 2013) and Tiana (born in May 2016).

The Pirates traded the 31-year-old Marte to the Arizona Diamondbacks in January for a pair of prospects, shortstop Liover Peguero and pitcher Brennan Malone.

Marte, who was Pittsburgh's longest-tenured player at the time of the trade, signed with the Pirates in 2007 and debuted with the big-league club in 2012. He won two Gold Gloves and was an All-Star in 2016, when he stole 47 bases and batted .311.

Marte was coming off a strong offensive year in 2019, when he set career highs in home runs (23), RBIs (82) and OPS (.845).
