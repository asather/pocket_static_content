The Clarks, Allman Betts Band and CAAMP added to drive-in lineups

The Clarks will perform two shows — Sept. 25 and 26 — at the Brownsville Drive-In in Grindstone, Pa., which is 42 miles from Downtown Pittsburgh.

The Pittsburgh band, which launched concerts at the Starlight Drive-In in Butler in July, will be joined by Blended Reality.

Tickets range from $110 to $150 per vehicle (up to four people) and are on sale now at eventbrite.com.

Gates for both dates will open at 6 p.m. with music starting at 8 p.m. Parking will be general admission (first come, first serve). There will not be any reserved parking.

The drive-in is at 6231 National Pike East, Grindstone (15442).

Also announced from Drusky Entertainment:

Roots band CAAMP will play the Starlight Drive-In on Sept. 24. Tickets on sale at 10 a.m. Friday ($150-$200).

Southern rockers The Allman Betts Band will play the Starlight on Sept. 17. Tickets on sale now ($140-$180).

Comedian Iliza Shlesinger performs at the Starlight on Oct. 2 (on sale Friday; $160-$300)) and Hannibal Burress performs Sept. 23 (on sale now; $120-$200).

Christian metal band Skillet plays the Starlight on Oct. 3 (on sale Friday; $200-$280).
