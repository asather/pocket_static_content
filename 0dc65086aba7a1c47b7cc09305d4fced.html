Broadway legend Chita Rivera set to kick off 2020-21 Trust Cabaret Series

Chita Rivera continues to be a force of nature at 87 years old, and she’s bringing that energy and numbers from her legendary career to Pittsburgh, to open the 2020-21 Trust Cabaret Series on Oct. 12.

The series also includes Tony- and Emmy-nominee Brandon Victor Dixon (“Shuffle Along,” “Hamilton”), who starred as Judas in NBC’s “Jesus Christ Superstar Live,” and Ali Stroker, the “Glee” alum who won a Tony as Ado Annie in Broadway’s revamped revival of “Oklahoma!”

Randal Miller, director of dance programming and special projects with the Pittsburgh Cultural Trust, said, “We are excited for this fantastic season and honored that these stars will share their extraordinary talents with all of us, beginning with the one of the most recognizable talents on stage and screen, the legendary Chita Rivera.”

The Trust Cabaret Series is in its ninth season at the intimate Greer Cabaret, Downtown. Showtimes are at 7 and 9:30 p.m. unless otherwise indicated.

• Oct. 12: “Chita: A Legendary Celebration” — The Tony Award-winner and Kennedy Center honoree will perform numbers from her career highlights, including “West Side Story,” “Sweet Charity,” “Chicago,” “Kiss of the Spider Woman,” “Bye, Bye, Birdie” and more, accompanied by her longtime trio. “You leave this show walking on air!” raved The New York Times.

• Nov. 9: Karen Akers — The cabaret artist, a recipient of the 2005 NY Nightlife Award for Outstanding Female Cabaret Vocalist in a Major Engagement and the 2009 Legends Award, Akers returns to the Pittsburgh with a voice that has graced Carnegie Hall, the Hollywood Bowl and other venues worldwide. She also was in the original Broadway casts of “Grand Hotel” and her Tony-nominated performance in “Nine.”

• Feb. 15, 2021: “Broadway Princess Party” — Broadway’s Cinderella, Laura Osnes, and other stage princesses (and a guest prince) bring the party back after making their Pittsburgh debut at the Improv at the Waterfront. Recommended for ages 6 and older, with sing-alongs a certainty and royal attire encouraged.

• March 15 and 16, 2021, at 7 p.m. — Ali Stroker won the 2019 Tony Award for Best Featured Actress in a Musical after making history as the first actor in a wheelchair to appear on Broadway, in Deaf West’s 2015 revival of “Spring Awakening.” Her TV appearances include Fox’s “Glee,” a role she won through “The Glee Project” competition series. An honorary board member of The National Disability Theatre, Stroker practices her motto: “Turning Your Limitations into Your Opportunities,” part of her mission to improve lives through the arts.

• April 12: Brandon Victor Dixon — On Broadway, Dixon recently starred as Aaron Burr in “Hamilton,” along with credits including “Shuffle Along” (Tony nomination), “Motown” and “The Color Purple” (Tony nomination). His screen appearances include Spike Lee’s “She’s Gotta Have It” and Amazon Studios’ “Modern Love.” Dixon’s debut single, “#WeAre,” is available on iTunes, with proceeds benefiting YoungNewYorkers.org.

Subscription packages for the 2020-21 Trust Cabaret Series are on sale now; single tickets go on sale in the fall of 2020; trustarts.org or 412-456-6666.
