PPG plans restructuring program to stem losses from COVID-19

PPG on Monday said it is implementing broad cost cuts, including voluntary employee buyouts, as it tries to recover from a severe economic downturn during the global pandemic.

The paint and coatings giant said sales in April dropped by about 35% compared with April 2019.

Sales in May improved slightly and were down less than 30%, PPG said.

The company is targeting $160 million to $170 million in annual pre-tax savings from its actions and expects savings of about $25 million to $35 million in 2020.

“These measures will enable the company to come out of the crisis with lower structural costs,” Michael McGarry, chairman and chief executive, said in a statement.

The actions will result in a restructuring charge in the second quarter of $160 million to $180 million pretax and $125 million to $140 million after-tax, or 52 cents to 58 cents per share.

Most of that cost is related to employee severance, PPG said.

Future restructuring costs of about $10 million will be incurred over future quarters.

In April, PPG said it had taken some immediate steps to cut costs in connection with the impact of COVID-19 including salary cuts for top executives, employee furloughs and deferred capital expenditures.

PPG said the voluntary separation packages were offered in the U.S. and Canada but did not specify how many employees were eligible or accepted the offer.

The company has 47,000 company employees worldwide and about 2,400 in the Pittsburgh region.

It is scheduled to release second-quarter earnings in July.
