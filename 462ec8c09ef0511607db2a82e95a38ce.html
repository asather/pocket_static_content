Whitehall man charged with stockpiling weapons and making threats is ordered detained

A Whitehall man arrested last month after police said he had homemade bombs and many guns in his car and house will remain jailed.

A federal magistrate judge on Wednesday ordered that Kurt Cofano, 33, be detained pending trial in U.S. District Court.

Mr. Cofano was indicted in July on firearms and explosives charges.

Police said they found him in possession of homemade bombs on July 9 and 10. He also was in possession of marijuana and had a gun. Authorities said that from May through July, he made destructive devices without registering them.

In all, federal agents said Mr. Cofano had 12 guns, 14 improvised grenades and nine destructive devices labeled "M101."

The U.S. attorney's office said Mr. Cofano had previously threatened on social media to blow up the Treasury Department and kill people at the CIA.

Police were looking for him on a mental health warrant when his car was located in Dormont and pulled over in Mt. Lebanon. The bomb squad searched the car and found what appeared to be bomb-making material and what looked like remote detonators.

He was taken to a hospital for a psychiatric evaluation while agents and the bomb squad searched his house.

Inside, they said, they found more bombs, chemicals, a sniper manual and explosive components.

Agents said they found three assault-style rifles, including one equipped with a rocket-launcher, a  shotgun, other rifles and three handguns.

Prosecutors asked that Mr. Cofano be detained as a danger to the public.
