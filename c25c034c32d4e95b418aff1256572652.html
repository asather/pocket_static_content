Longtime mob associate Robert Iannelli and son plead guilty to sports-betting, numbers crimes

Robert Iannelli, a fixture in Western Pennsylvania illegal gambling for decades, pleaded guilty Wednesday in a Westmoreland County courtroom to charges related to running a sports-betting and numbers operation, the state attorney general's office said.

Iannelli, 90, identified by the former Pennsylvania Crime Commission as an associate of the largely-defunct LaRocca-Genovese crime family in Pittsburgh, pleaded to a count of overseeing a corrupt organization and running illegal lotteries. His son, Rodney Iannelli, 60, also pleaded to illegal lotteries, bookmaking and a conspiracy-related charge.

Westmoreland County Judge Meagan Bilik-DeFazio sentenced both men to a decade of probation and ordered them to jointly pay $75,000. They were both also ordered to forfeit $225,000 in gambling proceeds as part of a plea agreement.

The charges stem from an investigation into numbers-running and sports betting conducted by the Iannellis and the late Mark Diulus, the attorney general's office said.

The enterprise operated throughout Allegheny and Westmoreland counties and reached into Washington County.

Robert Iannelli, of Pittsburgh, has long been associated with the Pittsburgh mafia and has numerous gambling convictions dating back 50 years.
