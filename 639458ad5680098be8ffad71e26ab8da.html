Comic book fans can get their fix locally, using mail orders and curbside pickups

You can’t browse the aisles, but you can still get your weekly comic book fix and support your favorite local stores.

Some stores have begun curbside pickups of pre-orders, and mail orders are another route to the latest from DC, Marvel and more during this time of closings and social distancing. Here are details on a few of the local shops and how to stay current on your comics:

• The upstairs Oakland haven known as Phantom of the Attic is closed, but you can place mail orders via comics@pota-oakland.com or call 412-621-1210. For information about in-stock orders through Friday, check Phantom’s Facebook page. Phantom Games also has switched to mail order; call 412-682-6290, email what@potagames.com, or send a direct message via Facebook.

• Eide’s Entertainment at 112 Penn Ave., Uptown, is taking mail orders and doing curbside pickup. Call 412-261-0900 or visit Eide’s on Facebook or www.eides.com for details. Eide’s also has an eBay store.

• New Dimensions Comics has six sites, at the Waterfront, Homestead, Pittsburgh Mills Mall, Tarentum; Cranberry; Butler; Ellwood City; and Ohio Valley Mall, St. Clairsville, Ohio. The stores previously had a mail-order subscribers list but now will take orders from all customers. Details of how it works are at http://ndcomics.com/mail-order-is-now-live/. Curbside pickups have ceased, but in-store events set for late April are so far intact.
