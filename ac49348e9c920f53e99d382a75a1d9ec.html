Trevor Williams, Pirates Charities to fund drive-up distribution for food bank

As part of the Major League Baseball’s “Home Plate Project,” pitcher Trevor Williams and Pirates Charities will donate $30,000 to the Greater Pittsburgh Community Food Bank, the team announced in a press release Friday.

The money will help fund a drive-up food distribution at the food bank’s Duquesne headquarters on Monday, April 27, as the donation will provide meals for up to 1,000 families in the Pittsburgh region.

“I am honored to again work with these incredible groups and make an impact locally with the Greater Pittsburgh Community Food Bank to support children and families throughout Pittsburgh,” Williams said in a statement. “The idea of the Home Plate Project is to support some of the most vulnerable in our communities, and during this pandemic, we felt this help was needed immediately.”

More than 50 MLB Players from all 30 clubs raised nearly $1 million through the "Home Plate Project," providing over 4 million meals to support childhood hunger prevention.

The Pirates and Pirates Charities made a $50,000 donation in March that supported multiple food distribution events throughout southwestern Pennsylvania.

Williams isn't the only Pirates player who has recently contributed to the food bank. Jameson Taillon donated approximately $5,000, which he won while playing Fortnite with Penguins winger Zach Aston-Reese in a TwitchRivals SuperGames tournament.
