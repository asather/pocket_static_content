Buying Here: Starter home in Edgewood priced at $180,000

Alicia and Daniel Lloyd love their neighborhood so much that they bought another home just three minutes away from 228 Elm St. in Edgewood.

“We were expecting our second child and thought we might want to make a move. We really love the area so much,” he said.

Mr. Lloyd, an agent for Howard Hanna Real Estate Services, has listed the home for $179,999 (MLS No. 1422388). It is open by appointment (www.howardhanna.com or 724-889-5206).

Built in 1920, the brick house has four bedrooms, two full baths and more than 1,500 square feet of living space. It has lots of original woodwork and sits on a level lot with three off-street parking spaces.

The entry has its original paneling and staircase. The floors were covered with brown carpet but now feature new Brazilian hardwood on the first floor and walnut laminate on the second floor. The entry and kitchen were redone with gray tile.

The dining room has a decorative fireplace and new hardwood floors. The adjacent living room has lots of windows.

In the kitchen, wood cabinetry and stainless-steel appliances complement solid-surface counters. The small cooking island is topped with butcher block. One cabinet is used as a pantry and a door leads to the backyard filled with mature trees and shrubs.

Three bedrooms are on the second floor. The master bedroom has an adjacent bathroom with a large walk-in shower. All of the doors are original and the hardware has been restored.

“All of the knobs in the house have been refurbished,” Mr. Lloyd said. “A local handyman took all of the handles off and refurbished them. The patina is wonderful.”

The dark gray nursery has a trio of tall windows and floating shelves. Another bedroom features a large map of the world against creamy white walls.

The hall bathroom, which has original rose-colored porcelain tile and an alcove tub, services the other two second-floor bedrooms. 

There are two large rooms on the third floor. One is painted bright blue and is used as a playroom. The other is used as a home office and has original linoleum tile.

“Technically, the home has two bedrooms up there, but we didn’t want to stage them as bedrooms,” Mr. Lloyd said.

The laundry is in the unfinished basement. There is central air conditioning and forced-air heat.

The property’s Allegheny County assessed value is $92,500. Over the past three years, five houses have sold in the 200 block of Elm Street for prices ranging from $40,000 in July 2019 to $215,000 in June 2019 (www2.alleghenycounty.us/RealEstate/Search.aspx).

The house is near the Regent Square business district, Edgewood Towne Center and the ramp to the Parkway East.

“We fell in love with the sidewalks, tree-lined streets and the nearby dog park,” Mr. Lloyd said. “It’s close enough to the city and far enough for a quiet stroll.”

The homeowner and agent said it is a great starter home for a first-time home buyer.

“A lot of people are getting pushed out of the market in the East End,” he said. “We are seeing the footprint push outward for more affordability.”
