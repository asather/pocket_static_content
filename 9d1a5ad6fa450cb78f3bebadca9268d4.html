Search for a new provost at Point Park University announced

Point Park University on Monday announced plans for a nationwide search for a successor to Provost John Pearson, who has held the position since 2016 and is retiring.

The chosen candidate will begin work in early summer 2021, following Mr. Pearson’s departure at the end of the 2020-21 academic year, officials said.

Mr. Pearson shared his plans with the campus in February, though Point Park did not make a public announcement at the time, spokesman Lou Corsaro said. Mr. Pearson came to Point Park from Stetson University in DeLand, Fla., where he was vice president of academic affairs.

He could not immediately be reached for comment Monday.

“We are seeking a candidate with a strong record as a teacher and scholar, combined with a successful record of increasing responsible academic leadership and administrative experience,” said Paul Hennigan, president of Point Park. “This is a time of change, and we need a leader that can articulate and advance the university’s vision and strategy with energy, passion and decisiveness.”

The president added: “John is a passionate educator who has left an indelible mark on Point Park with his leadership and guidance.”

The private university Downtown has about 3,900 students in five schools, including its Conservatory of Performing Arts.
