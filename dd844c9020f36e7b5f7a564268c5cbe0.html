Duquesne drug dealer and five-time offender gets 22 years in federal prison

A Duquesne man described by a federal prosecutor as a "recidivist drug dealer" was sentenced Tuesday in federal court to 22 years in prison for narcotics trafficking, his fifth conviction.

U.S. District Judge David Cercone imposed that term on Donte Taylor, 37, of Catherine Street.

Taylor was convicted of possession with intent to distribute crack cocaine, cocaine, heroin, fentanyl and marijuana.

At his trial, prosecutors presented evidence that on May 10, 2018, Taylor was on state parole for prior drug trafficking offenses when parole agents came to his house for a home visit. They smelled marijuana and Taylor's co-defendant, Ericka Smith, gave agents and police consent to search.

The search turned up 279 grams of crack, 33 grams of cocaine, 56 grams of heroin, 12 grams of fentanyl, 155 grams of marijuana, $14,000 in cash, drug scales and other drug trafficking material.

Assistant U.S. Attorney Mark Gurzo said this is Taylor's fifth conviction for dealing drugs.

Taylor's lawyer, James Brink, said the government's request for a 30-year term was excessive and suggested 10 years was more reasonable.

In addition to the 22 years, the judge ordered that Taylor be on probation for eight years.

The government withdrew charges against Ericka Smith.
