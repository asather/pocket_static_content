After 13 terms in office, state Rep. Harry Readshaw announces retirement

In the midst of facing a progressive primary challenge, Pennsylvania Rep. Harry Readshaw announced on Tuesday that he will retire at the end of the current legislative session.

Mr. Readshaw, D-Allegheny, has represented the 36th legislative district since 1995, and currently chairs the House Professional Licensure Committee.

His retirement will take effect after the end of the two-year legislative session on Nov. 30.

In a statement, Mr. Readshaw he's felt "honored" to have support from his constituents over 13 terms.

Mr. Readshaw hadn't yet announced if he would run again for his seat, which is up for primary election on April 28. He would have faced progressive activist Jessica Benham, a 28-year-old South Side Slopes resident who helped found an advocacy group in Pittsburgh that supports autistic people.

The primary and general elections will proceed as usual, but without Mr. Readshaw on the ballot, a spokesman for the House Democrats confirmed.

If elected, Ms. Benham would be the first autistic woman to serve in the state legislature, as well as the first LGBTQ+ female legislator.
