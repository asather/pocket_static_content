Kyle DeGregorio expected to be new girls basketball coach at Pine-Richland

Kyle DeGregorio was let go as Baldwin High School’s girls basketball coach early last month, but he wasn’t long without a job.

DeGregorio is expected to be the new girls coach at Pine-Richland. The Post-Gazette has learned that DeGregorio was offered the job and accepted, but his hiring is not official until the Pine-Richland school board approves it at a meeting Monday. DeGregorio declined comment on the matter.

DeGregorio has been a coach for 37 years, including stints as the Baldwin boys and girls coach, and the Belle Vernon boys coach. He also was a head coach in Eastern Pennsylvania. He had been Baldwin’s girls coach the past two seasons and had turned around the Highlanders after they won only 12 games in the two previous seasons. Baldwin was 13-11 in 2019-20 and had almost its entire team back next season.

But the Baldwin school board opened DeGregorio’s position May 6, a move that shocked DeGregorio. He said he wasn’t given a reason for his dismissal, but DeGregorio, 60, said he wanted to coach again. The Pine-Richland job opened when Tom Reighard resigned after this past season.

DeGregorio is retiring later this month as a guidance counselor at Baldwin.

Meanwhile, the Pine-Richland boys basketball position is still open. Jeff Ackermann resigned to become the Baldwin boys coach in May, and Pine-Richland athletic director Sean Simmons said the school board will most likely hire a boys coach later this month.

Kelly in line for Penn-Trafford job

In another WPIAL basketball coaching move, Doug Kelly is expected to be hired as Penn-Trafford’s boys coach. Kelly is the recommended candidate to the school board, but the board still has to approve his hiring later this month.

Kelly was a Penn-Trafford girls assistant coach this past season and before that spent time as a boys assistant under Jim Rocco, who resigned at the end of the season. But Kelly has head coaching experience. He was Franklin Regional’s coach for eight seasons (2002-10) and his 2008 team was 20-8 with a section championship.

Southmoreland hires Cernuto

Amber Cernuto is the new girls basketball coach at Southmoreland. She succeeds Brian Pritts, who resigned after leading the Scotties to the WPIAL Class 4A championship game this past season.

Cernuto was an assistant under Pritts from 2017-19. She is the mother of Olivia Cernuto, who was a standout freshman guard for the Scotties this past season.
