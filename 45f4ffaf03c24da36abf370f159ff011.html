Allegheny County adopts $15 demolition fee

A fee will now be imposed on real estate transactions in Allegheny County to help fund the demolition of derelict buildings.

The state-enabled legislation to impose a $15 fee on all new or transferred mortgage and deed recording transactions became effective immediately upon county council’s unanimous vote of approval Tuesday.

The county estimates that the fee could raise roughly $2 million a year to demolish vacant and run-down properties.

Adoption at the county level is enabled by Pennsylvania’s Act 152, effective January 2017, which allowed counties a 10-year window to collect the special demolition fee.

As of August, 19 counties had passed enabling legislation to collect the fee. Armstrong, Beaver, Butler, Fayette and Westmoreland counties were among them.

The opportunity to collect the money expires in 2027.
