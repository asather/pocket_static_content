Small earthquake recorded in Western Pennsylvania

A small earthquake was recorded in Westmoreland County early Tuesday morning, according to the U.S. Geological Survey.

The 2.9 magnitude quake was recorded in Bolivar, which is a little more than an hour east of Pittsburgh near Johnstown.

The USGS said the quake occurred around 4:30 a.m. UTC, which is about 12:30 a.m. EST. 

The earthquake occurred at a depth of 3.1 miles, the USGS reported. 

WKBN-TV, of Youngstown, Ohio, reported no damage was reported from the quake, and so far no one has reported feeling any tremors in the area.

The last time an earthquake was reported in Pennsylvania was in April, when a 2.1 magnitude tremor was reported in Wyomissing, Berks County.
