'Comfortable sophistication:' Another co-working company sets its sights on Pittsburgh

Another co-working provider could have its eye on the Steel City.

Pittsburgh is “one of the target cities” that Hana, a division of the CBRE real estate firm, is considering for future expansion, according to Jeffrey Ackerman, CBRE managing director.

Hana already has opened locations in Dallas, Tx., Irvine, Ca., and London. It also has spots planned in Arlington, Va., and Philadelphia.

In addition, it just announced a deal to partner with Silverstein Properties to open a flex space at 3 World Trade Center in New York City. The 85,000-square-foot space will be spread over two floors.

Mr. Ackerman said that Hana has yet to start an actual search for space in Pittsburgh.

On its website, Hana bills itself as a flexible workspace provider that marries “comfortable sophistication with thoughtful design.”

At its 2 1/2-floor space in Dallas, the shared workspace featured polished wood and stone, bright ceramic tiles and high-quality furnishings, according to a report in the Dallas Morning News.

Amenities listed on Hana’s website include a dedicated concierge, team suites with configurable workspace designs, premium shared space, large and small conference rooms, luxurious lounge seating options and Herman Miller sit-stand desks and chairs, state-of-the-art acoustic design and sound-masking technology, interactive whiteboards, and free coffee.

In Pittsburgh, Hana would join a growing number of co-work providers, including Beauty Shoppe, Regus, Industrious, Novel Coworking, Serendipity Labs, and ProSuites2.

Last October, co-working giant WeWork scrapped plans to open as much as 105,000 square feet of space in U.S. Steel Tower after it decided against an initial public offering.

U.S. Steel Tower also is the location of CBRE’s Pittsburgh headquarters, making it a possible candidate for Hana should it launch a search for space Downtown.
