Flush and boil water advisory in effect in Polish Hill

A precautionary flush and boil water advisory is in effect Thursday evening in Pittsburghâ€™s Polish Hill neighborhood due to a water main break.

The advisory affects around 400 households in the area between West Penn Park and the Bloomfield Bridge and will continue until further notice, according to the Pittsburgh Water and Sewer Authority.

A water buffalo for those affected will be placed at the West Penn Recreation Center on 30th Street.

An 8-inch water main broke this afternoon and resulted in low and no pressure in the neighborhood. A loss in pressure can cause contaminants to enter the water, leading to the need for the advisory.

The break was isolated around 3:30 p.m., and repairs were set to wrap up at 11 p.m.

The advisory applies to water used for drinking, making ice, washing dishes, brushing teeth and food preparation. More information can be found at the PWSA website.
