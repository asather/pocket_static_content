Working from home? We want to see your creative remote setups

Employees across the country have been asked to work from home if possible to try to prevent the spread of the coronavirus in the workplace.

For those who have office space in their homes, the transition from cubicle desk to home desk may have been an easy one. But for others who may be working from home for the first time, it might have taken some creativity, trial and error to figure out the best way to work remotely and be productive.

Major electronic retailers like Best Buy and Staples have seen an increase in demand for computers, monitors and other technologies that make at-home work more accessible.  

Whether you are working from your couch, kitchen, dining room table, or even your bedroom, the Post-Gazette has asked readers to share with us their creative work-from-home setups.
