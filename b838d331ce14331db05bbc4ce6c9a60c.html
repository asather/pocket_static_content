Two sentenced to home confinement in rare book case

John Schulman, the owner of Caliban bookshop in Oakland was sentenced Friday to four years home confinement and twelve years of probation for selling materials stolen from Carnegie Library’s rare book collection. He was also ordered to pay restitution of more than $55,000.

Gregory Priore, the library collection’s archivist, was sentenced to three years of home confinement and 12 years of probation for providing the materials.
