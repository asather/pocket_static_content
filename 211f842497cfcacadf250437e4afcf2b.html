Allegheny County reports 45 new COVID-19 cases

Allegheny County has reported 45 new positive cases — the highest number of new cases since May 18, when the county saw an increase of 38 new cases in one day.

This brings the county total to 2,220 cases of the virus. Of those, 296 of the cases — 13% of the county’s total — are in health care workers.

Because of the steep increase in cases, the county Health Department plans to hold a COVID-19 briefing at 3 p.m. Monday to address ways to prevent the number of cases from rising further. The briefing will be streamed on the Allegheny County Health Department’s Facebook page.

The county has reported no new deaths for the second day in a row, keeping the total at 178.

The state is reporting 456 new cases of the virus, bringing the statewide total to 82,186.

There are only three additional deaths in Pennsylvania -- one each in Berks, Lancaster and Lehigh counties -- making the total number of COVID-19 related deaths in the state 6,426.

The number of hospitalizations in the Allegheny County rose by one for a total of 365 past or present hospitalizations.
