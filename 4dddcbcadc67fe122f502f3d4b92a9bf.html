Pennsylvania reports 920 new COVID-19 cases and 25 deaths; Allegheny County 57 cases and one death

Pennsylvania reported 920 additional COVID-19 cases across the state Saturday and 25 additional deaths.

The state Department of Health said Saturday’s results were based on 28,365 test results, the most test results on any one day.

Allegheny County reported 57 new cases Saturday and one additional death.

The new numbers raise the statewide total for COVID-19 cases to 143,805 since the pandemic began in March. A total of 7,862 deaths have been attributed to the virus in Pennsylvania.

The state said Saturday’s results continue to show an increasing number of cases in younger people. Cases among people 19 to 24 now outnumber cases in the age groups 50 to 64 and 65 and older.

In southwest part of the state, the 19-24 age group accounted for approximately 5% of cases in April compared to approximately 29% so far in September. The numbers in other sections of the state are from approximately 7% in April to nearly 69% in September in the north central part of the state; from 6% in April to nearly 41% in September in the northeast; from nearly 5% in April to approximately 33% in the southeast; from nearly 7% in April to nearly 20% in September in the northwest; and from approximately 7% in April to nearly 18% in September in south central.

In Allegheny County, the report pushes the total number of virus cases to 11,126. The new cases are from positive tests taken from Sept. 2 through Friday and include 33 women and 24 men.

The patients have an age range from 2 to 90 years old with a median age of 29.

The new death was a person in their 80s who was associated with a long-term care facility. Overall, 361 residents have died in Allegheny County as a result of the virus. 

The new cases involved 27 patients between 25 and 49 years old.
