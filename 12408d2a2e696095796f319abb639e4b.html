Execution warrant signed for Washington County man who killed ex-girlfriend in 2012

The Pennsylvania Department of Corrections Secretary John Wetzel has signed an execution warrant for a Washington County man who was sentenced to death in 2015 for killing his estranged girlfriend.

A notice for 31-year-old Jordan Clemons’ execution was signed Monday and is set for March 13, prison records show. The execution likely won’t take place due to a moratorium issued by Governor Tom Wolf in 2015, however. A total of 470 warrants have been signed to date in the state — basement torture killer Gary Heidnik was the last person put to death by lethal injection in 1999.

Majority of death row inmates are held at the state prison in Greene County.

A jury in 2015 unanimously sentenced Clemons to death for slashing the throat of Karissa Kunco and abandoning his ex-girlfriend’s body in January 2012. Ms. Kunco’s naked body was found that day along Sabo Road in Mount Pleasant, covered in debris and leaves.

She died the day before when her neck was slashed.
