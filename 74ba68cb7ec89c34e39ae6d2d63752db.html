Funeral arrangements set for Robert Full, former Allegheny County chief of emergency services

Funeral services have been scheduled this week for Robert A. Full, of Swisshelm Park, Allegheny County’s former longtime chief of emergency services and fire marshal who died Friday.

Visitation will take place Tuesday and Wednesday at Patrick T. Lanigan Funeral Home in Turtle Creek followed by the funeral. Calling hours are from 5 p.m. to 8 p.m. Tuesday and noon to 8 p.m. on Wednesday.

Due to COVID-19 restrictions, the funeral home is limited to 25 visitors in the building at one time. Funeral home staff will direct visitors once they arrive. The funeral home also is requiring masks.

A “public safety service” will be held at 7 p.m. on Wednesday that will involve a procession. Details are still being worked out, according to Allegheny County and Pittsburgh officials.

A Mass of Christian Burial will be held at 10 a.m. Thursday at St. Maurice Church of St. Joseph the Worker Parish, 2001 Ardmore Blvd. in Forest Hills. St. Maurice Church is limited to 130 people.

Mr. Full, 65, was a lifelong volunteer firefighter in Forest Hills where he grew up. He had also served as chief deputy director of the Pennsylvania Emergency Management Agency.

He is survived by his wife, Geraldine, a son, a daughter and three grandchildren. 

The family is requesting that memorial donations be made to the fire department or emergency medical service organization of one’s choice.
