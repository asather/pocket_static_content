'Get the hell out of the country': Mike Ditka has strong words for those who protest during anthem

Mike Ditka doesn’t want to see national anthem protests in his new football league —- or anywhere else.

The former Pitt and Aliquippa standout told TMZ Sports as much Sunday in discussing the Extreme Football League, the women’s football division of which he serves as chairman.

“If you can’t respect our national anthem, get the hell out of the country,” Ditka said. “That’s the way I feel. Of course, I’m old fashioned, so I’m only going to say what I feel.

“You don’t protest against the flag and you don’t protest against this country who’s given you the opportunities to make a living playing a sport that you never thought would happen,” Ditka added. “So, I don’t want to hear all the crap.”

Ditka said he does not have the power to determine his league’s national anthem policy unilaterally ahead of its scheduled April 2021 debut. He was simply voicing his personal opinion.

The league will begin play in eight cities as large as Los Angeles and as small as Omaha, Neb.
