Seneca Valley math teacher multiplies food, generosity

Tracy Richards was in line for lunch at a Chick Fil-A when a Seneca Valley High School graduate she knew paid for her meal.

That generous gesture inspired the seventh-grade math teacher to organize a grassroots effort to feed hungry families in the Seneca Valley School District. The Butler County district has about 7,300 students.

On the same day in late March, Mrs. Richards heard from a school guidance counselor about yet another family that had no money or food. 

“I drove to Aldi and bought groceries,” said Mrs. Richards, who teaches at Ryan Gloyer Middle School in Harmony.

Soon, the one-car garage at her Zelienople home became the drop-off point for donations. Parents, students and school district employees stopped by with peanut butter and jelly, pancake mix, spaghetti, tomato sauce and toiletries. Last week, Mrs. Richards and other volunteers delivered food to 20 families. 

“Some people get a large bag that is jam-packed. Some people get many boxes. It all depends on the need,” she said in a telephone interview.

As word of the effort spread, a Cranberry  woman volunteered to be a drop-off point for food donations. Students on the Seneca Valley volleyball team, which Mrs. Richards coaches, stepped up and so did students who play on a district soccer team.

Mrs. Richards coordinated the food packaging at her house. Fourth-grade teachers from one school donated snacks. Treats also arrived from the track and field concession stand. Hundreds of dollars in donations came in, too.

“Parents of my students are coming to my house to drop off food,” said Mrs. Richards, who teaches math to 115 students. Lisa Kasper, a special education teacher, helped with deliveries and shopping. 

“We’re going to go on as long as the donations keep coming,” Mrs. Richards said. Her email address is richardstg@svsd.net
