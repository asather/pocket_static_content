Buying Here: Old storefront, cool loft in Ligonier priced at $375,000

Thirteen years ago, Jackie and Andy Horvath finished a beautiful conversion of a century-old hardware store at 114 N. Market St. in downtown Ligonier.

The couple took her dream of starting a business and married it with his can-do spirit to transform the brick building into retail space with history and a 2,000-square-foot loft apartment with a rooftop party deck.

Now, the Horvaths’ dreams have changed. They have set their sights on the beach and placed the building on the market for $375,000 (MLS No. 1399099) with Tracey M. Pelesky of Loyalhanna Realty (724-510-4010 or www.loyalhanna.com). It is open by appointment in accordance with COVID-19 guidelines.

The building was erected in 1906 and remained Ligonier Hardware until the couple purchased it in 2006. At that time, the business had been closed for a few years. The first floor was converted into an antiques shop called “Jackie’s House.”

“We had an antique shop in there for about eight years,” Ms. Horvath said. “Then we transitioned to a sports gear and clothing store for about four years.”

Her husband said the space fit their lifestyle.

“My wife lives on her bike,” he said. “There are a lot of wonderful biking trails and roads around here. I walk everywhere we need to go.”

The couple worked with architect P. Scott Moore of Latrobe and carpenters Pete Hamer and Troy Pritts on the 10-month renovation. Reminders of the building’s history include marks on the floor used to measure cable, wiring and fencing. The first floor also holds many of the original hardware display cases.

The second floor measures 100 feet long and 20 feet wide. The large, open living space has two bedrooms and two bathrooms. One wall was opened up to let in more light for a sun room. Five skylights were also installed during the renovation.

“It flows really nicely,” Mr. Horvath said. “My wife does really well with plants. It looks like a jungle out there.”

The kitchen’s backsplash is hand-made majolica tiles above Corian counter tops. The six aluminum stools around the island are circa 1930. They came from a drugstore in West Virginia and were recovered with red leather for a fun soda fountain vibe.

The rooftop deck is a spectacular space with parquet-style decking, a hot tub and lots of places to sit and gather.

The home is being sold with most of the furnishings, including several antiques.

“We have a Chinese apothecary cabinet,” Mr. Horvath said. “Those items will not fit in down there.”

The building is in the Ligonier Valley School District. Taxes are $3,064 per year.

The building is less than a block from shops and restaurants in the Diamond and the newly renovated YMCA, where Excela Health added a small medical center and expanded fitness facilities.

“We are next door to Carol and Dave’s Roadhouse and a half block from Kitchen on Main,” Mr. Horvath said. “There is a coffee shop, Abigail’s. We shop at the Giant Eagle six blocks away. There is a farmer’s market on the edge of West Main on Saturday mornings. It goes until the late fall.”

The couple have loved this place but are ready for the next phase of their lives.

“It’s just an extremely unique place to live,” she said.
