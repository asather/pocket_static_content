Wesco secures $4.5 billion deal to acquire Anixter after bidding war

Wesco International Inc. triumphed over a private equity firm in its battle to acquire Anixter International, a distributor of electrical and data communication products based in Illinois.

Station Square-based Wesco, which is among the largest distributors of construction, industrial, electrical and other supplies in the U.S., signed a merger agreement with Anixter on Jan. 10.

Anixter was previously due to be acquired by the New York-based private equity firm Clayton, Dubilier & Rice, LLC. Wesco has agreed to pay the $100 million penalty that Anixter now owes to its former suitor for breaking the deal.

The Wesco deal is valued at $4.5 billion and includes a combination of $70 in cash, Wesco common stock, and a newly-created category of preferred Wesco stock for each share of Anixter stock that shareholders own.

It is expected to close in the second or third quarter of this year, but still needs regulatory approvals in the U.S., Canada, and some other countries.
