AMC will showcase Greg Nicotero's 'Creepshow,' an homage to George Romero's cult classic film

AMC has announced it will air the first season of Greg Nicotero’s “Creepshow” reboot — a horror anthology series that’s an homage to the George A. Romero cult classic film that was filmed in Pittsburgh.

Beginning May 4, two episodes will be shown back-to-back at 9 p.m., with the series continuing on successive Mondays.

Creator Nicotero (executive producer and director, “The Walking Dead”), who grew up in the North Hills, called it a labor of love in honor of Romero’s 1982 film.

The six-episode, one-hour series ran on horror streaming service Shudder in late September. Each hour tells two lurid tales that are not for the squeamish.

Nicotero directs two episodes and worked on writing others. Oakland director and special make-up effects artist Tom Savini also directs an episode. Pittsburgh’s John Harrison, who collaborated with Romero on numerous projects, directs four episodes, including a particularly gruesome take on the old monkey’s paw fable.

Rick Catizone, also of Pittsburgh, creates 2D animations that reflect those he did almost 40 years ago for the film. Key special effects wizards include Gino Crognale of Mars.

Stephen King wrote the original “Creepshow” movie. His son, Joe Hill, was just a kid when he did a cameo in 1982. Now a successful author in his own right, Hill’s short story “By the Silver Water of Lake of Lake Champlain” has been adapted for a May 18 episode directed by Savini.

The second season of the new “Creepshow” was in pre-production when everything was shut down last month. But Nicotero recently shared an Instagram video of a big scary spider animatron.
