Firefighters respond to blaze at roofing company in Esplen

Emergency crews Monday responded to a fire that erupted at a roofing and sheet metal contractor in Pittsburgh’s Esplen neighborhood.

Firefighters and police received the fire call shortly before 8:30 a.m. at MTG Roofing (Miller-Thomas-Gyekis Inc.) in the 3300 block of Stafford Street, according to city fire officials. No injuries were reported.

Pittsburgh’s Hazmat Battalion Chief Bernard Mohan said insulation, roofing material and tar caught fire. Large plumes of smoke could be seen several miles away.

He said firefighters had a hard time fighting the blaze due to “access issues.”

“Based on the conditions that we had, we had to rotate crews out because of the temperature,” he said. “We had to have extra water delivered. Crews did a good job of getting in there and getting the fire under control. We had to wait for the power company to secure the power lines.

“It was a deep-seated fire” confined to a storage area, Chief Mohan said at the scene.

“It’s challenging, [but] we do have precautions in place [and] we have safety officers ... we have rehabilitation units so that we can monitor hydration levels ... we put a schedule together to rotate crews in and out so that we’re not overtaxing in this heat.”

He said the cause of the fire remains under investigation.
