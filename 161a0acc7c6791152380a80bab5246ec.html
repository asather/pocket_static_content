First Pittsburgh worker dies of COVID-19

An environmental services employee with Pittsburgh’s Department of Public Works has died from the coronavirus, Mayor Bill Peduto’s office announced Wednesday morning.

The man was a truck driver who started working for the bureau in 1995. He is the first Pittsburgh employee to die from the COVID-19 disease, city officials said.

Mr. Peduto offered sympathy and prayers for the unidentified man’s loved ones and coworkers.

“... The pandemic may be global but it’s still hitting us very close to home,” the mayor said in a statement.

City officials said the worker contracted the virus while he was away from work.

Black ribbon decals are being placed on refuse and recycling trucks to honor the worker.
