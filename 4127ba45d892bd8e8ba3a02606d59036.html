Pirates' projected playoff odds improve with shortened season, but not by much

When we last checked with the computers about the Pirates’ 2020 chances back in April, they were poor. At the time, MLB was reportedly considering a 100-ish game season played at spring training sites with realigned divisions.

The last two months have changed everything and nothing. MLB is now poised to instead implement a 60-game campaign in home ballparks and with traditional divisional alignments. The Pirates’ chances, though? Still poor.

Dave Szymborski’s industry-leading ZiPs projections for a 60-game season were released Wednesday at Fangraphs, and they expect the Pirates to come in a distant fifth in the NL Central division, six games off the pace at 26-34. Their odds to claim the crown come in at just 6.7% and their overall playoff odds are only slightly better at 13.8%. That’s the fourth-worst mark in the National League.

The good news? Those marks are up significantly, at least, from projections for a longer season. The Pirates’ original odds of making the playoffs over a full season in the NL Central were just 3.3%, and their odds in a shortened season at spring training sites were 1.7%. So possible variance is their friend here. 

The Chicago Cubs are your projected winners in the division with a 32-28 record. Their 27.6% odds, however, are closely in line with the rest of the division. Milwaukee (23.4%), St. Louis (21.9%) and Cincinnati (20.4%) are all projected to finish just one game back and all have odds of at least 33.7% to make the postseason bracket.

Around the league, the Los Angeles Dodgers have the best projected record at 38-22. They’re expected to breeze through the NL West, while in the East, Atlanta and defending World Series champion Washington are projected to tie atop their table at 34-26.

In the American League, the New York Yankees are expected to be the class of the field with a 37-23 mark. They’re slated to win the East by two games. Houston in the West (36-24) and Minnesota in the Central (35-25) are considered the closest competition.

The gambling industry takes a similarly dim view of the local team. BetOnline released its win totals for a 60-game season Wednesday, and its findings are right in line with those of Fangraphs. The current over/under is set at 25.5.
