Donations have been pouring in for a Beaver County family of four that was displaced after a fire destroyed their home Thursday night.

The fire was reported around 7 p.m. in the 300 block of Celestial Drive in Economy.

The Scott family — a single mother and her three children — were not home at the time of the blaze, county emergency officials said.

The family’s two dogs were inside the house. One was rescued, but the other dog could not be found. 

The entire block came out to support the family, Pittsburgh Post-Gazette news partner KDKA-TV reported.

“It just breaks my heart to see her cry and how hard she works,” neighbor Michelle Millor told KDKA. “I can’t tell you how much she works for these kids.”

Neighbors said the mother works two jobs to support her children. Two of the children are in college, and another child is high-school age.

The cause of the fire remains under investigation.

A GoFundMe page has been started for the family. About $14,000 had been donated as of 10:30 a.m.

“We all know that they will never get back what was lost in their home, but we can try to help them out as much as possible,” the GoFundMe post says. “They will need these funds to start rebuilding their lives.. clothes, food, furniture, everything! So let’s as a community come together to support a family that would undoubtedly do the same for us!”
