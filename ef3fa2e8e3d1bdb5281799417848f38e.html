Rivers Casino furloughs most employees amid COVID-19 closure

Rivers Casino on Thursday announced it would furlough “most” of its team members starting next week amid the state shutdown caused by the COVID-19 pandemic.

The furloughs are expected to last through June 30, a statement from the casino reads. Employees that remain will receive “substantial salary cuts.”

All employees will continue to receive health benefits from the company. It was not made clear exactly how many employees will be affected by furloughs or pay cuts.

“This was an extremely difficult decision and one we desperately hoped to avoid,” Rivers Casino CEO Greg Carlin wrote in a statement.

The North Shore casino closed March 15. Pennsylvania Gov. Tom Wolf ordered the closure of all “non-life-sustaining” businesses March 19.

Casino officials said the location will reopen “when the state determines that it’s safe to do so.”
