Allegheny County's COVID cases soar past 4,000; Butler County sets new single-day mark

The number of COVID-19 cases in the region continues to surge, as Allegheny County, for the fourth time in a week, had a single-day increase of more than 200, and Butler County set its highest-ever, single-day mark.

With 230 new cases on Wednesday, Allegheny County eclipsed the 4,000-mark with 4,209 total cases. It was the second-highest, single-day increase the county has had since its first case was confirmed on March 14.

The new positive cases come from 1,997 tests that were administered between June 23 and July 5. They range in age from 7 months to 96 years old, and the median age is 29.

Excluding Allegheny County, Western Pennsylvania saw an additional 149 new cases. Westmoreland County had 33 new cases, bringing its total to 896. Butler County reported 20 new cases, which was its highest single-day increase since its first case was reported on March 21. It now has 390 cases.

Allegheny County reported two additional deaths, bringing its total to 196. The deaths occurred between June 26 and July 5.

Pennsylvania saw an additional 25 deaths, making the state total 6,812. The only other county in Western Pennsylvania to report a death was Crawford County — the first death reported there. 

There also were eight additional hospitalizations in Allegheny County as a result of the virus, bringing the county total to 431 past or present hospitalizations. Of those, 164 have been admitted to intensive care units and 78 required mechanical ventilation.

According to data from the state health department, currently 1,063 ventilators out of 5,357 are currently in use. 

In the weekly COVID-19 briefing held by county officials on Tuesday, Dr. Debra Bogen, the county’s health director, said she hopes to release a new order sometime Wednesday about operating rules for restaurants and bars.
