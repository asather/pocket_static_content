Port Authority receives over $141 million in pandemic stimulus funds

Port Authority has received a $141.7 million grant Tuesday as part of stimulus funding during the coronavirus pandemic, the Federal Transit Administration announced in a release.

The funds are part of the Coronavirus Aid, Relief and Economic Security Act — better known as the CARES Act — which went into effect March 27, providing $25 billion for public transit operations to respond to COVID-19.

The Port Authority will use the funds to continue transit service as the pandemic persists and were expecting such a figure when CARES Act funding eligibility was released April 3.

The agency was projecting revenue losses and added expenses from the COVID-19 emergency in April to reach $30 million by June 30.

FTA acting Administrator K. Jane Williams emphasized the funding will aid in essential travel services and supporting transit workers unable to work due to public health concerns.

“We know many of our nation’s public transportation systems are facing extraordinary challenges and these funds will go a long way to assisting our transit industry partners in battling COVID-19,” Ms. Williams said in a statement.

In addition, the FTA issued a safety advisory for public transit agencies to develop and implement policies to carry out preventative measures consistent with the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention guidance such as sanitizing high-touch areas and providing personal protective equipment. 

CARES Act funding can be used to cover 100% of such costs.
