At least 1 killed in blaze at Somerset County restaurant

A man was found dead after a fire destroyed a bar and restaurant Wednesday morning in Somerset County.

Authorities responded just before 6 a.m. to Walat’s Bar and Grill on Lincoln Highway/Route 30 in Jenner, fire officials said. The facility was closed at the time of the blaze.

The county coroner was called to the scene.

One man, whose identity has not been revealed, was found dead inside the building, according to Jennerstown Fire Chief Dave Robertson.

“When you walk inside, the bar is to your right, and to the left is a seating area,” the fire chief said. “He was found near the seating area.” Whether or not the man was dead prior to the fire was not immediately known.

Chief Robertson said firefighters had the blaze under control within 45 minutes.

The building sustained extensive damage and the cause of the fire is under investigation by the state police fire marshal’s office.

Chief Robertson said the restaurant was a popular stop along Route 30 that catered to many tourists over the years.

“The bar’s been here for 50, 60 years ... it’s had a lot of skiers, snowmobilers ... it had a nice little clientele over the years,” he said.

A portion of Lincoln Highway that was closed due to the fire had reopened by 8:30 a.m.
