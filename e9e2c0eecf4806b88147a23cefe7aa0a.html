Authorities say Pittsburgh man died of gunshot wounds in 2015 Homewood shooting

The Allegheny County Medical Examiner’s Office early Friday ruled that a Pittsburgh man died in February from gunshot wounds in a Homewood shooting that took place five years ago.

Dominique Fuller, 25, died Feb. 19 at John J. Kane Regional Center in Glen Hazel but at the time a ruling on the cause and manner of death was not made.

Fuller died from gunshot wounds to the head in the shooting, the medical examiner’s office said Friday. The number of times he was shot was not made clear.

Police said Mr. Fuller was shot multiple times in the afternoon of June 23, 2015, at Fuchsia Way and Collier Street. He was shot at least once in the head, police said.

A man was initially charged in the shooting, but the charges were eventually dismissed.
