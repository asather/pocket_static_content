Westmoreland County man captured after escaping from police custody

A fugitive from Westmoreland County who was wanted for allegedly assaulting a police officer has been captured.

Pennsylvania State Police said Michael P. Boring, 44, of Seward was arrested Sunday night without incident at a convenience store in Cambria County.

Law enforcement had been searching for Boring since Aug. 29, when he escaped from police custody in Fairfield, Westmoreland County while being transported to a local jail.

State police said Boring is charged with aggravated assault, escape, resisting arrest and theft after he assaulted a Seward police officer and stole a car. The officer was not seriously injured. Earlier on Aug. 29, Boring had been apprehended on a separate outstanding warrant.

The U.S. Marshal’s Fugitive Task Force and Cambria County Sheriff’s Office Sunday night notified state police in Ebensburg that Boring was at a convenience store in Cambria Township, where he was taken into custody. A 36-year-old woman from Seward who was with Boring at the time was charged with hindering apprehension. 

Boring was being held at the Cambria County Prison awaiting arraignment on the charges.
