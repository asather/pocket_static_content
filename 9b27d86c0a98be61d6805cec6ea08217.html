Homestead man arrested for abuse of stepchildren

A Homestead man has been arrested after a five-month investigation on charges of beating two stepdaughters and a 2-year-old stepson with a belt, among other abuses.

Randall Blair, 30, faces one count each of aggravated assault, strangulation and terroristic threats; two counts of simple assault; and three counts each of endangering a child and reckless endangerment.

One girl, 10, said Mr. Blair gouged her eye, causing a hemorrhage, while also drawing blood with his fingernail. She also was beaten with a black belt while lying in a fetal position, causing bruises on her thigh, shin and upper arm, along with a broken thumbnail. The 7-year-old stepdaughter also sustained bruising from beatings with the belt while also describing how Mr. Blair strangled her, according to the criminal complaint.

The 2-year-old boy was found to have a scabbed abrasion on a cheekbone, and abrasions and bruising on his right upper ear, with possible bruising of his groin. He also tested positive for cocaine and a cocaine metabolite.
