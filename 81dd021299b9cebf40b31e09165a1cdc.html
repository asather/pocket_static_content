Fishing Report: Big muskies and catfish caught throughout the region

Lake Erie  (Erie County) Resident walleye went for live baits and plugs in nearshore areas of 10-25 feet. A few migrating walleye crossed the Ohio state line at 40-45 feet and went for blue or green lures. The Perch Pirate head boat reported good catches while drifting Erie Dearies with gold or bronze blades. About 80 boats were entered in last week’s Erie Spring Walleye Bash in Presque Isle Bay. Friday’s top catch was a 9.84-pound ’eye and a 9.88-pounder won on Saturday. Anglers trolling for walleye off Elk Creek caught some big catfish. Yellow perch, which have been hit-or-miss off the peninsula for several years, were caught in abundance in 35-40 feet off Shades Beach. The Father’s Day Buddy Tournament scheduled for June 21 at Presque Isle Bay was canceled.

Pymatuning Reservoir (Crawford County) A 42¼-inch muskellunge was caught. Walleye were taken by anglers drifting or jigging live bait among stumps and in shallow weedbeds, and by trolling diving lures in about 20 feet. The bigger ’eyes were in deeper waters. Crappies and white bass were caught on the Ohio side near the Route 85 causeway. Duck-n-Drake in Andover, Ohio, will sponsor a walleye tournament June 27. Visit the shop’s Facebook page for details.

Canadohta Lake (Crawford County) A 47-inch muskie was caught and released.

Justus Lake (Venango County) On June 13, the opening day of bass harvest season, two anglers boated 11 largemouth bass. Six were 15 inches or longer; one weighed 5 pounds.

East Hickory Creek (Forest County) In the Delayed Harvest Artificial Lures Only stretch, trout went for Blue Wing Olives in the morning and caddis patterns in afternoon and evening.

Allegheny River (Clarion County) Anglers reported good catches of smallmouths near Emlenton.

Clarion River (Clarion County) Smallmouth bass, walleye, crappies and yellow perch were caught.

Redbank Creek (Clarion County) Trout and smallmouths were taken above the dam, and anglers reported walleye and Northern pike off New Bethlehem and at the Allegheny River confluence.

Allegheny River (Armstrong County) Several walleye and Northerns were taken near the Parker Islands.

Lake Arthur (Butler County) Cheryl Riley of Plum boated a plump, 24-inch largemouth estimated at 10 pounds on Wednesday. The state record is 11 pounds, 3 ounces. Nate Heidish caught a 28-inch walleye using a Rapala crankbait on June 13.

Ohio River (Beaver County) Flathead catfish in the 25-inch range were taken at night using cut bait.

North Park Lake (Allegheny County) Several largemouth catches were reported Saturday, as well as panfish taken on worms under bobbers.

Ohio River (Allegheny County) A 28-inch flathead went for a bluegill fished near industrial structure and was released.

Monongahela River (Allegheny County) A shore angler landed six flatheads in the 25-pound range near the Braddock Lock and Dam this week.

Cross Creek Lake (Washington County) Good catches of panfish were reported.

Laurel Hill Lake (Somerset County) Trout remained active, particularly on cool evenings.

Penn’s Creek (Centre County) On her second fly fishing trip, Amy Haver, of the Strip District, caught a 17-inch native brown trout on a stonefly pattern in Pennsylvania’s longest limestone stream.

Stonewall Jackson Lake (West Virginia) Joe Baker, of Canonsburg, nearly scored a record when he boated a 53-inch, 32-pound muskellunge at the impoundment near Weston, W.Va., on June 6. The state record for muskies, set in 2017, is 53½ inches, 34.58 pounds.
