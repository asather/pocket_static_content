COVID-19 Update: Pa. reports 1,161 new cases, 19 more deaths

Pennsylvania has marked another day with 1,000-plus daily cases of COVID-19 as 19 more people died of the virus, the health department reported Friday.

With the newly reported 1,161 cases of the virus, the state now has a total of 161,284 cases since the pandemic began. In Western Pennsylvania, 283 of those cases were reported, with Allegheny County having the largest increase for Friday with 73 new cases, followed by Westmoreland County with 54 cases.

Sept. 30 through Oct. 2 is the first time since May 7 through May 10 that the state recorded at least three days in a row of more than 1,000 reported cases. Non-coincidentally, Friday’s seven-day rolling average is 1,012, the highest the average has been since it was 1,049 on May 10. The state’s positivity rate is currently at 7.4%.

The latest cases in Allegheny County resulted from 1,298 tests that were taken between Sept. 25 and Oct. 1. Three of Friday’s cases are considered to be probable, as they come from positive antigen tests. Those who have recently tested positive range in age from 11 days to 93 years old, with a median age of 35. Since the beginning of the pandemic in mid-March, 12,527 people in the county have tested positive for the virus.

In total, 8,179 people across the state have died as a result of the virus. Of Friday’s 19 new deaths, seven were in the Pittsburgh region, four of which were in Allegheny County. Beaver County also reported two deaths and Erie County reported one.

The four deaths in Allegheny occurred between Sept. 21 and 23 among one individual in their 60s, two in their 80s and one in their 100s. All of the deaths were associated with long-term care facilities, according to the county health department. With the addition of these newly reported deaths, the county has had a total of 386 people die as a result of the virus.

Currently there are 561 people hospitalized for COVID-19 across Pennsylvania, 54 of whom are in Allegheny County.
