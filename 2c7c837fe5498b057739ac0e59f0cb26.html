13 new COVID-19 deaths reported in W. Pa.; Allegheny County reports 9 dead

There were 21 new deaths reported Wednesday in Pennsylvania due to COVID-19, with 13 of them occurring in Western Pennsylvania, according to the state health department.

Allegheny County reported nine deaths, while Beaver County reported two and Armstrong and Butler counties reported one each.

The Allegheny County Health Department on Wednesday also reported 68 new COVID-19 cases.

The county’s deaths were reported between Aug. 20 and Aug. 28 and occurred in a person in their 50s, two in their 70s, four in their 80s and two in their 90s. Of the nine deaths, six are associated with long-term care facilities.

The county death toll has now reached 343.

In Pennsylvania, 7,712 people have died as a result of the virus. 

The new cases in Allegheny come from 1,088 tests that were taken between Aug. 24 and Sept. 1. Those who have recently tested positive range in age from 2 to 97 years old, with a median age of 40.

Fifty-eight of the cases are confirmed and 10 are probable, the county said. Since the beginning of the pandemic, Allegheny County has had 10,444 people test positive.

The state health department reported an increase of 816 new cases, bringing the state total number of cases to 135,611.

Western Pennsylvania had 175 of the new cases, with Allegheny having the highest daily increase, followed by Washington County with 20. 

In Allegheny County, 76 people are currently hospitalized with the virus. Statewide, 550 people are hospitalized, according to the most recent state data.

In total, 985 people in Allegheny County have been hospitalized because of complications involving the virus.
