WPIAL Class 6A roundup: North Allegheny roars back against Penn Hills

After it looked as if neither team would have a game the first week of the season, WPIAL Class 6A member North Allegheny defeated Class 5Aâ€™s Penn Hills, 27-6, Friday night.

Both teams had their initial opponents pull out of their matchups due to COVID-19 concerns. North Allegheny was scheduled to play Seneca Valley, while Penn Hills was going up against Kiski Area.

Penn Hills scored first Friday, on a 22-yard strike from Julian Dugger to his brother Jaden Dugger. It was all North Allegheny from there, as the Tigers scored four unanswered touchdowns. Mason Kress, J.R. Burton and Tanner Potts each scored a rushing touchdown. Sean Metcalf returned an interception 25 yards for another score.

More Class 6A

Central Catholic 45, Canon-McMillan 17: Eddy Tillman ran for 197 yards and a score as the Vikings opened the season with a big win against the Big Macs in a Quad County matchup.

Hempfield 28, Norwin 14: Christian Zilli threw for 144 yards and a score and also ran for 96 yards and a touchdown to pace the Spartans in a Quad County conference victory.

Erie Cathedral Prep 52, Butler 21: Playing outside the WPIAL, Butler did not have better luck. Cooper Baxter paced Butler with 123 yards passing and another 104 yards rushing.
