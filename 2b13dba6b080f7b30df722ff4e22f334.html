Penn State lands commitment from QB Beau Pribula

Even in most uncertain times, there are absolute universal truths in college football. Recruiting can't stop and won't stop, especially when you're a program like Penn State.

Head coach James Franklin tweeted the signal that another addition to the incoming class of 2022 was on the way. A few minutes later, York native and three-star quarterback Beau Pribula announced that he was joining four-star tight end Jerry Cross and four-star wide receiver Kaden Sanders as recent commits to Penn State.

Pribula is 6-foot-2, 195 pounds, and threw for 1,006 yards on 66 completions to go with six touchdowns. He also scored 10 touchdowns on the ground with 298 yards.

Offensive coordinator Kirk Ciarrocca was the primary recruiter for Pribula. He chose the Nittany Lions over schools such as Rutgers, Northwestern, and Nebraska.
