UPMC closing ambulatory surgery centers

UPMC is closing several ambulatory surgery centers and “moving medically necessary surgeries and procedures into hospital locations,” spokesman Paul Wood said Sunday afternoon.

Mr. Wood said the move “will help us to protect staff and preserve protective equipment.”

Affected include five facilities in Pittsburgh: UPMC Children’s Hospital of Pittsburgh North; UPMC Digestive Health and Endoscopy Center; UPMC St. Margaret Harmar Surgery Center; UPMC Center for Reproductive Endocrinology and Infertility; and UPMC South Surgery Center.

Closures outside the Pittsburgh region include the UPMC Altoona Surgery Center and UPMC Hamot Surgery Center in Erie as well as seven facilities in the central part of the state: West Shore Surgery Center and UPMC Pinnacle Procedure Center in Mechanicsburg; UPMC Specialty Care York Endoscopy; Hanover Surgicenter; UPMC Leader Surgery Center in York; Susquehanna Valley Surgery Center in Harrisburg and Carlisle Outpatient Surgery Center.

UPMC has resisted recommendations from county and state health officials who have urged hospitals to postpone non-emergent procedures to conserve resources for an expected surge in seriously ill patients from the COVID-19 outbreak.

But Mr. Wood insisted Sunday that UPMC is in compliance with state and federal guidelines regarding elective procedures.

“As the dominant provider in our region,” he said, “UPMC is fully prepared to meet the needs of our community now and in the future, even if COVID-19 cases grow exponentially as many anticipate.”
