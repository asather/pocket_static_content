General Electric shuttering research center in Findlay

General Electric Co. is closing its 3D research center in Findlay and transferring the work to Cincinnati due to the pandemic.

The closure of the GE Additive Pittsburgh facility will be conducted in phases through March 2021, the Boston, Mass.-based industrial giant said.

“Due to the unprecedented impact COVID-19 has had on our business and our ongoing efforts to streamline our operations, we are relocating our strategic print services business from Pittsburgh” to GE Additive Cincinnati, a spokesman said in an email Thursday. “The decision to relocate was difficult to make, particularly in the current environment.

“GE will provide impacted employees with comprehensive benefits packages, including transition support to new employment or other opportunities within locally based GE businesses, where appropriate,” he said.

He declined to disclose how many people work at the 125,000-square-foot facility, previously known as the GE Center for Additive Technology Advancement.

When the facility opened in 2016 promising at least 50 jobs, it was touted as a hub for developing new ways to use 3D printing and other so-called additive manufacturing technologies to design and make a wide variety of products, and for helping to make Pittsburgh a leader in advanced manufacturing.
