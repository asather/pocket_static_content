Mac Miller's breakout 'K.I.D.S.' is streaming for the first time

Some good news from Rostrum Records: Mac Miller's “K.I.D.S.,” his breakout mixtape, is available on streaming services for the first time.

The collection, released in August 2010, when the Point Breeze rapper was 18, includes early standouts like “Kool Aid & Frozen Pizza,” “Nikes On My Feet" and "Knock Knock” and is accompanied by a collection of never-before-seen images of Miller by Rex Arrow, with whom Miller collaborated on his most iconic videos.

“K.I.D.S.” was Miller's first release with the Pittsburgh-based Rostrum Records, whose founder, Benjy Grinberg, met Miller while working with fellow Allderdice grad Wiz Khalifa. Miller went on to top the charts the following year with debut album “Blue Slide Park.”

“Knock Knock” video: contains profanity.
