Fishing Report: Regional stocked trout waters loaded with fish

Lake Erie and tributaries (Erie County) Smallmouth bass and yellow perch were taken off the mouth of Walnut Creek. Perch were caught from shoreline locations on Presque Isle Bay.

Connoquenessing Creek (Butler County) A 25-inch rainbow trout took a wax worm and was landed by a Butler County man April 15.

Pine Creek (Allegheny County) Will Drexler, 17, of McKees Rocks landed a brightly colored 17-inch golden rainbow. This year, the state Fish and Boat Commission stocked some 13,000, 40% more than in 2019. The goldens are rainbow trout, hatchery-bred for their angler appeal. They are often incorrectly called “palominos,” which were a dull-colored hatchery creation discontinued years ago.

Big Sewickley Creek (Allegheny County) Emily Reiner, 12, of Glenfield landed a rainbow trout in the 20-inch range on an ultralight spinning rod. The big fish was released.

Loyalhanna Creek (Westmoreland County) A 22-inch brown and 23-inch rainbow went for a spinner fished in Delayed Harvest Artificial Lures Only waters stocked for the Keystone Select Stocked Trout Waters program. Those waters were stocked with trophy-size trout at a rate of 175 to 225 fish per mile, in addition to the creek’s regular stocking.

Standing Stone Creek (Huntington County) An Allegheny County angler caught and released two hefty trout taken on a spinning gear using small trout eggs.

Laurel Hill Lake (Somerset County) Trout took minnows and other live baits under bobbers.

Laurel Hill Creek (Somerset County) Todd Davis of Greensburg caught and released a 25-inch brown trout taken on a nymph in the upper Delayed Harvest Artificial Lures Only stretch, part of the Keystone Select Stocked Trout Waters program.
