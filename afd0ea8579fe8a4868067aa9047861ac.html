Trump supporters announce two events in the Pittsburgh area next week

A pair of events in the Pittsburgh area this week — one affiliated with Donald Trump’s re-election campaign and one in support of it — will feature a wide range of the president’s most outspoken supporters, some of whom have built prominent followings themselves around unabashedly provocative commentary.

On Wednesday, members of the Trump campaign’s official ‘Women for Trump’ coalition, one of its many coalitions aimed at organizing different demographics around the president’s 2020 bid, will visit Cranberry to hold a panel discussion.

The group is stopping there on a three-day bus tour through Michigan, Ohio and Pennsylvania, and feature appearances from senior campaign adviser Lara Trump, the president’s daughter-in-law, as well as adviser Mercedes Schlapp, press secretary and former GOP spokeswoman Kayleigh McEnany and the duet of Lynnette Hardaway and Rochelle Richardson — otherwise known as the Fox Nation commentators Diamond and Silk.

After their appearance in Cranberry, the women will head to York, Pa., to conclude the bus tour with one final event.

On Saturday, a group unaffiliated with the president’s campaign, Pennsylvania Women for Trump, will hold a gala in the North Side with conservative commentators and activists.

It will be headlined by radio host and former White House official Sebastian Gorka — who promised, in a Facebook video, the event would be the “party of a lifetime.” Other guests include commentators Alfonzo Rachel, Lucretia Hughes and Katie Hopkins, activist Scott Presler and Virginia organizer Alice Butler-Short.

Ticket packages for the gala range from a solo $100 general admission ticket to $2,500 VIP tables. It is unclear what the proceeds will benefit.

The event’s organizer could not be reached for comment. The organization of Pennsylvania women describes itself as a “Judeo-Christian Faith-based group of Patriots formed to support our President, our God, our country and our conservative values.”
