Food events throughout the region impacted by COVID-19

Concerns about the spread of COVID-19 have impacted food events in and around Pittsburgh. The following events are being canceled or postponed:

● Slow Wines Tasting, which was scheduled to be held Monday at Barsotti Warehouse in the Strip District, is being postponed. The $35 registration fee will be refunded via PayPal.

● PPG Wintergarden is canceling its first St. Patrick’s Party in the Garden. The event was to have been held on Saturday.

● The Great Temple Sinai Bake Off, patterned after "The Great British Bake Off," which was slated for March 28, has been canceled. It will be back next year in May.

● Empty Bowls Dinner, which was to be held on Sunday at Rodef Shalom Congregation in Shadyside, is being postponed. The Greater Community Pittsburgh Food Bank and Just Harvest will determine the date at a later time, and purchased tickets will be honored at the rescheduled event.

● The International Association of Culinary Professionals has moved its annual conference from the end of this month to Oct. 13-15, but it will still be held at the Omni William Penn Hotel, Downtown.

● The Diocese of Pittsburgh is encouraging its parishes to offer fish fry takeouts only or cancel their fish fries until further notice. It will be up to the individual churches to make the final decision. The churches also are being advised to follow the directives issued by the Allegheny County health department if they go the takeout route.

● Fueled by Hops has postponed its first Fueled Fest 412 beer fest Saturday at Spirit in Lawrenceville.
