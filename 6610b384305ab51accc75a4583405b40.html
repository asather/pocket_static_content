Meadville glass plant to downsize, lay off 108 as COVID-19 impacts market conditions

A plant in Meadville, Crawford County, that makes flat glass for automobiles is shutting down a production line and cutting 108 jobs effective June 10.

Vitro Automotive, a Mexican-based glass company which owns the facility, said economic fallout from the COVID-19 pandemic pushed the company to downsize.

Automakers have scaled back production dramatically since the onset of the global virus outbreak.

Also, Vitro said the production line will soon require $40 million worth of upgrades.

“Float-glass production lines require significant reinvestment and rebuild about every 10 years” and the line “was last rebuilt in 2009,” the company said in a statement.

Another glass line will continue to operate at the plant.

Those targeted for layoffs include 90 hourly and 18 salaried workers.

The plant currently employs 272.

Those laid off will receive health care through Aug. 31, Vitro said.

Vitro said both lines had been running at about 60% capacity prior to the coronavirus spread because of “lingering challenges in the U.S. automotive markets.”

Current market conditions don’t support a rebuild of the line that will be shut down, Vitro said.

“We already faced a difficult combination of factors … including a growing oversupply of automotive glass in the marketplace … but the latest economic impacts from the coronavirus outbreak are just too much of a hurdle to invest $40 million in rebuilding it,” said Dave Shaffer, Meadville plant manager.

The company said it will consider rebuilding the line, “if there is a business case to justify the investment.”

Vitro acquired the Meadville plant in 2017. It was originally operated by PPG and later, Pittsburgh Glass Works.

Vitro shuttered PPG’s original glass plant in East Deer in 2018.
