Fashion find of the week: Feel-good summer fashions from Whimsical Wardrobe Boutique

COVID-19 has not only slowed sales for brick-and-mortar stores but also for fashion retailers on wheels. 

Marissa Zimmerman decided before the new coronavirus outbreak to give her seven-year-old mobile boutique a makeover, rebranding it from the Vintage Valet to Whimsical Wardrobe Boutique. 

“The name was kind of misleading. I sold vintage-inspired clothes,” she said, “but I would get calls like, ‘My great-grandmother or aunt passed away and has a whole attic full of vintage. Do you buy this?’ I just wanted to rebrand with something more general.” 

The word “whimsical” is a nod to the aesthetic of the fashion truck’s clothing for sale: very romantic and feminine, with lots of lace, ruffles, embroidery, crocheted touches and a hint of sparkle. 

For summer, she’s stocked pleated dresses and plenty of bold, neon pieces, she said. Plus, there are colorful shell accessories to dress them up.  

It’s been tough, though, for Ms. Zimmerman to show off the truck’s new name — and her new inventory — because of restrictions prompted by the pandemic. 

“We primarily rely on events to do our business, so we’ve kind of had to pivot and focus more online and doing Facebook Live,” she said.

Now that Allegheny County and surrounding areas have entered the green phase, she’s optimistic about appearing at a few events this summer, including the farmer’s market in Bethel Park. Teaming with other businesses for a pop-up shop or holding a private shopping party with the truck by request outside someone’s house are other in-person options. Some items also are for sale inside Jacqueline’s Salon & Spa on Grant Street, Downtown.

In the meantime, people can shop Whimsical Wardrobe Boutique online at whimsicalwardrobeboutique.com and on Facebook @WhimsicalWardrobeBoutique. Keep an eye on the website for updates to the truck’s event schedule.
