'Dangerous' snow squall moving through Western Pa.

The National Weather Service has issued a warning alerting motorists that a “dangerous” squall is moving through Western Pennsylvania and will be affecting Beaver, northern Allegheny and parts of Butler, Indiana, Westmoreland and Armstrong counties.

The squall is producing heavy snow and wind gusts up to 30 mph and is causing “dangerous, life-threatening travel” conditions, according to the warning.

The event should be over by 11:30 a.m., according NWS forecasters

According to the NWS, the squall was moving southeast at 35 mph.

Forecasters said the squall will affect travel along the Pennsylvania Turnpike between mile markers 24 and 58; Interstate 79 near mile marker 65, and between markers 67 and 82; and Interstate 376 between mile markers 36 and 43 and between mile markers 70 and 84.

Forecasters said residents and travelers in the following areas should use caution: Pittsburgh, Penn Hills, Ross, McCandless, Monroeville, Cranberry, Franklin Park, Aliquippa, Monaca, Vandergrift, Apollo and Shaler.
