Food bank distribution scheduled today in Duquesne, Tuesday in Butler

The Greater Pittsburgh Community Food Bank is handing out more food to Pittsburghers in need with another drive-up distribution at its warehouse site in Duquesne on Monday.

The distribution will take place from noon to 3 p.m. at the intersection of South Duquesne Boulevard and South Linden Street. The food bank is asking people not to arrive before 11 a.m.

The nonprofit said it plans to serve up to 1,000 vehicles at this distribution, and that each vehicle will receive two boxes of food.

People are asked to stay in their vehicles and to unlock their trunk or backseat door so volunteers can place the food into their car. 

This will be the last weekly distribution at the Duquesne site. Food distribution will continue at this location every other Monday beginning May 11.

Another drive-up food distribution is planned Tuesday from 10 a.m. to noon at Big Butler Fairgrounds, 1127 New Castle Road, in Prospect. The Food Bank will be able to serve up to 1,500 vehicles at this distribution. Each vehicle will receive two boxes of food.

Those needing more food help should contact their local food pantry or contact the food bank for help in locating a pantry near where you live, according to the nonprofit.
