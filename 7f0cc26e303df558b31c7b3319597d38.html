Death of woman whose body was found stuffed into refrigerator ruled homicide

The death of a McKees Rocks woman, whose body was found partially dismembered inside an abandoned refrigerator left in the hallway of an apartment building in May, has been ruled a homicide.

The Allegheny County medical examiner’s office released the ruling on Tuesday, although it said the cause of Kristy Jefferson’s death remains undetermined.

The body of Ms. Jefferson, 38, was found May 4 at a building in the 100 block of Helen Street in McKees Rocks. She had been reported missing days earlier. Her body was discovered when residents reported an odor coming from a refrigerator that littered a second-floor hallway.

County police arrested Daryl Jones, 41, and charged him with abuse of a corpse. Police confirmed that Jones lived in the building where Ms. Jefferson’s body was found.

According to court records, neither Jones nor anyone else has been charged with homicide.

“We are in consultation with the District Attorneys office concerning possible charges,” said county homicide Lt. Venerando Costa.

Jones is being held without bond at the Allegheny County Jail.
