WPIAL basketball playoff highlights: South Allegheny finally gets through

South Allegheny had not won a WPIAL playoff game in recent memory. The Gladiators made sure that Friday’s victory was memorable.

Bryce Epps dropped in a pair of free throws with 19 seconds remaining to lift No. 3 seed South Allegheny (22-1) to a 48-46 triumph against Avonworth in the first round of the WPIAL Class 3A playoffs. It was just the fourth district playoff victory in school history. The Gladiators last won a postseason game in 2001, with wins in 1997 and 1995 before that.

Epps, a freshman, scored all of his 7 points in the final four minutes, accounting for 7 of the Gladiators’ final 8 points. He knocked down a 3-pointer that put South Allegheny ahead, 43-40, and scored again to make it 45-42.

The Antelopes (11-12) had a couple of chances to tie or win the game as time ran down. Deon Thomas’ 3-point attempt rimmed out with five seconds left. Jordan Kolenda’s attempt to tip in the rebound also missed before the Gladiators corralled the rebound and the buzzer sounded.

Antonio Epps scored 15 points in the first half and finished with 18 to lead South Allegheny. Avonworth’s Kolenda led all scorers with 22 points.

More 3A boys

Neshannock 55, Washington 54 (OT): The Lancers (16-7) denied Washington (15-7) a fifth consecutive trip to the district quarterfinals. Spencer Perry paced Neshannock with 17 points, followed by John Paul Mozzocio with 16 and Preston Turk with 14. Washington’s leading scorer was Ian Bredniak with 15 points.

Beaver Falls 70, Southmoreland 59: The Tigers stumbled into the playoffs having lost three of their final four regular season games, but made quick work of the Scotties. Noah Vaughan netted 23 points, while Michael Conley and Kai Hauser contributed 13 apiece to boost Beaver Falls (14-7). Riley Comforti of Southmoreland (11-12) led all scorers with 33.

North Catholic 86, Shady Side Academy 36: The Trojans (21-2) stormed to a 48-16 halftime lead and cruised to their 15th straight win. Isaac DeGregorio scored 16 points, Jackson Paschall 14 and Hans Rottmann 12 for North Catholic, which moved on to the quarterfinals. Mason Tomlin had 16 points for the Indians (10-11).

Lincoln Park 68, Deer Lakes 50: Andre Wilder netted 12 of his 18 points during a burst that spanned the second and third quarters to propel the Leopards (19-4) into the quarterfinals. An 11-point effort by Bryce Robson led the Lancers (12-10). The game was tied, 14-14 at the end of the first quarter. Lincoln Park ramped it up in the second quarter, outscoring the Lancers, 24-11.

Carlynton 66, Charleroi 60 (OT): Carlynton (15-8) took control in the extra period, gaining a 13-7 edge to secure the victory. Mark Phillips led the way with 18 points. For Charleroi, it was more postseason heartache. The Cougars (16-7) have not made it to the quarterfinals since 1997. Since then, they have made the playoffs 13 times, but haven’t advanced.

Seton LaSalle 62, McGuffey 50: Michael Bigley knocked down 9 of 12 free throws in the fourth quarter and finished with 17 points for the Rebels (16-6). The Highlanders (15-8) made the playoffs for just the fifth time in school history. They have one postseason win that came in 2017.

2A Girls

Riverview 32, Burgettstown 28: The Raiders (15-8) trailed, 25-15, in the third quarter before finishing with a 17-3 run to knock the Blue Devils (16-7) out of the 2A playoffs. Sydney McDonough hit a pair of free throws with 23 seconds left to seal the win and give her a team-best 15 points.

South Side Beaver 61, Frazier 47: Senior Sydney Payne knocked down 7 of 8 free-throw attempts in the fourth quarter to cap a 27-point performance that pushed the Rams (16-7) past Frazier (9-14). The Commodores’ Sierra Twigg poured in 20 points, but foul trouble kept her off the floor in the second half.

Winchester Thurston 30, Chartiers-Houston 24: Playing without an injured Nadia Moore for the sixth consecutive game, the Bears (15-8) pulled out the victory. Moore is a freshman and the sixth-leading scorer in the WPIAL during the regular season. Maya Roberts provided the scoring for Winchester Thurston, racking up more than half her team’s total output with 17 points. Chartiers-Houston (12-11) was shutout in the first quarter before battling back into the game.

Ellis School 59, Shenango 39: Natalie Jasper of Ellis School (17-6) is the highest scorer in the 2A playoffs, averaging 22.7 points per game. She outdid herself in the first round by pouring in a game-high 31 points.The Wildcats made a postseason appearance for the first time since the 2015-16 season. Kylee Rubin and Kassidy Peters each had 11 points for Shenango (12-11).
