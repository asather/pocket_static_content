Four COVID-19 case reported in Allegheny County

According to the Allegheny County Health Department, two more positive case of COVID-19 have been reported, bringing the number to four in the county.

The individuals are both adults in their 60s, the county said in a release on Sunday. One is in isolation at home. The other is hospitalized. That patient’s condition has not be released.

The number is slightly different than the one provided by the state Department of Health, which is reporting three positive cases in Allegheny.

A county spokeswoman said the numbers are different, as the state is reporting cases it received from the lab in Exton, Pa., while the county’s latest figure includes results from commercial testing. 

​According to the state, there are 446 persons under investigation. Of those, 205 are negative, 183 are pending, and 63 are positive.
