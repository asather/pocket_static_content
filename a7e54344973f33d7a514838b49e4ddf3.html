Rollier's Hardware in Mt. Lebanon reopens with limited days and hours

After being closed for two weeks because of short staffing, Rollier’s Hardware in Mt. Lebanon reopened Saturday at 9 a.m. and will be open from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. on subsequent Saturdays, Tuesdays and Thursdays.

Floor manager Kirk Satterfield said Rollier’s is considered an essential business and therefore is permitted under the statewide COVID-19 emergency to remain open because it sells cleaning products, some groceries and supplies for plumbing, among other items that would be needed in emergencies.

“We cleaned everything,” he said in a telephone interview Saturday. “This place is gleaming this morning. We had a few people waiting outside in their car when we first opened, and for the first hour or two, it was pretty busy. People did a good job keeping apart. Some people waved to each other.”

He said masks of any kind are out of stock, but that employees wore masks and gloves and that most customers had made their own masks and were wearing them — “a lot of scarves, a couple bandanas or ski-type hats.”

The most common purchases were cleaning supplies, paper towels and toilet paper, and a few people bought mulch, Mr. Satterfield said.

The store had closed two weeks ago because many employees were concerned about the risk of falling ill with COVID-19. The store has some elderly part-timers who were not comfortable coming in, he said, and other part-timers who are in high school and whose parents forbade them from continuing to work.

“But then some people said they wanted to come back,” Mr. Satterfield said. “We got a clear idea how to proceed from the employees who wanted to come to work.”

Rollier’s, at 600 Washington Road, has 10 full-time staff members, he said.

“We are meeting all the guidelines for essential businesses,” Mr. Satterfield said. “We’re not encouraging people to shop, but to get in and get out. I’m pleased that people have been very good about social distancing.”
