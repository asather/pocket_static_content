Bloomfield club Howlers will close in August, move to East Liberty

Howlers, the Bloomfield club that is home to the indie-rock, punk, metal and Americana scene, is moving to East Liberty.

The final shows there will take place during Little Italy Days in mid-August.

Bloomfield’s loss of the venue comes after the closures of the former Bloomfield Bridge Tavern and the storefront that held music stores Jim’s Records and Juke Records.

Owner Susan Coe said, “We got a new landlord and he refused to give us a long-term lease, but we talked him into staying until August.”

Coe says Howlers will move to East Liberty, where she also owns the Peace, Love & Zen Holistic Wellness Center. She did not reveal the location but said it will be about the same size as Howlers, that it will have a different look and will book a range of bands from punk to metal to country.

Mary Jo Coll, who began tending bar there 15 years ago and eventually took over the bookings, plans to retire. During her time at Howlers, she became a mother figure to the bands. The Harlan Twins even wrote a song about her called “Mama Jo.” Coll also had a hand in booking the now-dormant RANT (Rock All Night Tour) festival.

When Howlers first started doing live music, Coll said, “the stage was a couple drum risers pushed together. Bands ran their own sound. Sue upgraded the sound system and hired good techs to run it, and built a new stage. I got us an online presence and we went from just a punk dive to a viable venue.”
