Woman killed in Route 8 crash had just started coaching career at Chatham

The 23-year-old woman killed last week in a head-on collision in Hampton had just started her career as an assistant softball coach at Chatham University.

Kaitlyn Eckelberry was pronounced dead at 12:03 a.m. Saturday after the 11:51 p.m. crash Friday on the 3800 block of Route 8.

In a campuswide email Monday morning, dean of students Heather Black said Ms. Eckelberry “made a great impact within the [softball] program in her short time and we will miss her very much.”

Ms. Eckelberry was officially hired as a part-time assistant coach in January after working as a volunteer coach during the fall, according to university officials.

In a press release issued by Gannon University, Ms. Eckelberry’s alma mater, Gannon softball coach Michelle Wiley said: “When I think of Katie, I always smile because she had such a beautiful soul and such a passion for life and softball. She was an amazing student, teammate, athlete, sister, daughter and most recently coach. She had such a positive impact on everyone she came in contact with.”

Allegheny County homicide detectives and the department’s Collision Reconstruction Unit reported that it appeared a Jeep Cherokee crossed the center line and struck the Hyundai Elantra driven by Ms. Eckelberry. The Jeep’s driver, also 23, was critically injured.

Ms. Eckelberry was born in Akron, Ohio, and most recently lived with her parents in Canton, Ohio. She graduated from Gannon with a bachelor’s of science in 2018, and then graduated from Gannon with her master’s of physician assistance science in 2019, according to an online obituary.

In the Gannon University statement, Ms. Wiley said, “She was a perfect example of a successful student-athlete. She was one of the hardest workers that I have ever known both on and off the field. She pushed herself and her teammates to get better every day.”

A moment of silence will be held in Ms. Eckelberry’s honor at the women’s basketball playoff game on Monday at 7 p.m. in Chatham University’s Athletic & Fitness Center, and Tuesday night at the men’s basketball playoff game at 7 p.m. at the same location.

Funeral services will be held at the Queen of Heaven Church in Uniontown, Ohio, on Friday.
