New North Park cafe will be a stop for runners, bikers

Runners and cyclists who enjoy North Park will soon have a new place to get a hot cup of coffee before making their five-mile loop around the lake — or a cold beer afterward.

Owner Craig Cozza gave a preview Wednesday of Pro Bike+Run’s Cadence Cafe to a group of Honeycomb investors. The cafe in McCandless is expected to open to the public next month.

The shop and cafe on the ground floor of the 1-year-old store on Kummer Road will offer gear plus a coffee bar outfitted with a high-tech  Modbar espresso system that serves hot and cold-brewed Mechanic Coffee from Verona and a juice and smoothie bar managed by Salud Juicery.

Along with grab-and-go food items and sandwiches, there will be a farmers market with seasonal produce, honey and eggs from local farms. Customers can take with or eat at a large seating area with Amish-made tables and benches.

Just as enticing is the wraparound bar with room for 20 people and 20 taps serving local and national craft beer. There also will be a gathering space below the store with a bar for private parties, barbecues and food truck events.

When it opened in December 2018, the sprawling 7,000-square-foot barn became the bike and running store’s fifth and largest location. An even larger store is set to open later this year in the Strip District. 

In creating the cafe space, Mr. Cozza said he hoped to create and foster a community of people who are interested in a healthy lifestyle. 

The cafe was funded in part by a Honeycomb Credit campaign that raised $24,250 from 78 investors. By relying on customers, friends and family for support, Honeycomb offers a crowdfunding alternative to bank loans for homegrown businesses.
