PG Stratford trip: Shakespeare as the main course, with a 'Spamalot' dessert

This summer, the Post-Gazette offers a unique Critics Choice motor coach tour to Canada’s Stratford Festival, July 21-24.

The trip centers on three varied works by Shakespeare — a sunny comedy, a dark comedy and a witty history play that combines comedy and bloodshed. But the full menu also offers a contemporary comic appetizer and a farce-like musical-comedy dessert, resulting in a five-play package that should appeal to everyone, Shakespearean or not.

The famous Stratford Festival is the largest repertory company in North America, staging 15 shows in four theaters over eight months. We stay at the Arden Park Hotel on the edge of the main festival grounds, a short walk from the iconic Festival Theatre, shaped like a huge circus tent, looking down on the swans along the Avon River. Our plays are:

* “The Sisters Rez,” a recent Canadian comedy about seven women in competition, described as “ribald, harrowing and mystical.”

* “Much Ado about Nothing,” Shakespeare’s famous comic sex-duel between Beatrice and Benedict, full of juicy material on the gender wars.

* “Richard III,” featuring Shakespeare’s most intriguing anti-hero, who outrageously schemes his way to the crown and then battles to retain it.

* “All’s Well That Ends Well,” a comedy, ending happily, but with dark and ambiguous twists along the way.

* “Spamalot,” the musical comedy based on Monty Python, a satiric send-up of the legends of King Arthur – sort of a palate cleanser after a main course of Shakespeare.

The city of Stratford is an attraction in itself, with restaurants, shopping, gardens and bike paths. We stop at the border duty-free shops on our return.Included with the motor coach, hotel and plays are a welcoming dinner, picnic lunch, backstage tour and a post-show get-together over drinks, with discussions along the way led by PG senior theater critic Chris Rawson, who spent several decades at Pitt teaching Shakespeare, comedy and satire, preparing him well for the plays we’ll see.
