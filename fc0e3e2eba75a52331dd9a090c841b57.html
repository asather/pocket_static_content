Some Steelers aren't too happy with reports of NFL's escrow proposal

Is the NFL about to experience a small taste of the turmoil Major League Baseball just went through?

On Tuesday, NFL Network’s Tom Pelissero reported that the NFLPA informed its board of representatives that the league has had discussions about holding 35% of players’ salaries in escrow in an effort to make up for the inevitable lost revenue during the 2020 season due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

If this sort of situation feels like deja vu, that’s because MLB and its players’ union just went through a nasty negotiation process that was held up for weeks, partially over players wanting to be paid fair value for putting their health at risk. Those two parties were eventually able to strike a deal months after spring training was first suspended.

NFLPA executive Don Davis told Pelissero the union reacted to the NFL’s proposal by essentially telling the league to “kick rocks,” an indication that a battle between the two parties over pay may be just beginning.

Plenty of players voiced their opinion on the issue via social media, including several Steelers. Second-year linebacker Devin Bush went on a lengthy tirade with his thoughts.

Safety Terrell Edmunds retweeted all of Bush’s thoughts on the matter.

Cornerback Joe Haden quote-tweeted his agreement with a sentiment put out by veteran safety Darius Butler on Pelissero’s report. Steelers tight end Eric Ebron also retweeted Butler’s GIF reaction.

All-Pro and Steelers defensive captain Cam Heyward retweeted this snarky joke regarding the proposal by DEC Management, an agency that represents a number of NFL players.

Receiver Ryan Switzer retweeted an equally incredulous reaction from super agent Drew Rosenhaus.

Steelers offensive lineman Zach Banner — who also on Wednesday made a point to publicly denounce anti-Semitism after Eagles receiver DeSean Jackson’s social media posts landed him in hot water this week — might have accidentally said the quiet part out loud while expressing his thoughts on fellow players who may or may not want to play football in 2020.

And finally, Plum native and ex-NFL punter Pat McAfee said he thinks this proposal might be the opening salvo of a brewing war between the NFL and its players.

Other high-profile NFL players also responded with exasperation toward the escrow report, including Chiefs quarterback Patrick Mahomes (fresh off his massive new contract), Saints receiver Michael Thomas, Titans offensive lineman Taylor Lewan, Eagles cornerback Darius Slay and Bears receiver Allen Robinson II.

We’ll see how the story progresses. As any MLB fan could probably tell you, buckle up.
