COVID-19 Update: State reports 91 new deaths, 4 more in Western Pennsylvania

This story was updated at 1:30 p.m. on May 29, 2020.

Pennsylvania health officials on Friday reported 91 new deaths due to COVID-19 and nearly 700 new cases of the disease.

Friday marked the 19th straight day that the state Department of Health reported fewer than 1,000 new cases. With an increase of 693 more people being diagnosed, the statewide case count is now 70,735 while the death toll has reached 5,464.

Of those new deaths, four were in Western Pennsylvania — in Allegheny, Beaver, Mercer and Somerset counties — according to state officials. It’s Somerset’s first reported death due to the virus.

Allegheny County’s numbers do not agree with the state’s, however. The county said it has 151 deaths, a figure that’s not changed since Wednesday. A total of 140 are confirmed and 11 are probable, the county said.

Allegheny also reported 19 new cases of COVID-19, bringing its total to 1,870 cases — 1,758 confirmed and 112 probable — since the county’s first case of COVID-19 was reported on March 14.

As of Friday, 336 people in Allegheny have been hospitalized due to the disease. Of them, 130 required a stay at the intensive care unit, and 67 required mechanical ventilation. Allegheny County has administered at least 31,263 tests. It has the capacity to administer roughly 2,000 tests per day.

Philadelphia has the most cases and deaths in the state, with 18,156 cases and 1,300 deaths.
