MacDill Air Force Base in Tampa placed briefly on lockdown

MacDill Air Force Base in Tampa, Fla., was placed on lockdown for about an hour Friday morning after reports of an armed suspect outside the base, Air Force officials said.

The base was put on lockdown at 7 a.m. as local law enforcement and base personnel searched the grounds and “isolated the situation” at one of its gates, Lt. Brandon Hanner, a spokesman for MacDill said. 

No shots were fired and no one was injured, he said.

The lockdown was lifted at 8 a.m. and all gates were reopened except for the one gate, Tanker Way, officials said.

Further details about the incident were not immediately available. Officials said they would release more information later today.

MacDill is the headquarters for U.S. Central Command and U.S. Special Operations Command and home to the 6th Air Refueling Wing. More than 15,000 military personnel report to the base.
