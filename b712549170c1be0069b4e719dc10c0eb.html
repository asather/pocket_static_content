COVID-19 Update: Allegheny surpasses 13,000 total cases; Pa. has highest since March 22

Allegheny County on Thursday eclipsed the 13,000 mark in COVID-19 cases, with the health department reporting an additional 127 cases. The state reported 1,376 new positive cases, the highest statewide number since March 22, when 1,690 cases were reported.

The latest data bring the county total to 13,024 cases of the virus since March 14, when the first positive case was recorded.

Of the 127 cases, 117 are confirmed from 1,494 tests; 10 are considered to be probable cases, among which seven are from positive antigen tests. 

The tests were conducted between Sept. 22 and Oct. 7. Those who have recently tested positive range in age from 10 months to 100 years old, with a median age of 35.

Pennsylvania recorded another day of over 1,000 new cases; there has only been one day within the last week where state case counts were below 1,000. The additional 1,376 cases bring the state total to 167,928.

Western Pennsylvania accounted for 347 of the state’s total cases, with Allegheny having the highest increase for the day, followed by Westmoreland County with 69 new cases — a record number for the county since the outbreak of the pandemic. Beaver County reported 12 new cases, making its total number 2,007 cases. It is the third county in the region to have over 2,000 positive cases.

The state health department reported an additional 27 virus-related deaths, bringing the state death toll to 8,299.

Three counties in the Pittsburgh region reported new deaths: Indiana, Mercer and Westmoreland. Westmoreland County had two new deaths while the other two counties reported one death each.

With no new deaths in Allegheny County, the number of people who have died as a result of the virus remains at 393. 

Across the state, 687 people are currently hospitalized for the virus, 79 of whom are in Allegheny County, according to the state health department.
