GNC seeks filing extension to address debt

GNC officials Monday notified the Securities and Exchange Commission that it does not expect to have enough cash to cover its nearly $700 million in owed debt as of Dec. 31.

The filing requests extra time to finalize its annual report and financial statements.

The statements “are being prepared on a going concern basis which contemplates the realization of assets and the settlement of liabilities and commitments in the normal course of business,” according to the filing.

The Pittsburgh-based retailer’s debt includes $154.7 million under convertible senior notes from 2015 and $441.5 million under a 2018 loan credit agreement which matures in May.

GNC shares have lately faltered and once again Monday briefly fell below $1.
