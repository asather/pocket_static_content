One juror dismissed in Wilkinsburg mass shooting case

One juror was dismissed Tuesday from hearing the case against two men accused of killing five people and an unborn child at a family cookout in Wilkinsburg in 2016. 

That brings the total number of jurors in the death penalty case to nine. Ten had been seated Monday, split evenly between five men and five women.

A gag order has been issued in the case.

Cheron Shelton, 32, of Lincoln-Lemington, and Robert Thomas, 31, of Homewood, are accused in the March 9, 2016, shooting on Franklin Avenue in Wilkinsburg.

Killed were siblings Jerry Michael Shelton, 35, Brittany Powell, 27, and Chanetta Powell, 25, who was eight months pregnant; and their cousins Tina Shelton, 37, and Shada Mahone, 26. Three others were wounded.

Testimony in the case is expected to begin the week of Feb. 3 before Allegheny County Common Pleas Judge Edward J. Borkowski.

Jury selection will continue Wednesday. A total of 12 jurors and four alternates will be selected.
