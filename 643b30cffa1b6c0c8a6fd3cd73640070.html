Nova to sell stake in Texas chemicals venture

Nova Chemicals said it will sell its 50% stake in petrochemicals joint venture Novealis Holdings to its partner, Borealis, an Austria-based chemicals company.

Financial terms were not disclosed.

Nova and Borealis formed the joint venture in 2018 and Novealis subsequently entered a venture deal with Total S.A. to create Bayport Polymers in Houston, Texas.

Bayport’s operations include an ethane steam cracker and polyethylene plants.

Nova, a Calgary, Canada-based plastics maker, has about 250 employees at an executive center in Moon, and 200 at a plant in Monaca, Beaver County.

Reports surfaced last year that Nova’s owner, Mubadala Investment of the United Arab Emirates, was exploring options including a sale of Nova.

Reuters reported that Chevron Phillips Chemical made an offer for the company which Nova declined to confirm.

Nova’s sale of its joint venture stake to Borealis is expected to close in the first half of 2020.
