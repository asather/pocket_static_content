Community College of Beaver County's Yazid Powell pays tribute to Kobe Bryant with 81-point performance

Fans across the world have paid tribute to Kobe Bryant, the late former NBA star, since his death in a helicopter crash Sunday. Community College of Beaver County guard Yazid Powell put just a bit of extra effort into his. 

The freshman from Philadelphia, Bryant’s home town, went off for 81 points in a 147-61 beating of Butler County Community College at the school’s Center Township campus Monday. Bryant, you’ll likely remember, scored that total in a legendary performance in 2006. 

Powell had a chance to exceed 81 points with a free throw toward the end of the game, but as you can see around the 2:20 mark of the video, he intentionally missed to preserve the famous total in tribute to Bryant. 

Later, he posted a photo of himself holding a sign that reads “81” while wearing Bryant’s famed No. 24.

That’s teammate Chris Greene’s number, according to the CCBC roster. Powell normally wears No. 1. 

In another post, Powell explained his love for the former Los Angeles Laker, who retired in 2016.
