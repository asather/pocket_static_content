IUP educator named head of state professors union

An Indiana University of Pennsylvania professor and department chair has been chosen to lead the statewide union representing faculty at the14 state-owned universities.

Jamie Martin will succeed Kenneth Mash as president of the Association of Pennsylvania State College and University Faculties, according to a statement from the union. Statewide delegates selected her Friday

She will begin her two-year term in June.

Ms. Martin is professor and chair of the Department of Criminology and Criminal Justice at IUP. She previously served three terms as APSCUF’s vice president and was a member of the faculty and coaches’ negotiations teams in 2015–16 and 2019–20.

She is the second woman elected APSCUF president.

“I am honored and humbled,” she said in a statement.

Also selected as officers Friday are: Chris Hallen of Bloomsburg University, vice president, and Clifford Johnston of West Chester University, treasurer. Delegates reelected Dr. Michele Papakie of IUP as secretary.

The association represents about 5,000 faculty and coaches at the 14 universities belonging to the State System of Higher Education.

In addition to IUP, those campuses include: Bloomsburg, California, Cheyney, Clarion, East Stroudsburg, Edinboro, Kutztown, Lock Haven, Mansfield, Millersville, Shippensburg, Slippery Rock, and West Chester Universities of Pennsylvania.
