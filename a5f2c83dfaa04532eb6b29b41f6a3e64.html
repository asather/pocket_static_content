Allegheny County reports 4 new deaths as COVID-19 cases surpass 700

The number of COVID-19 cases in Allegheny County has surpassed the 700 mark with four new deaths, officials said Wednesday.

The 31 new cases brings the total to 720. The county, which has 10 total deaths, has not released information on the latest deaths. 

Of the previous six deaths, five included people who were hospitalized — one person in their 60s, two in their 70s, one in their 80s, and one in their 90s, officials said. One individual who died was not hospitalized; that woman was in her 70s.

The largest number of deaths in Western Pennsylvania have been reported in Beaver County, where the state is reporting 13 deaths and 128 confirmed cases.

Other deaths reported in the region have been in Butler County (102 cases) and Lawrence County (29 cases), which have two deaths each; Fayette County (and 35 cases) and Cambria County (9 cases) have each reported a death. And Westmoreland County reported its first death, according to state figures.

Throughout the rest of Western Pennsylvania, Armstrong County has 19 cases; Clarion, 8; Clearfield, 7; Crawford, 8; Erie, 29; Forest, 5; Franklin, 43; Greene, 17; Indiana, 21; Mercer, 27; Somerset, 7; Venango, 5; Washington, 59; and Westmoreland, 183.

Philadelphia County leads the state in the number of cases with 4,456 and in deaths at 87. Statewide, the number of cases increased by 1,680 to 16,239 confirmed cases. There have been 310 deaths in Pennsylvania.

In Allegheny County, the number of cases are broken down into the following age groups:

0-4: 2 (less than 1%)

5-12: 4 (less than 1%)

13-18: 9 (1%)

19-24: 58 (8%)

25-49: 294 (41%)

50-64: 203 (28%)

65 and over: 150 (21%)

Females — 369 cases — have tested positive for COVID-19 slightly more than males — 351 cases.
