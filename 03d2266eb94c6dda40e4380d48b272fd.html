Nine new COVID-19 deaths at Western Pa. nursing homes; 12 new deaths in Allegheny County

This story was updated at 12:30 p.m.

Several counties in the region reported new COVID-19 deaths on Tuesday, including nine new deaths at nursing/long-care homes in Western Pennsylvania, according to the latest statewide figures.

Allegheny (12), Beaver (7), Butler (1), Cambria (1), Lawrence (1) and Westmoreland (4) counties each reported at least one new death.

The region’s nursing/long-care homes that reported deaths are located in Allegheny (4), Beaver (4) and Westmoreland (1) counties.

Allegheny County reported 17 new cases of COVID-19, according to the figures.

The county health department is now reporting that there have been 1,059 cases and 67 deaths due to the coronavirus. Those are an increase from Monday when the county reported 1,042 cases and 55 deaths.

Statewide on Tuesday, the Pennsylvania Department of Health reported a total of 1,564 deaths, up 360 from Monday’s total. An additional 1,296 cases were reported, bringing the total number of cases to 34,528.  

 

Allegheny’s totals are the highest in Western Pennsylvania. Since the first case was confirmed on March 14, the county has administered 12,654 tests. Of its cases, 188 people have required hospitalization, with 76 of those requiring admission to the Intensive Care Unit and 46 needing mechanical ventilation.
