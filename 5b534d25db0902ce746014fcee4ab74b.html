Peduto bans public gatherings of more than 50 people in Pittsburgh

Pittsburgh Mayor Bill Peduto announced Sunday the city would limit public gatherings to 50 people in an effort to reduce the spread of the deadly COVID-19 disease.

An executive order signed by Mr. Peduto on Friday initially banned public gatherings to 250 people. But on Twitter Sunday night, the mayor announced he would be limiting occupancy levels even further.

“Today, the CDC recommended the cancellation of in-person events of 50 or more people. The city of Pittsburgh will adhere their advice,” Mr. Peduto tweeted.

Beginning 9 a.m. Monday, the city is requiring the cancellation of all events which exceed that level in the city.

Public safety will be enforcing the restrictions through regular measures of inspections and monitoring occupancy levels, mayoral spokesman Tim McNulty said.

As of Sunday, there are four cases in Allegheny County. County officials announced on Sunday they are calling on all nonessential businesses to close voluntarily for at least two weeks to help curb the spread of the virus.

“Looking at other communities and our own data, southwest Pennsylvania is about two weeks away from overwhelming our hospitals,” county Health Director Debra Bogen said in a video statement.
