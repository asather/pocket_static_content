Allegheny County courts adjust operations with amended emergency declaration

The president judge overseeing Allegheny County courts on Sunday amended an emergency declaration to include new information about how cases and hearings will be carried out amid the COVID-19 pandemic.

All criminal court events, except emergency matters such as bail hearings and motions to lift detainers, are suspended until further order of the court, according to the amended declaration signed by President Judge Kim Berkeley Clark of Allegheny County Common Pleas Court.

Emergency motions and petitions for injunctive relief will be held at 10 a.m. and 2 p.m. Monday through Friday at the Pittsburgh Municipal Court Building on First Avenue, Downtown. Matters involving public health or “human life concerns” will be handled using “advanced communication technology.”

Juvenile delinquency, juvenile dependency and protection from abuse proceedings also will be handled using advanced communication technology.

Magisterial District Courts will be closed to the public.

Temporary protection from abuse petitions will be handled between 8 a.m. and 2 p.m. Monday through Friday. Unless otherwise indicated, temporary orders will remain in effect for 15 days after the end of the judicial emergency.

Juvenile detention hearings will be heard Mondays, Wednesdays and Fridays, with emergency motions to be heard Tuesdays and Thursdays. 

Post-dispositional review hearings are suspended until further order, except for post-dispositional proceedings involving a probation violation or a failure to adjust an allegation when a juvenile is detained.

Emergency custody motions will be addressed on a case-by-case basis, and motions can be made from 9 a.m. to 3 p.m. Monday through Friday.

With the consent of all parties, any matter may be presented to the court by motion, without a hearing, pursuant to the judges’ procedures on the website, for entry of an order in cases of child support, divorce, alimony, equitable distribution of property and custody.
