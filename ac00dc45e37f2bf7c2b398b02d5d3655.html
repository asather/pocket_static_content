Pitt football just added a potential 2021 starting quarterback.

Former Arizona State quarterback Joey Yellen is transferring to Pitt, according to a report from Panther-Lair.com. He’ll be in Oakland for Pitt’s spring semester starting Jan. 6, the report indicated.

Yellen must sit out the 2020 season and will be eligible for the 2021 campaign as a redshirt sophomore, setting up a post-Kenny Pickett quarterback competition.

The 6-3, 210-pound Yellen was a true freshman last season and played in only one game for the Sun Devils, a 31-26 loss to USC. He performed admirably though, going 28-of-44 (63.6%) for 292 yards, four touchdowns and two interceptions.

Yellen is leaving Arizona State after losing a battle for that program’s starting quarterback role to fellow freshman Jayden Daniels.

He was a highly touted recruit coming out of California’s Mission Viejo High School. Rivals rated Yellen as a three-star prospect, while 247 Sports had him at four stars. He turned down offers from Georgia, Oregon State, Boise State and Washington State (among others) to play for Herm Edwards in Tempe, Ariz.

Yellen will join a football program that currently has three scholarship quarterbacks on its roster: Pickett, Nick Patti and Davis Beville. Pickett was the starter last season and is the presumed lead signal-caller going forward. Meanwhile, Patti enters 2020 as a rising redshirt junior, and Beville will be a redshirt freshman.

Pitt raised eyebrows when it didn’t sign a high school quarterback to its 2020 recruiting class. Yellen will provide, if nothing else, depth at a position the Panthers definitely need to address after Pickett’s final season of eligibility next year.
