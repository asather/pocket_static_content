IPSCO Tubulars to lay off hundreds of workers

IPSCO Koppel Tubulars LLC in Beaver County plans to lay off more than 500 workers this month amid struggles in the oil and gas industry.

The company will lay off 210 people at its seamless tube plant in Ambridge and 350 at its steel shop 20 miles north in Koppel, effective March 31, according to a Worker Adjustment Retraining Notification (WARN) filed with the state Department of Labor and Industry.

The company didn’t immediately return a call requesting comment.

In January, IPSCO was sold by its parent company — Russian steel giant TMK — to Luxembourg-based Tenaris, a global maker and supplier of steel pipes for the oil and gas industry.
