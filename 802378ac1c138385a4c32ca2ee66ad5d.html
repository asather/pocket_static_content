City Council meetings to now be 'pre-recorded and replayed' as chambers remain closed

As Pittsburgh City Council continues meeting remotely during the COVID-19 pandemic, the council on Tuesday will not broadcast or livestream meetings but instead will replay them later, according to an announcement from the city clerk’s office.

Council Chambers remain closed as a public health measure.

“Until further notice, both the regular and standing committees meetings will be pre-recorded and replayed on City Channel Pittsburgh. Please check your local listings for the station information,” according to the clerk’s office announcement posted on social media. “Videos of meetings can also be accessed online at pittsburgh.legislar.com/calendar.aspx.⁣”

The meeting will replay on the City Channel at 7 p.m. 

The City Channel can be found on Comcast channels 13 and 14 / Verizon channels 44 and 45.

A schedule has not yet been announced for when the meetings will be posted on the city’s website.

Council had been conducting meetings via conference call since March 17 that were broadcast live and livestreamed online. 

The meetings will now be conducted on the video conference platform Zoom, according to Council President Theresa Kail-Smith.

A reason for the change was not specified in the clerk’s announcement.
