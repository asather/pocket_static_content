Downtown parking enforcement will resume this week

Pittsburgh will resume enforcement of meter parking Downtown on Wednesday in response to Allegheny County’s move into Gov. Tom Wolf’s yellow reopening phase.

As certain businesses begin reopening, the Pittsburgh Parking Authority said Monday it will temporarily allow the first half-hour of parking to be free to ease takeout and pickup services. People who have parked their car must enter license plate information into parking kiosks or a phone app to receive the free half-hour, and only one free parking session can be used per day, the authority said.

The city said that since it suspended meter enforcement in mid-March, vehicles have remained in parking spots for long periods of time, leading to complaints that restaurants’ takeout customers were having trouble parking.

Enforcement officers on Monday began issuing warnings Downtown for the upcoming change, the city said.

Parking Authority garages Downtown remain open.

Parking meter and residential permit parking enforcement remains suspended in all other city neighborhoods and business districts.
