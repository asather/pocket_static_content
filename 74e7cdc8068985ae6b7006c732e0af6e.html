Ohio man indicted in Robinson bank robbery

An Ohio man has been indicted in federal court in Pittsburgh on a charge of robbing a Robinson bank on Jan. 15.

Montague Howard, 40, of Wintersville, is charged with one count of armed bank robbery.

A federal grand jury said Mr. Howard entered the First Commonwealth Bank on Park Manor Drive, pointed what appeared to be a gun at the tellers and made off with about $8,000.

Police caught him two hours later after stopping his vehicle near Steubenville.

A Robinson police officer initially saw a car driving fast and away from the bank around the time of the robbery, police said, and police then used traffic cameras footage to identify the license plate.

Officers tracked the plate and registration to an address in Wintersville, where police set up surveillance and saw Mr. Howard going into and coming out of a house.

Wintersville police then followed Mr. Howard's car and pulled him over without incident, according to Ohio authorities.

Police said they found a trash bag containing about $8,000 at Mr. Howard's home, as well as the ski mask authorities say he was wearing and the gun he is accused of using.
