$2 million pledge from Highmark targets groups in Pa., W.Va., and Delaware

Highmark Inc., the insurance arm of Highmark Health, this week promised $2 million in grants to groups in Pennsylvania, West Virginia and Delaware due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

Food banks in Pennsylvania and West Virginia will receive $575,000, $525,000 is targeted for human service agencies through COVID-19 relief funds and another $700,000 will go to safety net providers through the Highmark Foundation.

In addition, $200,000 is earmarked for relief efforts in Delaware.

Highmark has operations in all three states.

Key recipients in Pennsylvania include the Greater Pittsburgh Community Food Bank, the Westmoreland County Food Bank, the Emergency Action Fund/Pittsburgh Foundation and the United Way of Southwestern Pennsylvania’s Emergency Basic Needs Fund, as well as 25 community-based health care organizations.

The funding is being provided by Highmark’s corporate community giving programs and the Highmark Foundation.
