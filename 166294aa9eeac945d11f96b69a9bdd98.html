City looking to refinance $161M in bonds while rates remain low

The city is looking to take advantage of favorable market conditions and refinance just over $161 million in municipal bonds to reduce debt.

In an April 8 letter from the city’s Director of Finance Margaret Lanier to City Council members, the administration requested the approval of an issuance $161,250,000 and asked that council send the bill to committee this week. 

Because interest rates remain low, “it’s not unreasonable for a city to want to refinance some of its outstanding debt to get a savings, just like homeowners are refinancing mortgages because mortgage rates are so low right now,” said Matt Fabian, a partner and lead analyst at Massachusetts-based Municipal Market Analytics. “Cities really are in the exact same position. It works almost exactly like it would for a homeowner with a mortgage who can maybe free up a bit more cash every month.”

If approved, the bond issuance would be used in part to refund all or portions of bonds previously issued in 2012, 2014 and 2018.

“The city's financial advisors have been watching the constantly changing market, and determined the time is right to refinance. It should save the city an estimated $15 million,” said Timothy McNulty, mayoral spokesman.

Council in February approved a $52 million bond issuance.

The resolution for the new refinancing will be introduced Tuesday and fast-tracked for committee discussion Wednesday.

Council on April 7 passed a resolution authorizing the Peduto administration to negotiate and secure lines of credit “for the purpose of maintaining and supporting continuous operations during and immediately after the COVID-19 pandemic.”
