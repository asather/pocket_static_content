Pennsylvania's National Guard active in food deliveries

As the Pennsylvania Army National Guard truck arrived at UPMC Children’s Hospital of Pittsburgh on Monday afternoon, the Rev. Elizabeth Mayforth saw a woman follow along as it made its way around the building to deliver pallets of food.

The woman had been preparing to take her infant home after two months in the neonatal intensive care unit, said Rev. Mayforth, a chaplain at Children’s, and she needed the assistance. The Guard members also brought a load to the nearby Ronald McDonald’s House, where families of patients were “overjoyed,” she said.

The Guard team drove from Harrisburg with supplies of shelf-stable food to numerous Pittsburgh-area distribution points, including a church in Verona, a community center in Penn Hills and the Energy Innovation Center in the Hill District, said Capt. David Boyes, public information office for the Guard’s Task Force West.

Food deliveries are dispatched from Harrisburg based on requests approved by the Pennsylvania Emergency Management Agency. Rev. Mayforth said she put in the request for a delivery at Children’s so that anyone with financial needs during the coronavirus crisis could receive some help. “My intent was to support patients, family, employees and staff,” she said.

That delivery amounted to the equivalent of more than 3,000 meals, with large amounts also delivered to the other distribution points. 

The delivery was conducted by the Williamstown-based 131st Transportation Company, part of the Guard’s Task Force Support.

Pennsylvania Guard members distributed more than 326,000 meals between April 13 and Friday, said Duncan Williams of the task force.

“It’s pretty cool,” he said. “The soldiers are really motivated to help out.”
