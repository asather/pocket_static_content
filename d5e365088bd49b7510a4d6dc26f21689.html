Meet Maryland RB Anthony McFarland Jr., Louisiana OG Kevin Dotson

Round 4, 124th pick overall

Anthony McFarland Jr.

School: Maryland

RB • 5-8 • 208 pounds

About the pick: Maryland native who was a four-star recruit at prep powerhouse DeMatha Catholic. ... Not related to former NFL defensive lineman Anthony “Booger” McFarland. ... Ran for 1,648 yards and 12 touchdowns on 245 carries in two seasons at Maryland, leaving after his redshirt sophomore campaign. ... Also caught 24 passes over his two years but was never more productive than an eye-popping 298-yard performance against Ohio State in 2018. ... His 1,000-yard freshman All-American season came with Matt Canada as interim head coach; Canada now enters his first year as the Steelers quarterback coach. ... Once Canada left Maryland, Scottie Montgomery took over as offensive coordinator; Montgomery was Steelers receivers coach under Mike Tomlin from 2010-12. ... Was a teammate of Tomlin’s son Dino last year at Maryland. ... Ran a 4.44 40-yard dash at the combine, as expected for a player who was a home-run threat for the Terrapins. ... Only had two 100-yard games and 614 total rushing yards last year but was hampered by an ankle injury sustained early in the season. ... Turned 22 in March.

Round 4, 135th pick overall

Kevin Dotson

School: Louisiana

OG • 6-4 • 321 pounds

About the pick: Brawler of a lineman who exclusively played right guard at the school formerly known as Louisiana-Lafayette. ... Was overshadowed somewhat on the Ragin’ Cajuns line by right tackle Robert Hunt, a second-round pick, but still was named a first-team All-American by the Associated Press. ... Wasn’t invited to the NFL combine but had a pro day for himself filmed and claimed a 4.8 40-yard dash 32-inch vertical leap. ... Was Pro Football Focus’ highest-graded run-blocking lineman in the draft. ... Played in all 53 games of his college career after redshirting as a freshman and started 52 in a row. ... Turns 24 in September. ... Also a native of Louisiana.
