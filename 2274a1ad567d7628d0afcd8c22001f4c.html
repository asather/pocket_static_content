Man accused of throwing rocks at police during Pittsburgh protests turns himself in

A man accused of throwing rocks at police during May 30 protests in Downtown Pittsburgh has turned himself in.

Samuel Crawford, 30, of Elizabeth Borough, was charged June 30 with aggravated assault, possessing instruments of crime, propulsion of missiles onto roadways, and several counts reckless endangerment. 

He turned himself in on Thursday, according to Pittsburgh police.

A preliminary hearing is set for July 24, according to court documents.
