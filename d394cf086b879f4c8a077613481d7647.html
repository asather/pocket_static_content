Allegheny Health Network announces mobile COVID-19 testing van in Homewood Tuesday and Thursday

Allegheny Health Network is taking COVID-19 testing on the road.

The health network will begin sending a mobile testing site to underserved communities beginning with Homewood on Tuesday and Thursday before moving to different areas each week that have yet to be determined.

Nasal swab testing will take place by appointment only for people showing symptoms including fever, coughing and shortness of breath, or those who have come had contact with someone with a confirmed case. A physician’s order is not required, and a spokesperson said that 50 people will be tested each day.

To set up an appointment, the number to call is 412-687-7348.

The testing site will be outside the Alma Illery Medical Center at 7227 Hamilton Avenue in Homewood between 9 a.m. and 4 p.m on Tuesday and Thursday. Individuals will be contacted directly with their test results in about five-seven days, possibly longer depending on how many tests are being conducted in the region.

“Convenient access to health care should not depend on where a person lives, particularly during a public health crisis,” said Dr. Jerome Gloster, chief executive officer of Primary Care Health Services Inc., a countywide system of primary care centers including the Alma Illery Center, in a statement released by AHN on Monday.

The Heinz Endowments as well as the Henry L. Hillman Foundation provided grant funding for this initiative.

AHN also operates permanent COVID-19 testing sites in the North Side and Braddock and continues to offer drive-up testing sites in Wexford, Bethel Park, Monroeville, Erie and Natrona Heights. These sites require a doctor’s note to be tested.
