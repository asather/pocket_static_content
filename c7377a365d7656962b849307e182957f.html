People on the move: 6/22//2020

Associations and Organizations

Southwestern Pennsylvania Engineering Outreach elected officers. They are: chairperson: Sheena Sundin, marketing manager, Hayes Design Group Architects; vice chairperson: David Troianos, engineering director, water/wastewater, GAI Consultants; secretary: Brad Duda, technical manager, Michael Baker International; treasurer: Donna Chappel, president, Advanced Integration Group; and ex-officio: Suresh Ramanathan, president, Koryak.

Communication

Orion Strategies hired Jessica Pierce as an account executive.

Design

Allen Snyder joined Herbert, Rowland & Grubic Inc. as chief financial officer.

PWCampbell announces key ownership and management changes. Carlin Campbell, Erin Campbell and Amanda Campbell Caliendo have transitioned from vice presidents to principals and will continue to increase their ownership in the firm.

Health

Allegheny Health Network appointed two veteran clinician-leaders to key leadership posts at Allegheny Valley Hospital. William Bailey, DO, was named chief medical officer, and Milissa Hammers, MSN, RN, was appointed chief nursing officer.

Nonprofit

Dan Griffin was named a board member at VisitPittsburgh. He is president of Griffin-Spence.

Pittsburgh Opera said Sri Palanisamy joined its board. He is the vice president of enterprise strategy at Highmark Health.

Familylinks named Stacey Vaccaro president and CEO. Ms. Vaccaro will assume the post on July 29.

Professional Services

Abram’s Nation hired Rachel Winner-Eberhardt as director of sales and marketing.

Awards and Honors

Robb McKinney, founder of Bridgeview Wealth, completed the Whole Family Advisor certification curriculum from Legacy Capitals Academy. His practice focuses on inclusive legacy planning, communication and interpersonal skills, the main causes of wealth depletion. 

McAuley Ministries, Pittsburgh Mercy’s grant-making foundation, announces the 2020 Sister Susan Welsh Good Neighbor Award recipient, Christopher R. Roach Jr. Mr. Roach was nominated by Jeanne McNutt, executive director of Uptown Partners of Pittsburgh, and Raqueeb Bey, founder and executive director of Black Urban Gardeners and Farmers of Pittsburgh Co-op. The Good Neighbor Award was created to honor the legacy of Sister Susan Welsh, retired president and CEO of Pittsburgh Mercy and current board chairperson and founding board member of McAuley Ministries Foundation. The award recognizes residents of the Hill District, Uptown, or West Oakland who exemplify the ideals of a “good neighbor” willing to devote time, talents and resources to make their neighborhood a better place to live.

Business notes

CB Financial Services Inc., the holding company for Community Bank, said the company is set to join the small cap Russell 2000 Index and the broad-market Russell 3000 Index at the conclusion of this year’s reconstitution of the Russell stock indexes, effective after the U.S. stock market opens on June 29. The annual reconstitution of the broad-market Russell 3000 Index captures 3,000 of the largest U.S. stocks, ranking them by total market capitalization.

Mount Washington Community Development Corp. received a $25,000 award from the Richard King Mellon Foundation for habitat and view restoration in Grandview Park. MWCDC will coordinate project activities with  Pittsburgh Parks Conservancy to restore approximately 1 acre in Grandview Park using the habitat-and-view restoration model created by the development group and refined by the conservancy.
