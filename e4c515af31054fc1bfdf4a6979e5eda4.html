Ten charged federally in connection with '11 Hunnit' gang in the Hill District

Ten people from Pittsburgh, McKees Rocks, Pitcairn and other Allegheny County communities have been charged with drug dealing following an FBI-led wiretap investigation into the 11 Hunnit street gang in the Hill District that federal agents say was led in part by slain rapper Jimmy Wopo.

The 10 were charged in four complaints filed earlier this month in federal court and unsealed on Wednesday.

The first complaint charges five people with conspiracy to distribute crack cocaine from January 2020 until this month: Phillip Robinson, 30, of Pittsburgh; Joseph Clancy, 46, of Pittsburgh; Tyree Campbell, 29, of Pittsburgh; Tracy Bullock, 51, of McKees Rocks; and Sanzio Williams, 31, of Pitcairn.

Wiretaps and surveillance revealed that Clancy supplied Robinson, who redistributed the drugs to the other alleged co-conspirators.

All of those charged have at least one prior narcotics conviction, federal prosecutors said.

In a second complaint, Andre Moore-Coleman, 31, of Pittsburgh and James Bryant Jr., 52, of McKees Rocks are accused of dealing heroin. Wiretaps revealed that Moore-Coleman, identified as a supplier, conspired with Bryant and others to distribute heroin in Pittsburgh and McKees Rocks.

Both men also have prior drug convictions, prosecutors said.

A third complaint charges Ronald Lewis III, 42, of Turtle Creek and Victor Simmons, 24, of Pittsburgh with dealing in crack. Lewis, identified as a crack source, conspired with Simmons — who redistributed the drug in Pittsburgh and throughout Allegheny County — according to the FBI.

They also have prior drug convictions, prosecutors said.

A fourth complaint charges Derek Calloway, 26, of Pittsburgh with possession of a gun and ammunition by a felon based on a May 13, 2020, search of his residence.
