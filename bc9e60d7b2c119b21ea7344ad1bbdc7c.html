Suspected meth production supplies found dumped in Indiana County woods

Pennsylvania state police are investigating potential meth manufacturing in Indiana County after more than 100 items were found dumped in the woods.

Troopers on Monday found several discarded items along Lower Laurel Road in Buffington Township after receiving information about meth production in the area, according to state police.

A clandestine laboratory response team canvassed the area and found 42 “suspected” one-pot vessels, 100 acid generators and various empty chemical containers.

State police are asking anyone with information to call a 24-hour drug trafficking tip line at 1-877-PA-NODRUGS. Callers can remain anonymous.
