COVID-19 deaths jump by 2 in Allegheny County

The number of those infected with COVID-19 in Pennsylvania jumped by 989 cases in the last 24 hours, bringing the statewide total to 61,611, according to the daily update Saturday by the state Department of Health.

The health department also reported 61 new deaths, bringing the statewide total to 4,403. Two of the new deaths were in Allegheny County, both in a nursing or personal care home. Also in Allegheny County, there were 13 more cases reported, according to the latest figures.

Allegheny County’s death toll from the novel coronavirus now stands at 143 people — 132 confirmed and 11 probable. There have been a total of 1,595 COVID-19 cases reported — 1,511 confirmed and 84 probable cases — since the first county case was reported March 14. A case is probable “when the person displays symptoms of COVID-19 and has had close contact with a confirmed positive case but has not had a positive test themselves,” according to the county.

Additionally in the county, as of Saturday there are 292 past or present hospitalizations related to COVID-19, which is an increase of two, with 120 of those requiring admission to the Intensive Care Unit and 67 needing mechanical ventilation.

Allegheny County has the highest number of total cases and deaths in Western Pennsylvania. Since the first case was confirmed on March 14, the county has administered 23,596 tests. County health department Director Dr. Debra Bogen said Friday that the county has the capacity to administer roughly 2,000 tests per day.

Many state pandemic restrictions were lifted Friday for businesses in Allegheny and several surrounding counties that entered the “yellow” reopening phase. And Gov. Tom Wolf announced Friday that 12 more counties, including Beaver County, will move to the yellow phase on May 22.
