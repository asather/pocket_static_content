Some without power after high winds blow through Western Pa.

As high winds made their way through the area overnight Saturday and into Sunday, the electric utilities serving the region have reported outages.

Duquesne Light said — as of noon — it has 29 outages affecting 557 customers.

The majority of those customers are in Beechview, Hopewell, Penn Hills and Shaler.

West Penn Power is reporting that — as of 11:55 a.m. — 6,400 of its 2 million customers statewide are without power.

The utility said customers in the following counties were without power:

● Allegheny, 96

● Beaver, 250

● Butler, 389

● Fayette, 123

● Washington, 38

● Westmoreland, 202

The winds may have been responsible for a fire in West Oakland.

No one was hurt after power lines fell onto the roof and started a fire at a building on the corner of Robinson and Terrace Streets, according to the city’s public safety department.

No one was inside the building when the fire started around 4 a.m.

Firefighters had to wait for Duquesne Light to shut off power to the building before they could battle the blaze, the city said.

The cause of the fire remains under investigation.

The National Weather Service canceled the wind advisory shortly before 10 a.m. Sunday. It had been scheduled to end at 1 p.m.

“The winds are decreasing. We canceled the wind advisory early,” said Jenna Lake, a meteorologist at the National Weather Service. “The system is moving east, so things have calmed down significantly (here) over the last couple hours.” 

Ms. Lake said the region’s highest wind gust — 56 mph -—was recorded at Butler County Airport. A wind gust of 45 mph was recorded at Pittsburgh International Airport. A gust of 52 mph was measured at Washington County’s airport. And in Sewickley, a gust was recorded at 51 mph.

Sunday’s high will be lower than Saturday’s record-high, but still above the average.

Temperatures today are expected to peak around 45 degrees. Tonight’s low will be around 34 as winds continue to calm.

On Monday, temperatures will rise again and reach near 52.

On Saturday, Pittsburgh hit 70 degrees, breaking the all-time record of 68 degrees on Jan. 11, set in 1890, according to the National Weather Service.

It also marked the first time the temperature reached 70 or higher in the month of January since 2005, said meteorologist Jenna Lake.
