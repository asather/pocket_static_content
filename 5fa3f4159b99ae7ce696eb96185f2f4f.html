Pirates place Kevin Newman on 10-day IL with bruised knee

The bad news for the Pirates is that Kevin Newman’s season is over.

The good news is that there was no structural damage to his left knee, and the infielder won’t need offseason surgery.

Newman was hit in the left knee with a 97-mph fastball from Cardinals reliever Genesis Cabrera in the ninth inning of Saturday’s 5-4 loss. On Sunday afternoon, the Pirates placed Newman on the 10-day injured list with a contusion to the peroneal nerve of his left knee.

Derek Shelton said X-rays came back negative, though the Pirates manager said Newman was in a bunch of pain.

Thus ends a frustrating season for Newman, who finishes with a slash line of .224/.281/.276, down significantly from .308/.353/.446 in 2019, his first full season in the major leagues.

Newman also made a team-high seven errors in 44 games, as he bounced between shortstop and second base.
