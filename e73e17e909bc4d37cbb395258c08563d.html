COVID-19 Update: Allegheny County reports 46 more cases, no new deaths

In the last 24 hours, the Pennsylvania health department reported an increase of 672 cases of COVID-19 and 11 new virus-related deaths. 

The state now has a total of 164,207 people who have tested positive for the virus and 8,227 related deaths.

Western Pennsylvania accounted for 177 of the states cases for Monday. Allegheny County had the highest increase for the day with 46 new cases, followed by Westmoreland County with 45 new cases.

The newest positive cases in Allegheny County are the results from 848 tests that were taken between Sept. 30 and Oct. 4, health officials reported Monday. All of Monday’s cases are confirmed.

The 46 people who have recently tested positive range in age from 2 to 94 years old, with a median age of 35.

Since the beginning of the pandemic, 12,777 people in Allegheny County have tested positive for the virus and, with no new deaths reported for Monday, the death toll remains at 386.

Only two of the states newest deaths were reported in the Pittsburgh region, with Beaver and Westmoreland counties reporting one death each.

During the week of Sept. 29 to Oct. 5, the county saw a rise in the average number of cases per day, going from about 65 cases a day during the week of Sept. 22 to Sept. 29, to about 78 cases per day this past week.

The county has alternated between the average number of cases rising and falling for the past five weeks.

During the same time period, the state’s average number of cases per day increased from about 859 cases to 1,054 cases per day. This is the third week in a row that the states’ average has increased. 

As of Monday afternoon, 594 people across the state, 64 of which are in Allegheny County, are currently hospitalized for COVID-19.
