Pittsburgh mayor outlines push for police reforms in wake of George Floyd death

Pittsburgh Mayor Bill Peduto on Thursday called for "white elected officials" to step up as he proposed reforms to police use-of-force policies in the wake of the Memorial Day death of George Floyd while in police custody.

"Now is the time to show your support," Mr. Peduto directed at state lawmakers during remarks at a press conference outside the City-County Building Downtown. "We are calling on every other white elected official to support and sponsor these bills.

"Being quiet is not where the state needs to be."

Mr. Peduto was referring to the use-of-force bill introduced by Democratic state legislators last year governing use of deadly force during arrests. The bill never made it to a vote, however.

The mayor's office also highlighted its "8 Can't Wait" campaign, which calls for:

• Officers to de-escalate situations before using force

• Have force matrix in use of force policy, defining weapons/types of force

• Officers to give verbal warning before deadly force

• Explicitly prohibit chokeholds and strangleholds or limit these tactics

• Prohibits officers from shooting at people in moving vehicles unless person is deadly threat

• Officers to exhaust all other reasonable alternatives before using deadly force

• Officers to intervene to stop another officer from using excessive force

• Officers to report all uses of force, including threatening a civilian with a firearm

Mr. Peduto also said he supports annual implicit bias and de-escalation training for city police. Pittsburgh officers already undergo such training, but the mayor said he would like to see it done “more regularly.”
