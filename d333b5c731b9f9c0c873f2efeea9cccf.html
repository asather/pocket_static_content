Business notes

Feng Xiong, assistant professor of electrical and computer engineering at the University of Pittsburgh’s Swanson School of Engineering, is collaborating with Duke University’s Yiran Chen to develop a two-dimensional synaptic array that will allow computers to do this work with less power and greater speed. Mr. Xiong has received a $300,000 award from the National Science Foundation for this project.

Cozen O’Connor represented United Hydrogen Group Inc., a Pittsburgh-based, vertically integrated supplier of hydrogen to the automotive fuel cell and industrial markets throughout the United States, in its sale to Plug Power Inc., a provider of hydrogen engines and fueling solutions enabling e-mobility. Jeffrey A. Mills led the Cozen O’Connor team on the transaction. The team included Anna M. McDonough, C. Gregory Patton, Brock McCandless, Derek T. Luke, James W. Forsyth, Jesse Loffler and James F. Van Orden. Plug Power was represented by Goodwin Procter LLP.
