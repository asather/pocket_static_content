PWSA expands free lead line replacement program

The Pittsburgh Water and Sewer Authority said Monday that it will expand its free lead line replacement program by increasing the income-based limit for those who qualify.

The PWSA said it will increase the limit to 300% of the federal poverty level from the 250% that it was previously operating under.

To qualify for the program, here are some income limits for individual households:

If one person lives in the household, income may not exceed $38,280

If two people live in the household, income may not exceed $51,720

If three people live in the household, income my not exceed $65,160

If four people live in the household, income my not exceed $78,600

If five people live in the household, income my not exceed $92,040

PWSA says it has spent only $800,000 of the $1.8 million set aside for the free lead line replacement program.

Anyone interested in the program should call Dollar Energy at 866-762-2348 to begin the process.
