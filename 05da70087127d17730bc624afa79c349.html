Car crash in Hampton kills driver, injures another

The Allegheny County Police Department’s Homicide Unit is investigating a two-vehicle crash Tuesday afternoon that killed a driver and closed a portion of state Route 8 in Hampton, according to county police.

The collision between a gray 2008 BMW sedan and a blue 2007 Chrysler minivan occurred around 1:30 p.m. in the area of state Route 8 and Mt. Royal Boulevard. The two drivers, injured in the crash, were transported to local hospitals. There were no other vehicle occupants.

The 77-year-old man driving the minivan died at the hospital while the 35-year-old driving the BMW is in stable condition.

An investigation from county collision reconstructionists and homicide detectives showed the BMW was traveling south on state Route 8. A witness said the vehicle was moving erratically.

The BMW, crossing the middle line, struck the minivan at a high rate of speed.

Route 8 is closed from Wildwood Road Extension to Mt. Royal Boulevard, a stretch through the middle of Hampton.

The investigation is ongoing, and police are consulting with the district attorney’s office to determine if any charges are warranted.
