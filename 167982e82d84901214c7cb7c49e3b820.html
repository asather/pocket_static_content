Tenth resident at Kane Glen Hazel has died due to COVID-19

Ten residents have now died from COVID-19 at the Kane Living Center in Glen Hazel, according the the Allegheny County's official website.

The county said as of Monday, 62 residents at the facility have now tested positive for COVID-19. Seven of those patients are hospitalized; 22 patients have recovered from the virus.

Out of 74 staff members at Glen Hazel who tested for COVID-19, 36 have tested positive; four tests are pending.

In Kane’s Ross center, there are no positive resident tests, but one positive staff test. One staff test is pending.

In Kane’s Scott center, there are no confirmed cases within the residents, but one positive staff test. One staff test is pending.

In Kane’s McKeesport center, one resident and one staff member have tested positive for the virus.
