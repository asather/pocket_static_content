Police investigate a road rage incident related to a Fayette County homicide

Pennsylvania State Police in Uniontown are looking for information on a road rage incident prior to a Fayette County homicide.

The incident occurred between 3 and 4 p.m. on May 24 in Georges Township on Georges Fairchance Road, between Swaney’s Roller Rink and a Sunoco gas station that shares space with an Arby’s restaurant, according to a release.

A black Acura Integra passed a black Lincoln MKZ and stopped in the roadway. A white man exited the Acura and approached the Lincoln’s passenger side.

Soon after, the white man returned to the Acura and pulled into the Sunoco/Arby’s parking lot.

Police believe the road rage incident is related to a homicide that occurred that afternoon in a Long John Silver’s parking lot on Morgantown Road in South Union Township, Fayette County.

A victim was found in the parking lot with multiple gun shot wounds around 4 p.m. that day, according to Post-Gazette news partner, KDKA.

The suspect was identified as Justin Rittacco, 21, who was arrested and charged with criminal homicide.

Mr. Rittacco was at the restaurant to drop someone off for work when a black Acura pulled up behind him. The victim, Robert Lukehart, got out of the car and ran up to Mr. Rittacco’s window.

Mr. Rittacco told police he shot Mr. Lukehart, 39, because he approached in an “aggressive manner and felt his life was threatened.”

He fired three rounds, which struck Mr. Lukehart in the chest and arm, and fled the scene.

Witnesses to the road rage incident are  asked to call Trooper Kristen Zelechowski, lead investigator at (724) 415-1013.
