Pittsburgh police seek man suspected of punching horse during May 30 protests

A task force investigating the protest that turned violent near PPG Paints Arena on May 30 is asking for the public’s help in identifying a man they suspect of “striking and pushing a horse.”

The horse was part of the Pittsburgh Police’s Mounted Unit that was called in to disperse the crowd.

The public safety department said the Damage Assessment and Accountability Taskforce, which is made up of local, state and federal agencies, continues to analyze video and photos to identify individuals “who committed criminal acts during the primarily peaceful demonstrations.”

Anyone with information is asked to contact the task force at DAAT@Pittsburghpa.gov, or call (412) 323-7800. Contributors will remain anonymous.
