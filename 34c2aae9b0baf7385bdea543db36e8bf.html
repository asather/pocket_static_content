Disciplinary court denies Tranquilli's request to be paid during suspension

The Court of Judicial Discipline on Thursday denied a request by suspended Allegheny County Common Pleas Judge Mark Tranquilli that he be paid while on suspension.

The board denied his request in a one-line order. Judge John Foradora dissented, as he has before, on the grounds that Judge Tranquilli has not been charged criminally.

Judge Tranquilli had argued previously that his suspension without pay should be reversed because he did not have a chance to defend himself against an ethics complaint filed by the Judicial Conduct Board.

Judge Tranquilli was reassigned to administrative dues in February after multiple allegations of misconduct, including making racist statements about a juror.

Following an investigation into those comments, the conduct board filed a complaint against him with the Court of Judicial Discipline, which suspended him without pay on Aug. 26.
