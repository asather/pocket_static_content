Driver charged in fatal October crash along Tri-Boro Expressway

The driver of a truck that crashed along the Tri-Boro Expressway near Wilmerding and resulted in a man's death has been charged in the incident, Allegheny County police said Thursday.

Police allege that Edward Wharton, 57, was speeding, driving recklessly and under the influence around 8 p.m. on Oct. 20, when he struck a jersey barrier and overturned his 2000 Toyota Tundra.

Mr. Wharton and his passenger, William Guyaux, 55, of North Versailles, were ejected. Mr. Guyaux died soon after arriving at the hospital.

A reconstruction team determined that Mr. Wharton was traveling westbound between 57 and 67 mph in a 45-mph zone when he tried to pass another vehicle and hit the barrier.

The Allegheny County Crime Lab analysis of Mr. Wharton's blood also indicated that levels for an unspecified substance were "above the legal limit," police said.

Mr. Wharton has been charged with homicide by vehicle while DUI, homicide by vehicle, DUI of alcohol or controlled substance and counts related to speeding and reckless driving.

He's being held at the Allegheny County Jail.
