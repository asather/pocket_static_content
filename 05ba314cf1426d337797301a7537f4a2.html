Bethel Park High School warns parents about possible whooping cough threat

School officials confirmed Tuesday that a student at Bethel Park High School has whooping cough.

An email sent to parents from the school warned that their children may have been exposed to the disease.

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention describes the pertussis infection as a “highly contagious respiratory disease” that causes “uncontrollable, violent coughing which often makes it hard to breathe.”

The CDC states that infected people are most contagious for two weeks after the cough begins. The disease spreads person-to-person through coughing or sneezing, or when spending a lot of time near an infected person.

The Allegheny County Health Department has received an average of 86 reports of whooping cough per year since 2010, according to ACHD epidemiologist Dr. Kristen Mertz.

Ms. Mertz added that although the disease is contagious, it is vaccine preventable.

Bethel Park Principal Zeb Jasantye recommended in the email to parents that older children and parents get a Tdap vaccine booster if they haven’t already. 

Mr. Jasantye advised students with cold and cough symptoms to see a doctor and tell them they may have been exposed to whooping cough.

Health department officials said the Allegheny County Health Department is working with the school to control measures and monitor the situation.
