City controller willing to audit PPS device inventory

City Controller Michael Lamb on Thursday said he would be willing to audit the Pittsburgh Public Schools’ supply of devices for students and teachers after a district board member requested the inspection.

School board member Sala Udin asked for the audit because he said he has been unable to get a comprehensive response when he asked the district numerous questions including the number of devices that have been distributed to students and teachers since the COVID-19 pandemic forced schools to close.

Superintendent Anthony Hamlet has said that every student and teacher should be equipped with a district-owned device by the start of the 2020-21 school year, but it still remains a work in progress. The district has purchased thousands of Chromebook laptops and iPads and has received hundreds more through donations. 

The ability for all students and teachers to have a device with access to the district’s learning management system, a working camera and an internet connection could become even more important Friday as the school board will vote on a measure that could make the first nine weeks of school totally online. 

“The question is, if you do that, do you have the equipment you need to do distance learning?” Mr. Lamb said in a phone interview.

Mr. Udin made the request of Mr. Lamb after he read an article in the education newsletter Education Week that focused on the thousands of laptops that recently went missing in a South Carolina school district.

“Although I don’t believe the situation pertains to the Pittsburgh, the article did remind me of my frustration in trying to get a comprehensive response from the Pittsburgh Public Schools administration when as I have asked, on several occasions, for a complete report on the number of laptops we started with; the number and location currently on hand; the number that have been distributed to students and to teachers,” Mr. Udin wrote in a June 25 letter to Mr. Lamb.

A district spokeswoman did not provide answers to several questions the Pittsburgh Post-Gazette asked about the district’s device inventory Thursday afternoon.

Mr. Lamb said his office was willing and able to do an audit, but he said he needed cooperation from the district in order to do so.

“I don’t have the same kind of authority I do with the city,” he said.

Mr. Lamb said he has a meeting scheduled on Friday with school board President Sylvia Wilson to discuss the matter. He has not yet contacted district administration.

Although Mr. Lamb said Mr. Udin had valid questions, he suggested that perhaps an inventory review would be more appropriate than an “all-out” audit.  

“They are good questions,” Mr. Lamb said, “and they are worth asking.”
