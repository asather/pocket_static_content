Port Authority cuts service by 25% starting Wednesday due to low ridership

Due to a decline in ridership of more than 50% during the COVID-19 emergency, Port Authority will reduce service by about 25% beginning Wednesday.

The authority said in a news release it is taking the step “to protect frontline employees while maintaining service for essential trips taken by hospital staff, government officials and emergency responders.

“Our region is speaking with one voice: If you don’t need to go out, please stay home,” said authority CEO Katharine Eagan Kelleman.

The agency is reducing service with a “modified” schedule, which is less service than normal but not developed Saturday, Sunday or holiday service. This schedule was specifically developed to deal with the current situation, spokesman Adam Brandolph said.

Ridership has been down “significantly” for about two weeks because of the number of people who have been laid off of their jobs or are working from home.

The authority operates 98 routes and 720 buses during a regular day. It will continue to operate all routes, but it will use less than the full allotment of buses.

The authority said it developed the new schedule in conjunction with the Amalgamated Transit Union and healthcare providers to try to make sure essential employees can get to work

In addition, the agency said operating less buses will make it easier to sanitize buses.

The new schedules are available on the agency’s website.  

The agency will maintain its usual Saturday and Sunday schedules.
