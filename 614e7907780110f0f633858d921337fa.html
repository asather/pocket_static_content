Celebrating 10 years of Art on the Walls

One of the challenges facing young and mid-career visual artists is finding a gallery l to show their work. The Greater Pittsburgh Arts Council first addressed that need in 2009 when it launched its Art on the Walls exhibition program.

The organization is marking the program’s first decade with an exhibition, “Past, Present, Future,” that debuts from 5:30 to 10 p.m. Friday during the quarterly Gallery Crawl in the Cultural District, Downtown.

After the Gallery Crawl, the exhibition will be open to the public by appointment at GPAC’s office, 810 Penn Ave., seventh floor, Downtown.

All past exhibiting artists were invited to participate in this show.  The 34 artists who responded include Elizabeth Asche Douglas, Lori Hepner, Clayton Merrell, Jane Ogren, Suzie Sparks, Bill Wade and Jian Wang.

Over the last 10 years, work by more than 140 artists has been exhibited, with sales of over $60,000.

Art on the Walls now includes six corporate locations; its goals are to connect businesses to the arts, cultivate new arts patrons and help businesses build lasting collections. The corporate offices include the Allegheny Conference on Community Development, which is hosting the 2020 exhibition and is the program’s longest-standing business client.

Eighteen artworks will be displayed there by local artists Seth Clark, Ashley A. Jones, atiya jones, Richard Kelly, Susan Palmisano and Staycee Pearl.
