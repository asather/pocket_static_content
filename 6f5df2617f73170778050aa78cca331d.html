Carnegie drug dealer caught with $376K in heroin gets 12 years in federal prison

A Carnegie heroin dealer who led police on a high-speed chase on Route 28 in 2015 was sentenced Thursday in federal court to 12 years in prison.

Senior U.S. District Judge Joy Flowers Conti also sentenced William Thompson, 37, to five years probation.

Thompson pleaded guilty in July to possession of heroin with intent to distribute.

Police found $376,000 worth of the drug in a Porsche SUV that Thompson drove on Route 28, sometimes at more than 100 mph, as Pittsburgh detectives chased him on Aug. 7, 2015 after trying to pull him over.

Thompson crashed near the 40th Street Bridge ramp to Millvale and ran from the scene, but was later arrested.

He was driving without a license, prosecutors said.
