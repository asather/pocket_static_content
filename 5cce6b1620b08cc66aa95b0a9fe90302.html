FBI asks for public's help in identifying Pittsburgh graffiti suspect

The FBI is seeking the public’s help in identifying a person who spray-painted graffiti on a federal building during a protest last month in Downtown Pittsburgh.

The male was seen spray-painting “Free Us” and “BLM” on the William S. Moorhead Federal Building on Liberty Avenue after defacing other buildings along Grant Street on May 31, the FBI’s Pittsburgh office said.

The suspect is identified as a tall black male. He was wearing a red-hooded Nike sweatshirt, gray hat, black mask, black pants and red sneakers the day of the incidents.

The FBI is offering a reward of up to $2,500 for information leading to the identification, arrest, and conviction of the individual. Anyone with information is asked to call the FBI at (412) 432-4000.
