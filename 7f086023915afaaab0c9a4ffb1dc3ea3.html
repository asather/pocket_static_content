Pirates' poor season brings heavy TV viewership losses, according to Forbes

Not even the pandemic could save the Pirates from steep viewership losses in 2020.

Forbes reported Monday that the club posted the third-largest decline in local television audience throughout baseball during the truncated 60-game campaign. Average viewership for broadcasts primarily shown on AT&T SportsNet dropped from 70,122 in 2019 to 46,836 this year.

Only the Miami Marlins at 21,082 had worse average viewership, league-wide.

The data comes via Nielsen, which changed its tracking methods this year to better reflect viewership across all platforms, including digital.

Pure television ratings league-wide declined for this reason, as fewer TV households were measured. Total viewership, however, rose 4.2% as Americans generally gobbled up sports content amid advisories that staying home was the surest way to slow the spread of COVID-19.

Of 25 markets tracked by Nielsen, Forbes reported that 14 made viewership gains averaging 36%. Eleven markets bled viewers at a 22% clip. Only the Los Angeles Angels (34%), Seattle Mariners (36%) and Boston Red Sox (54%) bled viewers at an equal or greater rate than the Pirates.

Average viewership across the sport was 102,859 this year compared to 98,707 in 2019.

The Pirates’ losses come after modest ratings gains posted last year, which reversed a trend of decline since the club’s last playoff appearance in 2015. Not much of a surprise, considering they finished with baseball’s worst record at 19-41.
