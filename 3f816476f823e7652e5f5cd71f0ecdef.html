'You're my angel': Ambridge community gathers for vigil to remember 10-year-old boy

Ambridge residents gathered with the family and friends of 10-year-old Thomas “TJ” Jett for a solemn vigil Thursday evening to remember the boy who died after falling into the Ohio River.

“You rest in peace, you rest with God. You’re my angel, and I’ll never forget you. I’ll never stop loving you,” said Thomas’ mother, Janelle Jett.

Megan Clark, a community member, organized the vigil that took place at 11th and Merchant streets in Ambridge.

“I’m a mother myself, and if this would have happened to me I would have been devastated. I can’t imagine what the family is going through,” Ms. Clark said

“My boys went to school with him, and he used to give me hugs every time I saw him at school, so I just felt like I needed to do something for him,” she added. “Our community isn’t super small, but we need to stick together.

Thomas fell into the river Monday after he and two of his brothers and two other children left home to play along the river, police said. He fell in as he was trying to retrieve his shoe.

Several policemen, firefighters and river rescue teams spent many hours searching for Thomas since the incident before finding the boy’s body Wednesday near the Ambridge Sewage Treatment Plant. Pennsylvania State Police brought in special equipment from Philadelphia to help locate the boy.
