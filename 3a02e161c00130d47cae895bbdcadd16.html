Sheetz makes menu changes in response to COVID-19

Sheetz, the Pennsylvania-based convenience store chain, is changing its menu in response to the COVID-19 epidemic, the company announced Monday.  

Effective at 4 p.m. Monday, self-serve coffee has been temporarily suspended. Customers who would like to purchase a cup of coffee should use the touch-screen order points or ask an employee for assistance. The store will also handle fountain beverages, fresh brewed tea, frozen drinks and Made-to-Order ® premium beverages such as coffee, smoothies and more through its touch-screen order points.

Sheetz has also temporarily suspended availability of self-serve bakery items, such as doughnuts and muffins.

The company said expiration dates of its loyalty points, or loaded rewards, have been extended until July 1.

“We apologize for this inconvenience but please know we will continue to put your health first as we remain a vital resource for our communities. We will continue to closely monitor the situation and keep you informed of any additional adjustments we need to make to protect our customers and employees,” Travis Sheetz, president & COO of Sheetz Inc., said in the news release.
