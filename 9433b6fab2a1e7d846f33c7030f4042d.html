Allegheny County reports its 40th case of COVID-19; close to 500 cases in Pa.

Allegheny County said Sunday that is has confirmed its 40th case of COVID-19, a little more than a week after confirming its first case and a day after the county reported the first death because of the virus..

According to a statement, the Allegheny County Health Department “is also aware of a number of additional cases that have not yet been updated in the system and expects these numbers to continue to grow.”

No information was provided about the latest patient.

On Saturday, a patient in their late 60s who was receiving acute care at Jefferson Hospital died as a result of COVID-19.

Allegheny County Executive Rich Fitzgerald announced the county’s first death from the pandemic during a news conference.

The person was a resident of Clairton, Mayor Rich Lattanzi confirmed.

The Allegheny County death represents the second in Pennsylvania.

Washington County cases now stand at five. Officials in the Butler Health System, said in a Saturday release, that it has the county’s first patient with presumed positive results for the novel coronavirus.

No additional cases were reported by 11 a.m. Sunday in Westmoreland (4), Beaver (3) and Erie (1) counties.

State health department officials, meanwhile, said Sunday there have been an additional 108 positive cases of COVID-19 in Pennsylvania through Saturday, bringing the statewide total to 479 cases in 33 counties. All of those people are either in isolation at home or hospitalized.

“Our notable increase in cases over the last few days indicate we need everyone to take COVID-19 seriously,” Secretary of Health Dr. Rachel Levine said in a statement.

“Pennsylvanians have a very important job right now: stay calm, stay home and stay safe. We have seen case counts continue to increase and the best way to prevent the spread of COVID-19 is to stay home.”

To date, two deaths in Pennsylvania have been attributed to the virus.

As part of the state’s response to control spread of the disease, non-life-sustaining businesses have been ordered closed beginning 8 a.m. Monday.

Pennsylvania’s K-12 schools are already closed and organizers have been urged to postpone any in-person events that would include 10 or more people. All state correctional facilities have no-visitor policies for now and visitor restrictions are in place for state centers as well as assisted living and personal care homes.
