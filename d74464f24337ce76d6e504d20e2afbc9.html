Allegheny County council approves use of purple paint to designate no trespassing areas

Allegheny County Council on Tuesday approved the use of purple paint markings on trees to keep hunters from trespassing on private property.

The alternative to paper “No Hunting” and “No Trespassing” signs was already state law under Act 103 passed in November 2019, which allows three days a year of Sunday hunting.

But the state legislation doesn’t apply to first- and second-class counties — Philadelphia is the former, Allegheny is the latter — and requires local adoption.

The county ordinance, entitled “Offenses Against Property,” was passed on a 13-1 vote and enacted “to protect the property rights, health, safety and well-being of individuals within the county.”

To qualify as a trespassing notification the purple paint must be applied to trees or fence posts in 8-inch long, 1-inch-wide vertical strips, between 3 and 5 feet above ground level.
