Allegheny County has 12 new COVID-19 deaths, all at nursing homes

There were 12 new deaths reported in Allegheny County on Wednesday, and state health department indicated that all occurred at a nursing home or long-term care home.

The state does not specify the names of homes where deaths occur, and as of 12:20 p.m., no new deaths were reported at any of the Kane Community Living Centers.

In addition to the deaths, there are 19 new cases of COVID-19 in Allegheny County and 25 cases in the rest of Western Pennsylvania.

With the new figures, Allegheny has a total of 1,545 cases — 1,468 confirmed and 77 probable — of COVID-19 since the first case was reported March 14. Allegheny’s totals are the highest in Western Pennsylvania.

A case is probable “when the person displays symptoms of COVID-19 and has had close contact with a confirmed positive case but has not had a positive test themselves,” according to the county. Dr. Levine said that positive antibody tests are being counted as probable cases in the state’s figures as well.

There are now 139 deaths attributed to COVID-19 in Allegheny County — 129 confirmed and 10 probable. The state is reporting that 109 deaths have occurred at nursing or long-term care homes. At 35 facilities in Allegheny, there have been 372 cases of COVID-19 among residents and 104 cases among employees.

 

The county said 285 people, past or present, have required hospitalization. There have been at least 117 people admitted to an intensive care unit, and 67 of them have required mechanical ventilation. So far, there have been at least 21,488 tests administered.

There were no other deaths reported Wednesday in Western Pennsylvania.

Also on Wednesday, the state reported 707 new cases of COVID-19 and 137 deaths related to the disease.

The state has had 58,698 cases and 3,943 deaths from the virus. Philadelphia continues to have the highest totals in the state, with 15,376 cases and 904 deaths.
