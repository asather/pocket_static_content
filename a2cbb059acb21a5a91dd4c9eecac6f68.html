Police, DEA bust pot, psychedelic mushroom grow operation in Stanton Heights house

Pittsburgh police and the Drug Enforcement Administration dismantled what they described as a marijuana and psychedelic mushroom grow operation inside a Stanton Heights house and arrested the three occupants.

Narcotics detectives made the bust Friday around 6 a.m. after executing a search warrant in the 1600 block of Trinity Street.

Police said they found four guns, 72 marijuana plants, psychedelic mushrooms, butane hash oil extract, THC oil and wax and drug paraphernalia. 

Arrested were Robert Douglass, 73; Bruce Douglass, 27; and Quinnton Kouchak, 26. Police were not sure of the relationship between the Douglasses.

Detectives also found two dogs and a pet snake.

Robert Douglass told police that he was the renter and cultivated marijuana and psilocybin, according to a criminal complaint.

There were marijuana grow tents in the basement with lights, air filtration and irrigation systems, police said.

A THC manufacturing station was found on the kitchen counter, the complaint said.

“The large amount of marijuana throughout the house, marijuana plants, loose marijuana, THC liquid, THC wax, psilocybin (hallucinogenic mushrooms), marijuana growing lab stations, multiple weapons including pistols and rifles were all easily accessible by all occupants of the residence,” Detective Anthony Nesser wrote in in the complaint.

“I believe that this indicates that Robert Douglass, Bruce Douglass, and Quinnton Kouchak had knowledge of the ongoing illegal enterprise and conspired amongst each other to grow, cultivate and distribute the illegal narcotics found In the home. The majority of the marijuana and marijuana plants were in the dining room, a common area easily accessible and easily observed by all occupants of the residence.”

Police found the Douglasses when they executed the search warrant. They arrested Mr. Kouchak that night, around 8 p.m., as he loaded things into a car outside the house.

Officers said they found drugs, drug paraphernalia, ammunition and a concealed carry permit. 

All three defendants were arraigned. Bail was denied for Robert Douglass, who has a criminal record in Pennsylvania dating to 1986, and he was taken to the Allegheny County Jail. Bruce Douglass was unable to post $10,000 bail; and Mr. Kouchak was given $5,000 unsecured bond.
