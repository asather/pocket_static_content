Deutschtown Music Festival canceled due to pandemic

The Deutschtown Music Festival, the biggest summer event for the Pittsburgh music scene, has been canceled due to the Covid-19 pandemic.

The eighth annual North Side festival was scheduled for July 17-19.

“It is our responsibility to assure public health -- for the safety of our fans, musicians and venue operators. We feel that having the festival go on with thousands of people gathering for three days would be irresponsible and reckless at this unprecedented time,” said Cody Walters, co-founder of the festival. “As DIY organizers, we have always had the well-being of our participating musicians and attendees at the forefront of everything we do.”

According to the organizers, the 2019 festival, which featured more than 400 acts in 40 indoor and outdoor venues, drew more than 40,000 people.

“Please stay safe and thank the heroes who deliver the goods, stock the shelves, check out your groceries, and especially the first responders and medical professionals who keep us healthy and safe during these trying times,” Walters said.
