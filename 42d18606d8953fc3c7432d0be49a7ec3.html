Pittsburgh NAACP hires new executive director

Pittsburgh’s branch of the NAACP on Wednesday announced the hiring of a new executive director.

Kellie Ware-Seabron, a Pittsburgh native, was hired as executive director on Feb. 1. The executive director is responsible managing the NAACP’s programming, strategic planning, community outreach and fundraising efforts, according to a press release.

Ms. Ware-Seabron previously worked as Pittsburgh City Council’s first Equity, Diversity and Inclusion Policy analyst. She is a graduate of the University of Pittsburgh and Northeastern University School of Law.

“We’re at a crossroads as to what the future holds for black folks in this city,” Ms. Ware-Seabron said in the press release, noting racism is a “public health crisis,” just as city council declared in December.

Ms. Ware-Seabron is set to make her first presentation to the NAACP’s general membership Feb. 11 at 7 p.m at Jeron X. Grayson Center in Pittsburgh’s Crawford Roberts neighborhood.
