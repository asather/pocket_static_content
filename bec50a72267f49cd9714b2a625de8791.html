People on the move: 2/9/2020

People on the Move

Accounting

H2R CPA promoted Cara M. Colavita to partner.

Associations and Organizations

Mj Callaway of Mj Callaway Training & Development was elected co-president of the National Speakers Association Pittsburgh Chapter. 

Mount Washington Community Development Corp. appointed Amanda Abernathy as its volunteers program manager.

Master Builders’ Association appointed Alex Dick to its board. Mr. Dick is chief operating officer of Dick Build Co.

Banking & Financial

Robert Schwartz was named an investment officer in First Commonwealth’s Wealth Services division.

The Federal Reserve Bank of Cleveland announced the following appointments: Dmitri Shiry, managing partner, Deloitte LLP, Pittsburgh, was reappointed chair of its board of directors of the Federal Reserve Bank of Cleveland, Pittsburgh Branch, and was reappointed a director; Shelley Fant, president and chief executive officer, FCG Solutions Inc., and Kathryn Klaber, managing partner, The Klaber Group, were reappointed as directors.

Consulting

Cowden Associates Inc. promoted David A. Weaver to chief actuary.

Laboratories

Hemp Synergistics hired Daniel Kohler as chief executive officer.

Law

Leech Tishman said Sally Griffith Cimini, partner, was named chair of its Alternative Dispute Resolution Practice Group.

Meyer, Unkovic & Scott announced that it has re‑elected Robert E. Dauer Jr. and Christopher P. Smith Jr. to serve on its management committee. Mr. Smith will continue to serve as its managing partner.

Allison R. Brown, Emilie R. Hammerstein and Joshua C. Vaughn, attorneys in the Pittsburgh office of Littler, were elevated to shareholders. Ms. Brown counsels and represents employers in a variety of employment law matters. Ms. Hammerstein represents employers in a wide range of labor and employment law matters, including discrimination, harassment and retaliation charges, and arbitrations. Mr. Vaughn represents employers in collective, class and hybrid actions brought under the federal Fair Labor Standards Act and state wage and hour laws. 

Museums

Carnegie Museum of Art announced additions to its senior leadership team: Chris Fry, director of finance; Stefanie Mohr, director of marketing and engagement; Clarissa Morales, director of collections and exhibitions management; and Jason Segreti, director of visitor services. 

Nonprofit

Joseph Hall was named executive director of Kelly Strayhorn Theater.

ACHIEVA announced that Reid Wolfe, senior vice president and president, ACHIEVA Support, will retire effective May 1 and that Shayne Roos, senior vice president, will succeed Mr. Wolfe in both capacities. Mr. Roos starts his new role May 2.

Awards and Honors

Carlow University will honor six Women of Spirit awardees at its 15th Women of Spirit Awards Gala on April 2 at Carnegie Music Hall in Oakland. The awardees are the Hon. Kim Berkeley Clark, president judge, Fifth Judicial District, Pennsylvania; Kristen S. Kurland, professor of architecture, information systems and public policy, Carnegie Mellon University; Karen L. Larrimer, executive vice president, chief customer officer, head of Retail Banking, PNC Financial Services Group Inc.; Anne V. Lewis, board chair, Oxford Development Co.; Nancy D. Washington, educator and civic leader; and Sister Susan Welsh, president and CEO (retired), Pittsburgh Mercy Health System. 

VisitPITTSBURGH said its marketing department was named the PR Team of the Year at the annual Public Relations Society of America’s Renaissance Awards held Jan. 30 at Point Park University’s Pittsburgh Playhouse. Additionally, the successful “Pull Up a Chair. You Are Welcomed Here” national campaign was honored with a Digital Renaissance Award.

Business notes

The Press Club of Western Pennsylvania has extended the deadline for applications for its 14th annual $5,000 Bob Fryer Memorial Scholarship, as well as the $2,500 Press Club of Western Pennsylvania Scholarship awarded to aspiring journalists. The awards are designed to encourage outstanding undergraduate journalism students in print, broadcast and multimedia. The new deadline is Feb. 29. The scholarships will be awarded on May 27 at the Rivers Casino during the Press Club’s annual Golden Quill Awards dinner. Candidates must be current sophomores or juniors enrolled in an accredited college or university. Their primary residence must be in one of the 29 counties of Western Pennsylvania. An application can be downloaded from www.westernpapressclub.org or contact The Press Club of Western Pennsylvania at 412-281-7778.

The Stepping Stones Group, a national provider of therapeutic, behavioral and autism services to children in educational and community settings, announced the acquisition of STAR of CA, a California-based provider of home, community and school-based applied behavioral analysis and mental health services.

JLL Capital Markets closed the sale of certain subsidiaries of Columbia Property Trust that own the Westinghouse Electric Co. headquarters, an 823,979-square-foot Class A corporate headquarters building in Cranberry. JLL represented the seller, Columbia Property Trust, and procured the buyer.
