Parks conservancy closing facilities and canceling programs

The Pittsburgh Parks Conservancy is closing facilities and canceling all programs and special events through the end of March in response to the COVID-19 pandemic.

All conservancy facilities will close to staff and visitors through the end of day March 30, 2020, a release from the organization said. A “tentative reopening” is scheduled for March 31. Closed facilities include the Frick Environmental Center, the Parks Conservancy South Side offices, the Schenley Plaza maintenance building and the horticultural and forestry facility on English Lane.

The Porch, Asia Tea House and Mesa, all located at Schenley Plaza, will remain open, the release reads.

The first presumed positive case of COVID-19 in Western Pennsylvania is in Washington County, the health department announced Friday. There are no confirmed cases in Allegheny County.
