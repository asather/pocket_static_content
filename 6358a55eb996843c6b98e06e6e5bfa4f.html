VA Pittsburgh reports second patient death from COVID-19

The U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs reported a second patient has died from the COVID-19 virus in Pittsburgh, according to the department’s latest online statistics Wednesday.

The patient death occurred at the Pittsburgh VA Medical Center in Oakland. The University Drive hospital reported its first death due to COVID-19 on Friday and its first confirmed case March 26.

 

The VA’s national database showed that there are currently 26 patients at the Pittsburgh facility who have tested positive for COVID-19.

Also, the VA’s nationwide statistics show that more than 5,000 patients have tested positive across the country and that more than 300 patients have died.

VA facilities in New Orleans have been the hardest hit, with at least 436 positive cases and at least 30 deaths.
