Allegheny County reports 0 new cases and 3 deaths from COVID-19

There were no new positive cases and three new COVID-19 deaths reported Wednesday in Allegheny County, according to the county health department.

With the new deaths, the Allegheny has had 177 fatalities — 165 are confirmed and 12 are probable. Two other deaths — one each in Butler and Erie counties — were reported in Western Pennsylvania. Those counties now have 13 and nine deaths, respectively.

Allegheny County also reported no new positive cases of the respiratory disease. Since the first case was reported in Allegheny County on March 14, it has had 2,113 positive cases — 1,979 are confirmed and 134 are probable.

The total number of hospitalizations reported by Allegheny County throughout the pandemic is 358. Of them, 141 required intensive care and at least 70 needed a mechanical ventilator.

The state on Wednesday said it had 42 new deaths and 335 new positive cases of COVID-19 reported in Pennsylvania, the state health department said.

With the state’s new figures, it has reported a total of 6,319 deaths and 79,818 positive cases.
