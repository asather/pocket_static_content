People on the move: 3/9/2020

Architectural 

MossArchitects promoted Gina Konopack to associate. 

Associations & Organizations

Allegheny Regional Asset District elected its 2020 officers: chair: Dusty Elias Kirk; vice chair: Daniel J. Griffin; and secretary/treasurer: Sylvia Fields. In addition, the following committee and special assignments: Allocations Committee: Hilda Pang Fu, Daniel J. Griffin and Anthony Ross; Library Committee: Jackie Dixon, Daniel J. Griffin and Dusty Elias Kirk; Project Review Committee: Sylvia Fields, Hilda Pang Fu and Daniel Rosen; Audit Committee: Dusty Elias Kirk and Anthony Ross; Advisory Board Liaison: Jackie Dixon; and Gender Equity Task Force: Sylvia Fields and Daniel Rosen. 

Education

Kellee Van Aken, associate professor of theater and chair of the Theatre and Dance Department at Seton Hill University, was appointed dean of the School of Visual and Performing Arts.

Health

Heritage Valley Health System named Robert (Bob) Rosenberger vice president and chief financial officer.

Law

Sherrard, German & Kelly P.C. promoted Nicholas L. Fiske to shareholder; Richard W. Kelly Jr. to shareholder and director; and David J. Hall, Amy D. Rees and Brandon T. Uram to senior associates. Mr. Fiske is a member of its litigation and employment services groups. Mr. Kelly is a member of its real estate, litigation and construction services groups. Mr. Hall is a member of its energy and natural resources, financial and real estate services groups. Ms. Rees is a member of its estates and trusts and tax services groups. Mr. Uram is a member of its corporate, real estate, construction, energy and natural resources and litigation services groups.

Katherine Gafner, Ragae Ghabrail and David Osipovich were named partners at K&L Gates LLP. Ms. Gafner advises energy clients on litigation and appellate matters, government enforcement, real estate, crisis management, and oil and gas transactions. Mr. Ghabrail focuses his patent practice on post-grant proceedings, patent procurement and strategic portfolio management. Mr. Osipovich represents clients on high-stakes complex commercial disputes, as well as insurance recovery matters.

Manufacturing

Industrial Scientific hired Marc Osgoodby as vice president of global sales.

Tom Maziarz was appointed vice president, automotive refinish, Americas, at PPG. 

Media

Entercom named Kevin Battle as morning show co-host for News Radio 1020 KDKA (KDKA-AM) in Pittsburgh. Mr. Battle will join the “The KDKA Radio Morning News With Larry Richert,” weekdays from 5 to 9 a.m., effective March 9.

Nonprofit

The Pittsburgh Technology Council said Dave Passavant, CEO of numo LLC, was elected to its board and Dione Graswick, partner at PricewaterhouseCoopers, was appointed to its executive committee.

Professional Services

New Century Careers announced the retirement of co-founder Paul Anselmo, president and CEO, after two decades of service to the region’s manufacturing sector. Neil Ashbaugh, currently director of services, was named to succeed Mr. Anselmo.

Real Estate

Coldwell Banker Real Estate Services named John Perry regional vice president in the Greater Pittsburgh region, Dale Swanton sales director of the North Hills office, and Brian Bansemer was promoted to South Suburban sales director.

Technology

Evoqua Water Technologies appointed Lisa Glatch to its board. Ms. Glatch is chief operating officer of Sempra LNG, an affiliate of Sempra Energy.

Elsewhere

First Catholic Slovak Ladies Association of the United States of America re-elected Pittsburgh native Cynthia M. Maleski to her third term as national president (CEO).

Business notes

Franklin Madison Advisors, a new financial planning firm founded by Peter Donisanu, chief financial strategist and president, launched with the help of XY Planning Network the nation’s leading organization of fee-only financial planners serving Generation X and Generation Y clients.

The New Castle Community YMCA changed its name to the Lawrence County YMCA.

Awards and Honors

Ashley E. Horton, attorney, Burns White, was selected as a 2020 Fellow by the Leadership Council on Legal Diversity. Ms. Horton will join a group of young attorneys carefully chosen to participate in a landmark yearlong program created by the LCLD to identify, train and advance the next generation of leaders in the legal profession.

A project developed at the University of Pittsburgh’s Swanson School of Engineering was selected for the American-Made Solar Prize, a U.S. Department of Energy competition designed to incentivize entrepreneurs toward U.S. solar energy innovation and manufacturing. The project, “Durable Antireflective and Self-Cleaning Glass,” is led by Paul W. Leu, Ph.D., professor of industrial engineering, and Sooraj Sharma, a senior studying materials science and engineering who began developing this project in 2018 through the Mascaro Center for Sustainable Innovation  Undergraduate Summer Research Program.

Community College of Allegheny County English professor Stephen Wells received the 2020 Dale P. Parnell Distinguished Faculty Recognition from the American Association of Community Colleges. The award recognizes individuals who make a difference by demonstrating passion, showing a willingness to support students inside and outside of the classroom, participating in college committees, and going above and beyond what is required to ensure students are successful in their academic endeavors.
