FBI confirms 'law enforcement activity' at Ellwood City Medical Center

Pittsburgh FBI agents “conducted law enforcement activity” at Ellwood City Medical Center in Lawrence County on Thursday.

An FBI spokeswoman would not provide further details on the nature of the visit but the Ellwood City Ledger newspaper reported that local residents had seen several agents carrying boxes of material out of the facility.

The medical center has been closed since early December after state health officials ordered its emergency department and inpatient services shut down for various equipment and other violations.

Attorney General Josh Shapiro in December filed a petition to force Florida-based Americore Health, which owns Ellwood City Medical Center, to pay the wages, unemployment taxes and pension contributions owed to the 150 or so workers at the Lawrence County facility.

Americore Health, which acquired Ellwood City Medical Center in 2017, has filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection.
