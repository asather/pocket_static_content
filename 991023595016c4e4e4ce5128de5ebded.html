Allegheny County ordered four establishments closed in last week for violations, one reopens

The Allegheny County Health Department has closed four establishments in the last week, citing health violations.

Oddballs at 1505 East Carson St. in the South Side was ordered closed on Sept. 5 due to allowing indoor operations after 11 p.m.; exceeding 25% indoor occupancy; serving alcoholic beverages without meals; lack of face coverings; and failure to maintain physical distancing between patrons.

Oddballs must remain closed for a minimum of a week and a COVID-19 safety plan must be submitted prior to requesting a re-inspection and permission to reopen, according to the health department.

A call to the business went unanswered.

Nerdz/The Garage at 1902 Brownsville Road in Carrick was ordered closed on Sept. 5 due to several violations, including operating without a valid health permit; allowing indoor operations after 11 p.m.; facility operating as a nightclub; lack of face coverings; exceeding 25% indoor occupancy; and failure to maintain physical distancing between patrons.

Owner William Bagley said he’s confused why he is being cited since Nerdz is a computer-repair business. “I would like to know how the health department has jurisdiction over me. We fix computers and smartphones,” Mr. Bagley said.

Time Capsule at 38 Mt. Oliver St. in Allentown was ordered closed on Sept. 5 for several violations, including operating without a valid health permit; allowing indoor operations after 11 p.m.; facility operating as a nightclub; lack of face coverings; and exceeding 25% indoor occupancy.

The health department noted that inspectors were denied entry but observations were made from the entrance. The facility was observed admitting patrons with a cover charge, according to the report. A phone number was not available.

Roundz Lounge at 1814 S. Braddock Ave. in Swissvale was ordered closed on Sept. 2 due to several violations including inadequate refrigeration; evidence of smoking behind the bar; failure to wash, rinse and sanitize kitchenware; sewage on the floor in the customer area; and lack of hand soap at the hand sink. The facility was also cited for lack of face coverings for employees.

The facility was reinspected on Sept. 8 and has since reopened. 

Inspection reports for food facilities in the county are available at webapps.achd.net/Restaurant/.
