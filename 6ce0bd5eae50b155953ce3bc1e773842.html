COVID-19 Update: Pa. reports nearly 500 new cases, 87 more in Pittsburgh region

The state health department on Wednesday reported another day of nearly 500 new cases of COVID-19, with more than 50 additional deaths.

The additional 495 cases brings the new statewide total to 83,191 cases since the pandemic started in March. 

In Western Pennsylvania, 87 more cases were reported. Allegheny County had the highest daily case increase — with 45 — in the Pittsburgh region, and Westmoreland County had the second highest with 11 new cases.

It’s the second time this week that Allegheny County reported 45 additional cases. The number of cases now totals 2,284 cases — 2,146 confirmed and 138 probable, county health officials reported Wednesday. Prior to Monday, the county had not seen 45 new cases in one day since May 7, when Allegheny was still in the state’s red reopening phase.

Allegheny reported five more deaths related to the virus, bringing its total to 184. Of those, 172 are confirmed and 12 probable. The state is reporting 180 deaths for the county, according to figures released Wednesday. For Allegheny, there were also two additional hospitalizations as a result of the COVID-19 disease, bringing the total to 372 past or present hospitalizations.

The county health department will hold its weekly COVID-19 briefing via Facebook Live today at 4 p.m.

Indiana County reported one death, the only other county in Western Pennsylvania where a death occurred since Tuesday, the state said.
