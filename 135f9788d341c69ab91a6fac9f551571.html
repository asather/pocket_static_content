Lawrenceville's Row House Cinema will open online as streaming portal

Row House Cinema — the Lawrenceville movie theater known for arthouse fare, festivals and special events — is closed to customers coming in, but that won't stop it from bringing new movies to you.

Films that were to open there as Pittsburgh in-theater premieres already may be available through streaming services. But by buying a ticket through Row House, the cinema receives 50% of all ticket sales, “which will be an amazing way to help us — and our employees — survive while being shut down,” owner Brian Mendelssohn said.

Starting Saturday afternoon, tickets can be purchased at the Row House website for $12, and movies "can be seen on any computer, Roku, AppleTV, etc.," Mr. Mendelssohn said.

Row House is among the movie theaters nationwide partnering with distributors such as At House Convergence and the service Film Movement Plus to stream current releases digitally.

"These films were supposed to be released in theaters over the next couple of months, mostly arthouse titles,” Mendelssohn said. ”The process [for audiences] will be to start on our website, rowhousecinema.com, [where] we will have two ‘screens’ each week."

The first two movies available this way are "Corpus Christi," the Oscar-nominated film from Poland about a criminal who has a spiritual transformation, and "The Wild Goose Lake," a noir crime thriller from China.
