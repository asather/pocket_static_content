Western Pa. reports three deaths, 246 cases of COVID-19

Three new deaths and 246 new cases of COVID-19 were reported Sunday in Western Pennsylvania, according to the state health department.

Allegheny County recorded the region’s highest single-day total with 138 new positive cases. Washington had the second-highest total with 19 new cases. Westmoreland had 18 and Armstrong reported 17.

There also were three new deaths reported in Western Pennsylvania with one each in Allegheny, Mercer and Washington counties.

With the new data, Allegheny County has reported 6,263 positive cases — 6,070 confirmed and 193 probable — since its first case was reported on March 14. Westmoreland and Beaver Counties have 1,153 and 958, respectively. 

Allegheny’s latest cases came from 1,895 test results. The county said the cases range in age from 11 months to 93 years with a median of 40.

Allegheny also has reported 208 deaths. The county said the latest death was an 82-year-old who died on July 18.

The county has had 508 hospitalizations as a result of the viral disease, with 174 needing treatment in the intensive care unit and 81 of them requiring a mechanical ventilator.

Statewide, there were 786 new COVID-19 cases reported, giving Pennsylvania a total of 101,027 cases.  Eight new deaths were reported in the state for a new total of 7,015.
