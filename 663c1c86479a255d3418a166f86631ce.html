Penn State announces first student death from COVID-19

A 21-year-old Penn State student has died from respiratory failure related to COVID-19, the first known student death at the school from the virus, the university announced Thursday morning.

Juan Garcia, a student of Penn State’s College of Earth and Mineral Sciences, fell ill on June 19 and tested positive for coronavirus on June 20 after traveling home to Allentown, according to a university statement. Mr. Garcia, who lived in State College, died on Tuesday.

“We are profoundly saddened to learn about Juan’s untimely death during this pandemic,” said Damon Sims, the university’s vice president for Student Affairs, in a statement.

“While I did not know Juan personally, we have learned through conversations with those closest to him that this young man had a remarkable spirit and was greatly loved,” Mr. Sims said. “I know our entire campus community sends our deepest condolences to his family and friends as they grieve this unthinkable loss. It is a poignant reminder that no one among us is immune to the worst consequences of this virus.”

Penn State officials are working to trace those who may have been in contact with Mr. Garcia while he was contagious. According to the Centre Daily Times, it is not known whether or not Mr. Garcia had underlying medical problems.

As of June 26, less than five people in the state aged 20 to 24 have died from the viral infection, according to data from the Pennsylvania Department of Health.
