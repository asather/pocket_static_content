Mars' Ellie Coffield named Pennsylvania soccer player of year

The Mars High School girls soccer team won a PIAA championship this past season, and one of the Planets has been named the top player in the state.

Ellie Coffield, a junior midfielder, has been selected the Gatorade Pennsylvania Player of the Year. Although soccer in Pennsylvania is played in the fall, Gatorade waits until the spring to name a player of the year for each state.

Coffield was one of the main reasons Mars won WPIAL and PIAA Class 3A championships. She scored 31 goals and had 30 assists for an unbeaten team. Mars finished 22-0-1 and gave up only 10 goals all season. Coffield was also named to the Post-Gazette 10-player All-Area team. She was part of a potent one-two punch for the Planets. Taylor Hamlett scored 43 goals and was named the Post-Gazette Player of the Year.  

Coffield has 71 career goals and 65 assists. She has a grade-point average above 4.0 and already has made a verbal commitment to Pitt.

Coffield’s selection means Western Pa. swept both the boys and girls Gatorade awards this year. Quaker Valley’s Dom Reiter was named the boys Player of the Year last week.
