Duquesne Heights man arrested for allegedly placing explosives in Downtown

A Pittsburgh man was arrested on Thursday after he allegedly planted a backpack full of explosives in Downtown.

According to a criminal complaint, Matthew Michanowicz, 52, of Duquesne Heights was seen on surveillance video placing a backpack near bushes at 2 PNC Plaza on Sunday.

Police were alerted to the suspicious package on Monday and found a military-style backpack with makeshift explosives. When questioned by police, Mr. Michanowicz said he was downtown to see the property damage that followed Saturdayâ€™s protests, according to the complaint.

Detectives showed Mr. Michanowicz photo and video of him placing the backpack, the complaint reads. Mr. Michanowicz told officers he did not have a backpack on him.

He is charged with six felonies including possessing weapons of mass destruction. A preliminary hearing for Mr. Michanowicz is set for July 4.
