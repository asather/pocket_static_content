Woman wanted after escape from Pittsburgh Community Corrections Center

State police are searching for a woman who signed herself out of a halfway house Monday and didn’t come back, officials said.

Lindsay Maples, 31, is wanted for escape after she signed out of the Pittsburgh Community Corrections Center, a medium-security state prison in Shadyside. She is serving time for theft and “has an extensive criminal history,” according to a tweet from state police.

Maples is the second person to escape the corrections center this month. Amber Ellifritz signed out of the halfway house Feb. 12 and didn’t return. Court records show she is still wanted for escape.

Ellifritz was serving time for device fraud, which involved theft by using an access device — like a debit or credit card — that is illegitimate.

State police say anyone with information can contact them at 412-299-1607.
