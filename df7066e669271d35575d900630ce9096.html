Gov. Wolf orders flags flown at half staff in honor of 9/11

Gov. Tom Wolf ordered all U.S. and Commonwealth flags on state buildings to be lowered to half staff on Friday in honor of the victims of 9/11.

The governor’s office said the U.S. flag should remain lowered until sunset. The state flag has been flying at half staff since March 11 to honor the victims of the COVID-19 pandemic.

“We commemorate the lives lost during the tragic events of September 11, 2001,” Mr. Wolf said. “May their bravery and sacrifice remind us to value our communities and the bonds between every American, and to seek ways to serve one another.”

All Pennsylvanians are invited to participate in the tribute.

A special ceremony is planned this morning in Stonycreek Township, Somerset County, at the Flight 93 National Memorial, the site of the United Airlines plane crash on Sept. 11, 2001. A total of 40 passengers and crew died while thwarting a terrorist attack in Washington, D.C. The Boeing 757 was headed from Newark, N.J., to San Francisco when it made an abrupt turn near Cleveland and veered back east across Pennsylvania.

Flight 93 was one of four planes that terrorists hijacked on 9/11, including two that struck the World Trade Center in New York City and one that crashed into the Pentagon.

In all, nearly 3,000 people were killed.
