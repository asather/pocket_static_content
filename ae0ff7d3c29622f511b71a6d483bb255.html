Health department closes Rocky's in Bloomfield; posts alert at Sarafino's in Crafton

Rocky’s diner on Liberty Avenue in Bloomfield was shut down by health inspectors for failing to correct food safety violations.

“Owner failed to address the need for commercial refrigeration by the date determined during pre-suspension hearing,” according to the inspection report from the Allegheny County Health Department.

The restaurant, which must remain closed until proper refrigeration is installed, also was cited for lack of a certified food protection manager.

The operator of Rocky’s could not immediately be reached for comment.

Late last week, the health department posted a consumer alert at Sarafino’s restaurant on Crafton Avenue in Crafton for numerous health code violations, including foods held at unsafe temperatures, improper cooling procedures, lack of a food protection manager, lack of a hand sink at the server station and bare hand contact with ready-to-eat food.

The alert was lifted on Tuesday after fixes were made.
