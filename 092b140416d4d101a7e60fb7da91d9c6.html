A raccoon found in White Oak has tested positive for the rabies virus, the Allegheny County Health Department said Friday.

The infected raccoon was picked up near Jacks Run Road and White Oak Park.

It is the first animal in the county to test positive for rabies in 2020. Last year, 25 animals tested positive for rabies in the county. They included 18 raccoons, five bats and two cats, health officials said.

 

Residents are advised to avoid wild and stray animals to prevent exposure to rabies, and to make sure pets are up-to-date on their rabies vaccinations. If an animal is suspected of having rabies, residents should notify their local animal control service, the police or the Pennsylvania Game Commission.

Rabies is transmitted by an animal bite or scratch. Anyone bitten or exposed to the saliva of a wild animal is advised to seek emergency medical treatment and call the Allegheny County Health Department (412-687-2243) to report the incident.
