Man gets probation for daughter's accidental drowning

A Plum man pleaded guilty Wednesday to endangering the welfare of children stemming from the Aug. 1 drowning death of his 3-year-old daughter.

Charles Mitchell, 28, left his daughter and 4-year-old son unattended for about five minutes at the pool at the home on Ridgewood Court around 8 p.m., police said.

The girl fell into the deep end, and when Mitchell returned to the pool, he found her at the bottom. His son was playing in the shallow end.

Mitchell was ordered by Allegheny County Common Pleas Judge Thomas E. Flaherty to serve five years probation and continue treatment through Mercy Behavioral health.
