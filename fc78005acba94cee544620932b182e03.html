Our favorite new furniture at the High Point Market

HIGH POINT, N.C. — Everything that debuts at the furniture markets in High Point isn’t awesome, but there are plenty of truly stunning pieces that deserve a moment.  Here are a few large and small statement pieces that caught our attention at the Fall Market. Look for them in local showrooms this spring.

Matthew Frederick found that interior designers didn’t want to buy furnishings with another designer’s name on it. “So I came up with a name I felt reflected the brand and had a certain elegance and timelessness to it and Browne & Moore was born.”  

The Browne & Moore Collection manufactured by EJ Victor debuted at the Fall Market in October. One piece that drew lots of attention from buyers was the Double Abbey secretary.  “It has its DNA in Biedermeier design from the pediment to the feet to the overall silhouette,” said the designer.  

Mr. Frederick made the mid-1800s look relevant with a Shaker detail on the doors, custom hardware and outlets inside for computers and TVs. It could even be used as a bar, and it is made of maple with many paint and stain options.

“I think the painted finish gives it a lot of options for customization,” he said.

The signature piece was shown in a light gray with a bright Hermes orange interior. EJ Victor is available locally at Weisshouse Furniture.

Following in the footsteps of her famous father, Mark, Alexa Hampton displayed her 120-piece collection for Theodore Alexander. Her love of the dramatic was evident in the deep red walls at the entrance to her showroom, where she gave visitors mini tutorials on her style. 

“I love good metal detail. I wanted the collection to be incredibly eclectic so you will see things that are Regency inspired to Mughal Indian architecture and a French-inspired bed,” said Ms. Hampton.

She believes people love to be surrounded by a wide array of styles. “None of us lives in a museum, especially not Americans. That is the benefit of American design. It is a mix of what we love.”

Highland House showed off its new Catbird chair fully upholstered in a digitally printed fabric; it’s available in a variety of fabrics.

“We just took a small document and printed it,” explained creative director Lee Belmore. 

Highland House Furniture and Theodore Alexander furniture can be purchased through the Antiquarian Shop in Sewickley.

Four Hands introduced the Tracey Boyd Collection with the Trefoil sideboard, featuring an engineered oak veneer marquetry with a cross-hatch pattern.

“I find inspiration from the environment around me,” said Ms. Boyd. “If something stands out, I'll focus on that and draw ideas around it.

“I turned to my love of nature with lots of inspiration coming from a beautiful clover field at my country cottage. Examining the different shapes, colors and textures, the piece really came together to showcase the unique and natural patterns around us."

For Century Furniture, Thomas O’Brien added a few new pieces to his established collection, including the popular Compass side table.     

“Thomas O’Brien’s Compass table is a fantastic conversation piece to add to any room,” said Jeff Lenchner, president of Today’s Home.

Four Hands and Century collections are available locally at Today’s Home and other stores.

Another smaller more adaptable item came from Roberta Schilling.  The upholstered  Moove stool with a walnut base and handle can be easily transported to where it is needed for seating or to hold a tray.
