Seven Pitt players miss season opener due to 'COVID-related protocols'

As Pitt prepared to begin its 2020 football season Saturday, it had do so with reminders of the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic all around it, none of which are more obvious than playing in an empty stadium.

Such signs of the times extend even to the Panthers’ roster.

Pitt had seven players missing for the team’s game against Austin Peay due to what the university described as “COVID-related protocols.”

Earlier in the day, standout defensive lineman Rashad Weaver sent a pair of tweets expressing frustration with the testing process, noting that two companies in two separate tests had found he was negative, but that a third company, which tested him twice, found he was positive in both instances, with the first coming Sunday and the second Thursday.

“It’s not adding up,” he wrote. “Let’s call it spade for spade. Company 1 the common denominator. They playing with my money.”

In a later tweet, Weaver, who missed all of last season after tearing his ACL, said it was “no big deal” to miss another game before adding “see y’all next week!”

The Panthers completed their third and final round of COVID-19 testing Friday, with coach Pat Narduzzi saying Thursday that he and his program “feel good” about where they were at following the most recent round of testing.

In accordance with an ACC mandate, Pitt is undergoing nasal swab tests three times a week. On Friday, Pitt players and coaches were tested twice, with staff members from MAKO Medical, a lab in North Carolina, going up the left and right nostril with a nasal swab, putting it in a vial and closing it before repeating the procedure.

Pitt’s athletic department has not disclosed data related to COVID-19 cases since athletes returned to campus, though the university has a dashboard that provides an overview of cases on campus. That dashboard is updated twice weekly.
