Steelers' 2020 training camp roster at a glance

Steelers training camp 2020 effectively began Monday afternoon with full-contact practice in full pads and is now open to media, as well as including portions live-streamed for fans at home on the team website. Here’s a look at the 80-man roster that will eventually be whittled down to 53 as the Steelers get into their most intense preparations for the season opener Sept. 14 at the New York Giants:

Number, Name, Position, Height, Wight, Experience*, College, Skinny

*-players designated as 1st year have practice squad experience but zero full seasons on active roster

2 Mason Rudolph: QB, 6-5, 235, 2nd year, Oklahoma State — Threw 13 TDs to 9 INTs last season but also got benched.

4 Jordan Berry: P, 6-5, 195, 6th year, Eastern Kentucky — Will have to hold off another undrafted rookie to keep his spot.

5 Paxton Lynch: QB, 6-7, 244, 3rd year, Memphis — Not-too-long-ago first-rounder will push for No. 3 job.

6 Devlin Hodges: QB, 6-1, 210, 2nd year, Samford — ‘Duck’ hopes to hang around after rookie year surprise.

7 Ben Roethlisberger: QB, 6-5, 240, 17th year, Miami (Ohio) — Trying to come back from lost year due to elbow injury.

8 Corliss Waitman: P, 6-2, 210, R, South Alabama — Left-footed Belgium-born boot who sat out last year at Mississippi State.

9 Chris Boswell: K, 6-2, 185, 6th year, Rice — Doesn’t even have a camp leg to challenge him.

10 Ryan Switzer: WR, 5-8, 185, 4th year, North Carolina — Caught 8 passes for 27 yards before back injury.

11 Chase Claypool: WR, 6-4, 238, R, Notre Dame — Top pick has size, speed but missed first offseason.

13 James Washington: WR, 5-11, 213, 3rd year, Oklahoma State — Led team in receiving yards but has competition.

17 Deon Cain: WR, 6-2, 202, 3rd year, Clemson — Midseason pickup averaged 14.4 yards/catch.

18 Diontae Johnson: WR, 5-10, 183, 2nd year, Toledo — Team-best 59 grabs as rookie; can he take next step?

19 JuJu Smith-Schuster: WR, 6-1, 215, 4th year, USC — Much to prove in last year of deal after down season.

20 Cameron Sutton: CB, 5-11, 188, 4th year, Tennessee — Played more than 25% of defensive snaps seven times.

21 John Battle: S, 6-0, 201, 1st year, LSU — Spent last season on Tampa Bay’s practice squad.

21 Wendell Smallwood: RB, 5-10, 208, 4th year: Ran for career-high 79 yards and a TD for Eagles vs. Steelers in 2016.

22 Steven Nelson: CB, 5-11, 194, 6th year, Oregon State — Everyone agrees he’s underrated.

23 Joe Haden: CB, 5-11, 195, 11th year, Florida — One of the best; 5 interceptions, 17 passes defended.

24 Benny Snell Jr.: RB, 5-10, 224, 2nd year, Kentucky — Faced a lot of stacked boxes but ran hard as rookie.

25 Antoine Brooks Jr.: S, 5-11, 210, R, Maryland — Sixth-rounder has hybrid LB/S traits.

26 Anthony McFarland Jr.: RB, 5-8, 193, R, Maryland — Fourth-rounder brought in to be home-run hitter.

26 James Pierre: CB, 6-2, 185, R, Florida Atlantic — Undrafted rookie with good size to play outside.

27 Marcus Allen: S, 6-2, 215, 1st year, Penn State — Has yet to force his way onto the field in some capacity.

28 Mike Hilton: CB, 5-9, 184, 4th year, Mississippi — Career highs in tackles and pass breakups in slot.

30 James Conner: RB, 6-1, 233, 4th year, Pitt — Health is biggest question entering last year of rookie deal.

31 Justin Layne: CB, 6-2, 192, 2nd year, Michigan State — Third-round pick played some special teams in 2019.

33 Trey Edmunds: RB, 6-2, 223, 3rd year, Maryland — Do-it-all special teamer pulled backfield duty in pinch.

33 Curtis Riley: S, 6-0, 190, 6th year — Newest guy in town brings experience as a deep safety.

34 Terrell Edmunds: S, 6-1, 217, 3rd year, Virginia Tech — Improvement would make fifth-year option easier decision.

35 Alexander Myres: CB, 5-11, 192, 1st year, Houston — Spent nearly all of his rookie season on practice squad.

37 Jordan Dangerfield: S, 5-11, 199, 4th year, Towson — Steady special teamer, backup wants more respect.

38 Jaylen Samuels: RB, 6-0, 225, 3rd year, N.C. State — Pass-catching could give him a boost with Roethlisberger back.

38 Trajan Bandy: DB, 5-9, 186, R, Miami (Fla.) — Feisty UDFA had 3 sacks last year as blitzing corner.

39 Minkah Fitzpatrick: S, 6-1, 207, 3rd year, Alabama — Acquisition last year changed complexion of defense.

40 Kerrith Whyte Jr.: RB, 5-10, 204, 2nd year, Florida Atlantic — 5.1 yards/carry after being signed off Bears practice squad.

41 Robert Spillane: LB, 6-1, 229, 2nd year, Western Michigan — Led NFL in special teams tackles per game.

42 Leo Lewis: LB, 6-2, 245, R, Mississippi State — Undrafted inside LB was four-year starter in college.

44 Derek Watt: FB, 6-2, 234, 5th year, Wisconsin — Roosevelt Nix’s replacement, special teams beast.

48 Bud Dupree: LB, 6-4, 269, 6th year, Kentucky — Back, maybe for the last time, on franchise tag.

49 James Lockhart: LB, 6-2, 263, R, Baylor — Undrafted but productive playing DE last year.

49 Dax Raymond: TE, 6-5, 255, 1st year, Utah State — 3 catches for 22 yards last preseason with Bears.

51 Tuzar Skipper: LB, 6-3, 246, 2nd year, Toledo — Followed Ola Adeniyi as undrafted preseason darling.

53 Maurkice Pouncey: C, 6-4, 304, 11th year, Florida — Some snap troubles in 2019, but unquestioned leader.

54 Ulysees Gilbert III: LB, 6-0, 230, 2nd year, Akron — Would add speed next to Devin Bush if he can step up.

55 Devin Bush: LB, 5-11, 234, 2nd year, Michigan — Might be most important member of defense.

56 Alex Highsmith: LB, 6-4, 242, R, Charlotte — 40 sacks over last two seasons in college.

57 Kameron Canaday: LS, 6-4, 245, 4th year, Portland St. — Team’s lowest-rated Madden player, but snaps well enough.

60 J.C. Hassenauer: C, 6-2, 295, 1st year, Alabama — Never hurts to have another center-capable man in the mix.

61 Stefen Wisniewski: G, 6-3, 305, 10th year, Penn State — Versatile vet from here played every snap in Chiefs’ Super Bowl run.

64 John Keenoy: C, 6-3, 300, 1st year, Western Michigan — Played center alongside Chuks Okorafor in college before XFL.

66 David DeCastro: G, 6-5, 316, 9th year, Stanford — One of the best guards in league rarely misses a down.

67 Calvin Taylor: DL, 6-9, 310, R, Kentucky — Stands out at 6-9 but a raw project who was undrafted.

68 Anthony Coyle: OT, 6-4, 298, 1st year, Fordham — Another XFL signee who has flexibility to kick inside.

69 Kevin Dotson: G, 6-4, 321, R, Louisiana Lafayette — Fourth-rounder is locked into playing guard.

71 Matt Feiler: OL, 6-6, 330, 4th year, Bloomsburg — First full season as starter proved he can play G or RT.

72 Zach Banner: OT, 6-8, 360, 4th year, USC — Lovable giant wants to become a star on the field, too.

73 Carlos Davis: DT, 6-2, 320, R, Nebraska — Seventh-rounder has uphill battle to make team.

74 Christian DiLauro: OT, 6-6, 300, 1st year, Illinois — Practice squad holdover who has legitimate tackle size.

76 Chukwuma Okorafor: OT, 6-6, 320, 3rd year, Western Michigan — Has his best chance yet to win starting job at RT.

77 Derwin Gray: OL, 6-4, 320, 1st year, Maryland — Practice squad mainstay has G/T potential.

78 Alejandro Villanueva: OT, 6-9, 320, 6th year, Army — LT still hasn’t missed game; entering contract year.

79 Jarron Jones: OL, 6-6, 320, 1st year, Notre Dame — Intriguing XFL signee played defense in college.

79 Cavon Walker: DT, 6-2, 278, 1st year, Maryland — Undrafted in 2018 but led XFL with 4.5 sacks.

80 Saeed Blacknall: WR, 6-2, 208, 1st year, Penn State — Ran 4.39 out of college and was a big-play guy in XFL.

81 Zach Gentry: TE, 6-8, 265, 2nd year, Michigan — Fifth-rounder from 2019 is front-runner for No. 3 job.

82 Amara Darboh: WR, 6-2, 215, 3rd year, Michigan — Journeyman ended last season on active roster.

83 Anthony Johnson: WR, 6-2, 220, 1st year, Buffalo — Rudolph’s offseason workout partner signed in January.

85 Eric Ebron: TE, 6-4, 253, 7th year, North Carolina — Splashy free-agent signing has 27 TDs in six years.

87 Kevin Rader: TE, 6-4, 250, 1st year, Youngstown State — Pine-Richland grad showed enough to stay on practice squad.

89 Vance McDonald: TE, 6-4, 267, 8th year, Rice — Didn’t get chance to further rapport with Roethlisberger in 2019.

90 T.J. Watt: LB, 6-4, 252, 4th year, Wisconsin — Had an argument for NFL’s defensive MVP award.

91 Stephon Tuitt: DE, 6-6, 303, 7th year, Notre Dame — Durability is only real concern after hefty extension.

92 Ola Adeniyi: LB, 6-1, 248, 3rd year, Toledo — Played in 16 games in 2019, mostly on special teams.

93 Daniel McCullers: DT, 6-7, 352, 7th year, Tennessee — Still here and still huge with vacancy at NT, his niche.

94 Tyson Alualu: DL, 6-3, 304, 11th year, California — Tuitt’s fill-in in 2019; now must replace Javon Hargrave.

95 Chris Wormley: DL, 6-5, 300, 4th year, Michigan — Trade would be worth it if he can be No. 4 in rotation.

96 Isaiah Buggs: DL, 6-3, 295, 2nd year, Alabama — Faces stiff competition for roster spot he won as a rookie.

97 Cam Heyward: DT, 6-5, 295, 10th year, Ohio State — Huge piece of puzzle on field and in locker room but no new deal yet.

98 Vince Williams: LB, 6-1, 233, 8th year, Florida State — Snap share has dropped but may be needed more now.

99 Henry Mondeaux: DL, 6-4, 280, 1st year, Oregon — Limited physically, but still was stashed on practice squad.
