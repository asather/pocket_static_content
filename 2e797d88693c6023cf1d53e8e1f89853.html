Shutdown concerts are a big hit for Pittsburgh musicians

There’s an almost endless amount of slickly produced material — at least, we hope — to be found on cable and the various streaming sites like Netflix and Hulu.

But there’s still nothing quite like the live experience — even if it’s virtual.

With the social distancing caused by the coronavirus, a number of Pittsburgh musicians, following the cue from Code Orange and Bastard Bearded Irishmen, went live on Facebook over the weekend — some with great success.

Scott Blasey, frontman for The Clarks, did a storyteller session from his basement for “an audience of one” -- his wife on the couch — that drew 135,000 views.

He mixed Clarks songs and the stories behind them in a great-sounding set.

“It was a little odd at first but I got in a nice groove pretty quick,” he says. “Having my wife play Ed McMahon to my Johnny Carson was invaluable. I was genuinely moved and got a little emotional when I saw how many people were watching and realized how much our fans needed that.”

Blasey will be in the same spot, on his Facebook page, on Saturday at 8 p.m. with some new stuff.

“As soon as it ended,” he says, “I started thinking about how to make it better. First thing I did was learn ‘Nobody Told Me’ by John Lennon for this week: Strange days indeed.”

DJ Selecta, who can be found mixing soul, R&B, house, hip-hop and more in clubs and on the Friday evening WYEP show Grand Groove Radio, started what he planned to be a two-hour set at 10 p.m. last Saturday.

Selecta, whose real name is James Scoglietti, ended up spinning for almost six hours, until 3:45 a.m., for almost 25,000 fans. It’s the longest he’s ever gone, he says, without a break in the middle.

He’ll do another “lockdown mix” at 8 p.m. Saturday, as well as a “virtual” UNION party Friday from 3 to 7 p.m. with Blakk Steel and Big Phill.

Justin Sane, of Anti-Flag, hopped on Facebook last Friday afternoon with an acoustic set and an uplifting Q&A with fans that drew more than 41,000 views.

On Wednesday at 8 p.m., another Pittsburgh institution, Joe Grushecky of the Houserockers, will go live from his home with an acoustic set.

“I have a lot of songs and a lot of stories,” Grushecky says.

If you’ve followed his career over the last 40-plus years, you know that.

Saturday was supposed AntiFest night at the Roxian with Anti-Flag topping a bill with The Suicide Machines, Punchline and more.

One of those bands, Pittsburgh’s Swiss Army will do an acoustic live stream on Saturday to replace it.

Watch for Lo Fi Delphi and Liss Victory on Tuesday and Ryan Haynes on Saturday at 5 p.m.

There will be many more. We’ll keep you posted.
