Swearing-in now set for Uniontown treasurer who sued for racial bias

A woman who was elected Uniontown treasurer but claims that the city and two officials blocked her from taking office because she is black will be sworn in next week.

Antoinette Hodge will be installed at a ceremony Monday at 5 p.m. in city council chambers, Uniontown Solicitor Tim Witt said Thursday. Mr. Witt said he confirmed that a $150,000 surety bond secured by Ms. Hodge to protect taxpayers from any possible malfeasance was in good order. That determination, he said, removed the roadblock to Ms. Hodge taking office.

Earlier this week Ms. Hodge sued Uniontown, Councilman Martin Gatti and Clerk Kimberly Marshall in U.S. District Court claiming violations of the First and 14th Amendments. Ms. Hodge, 50, is claiming that Mr. Gatti and Ms. Marshall, who she said are white, conspired against her due to racial discrimination. She was elected in November and was supposed to take office Monday but was prevented from doing so.

In her lawsuit, she claimed that Ms. Marshall delayed providing her with information necessary to take office and told her that “we’ll get you.”

Ms. Hodge accused Mr. Gatti of getting her surety bond canceled and claimed that he referred to her as “colored” in a phone call with the bond surety wholesale agency.

She also said two bond agency employees mentioned race to her in phone calls about her bond being revoked. The agency denied Ms. Hodge’s claims. The defendants did not return requests for comment. Ms. Hodge’s attorney could not be reached Thursday evening.
