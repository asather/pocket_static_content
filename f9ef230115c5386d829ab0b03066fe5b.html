HHS Secretary Azar to visit region Friday, citing health benefits of reopening U.S.

U.S. Health and Human Services Secretary Alex Azar will be in Pittsburgh on Friday to participate in a roundtable discussion with Allegheny Conference members about the challenges of safely getting back to work.

Mr. Azar also plans to meet later with Heritage Valley Beaver Medical Center leaders and Beaver County commissioners regarding their COVID-19 response and what reopening will look like in that area.

Mr. Azar’s visit comes as Gov. Tom Wolf’s administration is easing restrictions related to the COVID-19 pandemic, allowing more businesses to reopen while limiting the risk of a resurgence in infections.

Last week, Mr. Azar argued in the Washington Post that reopening America businesses is a critical health, as well as financial, matter, citing increased rates of suicide and opioid deaths associated with a higher unemployment rate, now near 15% nationally.

“Shortening this economic crisis through a safe reopening could save thousands of lives,” he wrote.

Also, he added in the article, routine diagnostic procedures such as mammograms and colonoscopies that were postponed or canceled in preparation for an onslaught of COVID-19 patients may mean medical problems may not be identified as quickly. Mammograms have decreased by 87%, he wrote, and with more than 1.7 million new cancers diagnosed yearly in the U.S., “we could already have 200,000 or more undiagnosed cancer cases as a result.”

Mr. Azar advocates for a combination of surveillance, broad access to testing and containment of isolated outbreaks as work continues to develop a vaccine, the ultimate goal of the Trump administration’s Operation Warp Speed initiative.

In a statement Thursday, Mr. Azar said, “Reopening our country is about health versus health: balancing the important health benefits of safely reopening and the health costs of staying closed against the health risks of the coronavirus, which Pennsylvania is making progress against every day.”
