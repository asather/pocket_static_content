Fine Wine & Good Spirits sales continue to grow

Pennsylvania Liquor Control Board stores are continuing to ramp up sales of wine and spirits, even though all of them remain closed to customers due to COVID-19. 

At stores offering phone orders and curbside pickup, the purchase limit is now 12 bottles, up from six.

And this week, all but four retail stores across the state — closed to customers since mid-March — will be offering curbside pickup, as the PLCB adds 14 more. Those include stores in Russellton, Allegheny County; Conemaugh, Cambria County; Knox, Clarion County; Curwensville, Clearfield County; and Latrobe, Monessen, Irwin and Avonmore, Westmoreland County. That brings the total to 580 stores offering curbside pickup Monday through Saturday. 

Sales figures continue to trend upwards for curbside sales. After that service was widely expanded last week, sales rose from 66,959 orders for $6.42 million April 20-25 to 233,930 orders for $17.2 million April 27-May 2. 

Sales on the www.finewineandgoodspirits.com online store dipped slightly from 45,979 orders for $4.39 million April 15-21 to 38,048 orders for $3.65 million April 22–28. 

The PLCB says it will provide details this week about store operations in two dozen northwest and north-central counties moving from red to yellow status on Friday per Gov. Tom Wolf’s reopening plan. The plan, detailed this past Friday, did not address state Fine Wine & Good Spirits stores specifically, but said in-person retail in general is “allowable” while curbside and delivery are “preferable.” The administration is to provide more specifics this week. 

Per that reopening plan, the PLCB has resumed sales to some licensees such as bars and restaurants, which may need to order online and have limited access to stores and service centers. Bars and restaurants in yellow-designated counties still are limited to sales to-go.
