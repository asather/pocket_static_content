Man hospitalized with serious burns in East Hills fire

A man was hospitalized for serious burns he received in a fire at an East Hills residence early Tuesday.

Emergency officials responded around 2 a.m. to reports of a man burned in the 8600 block of Bricelyn Street.

The man was found in the basement of the home, according to Pittsburgh police spokesman Maurice Matthews. He was taken to a local hospital in serious condition.

According to Pittsburgh EMS Chief Ron Romano, the fire may have been ignited as the man was smoking.

"I'm not exactly sure, but I believe the man was smoking a cigar and at some point dropped it," the chief said.

Residents were not displaced. The extent of damage caused by the fire was not immediately clear.

Further information was not immediately available.
