Jeff Goldblum, Billy Porter, Don Roy King and CMU alums score Emmy nominations

The Emmy nominations announced Tuesday include a number of people with Pittsburgh ties.

Last year's best actor in a drama series, Pittsburgh native Billy Porter, was again recognized for FX's “Pose.”

West Homestead native Jeff Goldblum was nominated in outstanding hosted non-fiction series or special for the Disney+ show, “The World According to Jeff Goldblum.” Two Carnegie Mellon University alums were nominated: Ted Danson (best actor in a comedy series, NBC's “The Good Place”) and Cherry Jones (guest actress in a drama series, HBO's “Succession”).

Monroeville native and Penn State graduate Don Roy King picked up his 13th nomination for outstanding direction of a variety series (the Eddie Murphy episode of “Saturday Night Live” on NBC). King has won nine times, including last year.

Trent Reznor and Nine Inch Nails collaborator Atticus Ross received two nominations for their music in HBO's “Watchmen” (dramatic score and song music and lyrics). Reznor is a native of Lawrence County.
