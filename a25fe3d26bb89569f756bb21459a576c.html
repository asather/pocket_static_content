Allegheny County reports 2 more COVID-19 deaths; 76 more statewide

Allegheny County health officials reported Tuesday that two more people have died due to COVID-19, as the number of deaths statewide surged to 78 additional deaths.

The new total of six deaths in the county include five people who were hospitalized – one person in their 60s, two in their 70s, one in their 80s, and one in their 90s, officials said. One individual who died was not hospitalized; that person was in their 70s.

Health officials said that of the 689 county cases of the viral disease -— up by 47 from the preceding day — a total of 101 people have needed hospitalization, past or present. That was an increase of nine hospitalizations from Monday’s reported numbers.

In total, the pandemic has killed 240 people throughout Pennsylvania, according to state Department of Health figures released Tuesday. The largest number of deaths in Western Pennsylvania have been reported in Beaver County, where the state reported 9 deaths and 116 confirmed cases; the county coroner, however, has reported 13 deaths.

Other deaths reported in the region were in Cambria County, which reported its first death from the virus (and 7 cases); 2 deaths each were reported in Butler County (102 cases) and Lawrence County (29 cases); and Fayette County had 1 death (and 32 cases), according to state figures.

Statewide, health officials said the number of confirmed COVID-19 cases jumped to 14,559, rising in one day by 1,579. Philadelphia County leads the state in the number of cases with 4,012 and in deaths at 58.

The virus is affecting as many women as it is men in the county, health officials said.

Throughout the rest of Western Pennsylvania, Jefferson County saw its first case; Armstrong County has 18 cases; Clarion, 9; Clearfield, 7; Crawford, 8; Erie, 28; Fayette, 32; Forest, 6; Greene, 15; Indiana, 21; Mercer, 10; Somerset, 7; Venango, 5; Washington, 57; and Westmoreland, 177.

Gov. Tom Wolf last week extended his stay-at-home order to all 12.8 million people in Pennsylvania amid a growing number of COVID-19 cases and deaths.

The governor and state Health Secretary Dr. Rachel Levine also are asking all residents to wear a cloth or homemade mask whenever leaving the home. Both emphasized that mask-wearing does not change the primary social distancing guidance, to stay at home whenever possible and maintain 6 feet of distance from other people whenever in public. Mask-wearing in public, they said, should only be for “life-sustaining” excursions from the home, such as trips to grocery stores, pharmacies and to take care of the elderly.

“The continued rise in cases combined with our increasing deaths from COVID-19 reflects the seriousness of this situation,” Dr. Levine said. “We need everyone to listen to the orders in place and to stay calm, stay home and stay safe.

“We know that these prolonged mitigation effects have been difficult for everyone, but it is essential that everyone follows these orders and does not go out unless they absolutely must.”
