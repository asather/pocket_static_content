Shaler teacher charged with identity theft

A Shaler Area Middle School teacher was charged with identity theft after allegedly stealing several of her co-workers’ identifications.

Jeannine Vittorino, 45, was charged Saturday with retail theft, identity theft, corruption of minors and other charges, according to court documents.

Several teachers at Shaler Area Middle began noticing their driver’s licenses went missing after they interacted with Ms. Vittorino, according to a criminal complaint. After one teacher went to a TJ Maxx store to make a return, store employees told her she was banned from making returns at all TJ Maxx stores.

That teacher ran her driver’s license through a software that tracks store returns, and noticed over a dozen suspicious transactions. 

When police checked store surveillance images, they saw multiple instances in which Ms. Vittorino made fraudulent returns, the complaint reads. 

“The Shaler Area School District is cooperating with law enforcement on this matter,” superintendent Sean Aiken said in an emailed statement.

Mr. Aiken would not comment on whether the district had taken disciplinary action against Ms. Vittorino because it is a personnel matter.

Ms. Vittorino’s preliminary hearing is set for Feb. 13.
