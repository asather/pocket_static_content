Trump will hold campaign rally at Pittsburgh International Airport on Sept. 22

For the second time this month, President Donald Trump will hold a campaign rally at an airport in Western Pennsylvania.

Mr. Trump, known to rally his supporters at airport hangars where he can exit Air Force One and take the stage, will campaign at Pittsburgh International Airport in Moon on Sept. 22.

The rally, billed by his reelection campaign as a “Great American Comeback Event,” comes a few weeks after Mr. Trump visited Latrobe for a similar event.

The day of his Moon rally will mark 42 days out from the Nov. 3 election, in which Pennsylvania could play a critical role in the race between Mr. Trump and Democrat Joe Biden.

Mr. Biden hasn’t held a traditional campaign rally in Western Pennsylvania since he launched his bid for the presidency last April, but he’s said publicly that he wouldn’t hold rallies because of the risk of spreading COVID-19.
