Western Pa. counties report no new deaths; State reports 1 death in Allegheny

There were 75 new COVID-19 deaths reported in Pennsylvania on Wednesday, according to figures released by the state Department of Health.

The new cases means Pennsylvania has had 24 straight days in which it has reported less than 1,000 daily cases. The state has had 73,405 positive cases.

It also reported 75 new deaths from the infectious disease, increasing that total to 5,742.

Allegheny County reported no new deaths from COVID-19 and said its total is 152 — 141 are confirmed and 11 are probable. The state, however, said Allegheny County has one new death and listed its total at 166.

No other deaths were reported in Western Pennsylvania.

Allegheny County also reported 24 new cases on Wednesday. Since the county’s first case was reported on March 14, it has had 1,952 total cases — 1,829 are confirmed and 123 are probable.

Allegheny has the highest figures in Western Pennsylvania and is followed by Beaver and Westmoreland counties. Based on the state’s data through Wedneday, Beaver has had 594 cases and 74 deaths and Westmoreland 453 cases and 38 deaths.
