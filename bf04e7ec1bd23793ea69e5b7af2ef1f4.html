Netflix sets December premiere date for Pittsburgh-filmed ‘Ma Rainey’s Black Bottom’

“Ma Rainey’s Black Bottom,” a Netflix film adapted from Pittsburgh playwright August Wilson’s 1984 play, is officially set to premiere on Dec. 18.

Netflix posted on Twitter Wednesday announcing the release date of the film, which features Academy-award winner Viola Davis, who plays singer Ma Rainey, and the late Chadwick Boseman, who plays trumpeter Levee. The production was shot on Pittsburgh’s North Side last summer.

Mr. Wilson’s play was his first to make it to Broadway, which focused on the band members hired to play a recording session for singer Ma Rainey, and then on Rainey herself, who recorded the song “Black Bottom” in 1927 and was known as the Mother of the Blues.

Tony Award winner George C. Wolfe directed the Netflix-adapted film, with its screenplay written by Ruben Santiago-Hudson. Denzel Washington is also a producer on the film.

This film marks Mr. Boseman’s final movie. Mr. Boseman — who also played Black icons Jackie Robinson, James Brown and Marvel’s Black Panther — died at the age of 43 in August after battling colon cancer.

“He did a brilliant job, and he’s gone,” Mr. Washington said about Mr. Boseman in a recent New York Times article. “I still can’t believe it.”

In July 2019, sections of Pittsburgh’s North Side were transformed to replicate Chicago in the late 1920s. West North Avenue exteriors — between Brighton Road and Galveston Avenue — were repainted, restructured and decorated with props to resemble 1927 Chicago, outside the recording studio where the presence of Ma Rainey is anticipated by her session musicians.

In between filming, Mr. Boseman also took time to visit a Steelers practice at Heinz Field on Aug. 4, 2019.
