Brookline man turns himself in on shooting charges

A 44-year-old man who police say shot a man following a verbal dispute Sunday in Brookline turned himself in to authorities Wednesday morning, according to Pittsburgh police.

Edward Phillips, 44, of Brookline, is charged with two felony counts of aggravated assault, carrying a firearm without a license, recklessly endangering another person and driving with a suspended license. Police issued a warrant for Phillips on Sunday. He is currently being held at the Allegheny County Jail.

A preliminary hearing is set for July 28.

An unidentified man was hospitalized in stable condition Sunday after he was shot in the 900 block of Norwich Avenue, according to police. Authorities responded around 12:15 p.m. and found the man with at least one gunshot wound to the left groin area, a criminal complaint said. Phillips is a convicted felon on prior drug charges, court records show.
