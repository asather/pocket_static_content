Biden says he would require wearing masks in public to prevent the spread of COVID-19

Former Vice President Joe Biden said on Thursday that if he were president, he would require that every American wear a mask in public to combat the spread of the coronavirus.

“The one thing we do know is these masks make a gigantic difference,” Mr. Biden said in an interview with Pittsburgh Post-Gazette news partner KDKA-TV. “I would insist that everybody out in public be wearing that mask. Anyone to reopen would have to make sure that they walked into a business that had masks.”

The presumptive Democratic nominee, who was in Lancaster, Pa., for an event on Thursday, suggested he would use executive action or other means to leverage federal power and mandate wearing masks in public.

“I know, as Americans, it’s not something we’re used to. But it matters,” Mr. Biden said of wearing a mask, noting he wears one “everywhere I go.”

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention recommends wearing masks in public areas where social distancing is hard to maintain. On its website, the CDC says that "cloth masks may slow the spread of the virus and help people who may have the virus and do not know it from transmitting it to others."
