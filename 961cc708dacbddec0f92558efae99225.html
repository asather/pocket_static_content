3 new deaths in W. Pa.; Allegheny County's COVID-19 case count surpasses 1,500 mark

There were three new deaths blamed on COVID-19 in Western Pennsylvania, according to the latest figures released Sunday by the state health department.

Two of the deaths were in Allegheny County, and one occurred at nursing home/long-term care facility in Armstrong County.

Allegheny County also reported 17 new cases of COVID-19, according, bringing its total to 1,503 — 1,431 confirmed cases and 72 probable cases — since the first case was reported March 14.

A case is probable “when the person displays symptoms of COVID-19 and has had close contact with a confirmed positive case but has not had a positive test themselves,” according to the county.

Of those Allegheny County deaths, 112 were confirmed by testing to have been positive for COVID-19, while 10 are probable. The county said 273 people past or present have required hospitalization. There have been 116 people admitted to an intensive care unit, and 66 of them have required mechanical ventilation. So far, there have been 20,967 tests given.

On Saturday, Pennsylvania's death toll from COVID-19 jumped by 72 Saturday to 3,688, with one of those deaths reported in Allegheny County.

Statewide there were 1,078 new cases of people infected with the novel coronavirus, bringing the commonwealth's reported total to 55,316.
