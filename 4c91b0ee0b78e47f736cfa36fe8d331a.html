Airport van service SuperShuttle shutting down

SuperShuttle — the shared-van service that has for decades carried passengers to and from airports nationwide and beyond — will cease operations at Pittsburgh International Airport next month.

Created in 1983 to serve Los Angeles International Airport, SuperShuttle has been facing increased competition from ride-hailing services Uber and Lyft. The company had already pulled out of many locations in recent weeks, including at LAX on Dec. 31.

According to the company’s reservations line Monday, SuperShuttle currently serves just a handful of airports besides Pittsburgh: Las Vegas; Austin, Texas; Miami; New Orleans; Cleveland; West Palm Beach, Fla; and Kansas City, Mo. The company also continues to operate in Hawaii and Cancun, Mexico.

SuperShuttle’s last day of operations in Pittsburgh and its remaining markets will be Feb. 12, according to two reservations agents reached by telephone.

SuperShuttle executives could not be reached.

According to a story in the Los Angeles Times last month, a letter sent to a Los Angeles-area franchisee cited “a variety of factors” for its demise, “including increasing costs and changes in the competitive and regulatory landscape [that] have called into question the economic and operational viability of the company’s operations.”
