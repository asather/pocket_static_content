Pierogi Festival returns to Kennywood on Sept. 27

It’s never too early to start planning a pierogi pig-out, right? Especially when said binging involves a trip to an iconic Pittsburgh amusement park.

The Pittsburgh Pierogi Festival is heading back to Kennywood Park in West Mifflin on Sept. 27. In celebration of another day that makes you feel all warm and fuzzy inside — Valentine’s Day — it is offering some special bling for early purchasers when tickets go on sale at 6 a.m. Friday.

Along with an official Pierogi Festival T-shirt, the first 100 people to buy tickets also receive an official pierogi hat and festival bag that will be handed out at the main gate at the festival. The next 900 people to buy will get early bird pricing of $19.99 for ages 13 and up and $9.99 for ages 3-12 through April 12. 

After April 12, advance tickets will cost $24 for adults ($28 at the door) and $12 for kids. Children 2 and under are free. That’s the same price as last year’s festival, which drew nearly 6,000 people.

This is the seventh year for the annual fest, which started relatively tiny at South Shore Riverfront Park in 2013. It runs from 1 to 6 p.m., and parking is free. 

Guests will see the return of the Pierogi Pinching Contest, which last year drew dozens of participants from across the region. There also will be live music, vendors selling pierogi-inspired merchandise, a pierogi toss, opportunities to “Paint Your Own Pierogi” and appearances by Pittsburgh celebs and the famed Pittsburgh Pirates’ racing pierogies.

Ticket holders also will be able to ride a half-dozen classic Kennywood rides along with food from The Potato Patch and Swhinery Barbeque Co.

For tickets, go to Showclix.com and search Pittsburgh Pierogi Festival.
