Robert Morris, Andy Toole agree to contract extension

Robert Morris and men’s basketball coach Andy Toole have agreed to a contract extension that will keep the 39-year-old in charge of the program through the 2025-26 season, sources told the Pittsburgh Post-Gazette.

Toole has a 188-154 record since becoming head coach in 2010, and the Colonials program is currently on an upswing. The team won the Northeast Conference tournament at its new UPMC Events Center last season though, its trip to the NCAA tournament was canceled due to the coronavirus pandemic.

Toole, the NEC coach of the year in 2014, also won two regular-season conference titles in 2013 and 2014 and a conference tournament championship in 2015. That season, Robert Morris advanced out of the NCAA tournament’s First Four in Dayton by beating North Florida before losing to No. 1 seed Duke.

The competition going forward will get tougher for Toole and Robert Morris as they head to the Horizon League for the upcoming season. Toole is well-respected in coaching circles, so locking him down ahead of the step up in conference competition is a wise move for Robert Morris.

CBS Sports first reported Toole’s extension.
