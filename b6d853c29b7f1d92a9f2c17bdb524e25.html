Here's what you'll pay for Rolling Stones tickets at Heinz Field

Flash back to 1969.

The Rolling Stones announce their U.S. tour and Rolling Stone magazine bristles at the price, noting that the $5.50 to $8.50 for tickets far exceeds what The Doors are charging at $3.50 to $6.50.

“I really don’t know whether this is more expensive than recent tours by local bands,” Mick Jagger says in a press conference. “I don’t know how much people can afford. I’ve no idea. Is that a lot? You’ll have to tell me.”

Putting it through the handy U.S. Inflation Calculator, that $5.50 to $8.50 in 1969 would adjust to $38 to $58 in 2020.

When the presale began Wednesday for The Rolling Stones at Heinz Field, we finally learned the ticket price and it was a bit more than that, especially on the high end. Tickets are $61 to $675, plus $45 in fees.

Fans logged on with a presale code to find themselves in waiting queues generally with 2,000-plus other fans — and, surely, ticket brokers. It was taking about a 30 minutes for some people in those 2,000-plus queues to get through it.

The June 23 date, part of the No Filter Tour, will be the legendary British band’s first Pittsburgh show in five years. Tickets go on sale to the general public at 10 a.m. Friday at rollingstones.com.

The No Filter Tour, which began in May 2017, has played to 1,506,259 million fans and grossed $237.8 million.
