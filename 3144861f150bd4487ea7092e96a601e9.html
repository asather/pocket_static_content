What's happening in Pittsburgh (and online) this weekend: June 18-21

FRIDAY

Juneteenth observances

The 2020 Western Pennsylvania Juneteenth Freedom Day Walk, presented online by Stop the Violence PGH, will guide us through historic black spaces on June 19, the date in 1865 when the message of emancipation was delivered in Texas, the last of the states to release its enslaved people.

The Facebook livestream event begins at 1 p.m. Friday, at https://www.facebook.com/events/588557801637171. Historic sites will include: Fort Pitt (1758), Bethel AME Church (1808), Monongahela House (1840), Avery College on East Ohio Street (1849), John C. Peck Oyster House, (1838), John Vashon Bathhouse & Barbershop (1832), Martin Delany Print Office (1846), Little Hayti/Arthurville/Freedom Corner (1835) and Benjamin/Dave Richards Butchery/Negro Tavern (1790).

Speakers are Lawrence Glasco (University of Pittsburgh), Sarah J. Martin (Underground Railroad Historian), Dr. Robert Hill (Pitt), Rev. Dr. Johnnie Monroe (Grace Memorial Church), Samuel W. Black (Heinz History Center), Charlene Foggie-Barnett (Carnegie Museum of Art historian) and Wali A. Jamal (actor/historian).

• June 19 is the anniversary of the death of Antwon Rose II, who at 17 was killed by police during a traffic stop. An event in Antwon’s honor will be held at 10 a.m. at 827 Linden Ave. in East Pittsburgh.

• Magician Eric Jones is taking over as host of Liberty Magic @ Home for a Black Lives Matter episode, featuring the magicians Ran’D Shine and The Evasons for conversation and magic tricks. Discussion topics include cultural appropriation in the history of the performance of magic and social injustice in the world of entertainment. 

Cats on film

Who can resist a cute cat video? Or better yet, lots of them, all in one place!

The “Quarantine Cat Film Fest” will have its virtual cinema debut Friday, featuring the best of more than 1,100 cat videos submitted from around the world.

As a "virtual cinema" initiative,” the “Quarantine Cat Film Fest” is designed to support independent cinemas nationwide. That means you can watch the feature-length feline festivities at home for three days, while about half of the cost of each ticket sold goes directly to local independent theaters.

Tickets purchased in advance at Row House Online, https://rowhousecinema.com benefit the Lawrenceville theater.

SATURDAY

Jazz fest

The Pittsburgh International Jazz Festival presented by the August Wilson African American Cultural Center and Citizens Bank will mark its 10th anniversary with a free event that will stream live at noon on the Center’s website and Facebook page.

It will feature legendary jazz musicians from around the world, including Dianne Reeves, Sean Jones, The Bad Plus, Orrin Evans, Jeff Tain Watts, Joe Locke, Tia Fuller, Laurin Talese, Brett Williams + Kate K-S and Lakecia Benjamin.

There will also be a jam session led by Sean Jones and Pittsburgh-based artists, special tributes, a look back at archival interviews and footage from the Festival’s first 10 years plus an interactive dance party featuring DJ Nate Da Barber and DJ Selecta.

Cindystock 7

Cindystock, the annual outdoor concert held at the home of Cindy and Ted Yates for the Yates Fund for Cancer Hope, will be a virtual event on cindystock.org and on Facebook.

It will feature live performances by Kyshona, Erik Parian, Piper and Carson, Miguel Sague, Dan Navarro and Jay Nash of the Contenders.

It will be donation-based at https:/www.paypal.com/us/fundraiser/charity/77255.

The Yates family notes, “We invite you to bring out a viewing device into your yard, set up your chairs and coolers, pour your favorite beverage and have your own picnic dinner ready to go. Wear your Woodstock gear, and take pictures.”

Funds support five agencies that provide either free cancer screening or support services: Allegheny Health Network community screenings, UPMC colon cancer screening, Adagio Health breast and cervical screenings and support services from Cancer Caring Center and Hair Peace Charities.

SUNDAY

Music making

Make Music Pittsburgh will head to the web to take part in Make Music Day, the worldwide music celebration held each June 21, on the summer solstice. Typically featuring free outdoor performances, lessons, jam sessions and other events in public spaces, this year’s Make Music Day will "take a virtual approach to encourage all people, of all musical abilities, to celebrate the joy of making music."

More than 85 U.S. cities will take part in Make Music Day 2020, an event that began in France in 1982 and has spread to more than 1,000 cities across 120 countries.

Highlights of Make Music Pittsburgh 2020 include: Bash the Trash, Piano and Yoga, community-minded musicianship in the age of COVID-19, interactive drumming and a virtual open mic. A full schedule is at www.makemusicpittsburgh.org. All events will be livestreamed at https://www.facebook.com/makemusicpittsburgh/
