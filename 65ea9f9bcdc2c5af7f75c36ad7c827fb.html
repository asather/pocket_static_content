Oriental Market in Ross hit with consumer alert

The Oriental Market grocery store on McKnight Road in Ross drew a consumer alert from the Allegheny County Health Department for numerous health code violations.

Some of the violations included holding food at unsafe temperatures, problems with cleaning and sanitization, and problems with cross contamination.

“Certified person in charge does not appear to be enforcing basic food safety principles,” according to the inspection report.

The store also was cited for food in unsound condition. “Eight crates of sliced fish sitting directly outdoors [drying] under no protection in parking lot. Flies observed on the fish carcasses,” the inspection report said, noting that it was a repeat violation. “When inspector requested all fish be discarded, operator was hesitant and placed only a portion of fish in the dumpster and concealed rest of the fish from inspectors in the walk-in cooler.”

Manager Cindy Liu said the store will stop drying fish.

The inspection report noted that all employees were wearing face masks as required during the state’s green reopening phase.

Inspection reports for food facilities in the county are available at webapps.achd.net/Restaurant/.
