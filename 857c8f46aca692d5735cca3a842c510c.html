Lawyer takes the case of Shylock for new look at 'Merchant of Venice'

It’s “The Merchant of Venice” as you’ve never seen it before, and not just because it’s a Zoom presentation.

Pittsburgh Public Theater’s PlayTIme will present a new adaptation of Shakespeare’s play that challenges the idea of who is the wounded party in a dispute when one side is automatically dehumanized by society.

The adaptation by W. Thomas McGough, Jr., chief legal counsel for UPMC, looks anew at the Bard’s tale of anti-Semitism and justice in 1594 Venice.

The play is presented over two days, Thursday and Friday at 7 p.m., at ppt.org/playtime, followed by a conversation with dramaturg Caley Chase (Huntington Theater), Post-Gazette theatre critic Sharon Eberson and Public Theater artistic director Marya Sea Kaminski.

“Shylock first engaged me to represent him in 2016,” Mr. McGough says of his first interaction with the play as a participant in a gala four years ago.

Shylock, the Jewish merchant of the play’s title, is the lender who demands “a pound of flesh,” but has the tables turned on him in the most humiliating of ways.

His defense, in the Bard’s words, speaks to the prejudice that ruled the day: “Hath not a Jew hands, organs, dimensions, senses, affections, passions; fed with the same food, hurt with the same weapons, subject to the same diseases, healed by the same means, warmed and cooled by the same winter and summer as a Christian is? If you prick us, do we not bleed? If you tickle us, do we not laugh?”

According to a press release, Mr. McGough “followed a trail of textual evidence and a novel and fascinating narrative began to emerge. The result ... allows Shylock to preserve a shred of dignity.”

Maury Ginsberg, star of “Indecent” at the Public in 2019, plays Shylock. “This adaptation highlights Shylock's connection to family — his deceased wife and his daughter. They are the motor behind who and what he is.”

The cast also includes Karen Baum (Portia), Patrick Cannon (Bassanio), Tony Bingham (Antonio), Alan Stanford (The Duke/Narrator), Emily Fury Daly (Jessica), Quinn Patrick Shannon (Gratiano), Olivia Vadnais (Nerissa), Ryan Patrick Kearney (Lorenzo) and, in multiple roles, Malic Maat, Michael Trimm and Marc Moritz.
