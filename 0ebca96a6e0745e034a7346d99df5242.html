Nearly 40 Pittsburgh firefighters certified as severe flood response teams

The Pittsburgh Bureau of Fire announced on Friday that it has completed training to be certified as a Type-3A swift water and flood response team.

Thirty-eight city firefighters were trained and certified at the technician level under the program, and split between two separate teams, city officials said. The Type-3A certification makes the teams regional assets that can be deployed outside of Pittsburgh and Allegheny County for flash-flood and disaster relief.

The new certification is recognized by the Pennsylvania Department of Health, Bureau of Emergency Services and the Office of the State Fire Commissioner.

“Pittsburgh Bureau of Fire is on PEMA’s radar now. If a hurricane comes through the Eastern part of the state, PEMA can request our Type-3A team.” Capt. Patrick Shaw said. “We’re actually providing a better service to the citizens, they pay their taxes and we’re here to give them a better service. ... a top notch rescue team is what they have now.”

Capt. Shaw has headed the training program with Engine Truck 17 for the past eight years under city Fire Chief Darryl Jones, city officials said.

Chief Jones began assembling the team after a severe rainstorm caused flash flooding that killed four people along Washington Boulevard in August 2011.

“We were not prepared at that time and now we are prepared.” Chief Jones said. “We’ve been working toward this certification that now recognizes the technician-level training our teams have achieved and officially states that we meet the standard to perform this task, this rescue, this duty.”
