Five Catholic churches to temporarily close due to COVID-19 case in South Hills parish

Five churches in the Catholic Diocese of Pittsburgh will close for at least a week after an employee tested positive for COVID-19.

The employee worked at the St. Albert the Great church in Baldwin, part of the Blessed Trinity Parish in the South Hills, church officials said. That church and four others will be closed for daily Masses.

The other churches in the parish are St. Basil and St. Wendelin in Carrick, Holy Angels in Hays and St. Sylvester Brentwood. Those locations are being cleaned, and people who had direct exposure to the employee are being tested for the virus.

“As important as the spiritual health and well-being of our parishioners and local community is to me as the pastor of Blessed Trinity Parish, so too is their physical health and well-being, especially during these challenging times of the current pandemic,” Father Steve Kresak wrote in a statement. 

A reopening schedule for the church is forthcoming, officials said.
