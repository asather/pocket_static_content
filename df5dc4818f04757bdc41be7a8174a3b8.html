Monmouth poll: Biden's lead over Trump narrows in Pennsylvania

The sizable 13-point lead that Joe Biden held over Donald Trump just six weeks ago in a poll of registered voters in Pennsylvania has since narrowed, according to a new poll published Wednesday. 

Mr. Biden, the Democrat, leads 49% to 45% among registered voters in Monmouth University's latest poll -- down from a 53% to 40% advantage over Mr. Trump in Monmouth's mid-July survey. 

The trimming of Mr. Biden’s lead was thanks to “declining support for the challenger among men, voters under age 50 and voters in key swing counties,” according to the pollster. Mr. Trump was ahead among men 47% to 45% in July; he’s now up 56% to 37%. Mr. Biden led voters under 50 by 31 points in July; he’s now up by 9. In swing counties with close 2016 vote margins, Mr. Trump was trailing by 19 points in July; he’s now ahead by 2.

And the share of voters who say they are certain to vote for Mr. Trump jumped by four points to 40%, while the number of certain Biden voters decreased by 2 points to 43%.

But the poll revealed some reassuring facts for Democrats, as well. Mr. Biden’s lead among women stayed solid at 59% to 35%, and though his margins with voters of color dropped by a few points, he continues to hold a 72% to 15% advantage over Mr. Trump. Mr. Biden’s edge among voters aged 65 and above stayed wide, too, at 53% to 42%.

The public’s perception of the president’s handling of the COVID-19 pandemic, described as a “weak spot for the president” by the pollster, hasn’t changed much. More than half — 53% — say Mr. Trump has done a bad job, while 45% say he’s done a good job. The numbers shifted a few points in Mr. Trump’s direction in both cases.

Meanwhile, Gov. Tom Wolf’s handling of the virus continues to be received positively — with 62% saying he’s done a good job and 36% saying he hasn’t. Those percentages were 67% good and 29% bad in mid-July.

In the generic House ballot, 48% say they’re supporting Democratic candidates in their district and 45% say they’re backing Republicans — numbers quite similar to July. But the GOP is ahead by 10 points, 50% to 40%, in Pennsylvania’s six most competitive districts, which includes the 17th that’s currently held by U.S. Rep. Conor Lamb. Democrats were ahead in those competitive districts by 5 points in July.

“Republican gains on the generic ballot in competitive seats should worry Democrats,” wrote poll director Patrick Murray in a statement. “Only district-level polling can tell if this is because GOP incumbents are bulking up their margins or if GOP challengers are eating into support for Democratic incumbents.”

In statewide races, Democrats have an advantage. Incumbent Attorney General Josh Shapiro leads by 10 points over Republican challenger Heather Heidelbaugh, incumbent Treasurer Joe Torsella is beating Republican Stacy Garrity by 5 and in the open race for auditor general, Democrat Nina Ahmad is ahead of Republican Tim DeFoor by 2.

The Monmouth poll was conducted in the days following the Republican National Convention — between Aug. 28 and Aug. 31 — and surveyed 400 registered voters. The margin of error is 4.9 percentage points.
