For second straight day, no deaths reported in Allegheny County

This story was updated at 12:20 p.m.

For a second straight day, there are zero new deaths from COVID-19 in Allegheny County, according to the latest data released Monday, although the county said that figure should be treated cautiously.

“For full transparency, the reporting of no additional deaths today and yesterday may be due to a delay in reporting for the weekend,” the county health department said in a release. “ACHD reports data as soon as it is received but is uncertain at this time whether deaths have occurred this weekend but have not yet been reported.”

Statewide, the case count eclipsed the 50,000 mark, but there were only 14 new deaths reported. Eleven of them occurred at nursing or assisted living facilities.

The only county in Western Pennsylvania to report a death was Westmoreland, where its coroner reported one. Of Westmoreland’s 31 deaths, the office said 17 were female and 14 were male. All of the victims were white.

Allegheny County reported 20 new cases of COVID-19. With the new data, Allegheny County has 1,365 total cases and 102 deaths since the first case was announced on March 14.

Of the cases, 1,310 are confirmed and 55 are probable. Of the deaths, 92 are confirmed and 10 are probable. The county also said 194 cases — or 14% of the total cases — represent healthcare workers.

Allegheny County has the highest totals in Western Pennsylvania. Through Monday, there have been 238 hospitalizations in the county. 

The Pennsylvania Department of Health said Monday that it had 50,092 cases COVID-19 and 2,458 deaths. Philadelphia continues to have the highest totals in the state with 13,316 cases and 424 deaths.
