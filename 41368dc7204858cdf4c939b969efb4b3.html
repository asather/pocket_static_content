3 students, 1 staff member of PPS schools potentially exposed to COVID-19

Three students and a staff member at four Pittsburgh Public Schools may have had indirect exposure to someone with a confirmed case of COVID-19, a school district spokeswoman said in a statement Friday morning.

PPS spokeswoman Ebony Pugh said families and staff at Pittsburgh Brashear High School, Pittsburgh Carrick High School, Pittsburgh Oliver Citywide Academy and Pittsburgh South Hills received a robocall Friday morning alerting them of the potential indirect exposure to the virus. The indirect exposure occurred prior to the closure of the district.

The potential exposure happened while students were completing a vocational education program off-site at a community organization. In addition, a staff member assigned to Pittsburgh Oliver Citywide Academy had contact with the students before the closure of schools, Ms. Pugh said in a statement.

The Grab and Go meal site at Pittsburgh Oliver Citywide Academy will discontinue as a result of the exposure. Today was the last day for the site.

Pittsburgh Carrick has been cleaned since the exposure, and Pittsburgh Brashear and Pittsburgh South Hills are currently scheduled for deep cleaning, Ms. Pugh said.

The school district said it will continue using its robocalling system to keep families and staff informed. Families can submit a ticket through the district’s Let’s Talk system at www.pghschools.org/LetsTalk to update their telephone number to receive calls.
