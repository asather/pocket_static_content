Two Pittsburgh officers assaulted overnight in Homewood

An adult and two juveniles have been charged with assaulting two Pittsburgh police officers and using pepper spray on them following a domestic dispute in the city’s Homewood North neighborhood early Monday.

 

The officers were on routine patrol along Collier Street when they saw a group of people chasing a man near Idlewild Street shortly before 2:25 a.m. The officers approached the group and members of the group “became confrontational,” police said.

A woman punched one of the officers in the face and two juvenile males joined in the assault, according to police. At least one of them used pepper spray on the officers.

The officers were able to call for backup and other officers from Zones 4 and 5 arrived on the scene.

The officers were treated by paramedics at scene and were not seriously injured.

The suspects were taken to the hospital for medical evaluation.

They each face charges of aggravated assault, resisting arrest, and obstructing.

Police said the incident started from a domestic dispute.
