A really big dill: Seth Rogen's 'An American Pickle,' shot in Pittsburgh, will stream on HBO Max

“An American Pickle,” the Seth Rogen comedy shot around the Pittsburgh area in 2018, will be streaming on HBO Max this summer.

The feature film stars Rogen in a dual role as immigrant laborer Herschel Greenbaum, who falls into a vat of brine in 1920 and emerges 100 years later, perfectly preserved but eminently confused by a new world. Rogan also plays the pickle man’s great-grandson, Ben.

Brandon Trost directed the project, which is from producers Rogen, Evan Goldberg and James Weaver’s company, Point Grey.

The Warner Max label will release “An American Pickle,” having acquired the worldwide rights from Sony Pictures.

With the pandemic forcing theater releases back into the calendar, smaller films such as “An American Pickle” face getting overlooked, one of the reasons Sony might have wanted it online.

“I couldn’t be more thrilled to be partnering with HBO Max to release this film. We worked very hard and put as much of ourselves in this story as possible,” Rogen said in a statement.

The screenplay is by Simon Rich, who adapted his 2013 story from The New Yorker. HBO Max is scheduled for launch May 27.
