Fishing Report: Successive cold fronts push fish to deeper waters

Lake Erie and tributaries (Erie County) A few resident walleye were taken in waters ranging from about 45 to 50 degrees. Smallmouth bass were caught while making spawning runs up the larger tributaries. Big Bass Program regulations requiring no harvest catch-and-immediate-release remain in effect through June 12, and deliberately fishing for bass on nests is prohibited.

Justus Lake (Venango County) Despite a cold front that drove fish to deeper water, anglers reported good fishing for trout and largemouth bass from the southern boat launch to the dam. No crappie action was reported.

Allegheny River (Venango County) Smallmouths and some walleye were taken on crankbaits.

French Creek (Venango County) Crankbaits, spinners and jigs with soft plastic tails attracted Northern pike and smallmouth bass.

Allegheny Reservoir (Warren County) Anglers boated bass, walleye and muskellunge trolling crawler harnesses near the shore from Kinzua Dam to Kinzua Beach Bridge. Walleye were taken on the western end of Sugar Bay.

Allegheny River (Clarion County) Anglers fishing off the Parker Islands caught smallmouths, Northerns and several walleye.

Redbank Creek (Clarion County) Muskies and Northern pike were caught below the New Bethlehem Dam and above the creek’s mouth at the Allegheny River east of Philipston..

Lake Arthur (Butler County) Hybrid striped bass and walleye were taken over structure including submerged railroad beds. A series of cold fronts continued to hamper fishing, but crappies were caught on minnow-tipped jigs.

Harbar Acres Lake (Butler County) The waters at State Game Lands 95 delivered trout for anglers who baited with live minnows.

Private lake (Butler County) Annie Betler of Harrisville brought in an 18-inch rainbow trout.

Pine Creek (Allegheny County) Good-sized trout continued to be caught in the Delayed Harvest Artificial Lures Only area.

Chartiers Creek (Allegheny County) A 25-inch, 5-pound brown trout was caught by a fisherman from Canonsburg.

Youghiogheny River (Fayette County) A 22-inch golden rainbow was taken at Ohiopyle.
