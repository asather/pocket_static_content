Gun permit office closed to protect workers

The temporary closing of the Firearms Division at Allegheny County Courts had nothing to do with curtailing concealed-carry firearm permits, said Allegheny County Sheriff Bill Mullen. The office was closed to protect its workers from contact with people who might have COVID-19.

“We usually get 75 to 100 people a day coming in for concealed-carry permits. Yesterday we had more than double that, 197,” he said.

With concerns that people infected with COVID-19 may interact with employees of the Firearms Division, at 3 p.m. Monday the office was temporarily closed for two weeks.

“We’ll take a look at it then and decide what to do,” said Sheriff Mullen.

A message on the sheriff’s office website stated that for Allegheny County residents who want to renew expiring concealed-carry permits, “as long as you are eligible to renew your license, said license is still valid for six months beyond the printed expiration date on the license.”

Those seeking applications for new concealed-carry permits will have to wait.

“We’re obligated by the Constitution to make sure people have access [to firearms] and we’ll catch up with [concealed-carry] permits when this is over,” he said.
