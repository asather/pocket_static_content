Car rear-ends Port Authority bus in the Strip District

A Port Authority bus was rear-ended Friday evening in the Strip District.

The bus operator and the driver of the private vehicle were transported to hospitals with non-life-threatening injuries, PAT spokesperson Adam Brandolph said. The bus driver went to West Penn Hospital, and the driver of the vehicle went to UPMC Presbyterian. 

The collision between the vehicle and the 87 outbound bus occurred at the intersection of Liberty Avenue and 25th Street just before 6 p.m, Mr. Brandolph said. The bus was towed after it sustained rear bumper and engine damage. The private vehicle was towed due to front-end damage. 

There were 10 passengers on the bus at the time of the incident, Mr. Brandolph said. 

Port Authority police responded. The scene was cleared before 7 p.m.
