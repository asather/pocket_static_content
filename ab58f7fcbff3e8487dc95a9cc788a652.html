Pittsburgh International Jazz Festival is postponed

The Pittsburgh International Jazz Festival is the latest festival to be postponed due to the pandemic.

The annual event, presented by August Wilson African American Cultural Center, was set to take place Downtown on the weekend of June 18. 

“In its first 10 years, the festival has become a hallmark of our city, a beacon for some of the world’s greatest musicians, and a destination for their most devoted fans,” AWAACC director Janis Burley Wilson said in a statement. “However, your health and safety—along with the health and safety of our staff and the musicians—remain our highest priority.”

The Center hopes to move the festival to a later date, to be announced in the coming weeks.

Wilson added that it is looking at ways to bring people jazz online that weekend.

They’ve also launched virtual tours of the two visual arts exhibitions that closed early in response to COVID-19: “Vanishing Black Bars & Lounges: Photographs by L. Kasimu Harris” and “I came by Boat so Meet me at the Beach: Ayana M. Evans and Tsedaye Makonnen.”

Beginning April 27, they will present Lit Fridays, a new conversation series, in celebration of what would have been August Wilson’s 75th birthday. The first session will feature actors Ruben Santiago-Hudson and Nathan James, poet jessica Care moore, and August Wilson scholar Dr. Sandra Shannon in conversation with Curator of Literary Programs Deesha Philyaw. It will continue on the final Friday of each month.
