Mohawk girls win first WPIAL Class 3A championship

Mohawk had never played in the WPIAL girls basketball championship game, but the Warriors certainly made the most of their first appearance.

Junior guard Nadia Lape scored five of her game-high 14 points in the second quarter to lead a balanced Mohawk (22-3) attack as the second-seeded Warriors won their first title in school history with a 44-26 victory over Section 1 rival and No. 1 Beaver (21-4) in the WPIAL Class 3A final at Petersen Events Center.

Beaver and Mohawk had played twice in the regular season and split the two meetings.

Mohawk took control of the game in the second quarter when Beaver opened the period 0 for 8 from the floor and 2 for 13 in the quarter. The Warriors, meanwhile, opened the second quarter on an 8-0 run and, eventually led 21-9 at the intermission.

Emma Pavelek led Beaver with 10 points.
