Thousands without power after severe thunderstorms roll through Pittsburgh region

More than 16,000 households are without power Wednesday morning after overnight heavy thunderstorms downed tree limbs and flooded roadways.

The National Weather Service in Pittsburgh has ended the severe thunderstorm watch that was to expire at 6 a.m. Wednesday.

A line of thunderstorms moved through the area after midnight Wednesday, producing hail and 60 mph wind gusts. Heavy rainfall followed the storms.

There were several reports of downed power lines and trees throughout the region. 

Duquesne Light reported about 16,037 customers were without power as of 4:30 a.m. 

As of 4:30 a.m., First Energy reported 7,589 outages in Allegheny County; 160 in Armstrong County; 638 in Beaver County; 844 in Butler County; 1,746 in Fayette County;  2,935 in Washington County; and 15,998 in Westmoreland County. 

According to the Weather Service, a tornado was spotted near Apollo, Armstrong County, around 1:19 a.m. A tornado warning for northeastern Allegheny County and northwestern Westmoreland County was canceled before 2 a.m.
