Don’t expect a mailer about that Citizen Police Review Board ballot question

Pittsburgh voters should not expect to receive anything in the mail spelling out the full language of the Citizen Police Review Board ballot referendum ahead of Nov. 3, with council members and the city clerk’s office citing high costs.

The question asks voters if they support “expanding the powers of the Independent Citizen Police Review Board to allow the Board to require police officers to participate in investigations, conducting performance audits of the Police Bureau and preventing the removal of Board members except for just cause and with City Council approval.” 

The cost to mail 137,000 voters in the city would be $66,000, according to City Clerk Brenda Pree. Ms. Pree said the expanded language would not fit on a postcard and that a letter-size mailing would be required to print the entire Home Rule Charter amendment.

“Given the pandemic and our fragile financial state as a city, we just think it’s an unnecessary expense at this time,” said Councilman Ricky Burgess. “This was a provision we put in really not thinking that the pandemic would be this exhaustive. It’s also not something we normally do.”

But some council members expressed concern.

“I still feel like there’s a lack of awareness that there is both a ballot text and full text and what the meaning of it is,” said Councilwoman Deb Gross. “… I would like us to put some energy into doing better outreach.”

Ms. Pree said the referendum language will be available on the city’s website and that requests for the full language will be honored.

“I generally don’t feel comfortable taking away a method of educating voters and the public without having a plan of action in its place,” said Councilwoman Erika Strassburger. “I think posting on the website is a good start. … But what are all the options?”

Council gave an affirmative recommendation to removing mailing requirement in a 6-2 vote. Ms. Gross and Ms. Strassburger abstained.

Council will take a final vote Tuesday.
