Robert Morris loses close one with Fairleigh Dickinson, Toole blames refs

With just over 30 seconds left in Robert Morris’ game against Fairleigh Dickinson, the Colonials clung to a two-point lead and the ball. Fifth-year senior Josh Williams threw it away, though, and the Knights hit an and-one layup to take a one-point lead. Not to be outdone, Robert Morris forward AJ Bramah drew a shooting foul with eight seconds left, made both free throws and put his team back in front, 71-70.

Fairleigh Dickinson answered yet again, hitting a tip-in layup with 1.3 seconds left to swing the pendulum back toward the Knights.

Then came the drama and the focus of Colonials coach Andy Toole’s post-game attention.

Guard Jon Williams was the in-bounder. He ran the baseline to his right, while his brother, Josh Williams, set a screen on the defender guarding the in-bounder, Kaleb Bishop. A big collision ensued, but no whistle, and Robert Morris (15-12 overall, 11-3 NEC) fell, 72-71.

Toole went chasing after the referees after the final buzzer sounded, convinced there should have been a foul called, but no explanation was given.

“Hard to get a catch and shoot late. They’re gonna make you catch it in front of them. The play is for him to get run over,” Toole said. “If you watch late-game college basketball all across the country, that’s the No. 1 job of the assistant coach, to yell at the guy guarding the in-bounder, ‘Don’t foul!’ 

“[Fairleigh Dickinson coach] Greg Herenda says to me on the sideline, ‘I didn’t go over that with my guys. Complete foul.’ That’s where it’s so frustrating. If the opposing coach is saying it’s a foul and the refs don’t know it’s a foul, that’s a major problem.”

Whether or not there was a missed call, Robert Morris seemed to have things in control late in the game. The Knights (8-16, 6-7), however, made clutch shot after clutch shot to keep things close.

Altogether, Fairleigh Dickinson scored 16 points in the last four minutes, culminating with the tip-in layup to win.

“We didn’t do what we needed to do defensively down the stretch,” Toole said. “... We were up eight and they were able to get free for some threes and create some opportunities to get back into the game, which is a let-down on our part.”

The game as a whole was wildly entertaining, including the first half, which contained a 14-0 run for the Colonials followed by an 8-0 Knights run to draw within a point at halftime. But Toole seemed to find it hard to think about any of those stretches after the game, given how it all ended.

“This is home-court advantage,” Toole said. “People are trying to win a championship, so they can host games in the NEC Tournament. So for guys to miss calls like that is just inexcusable. It’s inexcusable. And that can have a direct impact on where people go late in the season, in terms of hosting a semifinal game, hosting a quarterfinal game, hosting a championship game. You can’t miss those ones. I get it. Block/charges are hard, there are certain calls that are hard. You can’t miss that one right in front of you like that. You can’t.”
