Derek Shelton says Cole Tucker will get a shot in the Pirates' outfield

Cole Tucker could be on the move.

Manager Derek Shelton said after the Pirates’ 5-3 exhibition loss to the Indians on Saturday at PNC Park that the team plans to try Tucker, their first-round pick in 2014, in the outfield as a way to potentially get him a little more playing time.

“We’ve exposed him in the last couple days,” Shelton said. “I think there’s a chance that we could see him bounce around. He’s extremely athletic, which makes him versatile. If there’s a chance we could get him on the field in different spots, then I think we’ll probably try to do that.”

The Pirates haven’t determined whether Tucker would play left field, center or right. It’s been more about giving him a chance to try it out and see how he does with the position change/addition.

Shelton said Tucker, for the past two days, has been working with first-base coach Tarrik Brock, who oversees the Pirates’ outfielders.

With Adam Frazier and Kevin Newman entrenched in the middle infield, playing time was potentially scarce for Tucker, especially if they look to use Erik Gonzalez and JT Riddle — a pair of utility guys — as backups.

But with Gregory Polanco (COVID-19) out, there could be more opportunity for Tucker in the outfield if he’s able to handle the work.

“He’s a really good athlete, and he’s taken to it,” Shelton said. “He’s excited about being out there. He played second base [Saturday] because [young shortstop] Oneil [Cruz] was here. I think he has the ability to play all three with not much issue.”

Walking wounded

The Pirates trotted out an opening day-type lineup Saturday, with the lone exception being Newman.

Shelton said Newman was a little dinged up, and the manager made the decision to hold him out as a precaution.

“If this were next Friday in St. Louis, he would’ve played,” Shelton said.

Riddle did not participate in the game, as he, too, is not quite 100%. Shelton said Riddle took individual work before reporters entered the ballpark.

With catcher Luke Maile lost for the year, Shelton said catcher Andrew Susac will likely stick around in Pittsburgh for the time being.

‘Wear your mask’

Shelton did his postgame interview wearing a T-shirt featuring a masked Pirate. He said it’s going to be a theme for him this season — to wear something with meaning, perhaps directed toward some sort of charity.

“I think we’ll try to do something after games to try to represent different things that are going on,” Shelton said. “I think everybody should wear a mask. It’s important to wear a mask. We’ve kind of taken our logo and made it cool so I figured I would rock it for the first night here. So wear your mask.”
