Law enforcement, community gather at funeral for Jefferson Hills police officer

Family, friends and fellow law enforcement officers turned out in large numbers Saturday for funeral services for veteran Jefferson Hills police Officer Dale Provins Jr., who died June 13 of injuries suffered in a head-on crash 10 days earlier.

A funeral procession carried Officer Provins’ body to Round Hill Cemetery in Elizabeth Township, where loved ones and police officers from around Western Pennsylvania gathered for graveside services.

Borough police said Officer Provins, 50, of Belle Vernon, was driving along Old Clairton Road on June 3 when an oncoming vehicle crossed the center line and struck the officer’s patrol vehicle in the 200 block.

Officer Provins, a U.S. Marine Corps veteran, worked 15 years with the Jefferson Hills Police Department and was a second-generation police officer.

He is survived by his daughter, fiancée, parents, and siblings.

A crowd turned out for a public visitation on Friday at the Jefferson Hills Municipal Complex.
