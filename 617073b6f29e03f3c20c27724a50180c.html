Allegheny County courts extend judicial emergency through December

Allegheny County’s Court of Common Pleas will continue its judicial emergency through Dec. 31 and jury trials remain suspended amid the COVID-19 pandemic.

The judicial emergency began in March to mitigate the spread of the novel coronavirus. 

The latest order of the court, issued Monday and signed by President Judge Kim Berkely Clark, says jury trials in the civil division will commence “on a limited basis” and only when the court specifically schedules a jury trial.

The court is still evaluating how to bring back criminal jury trials, and will “determine an appropriate time to resume” them with additional requirements. 

The Pennsylvania Supreme Court ended its judicial emergency June 1, giving county president judges the authority to keep restrictions in place at their own discretion.

On Thursday, Chief U.S. District Judge Mark Hornak issued an order halting all civil and criminal jury trials at the federal level in the Western District of Pennsylvania.
