Desi Market in Carrick hit with consumer alert

The Allegheny County Health Department posted a consumer alert at Desi Market on Brownsville Road in Carrick because of a roach infestation and other food safety concerns.

“All stages of roach development found throughout the meat department of the facility, in the unsealed grain storage area and the non-refrigerated produce shelving,” according to the inspection report.

Live roaches were found under cutting boards, in the grain and fresh produce aisles, and inside the meat display coolers. Roach droppings were found on cutting boards, the crack in the butcher blocks and on shelves.

Other problems included fish being sold from unknown sources and band saws and cutting boards being stored soiled with raw meat debris.

The report indicated that the inspection was triggered by a complaint.

The operator of the store could not be reached for comment.
