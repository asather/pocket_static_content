Man accused of damaging Pittsburgh police cruiser during protest pleads not guilty

A Shaler man accused of damaging a Pittsburgh police car during a May 30 protest in Downtown Pittsburgh entered a not guilty plea Tuesday by video in federal court.

Brian Bartels, 20, was indicted last week on a count of obstruction of law enforcement during a civil disorder.

Police and prosecutors said he spray painted an unoccupied police cruiser during a protest near PPG Paints Arena, smashed the windshield and enticed others to join in damaging the car.

The protesters were demonstrating against social injustice and racism in the wake of the death of George Floyd, who died while in the custody of Minneapolis officers.

The U.S. attorney's office said Mr. Bartels went to the protest to instigate violence and was carrying rocks and spray paint in a backpack. Prosecutors said he admitted to police that he was the man in the video and that he was upset because of incidents of police abuse, according to the criminal complaint.
