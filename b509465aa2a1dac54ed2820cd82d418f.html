Pitt RB V'Lique Carter enters the transfer portal

Pitt running back V’Lique Carter has entered the transfer portal, a source told the Post-Gazette.

Carter, who’s entering his junior season, will need to sit out the 2020 campaign if he transfers to another Football Bowl Subdivision program, unless he’s granted a waiver by the NCAA.

A Pitt running back pursuing other options should not come as a huge surprise.

Andre Powell’s room is crowded entering winter workouts and spring camp, with rising senior A.J. Davis, redshirt junior Todd Sibley and sophomores Vincent Davis and Daniel Carter returning. Israel Abanikanda — a 2020 three-star prospect, per Rivals — has also signed and entered the fold as an early enrollee.

Still, Carter, a three-star defensive back in Pitt’s 2018 recruiting class, made his mark as a versatile piece of the Panthers’ offense.

The Florida native switched from defense midway through his true freshman season and dusted Duke for 137 rushing yards and two touchdowns in a 54-45 win. That was Carter's only expansive look at carries in 2018, but it offered hope for what was believed to be ahead.

Listed on Pitt's 2019 preseason depth chart as a running back/wide receiver, the Florida native played exclusively the former last season. But the 5-9, 185-pounder served as a real passing option out of the backfield. Carter recorded 17 receptions for 183 yards, including a game-winning, 26-yard catch-and-run at Duke on Oct. 5.

Carter also had a role in Pitt's (largely disappointing) 2019 rushing attack. He logged 65 carries and 243 yards, ranking third and fourth on the team, respectively.

Carter's versatility could have again been utilized in 2020, but how much would have been the question.

Davis, a speedy 5-8 rusher, offers a similar skillset and emerged as the 2019 season progressed. The 170-pounder popped a 61-yard score at Georgia Tech on Nov. 2, ripped off a 39-yard rush versus Boston College in the regular-season finale and tallied 96 yards on 17 touches (15 carries, two catches) in Pitt's Quick Lane Bowl win.

In addition to Vincent Davis, Sibley and A.J. Davis remain options. Abanikanda expects to push for playing time immediately, as well.
