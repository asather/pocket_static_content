Pirates sign reliever Robbie Erlin to minor league deal

The Pirates on Wednesday signed left-handed reliever Robbie Erlin to a minor league contract with an invite to spring training.

Bob Nightengale of USA Today Sports first reported the signing. If the 29-year-old former Padres hurler makes the big league roster, he will make $1.5 million, with $100,000 in incentives. Erlin can opt out of the deal if he doesn't make the opening day roster.

The Rangers drafted Erlin in the third round of the 2009 draft. Like many pitchers, he began his career as a starter but later caught on as a reliever.

Erlin, who had Tommy John surgery in 2016, had his best season in 2018 with 44 strikeouts and 2.05 ERA across his 52⅔ innings of relief. He also made 12 starts and posted an overall record of 4-7. The next season wasn’t so kind, as Erlin posted a 5.37 ERA in 2019.

The Pirates’ 40-man roster currently sits at 39 players. Sam Howard is the only other left-handed reliever on the 40-man. Steven Brault, another lefty, is expected to compete for a spot in the starting rotation this spring.
