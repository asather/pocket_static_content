Pittsburgh man arrested for criminal acts during Downtown protest

Authorities arrested a Pittsburgh man Tuesday in connection with criminal acts during a protest May 30 in Downtown, according to police spokeswoman Cara Cruz.

Damage Assessment and Accountability Task Force investigators arrested George Allen, 31. Mr. Allen is charged with aggravated assault, propulsion of missiles, riot, recklessly endangering another person and criminal mischief during the primarily peaceful protest against police brutality.

He was being transported to Allegheny County Jail on Tuesday.

Mr. Allen was connected with images of a man who allegedly threw a cinder block through the window of a police support truck parked at Washington Place and Fifth Avenue during the May 30 protest. The vehicle was occupied by a driver and passenger at the time.

DAAT also arrested Raekwon Blankenship, 24, of Crafton, who is suspected of assaulting a police horse and vandalizing a police vehicle during the same protest.

Mr. Blankenship faces charges of aggravated assault, criminal mischief, riot, illegal taunts to police animals, propulsion of missiles, recklessly endangering another person, failure to disperse and animal cruelty.

He was also being transported to the county jail Tuesday.
