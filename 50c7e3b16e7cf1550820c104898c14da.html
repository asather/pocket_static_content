Steelers sign two more rookies, WR Chase Claypool and S Antoine Brooks

The Steelers are now officially two-thirds of the way through making sure their 2020 rookie class are inked to shiny new contracts.

On Wednesday evening, safety Antoine Brooks, the Steelers’ sixth-round selection out of Maryland, posted a photo on Instagram of himself putting pen to paper.

Not long after Brooks announced his signing, receiver Chase Claypool did the same on Twitter.

The Notre Dame product was picked by the Steelers 49th overall in the second round of April’s draft. Claypool signed his rookie contract almost exactly a month after buying his dad a brand new truck for Father’s Day.

Pittsburgh is inching its way toward getting all its rookies signed before training camp starts on July 28. On Tuesday, both linebacker Alex Highsmith (third-round pick, Charlotte) and running back Anthony McFarland (fourth-round pick, Maryland) agreed to terms with the team.

That just leaves guard Kevin Dotson (fourth-round pick, Louisiana) and nose tackle Carlos Davis (seventh-round pick, Nebraska) who have yet to finalize their rookie deals with the Steelers.
