Good news about jobs: Pittsburgh MSA's May unemployment rate falls

Some good news about jobs: unemployment in Pittsburgh and surrounding six counties was down in May compared to April.

The seasonally adjusted unemployment rate for the seven-country Pittsburgh metro area fell 3.3 percentage points to 13.7% in May when compared to the previous month, the state Department of Labor reported Tuesday.

That compares to a statewide unemployment rate of 13.1% in May, down three percentage points over the month, and the national rate of 13.2%, down 1.4 points from April.

The number of seasonally adjusted total nonfarm jobs in the Pittsburgh MSA rose 4.3% in May, up by 41,000 jobs from April to a total of 1.01 million jobs. Over the year, jobs were down 14.6% or 174,500 jobs in the metro area, while statewide jobs fell 14.3%.

Fayette County led the Pittsburgh metro area with the highest jobless rate of 16.7%, followed by Beaver County at 15% and Westmoreland County at 14%.

Statewide, the East Stroudsburg MSA topped the jobless rate in Pennsylvania at 18%.
