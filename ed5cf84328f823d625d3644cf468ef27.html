UPMC newborns get special outfits in honor of Pittsburgh's 'H' Day

It was a historic day to be born in Pittsburgh. 

UPMC Magee-Womens Hospital dressed newborn babies in a special outfit Sunday to honor “H” Day — the day when Pittsburgh got the “h” back at the end of the city’s name 109 years ago. 

In 1880, the U.S. government dropped the “h” from all names that ended in “burg” to keep them consistent. 

On July 19, 1911, however, Pittsburgh was given its “h” back, marking the annual date “H” Day in the Steel City. 

UPMC posted photos to its social media pages Sunday of newborn babies sporting “H” Day onesies that read “Pittsburgh’s newest neighbor.” The onesies were donated by PGH Apparel, a clothing company in Forest Hills. 

“In honor of this occasion, PGH Apparel donated these onesies to Pittsburgh's newest neighbors. All babies born at Magee today will receive a special onesie to commemorate the anniversary of H Day. We thank PGH Apparel for their generosity,” the hospital’s post read. 

The City of Pittsburgh Twitter account said in a tweet Sunday that the city “fought for decades” to restore the “h” in its name. 

Pennsylvania’s official government Twitter account also commemorated the day.
