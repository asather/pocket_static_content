Penguins on pause: Injuries derail Nick Bjugstad's season

The NHL has “paused” its season due to the COVID-19 pandemic. It is unclear if it will resume. So, with one eye still on the future, the Post-Gazette’s Matt Vensel and Mike DeFabo are looking back at what each Penguins player did in 2019-20. We started with the captain, No. 87, and will count down by jersey number.

The story of Nick Bjugstad’s season is essentially a rewriting of the injury report.

Just two games into the season, the 6-foot-6 forward went on injured reserve when a core muscle injury began to flare up. He returned only briefly, playing in just eight more games before he was forced to undergo surgery.

Initially, the Penguins estimated he’d miss at least eight weeks. It turned out to be double that. The long, non-linear rehab process was sidetracked by setbacks. In total, he was on the shelf from Nov. 15 to March 5.

Finally, Bjugstad returned for the Penguins’ victory over Buffalo on March 5. But after playing in just three games, Bjugstad’s season was once again suspended. This time, coach Mike Sullivan said he would be week-to-week with a different lower body injury not related to the surgery.

In total, Bjugstad played in just 13 of the 69 games, tallying one goal and one assist.

DEFINING MOMENT: In his first game back from injury on March 5, Bjugstad made an immediate impact. He plowed over Buffalo goalie Jonas Johansson, giving Patric Hornqvist a wide open net.

STAT THAT STANDS OUT: Bjugstad missed 56 of the 69 games this season.

IF THE SEASON RESUMES: The extra time off should give Bjugstad an opportunity to recover. Sullivan said the forward would be week-to-week, and it’s already been a month since the NHL paused the season. But where will he play? The right-handed Bjugstad centered the third line for three games in March, playing between Jared McCann and Patrick Hornqvist. However, in some instances, the left-handed McCann shifted to center.

While we’re discussing where Bjugstad will play, it’s also fair to question if he’ll play. Assuming Jake Guentzel, Zach Aston-Reese, Dominik Simon and Anthony Angello also return from injuries of their own, the Penguins would have 17 healthy forwards. It’s not necessarily a given that Bjugstad would crack the lineup. 

LONG-TERM OUTLOOK: Bjugstad is signed with the Penguins through the 2020-21 on a deal that carries a $4.1 million cap hit. The Penguins are almost always fighting to stay under the upper limit. The COVID-19 pandemic creates another layer of uncertainty. The cap was projected to go up from $81.5 million this year to somewhere between $84 million and $88.2 million in 2020-21. However, after all the revenue the league lost during the pandemic, it’s likely to go down instead.
