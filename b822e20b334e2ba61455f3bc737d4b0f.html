Two more jurors seated Thursday for Wilkinsburg mass shooting case

Two more jurors were seated Thursday to hear the case against two men accused of the 2016 mass shooting at a Wilkinsburg backyard cookout.

The total now stands at 12 primary jurors and one alternate. Three alternate spots still remain.

Testimony in the case against Cheron Shelton, 32, of Lincoln-Lemington and Robert Thomas, 31, of Homewood is scheduled to begin Feb. 3 before Allegheny County Common Pleas Judge Edward J. Borkowski.

Shelton and Thomas are accused of killing Jerry Michael Shelton, 35, Brittany Powell, 27, and Chanetta Powell, 25, who was eight months pregnant, Tina Shelton, 37, and Shada Mahone, 26, during a cookout on Franklin Avenue on March 9, 2016.
