First Allegheny County COVID-19 death reported

The first COVID-19 death has occurred in Allegheny County, the Health Department has confirmed.

The only detail available is that the person was an adult who had been hospitalized. 

“There will not be any additional information provided at this time in order to respect the family’s privacy,” it said in a release. “A media briefing will be held later this afternoon with details forthcoming.”

In addition, there now are 31 positive cases of COVID-19 in Allegheny County, including five people hospitalized. “Additional results” are expected to be received throughout the day, the county Health Department stated.  

The Allegheny County death represents the second in Pennsylvania, which now has 371 confirmed cases — 103 more than Friday, according to State Department of Health totals.

Washington County cases now stand at five, with the first confirmed cased in Butler County.

Officials in the Butler Health System, said in a Saturday release, that it has the first patient with presumed positive results for the novel coronavirus that causes COVID-19 illness.

“The patient, who had been sick, was directed by his physician to be tested at the health system’s outdoor site earlier in the week. The patient, a Butler County resident, is recovering at home.”

No additional cases were reported midday Sunday in Westmoreland (4), Beaver (3) and Erie (1) counties.

This story will be updated.
