Five high school students injured in two-vehicle crash in Mount Pleasant

Five teenagers — all students at Mount Pleasant Area Senior High School — were hospitalized Monday evening in a two-vehicle crash in Westmoreland County, the school district said Tuesday.

State police responded around 5:30 p.m. to the collision that occurred in the 900 block of Claypike Road in Mount Pleasant, dispatchers said.

The district confirmed that four boys and a girl were involved in the crash. One of the teens was listed in critical condition and the others were stable Monday evening, the school district said. It wasn’t clear how many occupants were in each vehicle.

Westmoreland County’s dispatch center said four were taken to local hospitals by helicopter, and the other by ground.

One teen had to be cut from the vehicle, and the others appeared to suffer compound-like fracture injuries, according to Pittsburgh Post-Gazette news partner KDKA-TV. They were all wearing seat belts.

Further information was not immediately available.
