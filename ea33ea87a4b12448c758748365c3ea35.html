Firefighters battle roof blaze at former Horace Mann school

Parts of the roof of the former Horace Mann School caught fire late Thursday evening in Marshall-Shadeland neighborhood of Pittsburgh.

Firefighters were called on the scene just after 11:30 p.m. Thursday on Shadeland Avenue.

Firefighters were on scene for about three hours, according to Pittsburgh Post-Gazette news partner KDKA.

City arson investigators were on the scene.

The cause of the fire is under investigation.

The elementary school closed in 2006. Fourth River Development was hired by the school district to market the building, which was built in 1874.
