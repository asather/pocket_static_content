Seneca Valley School Board approves plan to return to school

The Seneca Valley School Board approved its plan for students and teachers to return to school this fall.

Members of the Seneca Valley School Board of Directors approved the 2020-21 Seneca Valley Health and Safety Plan for Return to School on Monday evening with a viote of 8-1.

Among the details of the plan, parents will be asked to choose between five days per week in class, fully online or a mix of online and in-person classes.

If Butler County remains in the “green” phase, the board said reopening will be on Aug. 26 as scheduled, with a five-day school schedule that adheres to safety and social distancing guidelines. If the county goes into the “yellow” phase, students attend school based on a cohort model. 

Parents will receive an email with the options on Wednesday and have until July 29 to select the learning model they would like for their child.

According to Pittsburgh Post-Gazette news partner KDKA, a school survey found 79 percent of parents said they plan to have their kids in school every day.

To read more details of the plan, visit https://www.svsd.net.
