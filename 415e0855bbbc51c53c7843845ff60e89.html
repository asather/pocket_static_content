Pirates claim Sean Poppen; designate JT Riddle for assignment

The Pirates claimed another project arm, this time from the Minnesota Twins, and designated JT Riddle for assignment on Thursday.

The pitcher they claimed was right-hander Sean Poppen, a 26-year-old who made six relief appearances in Minnesota this past season, allowing four earned runs over 7 2/3 innings while walking four and striking out 10. He also pitched in four games with the Twins in 2019.

Poppen is a Harvard graduate and someone the Twins drafted in the 19th round back in 2016. A Virginia Beach native, Poppen has also started plenty; 16 of his 20 minor league appearances came that way in 2019, as he pitched across two levels.

Poppen went 7-4 in those games with a 4.01 ERA and 107 strikeouts in 89 2/3 innings, relying on a sinker that sits 94-95 mph.

He typically throws that more than half the time and sticks to two pitches, pairing the sinker with a slider and occasionally mixing in a changeup.

It's probably worth noting that the Pirates found success this season with two guys who have similar profiles; Geoff Hartlieb went all sinker-slider, and Sam Howard dramatically upped his slider usage.

The Pirates signed JT Riddle this past offseason to a deal designed to give them infield depth. It never came close to panning out. In fact, Riddle became a source of fan frustration, as it became quite puzzling why he was even playing.

The 28-year-old wound up hitting just .149/.174/.224 in 23 games, including 19 starts. Riddle also struck out 13 times and walked twice in 69 plate appearances.
