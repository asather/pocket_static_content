COVID-19 Update: Pa. sees another 1,000-plus new cases, 17 deaths

The Pennsylvania Health Department reported another day of 1,000-plus new COVID-19 cases as 17 more people died of the virus statewide.

With the 1,036 new cases for Tuesday, the state has now had a total of 165,243 people test positive for the virus. In six out of the last seven days, the state has had daily increases surpass 1,000 cases.

In Western Pennsylvania, Allegheny County saw the highest daily increase with 77 new cases, followed by Westmoreland County with 39 more cases.

With the addition of the 77 new cases, the county has had a total of 12,854 cases of the virus since the start of the pandemic. Of Tuesday’s cases, 72 are confirmed and come from 870 tests that were performed between Sept. 13 and Oct. 5. The majority of the new cases are results from tests that were taken within the last week, said the county health department. Five of the 77 cases are probable.

Those who have recently tested positive range in age from 11 to 90 years old, with a median age of 39.

The Pittsburgh region accounted for 239 of the state’s total cases for Tuesday.

The health department also reported an additional 17 deaths, eight of which were in Western Pennsylvania. Allegheny County reported four new deaths, Mercer County reported two and Beaver and Fayette counties reported one death each.

The four new deaths in Allegheny occurred between Sept. 30 and Oct. 4. Three of the individuals were in their 80s and one was in their 90s; all four of the deaths are associated with long-term care facilities. 

Since the pandemic began in mid-March, 8,244 people in Pennsylvania have died as a result of COVID-19 and 390 in Allegheny County.

Statewide there are currently 633 people hospitalized for the virus, 76 of which are in Allegheny County. Among those hospitalized throughout the state, 74 are on mechanical ventilation.
