Fourth resident at Kane Glen Hazel has died due to COVID-19

A fourth resident at the Kane Community Living Center in Glen Hazel has died due to COVID-19, according to Post-Gazette news partner KDKA.

KDKA reported 34 residents at the nursing home have tested positive for COVID-19. Two additional residents are still awaiting results for tests. 

Among the staff at the facility, 19 workers have tested positive for the virus, while an additional 7 also wait for pending results, according to KDKA.

The assisted living facility has suffered a rash of infections, with 25 residents and 15 staff testing positive for the new coronavirus as of Friday. 

Kane’s other three centers, in Scott, McKeesport and Ross, have had no confirmed cases as of Friday, although about half a dozen tests are still pending.

It’s been almost a month since Kane implemented a no-visitor policy and suspended communal dining and group activities.

Tiny Glen Hazel is by far the most impacted neighborhood per capita in Allegheny County, but updated numbers aren’t yet available.
