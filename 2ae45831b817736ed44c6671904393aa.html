Air quality alert issued for southwest Pa.; heat-index temps may reach 102

Hot temperatures and high humidity on Thursday are going to create miserable and dangerous conditions.

The National Weather Service said the heat index will make temperatures in Western Pennsylvania feel like 102 degrees. It has issued a heat advisory from 1 p.m. to 8 p.m. Thursday and warned that the conditions can cause heat illnesses.

With that, the state Department of Environmental Protection and the Southwestern Pennsylvania Air Quality Partnership issued a Code Orange air quality alert for much of southwestern Pennsylvania.
