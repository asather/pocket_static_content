Allegheny County cases fall, but new deaths rise by 6

Allegheny County continues to see a decrease in cases of COVID-19, as 27 new cases were reported Wednesday, but there was a rise in virus-related deaths, with six. In total, eight new deaths were reported in Western Pennsylvania.

The county’s new cases come from 875 tests that were administered between July 31 and Aug. 18. All but two of the test specimens were collected within the past week, the county health department said.

Those who have recently tested positive range in age from 1 to 80 years old, with a median age of 33.

The county now has had 9,576 cases of the virus since the pandemic began in mid-March.

Pennsylvania saw an increase of 570 new cases for Wednesday, bringing the state total to 126,149.

Western Pennsylvania accounted for 124 of the newest cases, with Allegheny seeing the largest increase, followed by Westmoreland County with 18 new cases. 

The new deaths in Allegheny County were associated with long-term care facilities and occurred between Aug. 3 and Aug. 18, the county said. Four of the deaths were people in their 80s, one was in their 90s, and one was over 100.

Wednesday had the highest jump in deaths since Aug. 12 when the county reported 13 additional deaths. In total, 297 people in Allegheny County have died as a result of COVID-19.

Washington and Westmoreland counties each reported one death.

Statewide there were 24 new deaths, bringing the state death toll to 7,523.

Of the most recent COVID-19 related deaths, six were in Allegheny Count, and Washington and Westmoreland counties reported one death each. 

Since the beginning of the pandemic, 911 people have been hospitalized in Allegheny County. Based on the most recent data from the state, the county currently has 94 people hospitalized with COVID-19.
