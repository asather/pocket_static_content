Boy, 1, dies after ingesting illicit drugs at Sharpsburg home

An infant who was pronounced dead nearly eight months ago had ingested several narcotics, Allegheny County officials said Wednesday.

The Allegheny County Medical Examiner’s Office released its findings into the death of Latayvis Kane LeRoy Gervasoni and said the 1-year-old died of complications that resulted from a mixed intoxication of heroin and fentanyl he ingested at a home in Sharpsburg.

County police said in a statement that 911 emergency dispatch was notified of the unresponsive infant at approximately 3:20 a.m. on Sept. 4 at a residence in the 1000 block of North Canal Street.

He was then taken to a local hospital, where he was pronounced dead approximately one hour later.

“Through the course of the investigation, detectives suspected that the child may have ingested illegal narcotics,” the statement said. “The police investigation is ongoing.”

Police have not said owned the home or the drugs.

Anyone with information regarding this case is encouraged to call the County Police tip line at 1-833-ALL-TIPS.
