Black bear spotted roaming through Cranberry yards and Graham Park

As Cranberry Township residents celebrated Memorial Day, a large furry visitor decided to join in on the festivities and catch some sun. 

Cranberry police responded to a call that a black bear was spotted in the backyard of a home on Robinhood Drive at about 1 p.m. Monday. Sgt. Chuck Mascellino said Tuesday that shortly after police arrived, the bear climbed a tree and proceeded to “laze about” for the next few hours. 

“There’s not really a protocol [to responding to bear sightings],” Mr. Mascellino said. “We go and try to see the bears ourselves and make sure it isn’t causing a disturbance. Yesterday, there were a lot of kids out and that neighborhood has a lot of homes with yards that are fenced in. So we wanted to let people know to keep the kids and animals inside.” 

A few doors down, Heather Kreke and her children were playing in her backyard pool when an officer pulled up and alerted them that there was a bear up the street. 

Ms. Kreke said she walked down to see the bear and later posted photos of it to her Facebook page. 

“It was there all day, at least eight or nine hours,” she said. “There were people all day driving past, stopping and looking.” 

Ms. Kreke, 38, who has lived in Cranberry the last 16 years, said that was the first time she had seen a bear in her neighborhood. The person who owned the house where the bear camped out was not home at the time, she said. 

Ms. Kreke said the sighting brought about some much needed quarantine excitement for her kids. “They weren’t scared. My 7-year-old was a little nervous that it was going to come swim in the pool, but other than that they were pretty excited. We took a bunch of walks down there during the course of the day to check it out and see what it was doing,” she said.

Mr. Mascellino said officers advised on behalf of the Game Commission that residents keep a safe distance from the bear and leave it be, as it would eventually move on elsewhere.

Cranberry police got a call just after 10:30 a.m. Tuesday that the bear was spotted again near the entrance of  Graham Park which is only about a mile away from Robinhood Drive. 

“If you see a bear, the best corse of action is to leave it alone,” Mr. Mascellino said. “Do not approach it. Keep pets and children inside and they’ll move on eventually. 

“The only time the police department or Game Commission needs to be notified is if the bear is causing a disturbance or causing any damage like getting into trash.”
