For the most vulnerable moms, a chance to connect with family

Celebrating Mother’s Day has been made more difficult by the COVID-19 pandemic, especially for elderly mothers living in nursing and assisted-living homes.

To help families celebrate, the Charles Morris Nursing and Rehabilitation Center, located at the the Jewish Association of Aging campus in Squirrel Hill, organized “window visits” on Sunday with “some extra love and very careful planning.”

With mom sitting on one side of a plexiglass window and family members on the other side, Mother’s Day was celebrated, albeit unconventionally.

“We recognize it has been a long time since our families last visited with their moms, grandmothers, step-mothers, aunts, etc.,” the center said in a press release. “Due to the vulnerability of our nursing home residents, the opportunity to visit in person is a carefully structured event to assure the safety for our residents, our staff, and visiting families.”

The visits were by appointment only, with each visit lasting about 20 minutes.
