State Department of Health cites three area nursing homes for deficiencies

The state Department of Health announced Saturday it has issued sanctions against 10 nursing homes, including two provisional licenses, for problems from inspections last year.

Sanctions against homes in Western Pennsylvania include a provisional license for the Twin Lakes Rehabilitation and Healthcare Center in Westmoreland County and fines against two others.

Although concerns have been raised about nursing homes because a large number of the state’s deaths from COVID-19 have occurred there, these inspections occurred before the pandemic and have nothing to do with the virus. 

Twin Lakes, in Greensburg, was cited following an inspection Aug. 8 and also was fined $8,750. In a 113-page report, an inspector cited problems that included allowing an inappropriate patient to self-medicate, not storing and labeling medication properly and dirty kitchen facilities.

A provisional license means the facility has failed to correct items found during an inspection and it can be inspected every six months rather than yearly. After three provisional licenses, a facility can be closed.

The agency also fined Baldwin Health Center $7,500 after a Sept. 22 inspection for medication that wasn’t marked appropriately and for not maintaining a proper infection control program, among other things.

Uniontown Healthcare and Rehabilitation Center was fined $13,750 following an Oct. 7 inspection for not providing proper monitoring for at least one patient injured in a fall.

Overall, fines for sanctions at 10 homes across the state totaled $93,500. The facilities all have agreed to pay the fines.
