COVID-19 Update: Pa. reports nearly 800 new cases, Allegheny County has 61

Pennsylvania health officials on Saturday reported nearly 800 new cases of COVID-10, with Allegheny County accounting for 61 of those infections.

 

With the latest data, the county’s total is now at 9,817 cases of the virus since the pandemic began in mid-March. Statewide, the total number of cases is 128,429, including the 796 new infections reported Saturday.

The newest Allegheny County cases come from 956 tests administered Aug. 9 through Aug. 21. The cases range in age from 5 months to 96 years with a median age of 31. Since the pandemic began, 922 people in Allegheny County have been hospitalized.

Allegheny also reported two new deaths: one person in their 50s and one in their 60s. Both died Aug. 19 and both were associated with a long-term care facility. The county death toll now stands at 306.

The state health department reported 18 new virus-related deaths Saturday, bringing the statewide total to 7,576 people.
