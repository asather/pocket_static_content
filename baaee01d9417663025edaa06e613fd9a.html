Treat Mom to a great restaurant meal, takeout style

Mother’s Day tradition will be broken for many moms this year because restaurants remain shuttered.

While in some states and regions, restaurants can open provided social distancing measures are observed, the situation is unclear in Pittsburgh. Gov. Tom Wolf has announced that some lockdown measures in some parts of the state will be relaxed starting Friday, but it is not known yet whether restaurants in the area will be allowed to open on Sunday.

Even if restaurants are open, they might be able to handle only a fraction of the usual clientele because the tables need to be six feet apart. And many are just fearful to dine out yet. Also, if Mom falls into a high-risk category, it would be unwise to take her out.

As a result, dining out is ruled out for many moms. But that doesn’t mean they have to slave over a hot stove.

Restaurants are trying to stay afloat by selling takeout food. So what better time to support local business and show Mom some love at the same time?

Make sure to call ahead, or order online if available. Many restaurants offer delivery through GrubHub, DoorDash or Uber Eats. Some are doing free delivery within a certain radius and some offer curbside takeout. So be sure to ask about your pickup and delivery options when you order.

Here is a sampling of takeout options that accommodate different budgets:

Affordable budget

Bonefish Grill, Upper St. Clair or McCandless: The Mahi & Shrimp Family Bundle ($49.90) feeds up to five people and includes a choice of house or Caesar salad, garlic mashed potatoes, seasonal veggies, fresh bread with pesto, and a dessert duo of classic cheesecake slices and cookies. The bundle is available Friday through Sunday. 

Eat’n Park, various locations: It’s that time of the year again! Eat’n Park is making its famous strawberry pies. Order a whole pie for $11.99. Many of the regular menu items are available too, including Whale of a Cod, chicken tenders and fried chicken meals, Breakfast Smiles ($7.99), Classic Eat’n Park Salad with fries on top ($10.49), Original Superburger ($6.99 with one side) and, of course, Smiley Cookies ($7.49 for a dozen). 

KLVN Coffee Lab, Homewood: If Mom is really just about her morning caffeine and you’re on a serious budget, why not order her Front Porch Coffee ($5) — a drop-off of the day’s batch brew. Delivery is available in neighborhoods near Homewood, including Larimer, East Liberty and Squirrel Hill. Flower bouquets, letterpress cards from Meshworks Press and a variety of pastries will be available as well. 8 a.m. to 3 p.m. 

Max’s Allegheny Tavern, North Side: Give Mom a slice of German-Pittsburgh food. Knackwurst, bratwurst and weisswurst (white German veal sausage) sandwiches are $8.99 and come with french fries. Other sandwich choices include pastrami, fish and Reuben. Dinner platters of knackwurst, bratwurst and weisswurst are $10.99 each and include sides. Noon to 7 p.m. 412-231-1899.

Pabellon, Banksville: The Venezuelan-Mexican restaurant is offering 10 percent off on all its takeout orders during the pandemic. Options include empanadas ($4), arepas ($7.50-$9), tacos ($4) and enchiladas ($10-$14 for three), along with a variety of meats such as steak, grilled or pulled chicken, barbacoa, chorizo, chicken with mole sauce, sauteed beef, fish, shrimp, al pastor (pork with pineapple) and ground beef. Vegetarian options are available, too. Mother’s Day hours are 11:30 a.m. to 8 p.m. 412-344-1122.

Thai Gourmet, Bloomfield: Specialties include spring rolls ($3.50), pork dumplings ($7), wonton and tum yum soups ($4), lotus root salad ($8.50), duck curry ($14), Japanese eggplant stir-fry ($12), pineapple yellow curry fried rice ($12) and General Tso’s chicken or tofu ($12). Mother’s Day hours are 4 to 9 p.m. 412-681-4373.

Midrange budget

Caliente Pizza & Draft House, various locations: Heart-shaped pizzas, usually available for Valentine’s Day, are back for Mother’s Day. Order a heart-shaped pizza and a bouquet of flowers for $24. The local pizza chain has locations in Bloomfield, Hampton, Mt. Lebanon, Aspinwall, Monroeville and Green Tree. 

LeoGreta, Carnegie: The takeout menu includes an antipasti plate ($15), several salads ($6), some pasta dishes including gnocchi and manicotti ($16) and desserts ($7) such as coconut cream or key lime pie. Takeout wines by the bottle and beers by the four-pack and six-pack are available. Open 3 to 8 p.m. 412-489-6382.

Osteria Pasqualino, Murrysville or Huntington: The extensive menu includes soups, salads, small orders or pasta, family-size pasta orders, chicken, beef, fish, sandwiches and pizzas. There are some differences between the menus at the two locations, so check to make sure you’re looking at the proper menu. 

Sakura Teppanyaki and Sushi, Squirrel Hill: The extensive menu includes items like baked salmon in a miso marinade ($19.95), dinner sushi and maki combos ($17.95-$39.95, served with miso soup and house salad), hibachi entrees ($16.95-$29.95 with miso soup, salad and fried rice), Chinese dishes and a long list of appetizers. Noon to 9 p.m.; order early because delivery (within three miles) takes between 60 and 90 minutes. 

Spello, Cranberry: Family-style dinners are available for $15 per person; orders can be placed for anywhere between  three and eight people. Entree choices are grilled garlic and fennel sausage with rosemary roasted potatoes, spaghetti and meatballs and rigatoni primavera. Each dinner package includes baked focaccia and a choice of one side item. Monday’s Day hours are 4 to 9 p.m. 724-553-5212.

Walnut Grill, various locations: The restaurants in Wexford, Robinson, Fox Chapel and Bridgeville have a few differences in their menus. Options include a range of appetizers, soups, salads, sandwiches, flatbreads, tacos, bowl meals, entrees and desserts. Treat Mom to Buffalo chicken wrap with a side ($12.50), Korean BBQ Salmon Bowl ($16.75), Parmesan-crusted chicken and gnocchi ($18.75 with a salad), filet mignon with side and vegetable ($31.50). 

Splurges

Acorn, Shadyside: For a breakfast splurge, order Mom a half-dozen bagels ($15), which come in sesame, sea salt, onion or “everything” varieties, and a topper like smoked salmon schmear or smoked trout schmear ($14 for 8 ounces) or caper schmear ($9 for 8 ounces). Add some beignets ($10 for a half dozen) and a mimosa kit — a quart of fresh-squeezed orange juice and a bottle of Revino Prosecco ($28).

Bella Sera, Canonsburg: The extensive Mother’s Day menu features salads, soups, casseroles and desserts. A sample menu you could create for four people could include Mediterranean salad ($17.95), quart of shrimp bisque (($14.95), half-dozen ready-to-bake rolls ($4.95), jumbo crab cakes ($29.95), roasted Yukon gold potatoes with truffle oil and Parmesan ($15.95) and tiramisu cake ($15.95). Order online; pick up on Saturday at the time you choose when ordering.

Black Radish Kitchen, Point Breeze: Chef Kate Romane has made a transition to stocking her Little Radish Market with prepared foods and grocery items during the pandemic. Her “Mama’s Day Brunch Love” package for four includes a milkbread roll with caraway salt, cold poached salmon with salsa verde, torta rustica with genoa salami and ricotta, a spring salad with asparagus and cucumber, rampy twice-baked potato mash, bacon and a chocolate mayo cake with berry whipped cream. The $160 price tag includes delivery, which will be on Saturday. 

DiAnoia’s Eatery, Strip District: The six-course menu-to-go features asparagus and fennel spring salad, five-cheese lasagna, saffron and spring pea risotto, braised veal with herb sauce, rosemary focaccia and assorted Italian cookies. The cost is $40 per person, and the meal comes with instructions for reheating. Order in advance and receive a designated pick-up time for Saturday. Beer and wine are available for an extra charge. 

Grand Concourse, Station Square: Family meals for four come with bread and dessert and include such entree choices as shrimp scampi pasta ($69), crab-stuffed salmon with potato and vegetables ($99) and prime rib with vegetables and potatoes ($119). Single dinners are available from a limited menu. 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. 412-261-1717.

The Hartwood Restaurant, Hampton: Two meal packages — brunch and dinner — are available, each designed to feed four to six people. The $90 brunch package includes four cinnamon rolls with cream cheese icing, a 9-inch Goat Rodeo Hootenanny frittata with ham (or vegetarian option with caramelized onions), a pint of shrimp and crab salad, a quart of beef tips and mushrooms with a quart of herbed rice pilaf (or the vegetarian option of Goat Rodeo chevre ravioli), and a cheese and charcuterie plate.

For $130, the dinner package includes a 16-ounce filet of beef with horseradish cream and onion jam, roasted asparagus and bell peppers, a pint of shrimp and crab salad, creamy cheese potatoes, a cherry cobbler and a cheese and charcuterie plate. Order by Wednesday; pick up is between 11 a.m. and 4 p.m. on Mother’s Day. 412-767-3500, or e-mail randy@hartwoodrestaurant.com.

Paris 66, East Liberty: Give Mom the gift of French comfort food. The $66 special serves six to eight people and includes a quiche Lorraine or Florentine, two baguettes and salad. A bottle of bubbly can be added for $30. The regular to-go menu, listed online, also is available. Mother’s Day hours are 10 a.m. to 8 p.m. 412-404-8166.

Food news

ServSafe online training: Food service managers, chefs and others working in the food industry need not fear if their ServSafe certificates are about to expire. There’s now an online course they can take in place of the former on-site course. The course still requires a 75% pass rate on a multiple-choice exam. English and Spanish sessions are offered. Extension.psu.edu/tap-online.

Food guides: Farm to Table’s “2020 Western PA Local Food Guide” is available at East End Food Co-op, or find the lists of farms, grocery stores, bakeries, butchers, CSA providers and other food businesses on the Western Pennsylvania food guide site. 

Also, as a response to COVID-19, Chatham University is working on a Pennsylvania Farm Product Directory featuring the location, hours and other details about local farms. To see the directory, go to craft.chatham.edu and click on “COVID-19.” Farmers can click the “Add your farm” button to add to the directory.
