COVID-19 Update: Allegheny County surge continues with 218 new cases

For the second time in less than a week, Allegheny County has reported a single-day increase of more than 200 new COVID-19 cases.

With 218 new cases reported on Monday, the county total now stands at 3,775 since the first case was diagnosed in mid-March. In the last seven days, the county has had an increase of 1,176 new cases. 

According to the county health department, the latest results are from tests taken between June 24 and July 5. Of the positive cases, 432 — or 11% — are healthcare workers who have become infected.

Western Pennsylvania saw an increase of 315 new cases. Westmoreland had the second highest increase for Monday with 27 additional cases. 

The county on Monday also reported one new death due to the contagious virus, bringing the death toll to 188. Data released from the state on Sunday has the death toll for the county at 190.

There were an additional four hospitalizations for a total of 416 past or present hospitalizations, 162 of those were admitted to intensive care units and 77 needed mechanical ventilation.

Allegheny County is continuing to ask residents to limit travel and errands to only those that are necessary. In response to a spike in cases over the previous week, county officials ordered all bars, restaurants and casinos to close for a week, starting last Friday, and prohibited gatherings of more than 25 people.

Statewide, there was an increase of 450 positive cases reported on Monday, with one additional death. The only virus related death in the state was reported in Bucks County, according to state health department data.

Pennsylvania now has 90,304 cases of COVID-19 and 6,754 deaths as a result of the virus.
