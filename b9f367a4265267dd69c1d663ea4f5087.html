Pitt gives students option to forgo letter grades

The University of Pittsburgh on Friday joined other universities in saying students will have the option of forgoing letter grades this semester, given the upheaval caused by the COVID-19 pandemic that has emptied Pitt’s five campuses and required a switch to remote instruction.

Provost Ann Cudd outlined the changes in a message to faculty.

“After the conclusion of this term, all undergraduate and graduate students will be permitted to convert any of their courses during this spring term from a letter grade basis to a satisfactory (S) or no credit (NC),” she wrote.

“Moreover, all courses that are awarded S grades will count toward graduation or degree requirements and satisfy future course prerequisites, while those courses awarded NC grades will not negatively impact a student's GPA.”

Pitt has 34,000 students on its main Oakland campus and branches in Bradford, Johnstown, Greensburg and Titusville.

Carnegie Mellon this week said its students now have the option of switching to pass/ fail. Penn State University is weighing a recommendation from its Faculty Senate to allow some courses to be transitioned to satisfactory/unsatisfactory.

The moves come as professors and students on campuses in Pennsylvania and the nation adapt to a new reality of online and other remote instruction to slow the spread of the sometimes deadly virus.

Face-to-face instruction has been suspended on Pittsburgh-area campuses and elsewhere, and both Pitt and Carnegie Mellon announced this week plans to drastically reduce their operations.

Pitt earlier this week postponed commencement, as did Penn State.

The message from Ms. Cudd touched on a variety of topics from tenure extensions for faculty to plans for summer programming.
