Man dies of injuries two days after Homewood assault

A man assaulted by two people in Homewood North on Friday evening died of his injuries in the hospital on Sunday, authorities announced on Monday.

Police say that Roger Brown, 63, was assaulted near the 7200 block of Frankstown Avenue in Homewood North on Friday by Marlon Johnson, 31, and his cousin, Madera Johnson, 47.

When police and EMS arrived at the scene at around 4:40 p.m., they found Mr. Brown unresponsive and had him transported to a local hospital in critical condition. The affidavit of probable cause for Marlon Johnson said that Mr. Brown was bleeding from his head.

Mr. Brown died at the hospital two days later.

The affidavit implies that Mr. Brown and Madera Johnson had an antagonistic history.

An eyewitness to the crime, identified as Witness 1, told police that Madera Johnson had robbed Mr. Brown of $20 the week prior, and that when the two randomly met on Frankstown Avenue near the Rosing Lounge on Friday, Mr. Brown called her an expletive.

The assault was captured on a camera positioned above Frankstown Avenue, according to the affidavit.

Witness 1 was later able to identify both Marlon and Madera Johnson, who was also found and arrested Saturday.

The cousins have criminal histories stretching back to the late 2000s, including several other assault and reckless endangerment charges, among others.
