3 protesters charged in clash with diners in Downtown Pittsburgh

Three people protesting racial injustice have been charged in connection with a confrontation with diners earlier this month in Downtown Pittsburgh in which the protesters were seen yelling and breaking a glass.

The Sept. 5 incident outside Sienna Mercato restaurant on Penn Avenue — which started as a peaceful protest and march — was captured on videos that went viral on social media.

Monique ‘Nique’ Craft, 35, of the South Side; Kenneth McDowell, 33, of the North Side; and Shawn Green, 24, who also goes by the name of Lorenzo Rulli and is considered homeless according to police, were charged with multiple misdemeanors Monday. Craft, who identifies as nonbinary, is charged by summons with theft, conspiracy and simple trespass; Mr. McDowell is charged with possessing instruments of crime, conspiracy, harassment and two counts of disorderly conduct; and Mr. Green faces charges of disorderly conduct, conspiracy, criminal mischief and simple trespass. 

Police issued arrest warrants for Mr. McDowell and Mr. Green, a criminal complaint states, because their appearance in court is “not guaranteed.” A preliminary hearing for Craft is scheduled for Nov. 3 at City Court in Downtown, according to court records.

The trio engaged in a course of disorderly conduct from the McDonald's at 500 Liberty Avenue to the Sienna Mercato restaurant on Penn Avenue, police said in the criminal complaint. They used a megaphone and offensive language in an attempt to cause violence among patrons, police said. They also harassed bicyclists to the point that they had to defend themselves, the complaint said. 

The videos, which have been viewed millions of times on Facebook and Twitter, showed anti-racism protesters shouting profanities at diners at the restaurant. In the footage, some people who are eating outside begin to leave their tables.

Police said Craft approaches a table and drinks a diner’s beverage. A pair of patrons look on as the beer is consumed, and Craft sets the beer back on the table.

Another protester approaches and swipes a glass off a table, causing it to shatter on the ground.

Another video shows a protester — identified later in court paperwork as Mr. McDowell — yelling through a megaphone at a man holding a bicycle. The bicyclist smacks the megaphone, and another protester hits the bicyclist in the head with a skateboard. It is not clear from the video what started the confrontation.

Warning to our readers: The footage in these two videos contains violence and graphic language.

Craft said the videos show only one side of the story and that there were three men from outside the protest who were screaming “Blue lives matter” — in support of police — at the protesters. When some protesters began to confront them, one man began swinging a bike at people.

According to Craft’s account, a man began grabbing Craft’s hair and placed his hand on Craft’s chest, saying he was trying to stop her from engaging with another man.

When the confrontation ended, Craft said, a man in the restaurant began condemning the protesters’ actions. The woman who was with him offered Craft to come over, have a beer and talk about the situation.

President Donald Trump weighed in on the incidents several days later, publicly calling the protesters “thugs.”

In a pair of tweets, Mr. Trump said the protesters’ actions were “disgraceful.” 

“[Black Lives Matter] Protesters horribly harass elderly Pittsburgh diners, scaring them with loud taunts while taking their food right off their plate,” Mr. Trump wrote in one tweet. “These Anarchists, not protesters, are [Joe] Biden voters, but he has no control and nothing to say. Disgraceful. Never seen anything like it. Thugs!”
