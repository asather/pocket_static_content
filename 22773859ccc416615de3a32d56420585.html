Allegheny County marks fifth straight day of triple-digit increase in COVID-19 cases

Allegheny County marked the fifth straight day of triple-digit increases in positive COVID-19 cases on Monday, but no new deaths for the second day in a row.

The county health department reported 180 new cases of the virus and two new hospitalizations. The last time the county saw a daily increase under 100 was on July 22, where the health department reported 96 new cases.

The 180 new cases come from 1,929 tests that were administered between July 9 and July 26. They range in age from 2 to 101 years old, with a median age of 45.

The county has had 7,474 positive cases since the first recorded case in mid-March.

Pennsylvania reported an additional 839 new cases for Monday, bringing the state total to 108,264 cases of COVID-19. Western Pennsylvania accounted for 256 of the newest cases. Allegheny County had the highest increase in the region, followed by Washington County with 12 new cases.

The Pennsylvania Health Department estimates that about 7,974 of the positive cases are among healthcare workers. 

The two new hospitalizations reported in Allegheny County Monday bring the county to 583 past or present hospitalizations. Currently, across the state there are 704 people hospitalized for COVID-19.

According to data from the state health department there were 4 new deaths across the state, bringing the death toll to 7,122. There are no new deaths reported in Western Pennsylvania for Monday. 

The average number of daily cases for the county from July 21 to July 27 is about 148 cases per day. This is down from about 200 cases a day for the previous week, dating July 14 to 20. The state though saw an increase in the average number of cases per day, going from about 857 cases to about 932 for the past week.
