Some Universal movies, including "Trolls World Tour," will get early video-on-demand releases

If the audience cannot get to the movies, the movies will go to them.

NBCUniversal announced Monday it will make available as video on demand (VOD) a number of its current and upcoming headliners. “Trolls World Tour” has an April 10 theatrical release and will be available for home rental that day.

Other films currently in theaters, including “The Hunt” and “The Invisible Man,” could be available as early as this weekend.

Citing unprecedented closings of movie houses due to the threat of the novel coronavirus, The Hollywood Reporter indicates Universal’s films will be available on various on-demand services for a 48-hour rental period. Price is suggested at $19.99 in the United States. “Emma,” a Focus Feature, will also be an early release.

THR reports this is not a blanket policy; releases will be made on a film-by-film basis.
