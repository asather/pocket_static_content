VA Pittsburgh reports first patient death from COVID-19

WASHINGTON — The U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs reported the first patient death due to COVID-19 in Pittsburgh, according to the department’s online statistics updated Friday.

A spokesperson for the Pittsburgh VA Medical Center in Oakland, where the patient death occurred, confirmed the in-patient death.

“Of course, our thoughts and prayers are with the veteran’s family,” Shelley Nulph, a spokeswoman for the VA Pittsburgh Medical Center, wrote in a statement. She declined to provide more information, citing privacy laws and “out of respect for the veteran's family.”

The VA’s national database showed that there are currently 21 patients at the University Drive hospital who have tested positive for COVID-19. 

The hospital reported its first positive COVID-19 case on March 26. At that time, the VA’s nationwide statistics showed that 484 patients across the country had tested positive and that six patients had died.

In the last three weeks, those nationwide figures have grown to more than 5,000 positive cases as of Friday’s updates, with more than 300 deaths across more than 120 health care facilities.

VA facilities in New Orleans have been the hardest hit, with 436 positive cases and 30 deaths.
