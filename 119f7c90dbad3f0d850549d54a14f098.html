AIU experiences malware attack on servers

The Allegheny Intermediate Unit said Thursday that an unknown entity recently encrypted portions of its network with malware and demanded a ransom payment in exchange for the software to be released.

The agency said it worked with third-party forensic specialists to investigate and restore the network, and they have not found evidence that the entity accessed or acquired any personal or protected information stored on the servers. 

“The AIU had backup versions of the most critical information and was able to restore access to the vast majority of the impacted files without engaging or paying the intruder,” the agency said in a statement.

The investigation, however, remains ongoing, and the AIU said it will provide formal notification to anyone whose sensitive information was contained on the serves involved in the incident.

The AIU said it was reviewing its policies to prevent similar incidents in the future.

A spokeswoman for the AIU declined to comment on whether the agency had contacted law enforcement.

The AIU is a public education agency that provides services, resources and research to Allegheny County's 42 suburban school districts as well as non-public, charter and vocational-technical schools.
