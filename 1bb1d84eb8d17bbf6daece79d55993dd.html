Shursave IGA in Bloomfield to reopen as Community Market

Bloomfield will be keeping a grocery store after all, just under a different name. 

The Shursave IGA on Liberty Avenue is expected to close later this month and will reopen under the Community Market banner, according to O’Hara-based Echo Realty, which bought the property in January for a sale price of $5.78 million.

“Echo Realty is aware of the value and need of a grocery store in the Bloomfield neighborhood,” the firm said in a press release.” Giant Eagle, the grocery chain that also is affiliated with Echo, will act as the interim operator of the store.

“The plan is for the Community Market to serve Bloomfield residents until Echo Realty’s long-  term site redevelopment plans are realized. During this time, Echo Realty will also begin the dialogue with community leaders regarding those future plans for the property,” the firm said.

It has been a couple years since one plan to redevelop the Bloomfield Shursave IGA had fallen through. Indianapolis-based Milhaus proposed turning the site into a 200-plus unit apartment complex in 2018, but the plans never materialized.

Echo Realty has over 230 properties totaling more than 9 million square feet along the East Coast and Midwest.
