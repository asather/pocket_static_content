Duke's Mike Krzyzewski sticks up for Pitt's Jeff Capel with tirade at student section

Duke students drew the ire of their coach in an uncharacteristic outburst during the Blue Devils’ win against Pitt on Tuesday night. 

Late in the first half, students began chanting “sit with us” at Panthers coach Jeff Capel, a Duke alum and former assistant coach. That prompted his former boss, Mike Krzyzewski, to holler an admonishment from across the court. “Shut up” he appeared to yell loudly several times, before a referee intervened. 

His fury continued at the end of the half, when he strode over to the students and reportedly yelled “he’s one of us,” referring to Capel. 

After the game, Krzyzewski apologized and explained that he interpreted the chant as personal. 

“I’m not going to go say, ‘Will you please tell me exactly what you’re doing,’ ” Krzyzewski said. “So it’s a mistake on my part. But I’d rather make the mistake in protection of my guy. And then I went just at the end of the half and said ‘Look, he’s our guy. He’s our guy.’ That’s it. So I apologize.” 

The game was Capel’s first at Duke as Pitt coach. He is seen by some as a potential successor to the legendary Krzyzewski, who is 72.

Capel, for his part, said that he wasn’t sure what the Duke coach was yelling about.

“I think he was trying to tell me what happened,” he said. “I honestly didn’t know. He was maybe trying to apologize for what happened.”
