County fines U.S. Steel more than $360K for violations at Clairton plant

This story was updated at 9:00 p.m. on May 28, 2020.

The Allegheny County Health Department on Wednesday fined U.S. Steel $361,400 for over 300 air pollution and permit violations at the Clairton Coke Works dating back to October.

The violations outlined in the health department’s letter include fines for coke oven processes such as pushing and soaking. Those fines are based on the numbers laid out in a June 2019 consent agreement.

U. S. Steel is reviewing the county’s request for payment and verifying that the data are consistent with the agreement. The company is “committed to working with ACHD and all stakeholders on improving air quality,” officials wrote in a release.

The six-figure fine follows a $743,625 penalty levied against the company in January for more than 400 air pollution violations between April and September 2019.

“U.S. Steel’s environmental performance is continuing to improve, as a result of the hard work and dedication of our Mon Valley Works employees,” reads a statement from U.S. Steel spokesperson Meghan M. Cox. “The Clairton Plant had a record-breaking month in April for battery stack opacity compliance, which is monitored 24 hours, seven days a week.”

Matthew Mehalik, the executive director of the clean air advocacy organization Breathe Project, has been unsatisfied with the fine system that has come out of the 2019 agreement.

“It’s less expensive for them to pay the fine than it is to actually raise the capital and do the necessary repairs so that all of those pollution emission stop,” Mr. Mehalik said. The need for clean air is even more urgent during the COVID-19 crisis, since air pollution has been found to make the disease more deadly.

“What’s required is repairing coke batteries that are very old, that leak and release emission that reach outside of what U.S. Steel is allowed to emit,” Mr. Mehalik said.

Per the consent agreement, 90% of the penalties, totaling $325,260, should be paid to the Community Benefit Trust, which benefits Clairton, Glassport, Liberty, Lincoln and Port Vue. The remaining $36,140 of the potential penalties is to be paid to the Allegheny Clean Air Fund.

U.S. Steel has 30 days to make the payment.

The Clairton Coke Works is the largest in the country. It employs 1,200 people, and produces about 4.3 million tons of coke every year.
