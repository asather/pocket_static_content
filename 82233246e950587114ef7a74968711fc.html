Pa. Supreme Court rules in favor of mail-in ballot extension

Ballots that are postmarked by 8 p.m. on Election Day and received by counties in the three days following the election will be counted, the state Supreme Court ruled Thursday.

In a 63-page majority opinion http://www.pacourts.us/assets/files/page-1305/file-10077.pdf, Justice Max Baer wrote that mail-in and absentee ballots mailed by voters using the United States Postal Service — so long as they are “valid and received by the county boards of election on or before 5 p.m. on Nov. 6, 2020” — shall be counted.

The court also ruled that counties are allowed to collect hand-delivered ballots at locations “other than their office addresses, including drop-boxes,” Justice Baer wrote.

Before this ruling, the hard deadline for counties to receive ballots was 8 p.m. — the time traditional polls close — on Election Day.

Drop boxes for ballots, intended to give voters more options to return their ballots, were the subject of a months-long legal battle, in which the Trump campaign — among other entities — sought to limit their use because of allegations they’re susceptible to fraud and abuse.
