Cranberry fire department remembers brothers who died just hours apart

Two local firefighters passed away within hours of one another last week. The pair, who dedicated their lives to the Cranberry Township Volunteer Fire Company, were honored in a last call ceremony on Sunday in Cranberry.

Mark Nanna passed away on Wednesday due to complications of an infection, according to KDKA-TV, the Pittsburgh Post-Gazette’s news partner.

After hearing of his brother’s death, Lee Nanna died on Thursday morning from a heart attack, KDKA reported.

Mark, 56, served as fire chief as well as fire prevention administrator for the township’s Building and Zoning Codes Department. Lee, 69, was a former president of the fire company.

Cranberry Township Fire Department member Gary Brewer told KDKA that many believe “Lee died of a broken heart.” 

Public visitation hours will take place 3 to 8 p.m. Monday at Boylan-Glenn-Kildoon Funeral Home in Cranberry.

A funeral will be held at 11 a.m. Tuesday at Dutilh United Methodist Church in Cranberry. A luncheon will follow the interment.
