Steelers WR Diontae Johnson practices after missing previous session with a foot injury

Second-year receiver Diontae Johnson was back on the practice field Friday one day after missing practice due to a foot injury. It was a bit of good news for the Steelers, as they already know they’ll be without starting right guard David DeCastro, who will miss the opener with a knee injury.

Johnson first appeared on the injury report Thursday, but it appears the starting receiver opposite JuJu Smith-Schuster will be able to play.

“He means a lot to us,” Smith-Schuster said. “He knows the playbook, he can play inside and outside. He did a great job today. He’s going to be a huge asset for this offense moving forward.”

The Steelers will release the injury report later this afternoon.
