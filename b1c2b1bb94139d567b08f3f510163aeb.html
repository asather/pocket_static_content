Scottdale crane hauler pleads guilty in fatal accident in Pine

A Scottdale man has pleaded guilty to manslaughter in connection with a collision on Gibsonia Road in Pine. The crash, in March of 2018, killed a pickup truck driver.

Charles Wayne Hunter, 71, appeared before Allegheny County Common Pleas Judge Randall Todd Monday. He pleaded guilty to involuntary manslaughter and recklessly endangering another person.

In exchange for the plea, the District Attorney’s office withdrew a homicide by vehicle charge.

Both sides agreed to a sentence of two years of house arrest and five years probation, with the first two years of probation overlapping with the house arrest.

On March 12, 2018, Hunter was traveling eastbound on Gibsonia Road hauling a crane. While rounding a bend between Karrington Drive and the Pennsylvania Turnpike, he lost control of his pickup truck and the 43,500-pound crane attached. According to the criminal complaint, his crane began to tip over, and he crossed the double yellow line into the oncoming lane of traffic.

The crane first struck a tractor-trailer combination, forcing the trailer through the guardrail, tearing off its left front tire and causing major damage to an attached 18,000-pound log skidder, police said.

Still going approximately 50 mph, the crane landed on top of another pickup truck.

Jay Watts, the driver, was pronounced dead at the scene of head trauma.

Just before coming to a stop, the crane slid into a third vehicle — a Chevrolet pickup truck.

According to police reconstruction, Mr. Hunter was traveling between 53 and 57 mph in a 35-mph zone. The bend in the road was marked with a speed limit of 25 mph.

Police also said Hunter was operating an unlicensed crane company at the time of the crash.
