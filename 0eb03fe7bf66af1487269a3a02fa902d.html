City Council extends COVID-19 emergency declaration until Aug. 25

Pittsburgh City Council on Tuesday voted 8-0 to extend the cityâ€™s COVID-19 emergency declaration until Aug. 25.

Mayor Bill Peduto declared the disaster emergency on March 13.

The state of emergency, supported by state law, allows the city to cancel or limit large gatherings, limit city permits, cancel or limit some city services, and cancel or limit public and private activities that could increase exposure or transmission of disease.

COVID-19 cases in Allegheny County have soared into the hundreds since June 30, with cases reaching above 200 for the past three days. County officials on Thursday ordered a one-week closure of bars, dine-in restaurants and casinos, and limited gatherings to 25 or less.

Council must vote to keep the emergency status in effect at least every seven days. 

The body will go on summer recess from July 29 to August 20.
