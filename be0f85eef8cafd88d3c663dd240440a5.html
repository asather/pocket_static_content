COVID-19 Update: Pa. reports nearly 700 new infections, Pittsburgh region up by 206

Nearly 700 new cases of COVID-19 were reported statewide on Friday as Allegheny County topped 300 coronavirus-related deaths.

Of the latest infections, 206 are in Western Pennsylvania, with Allegheny having the highest increase — 80 new cases — followed by Beaver County with 38 new cases. 

The new infections in Allegheny County come from 1,397 tests that were administered between Aug. 11 and Aug. 20. Those who have recently tested positive range in age from 1 to 98, with a median age of 46.

With the newly reported cases, the county’s total now stands at 9,756 cases of the virus since the pandemic began in mid-March. Statewide, the total number of cases is now 127,633.

The state health department reported 20 new virus-related deaths, seven of which were in Western Pennsylvania. Of those, six occurred in Allegheny County and one was in Washington County.

Of the six new deaths in Allegheny, four were at long-term-care facilities. The dates of the six deaths range between Aug. 13 and Aug. 19. The newly reported deaths were patients in their 50s, 60s and 70s, two people in their 80s and one person in their 90s, health officials said. The county death toll is now 304.

In Pennsylvania, 7,558 people have died overall as a result of the virus. 

Based on the most recent state data, the county has 91 people currently hospitalized for the virus. Since the pandemic began, 915 people in Allegheny County have been hospitalized.
