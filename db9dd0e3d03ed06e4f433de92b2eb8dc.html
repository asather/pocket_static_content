Greene County reports 1st COVID-19 death; Allegheny reports 12 cases, but no deaths

This story was updated at 12:45 p.m.

Nearly 1,000 new cases of COVID-19 were reported in Pennsylvania, according to the latest statistics released Sunday.

Meanwhile, Greene County reported its first death, which means each county in the southwestern corner of the state has at least one fatality as a result of the viral disease.

Allegheny County, which has the most cases and deaths in Western Pennsylvania, reported no new deaths and 12 new cases of COVID-19, according to the latest figures released on Sunday.

With the new totals, the county has had 1,345 COVID-19 cases —1,291 confirmed and 54 probable — and 102 deaths — 92 confirmed and 10 probable — since the first case was announced on March 14.

Allegheny County’s daily case totals have been in general decline since March 30; total deaths, while showing some dramatic spikes and troughs, also reflect a declining trend since April 20, according to county data.

To date, there have been 238 hospitalizations in the county. Of those, 101 required a visit to the intensive care unit and 61 needed a mechanical ventilator. The county has administered a 18,172 tests, with 16,851 resulting in a negative result.

Meanwhile, the Pennsylvania Department of Health announced Sunday that there were 1,962 additional positive cases of COVID-19 statewide for a total of 49,267 cases to date. There also were 26 new deaths, raising the statewide total to 2,444. Philadelphia continues to have the highest totals in the state with 13,179 cases and 423 deaths.
