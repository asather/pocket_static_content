COVID-19 Update: Pa. reports 667 new cases, Allegheny County sees 177 more

For a fourth straight day, Allegheny County on Friday reported a triple-digit increase in new daily COVID-19 cases, with 177 more infections. Those were among a spike of 667 new diagnoses reported statewide.

The new data come a day after Allegheny County hit a record 233 daily cases -— the first time new positive cases had eclipsed the 200 mark. Since the county reported its first confirmed case of COVID-19 on March 14, it has had a total of 3,280 cases.

There was no change in the county’s death toll, which remains at 187. Pennsylvania’s health department is reporting 188 deaths for the county.

There’s been an upswing in newly reported cases for the past two weeks. County health officials said the percentage of positive coronavirus tests has now doubled to 10% from a week ago.

To contain the surge in infections, county health officials on Thursday ordered all bars, restaurants and casinos closed for a week starting Friday.

Eateries are allowed to continue take-out and delivery services during the week-long shutdown.

Gatherings of more than 25 people during that period also will be prohibited. Health director Dr. Debra Bogen recommended that residents stay at home to prevent transmission of the virus.

In another effort to slow down the rise in cases, the state made mask wearing a requirement anytime someone leaves their house, rather than just inside businesses.

Data released by Allegheny County also shows that there were five additional hospitalizations, bringing the total number to 406. Newly infected individuals in the county ranged in age from 1 to 90 years, with the median age of 28.

In the 20-county region of Western Pennsylvania, between Erie and Fayette counties, there was an increase of 296 cases of the highly infectious virus. The region’s second-highest daily increase was Westmoreland County, which reported 33 new cases.

Pennsylvania saw an increase of 34 deaths, with no new deaths reported in the Pittsburgh region. The statewide death toll is at 6,746, according to the latest figures from the state on Friday.

The state saw an increase of 667 new cases of the virus, bringing the total to 88,741.
