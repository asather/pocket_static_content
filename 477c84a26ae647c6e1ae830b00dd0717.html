People on the move: 3/2/2020

Associations & Organizations

Pittsburgh Young Professionals named new board members: President: Jacquie Foradori, business operations manager, Locomation; vice president: Tyler Andrews, marketing manager, We-EF Lighting USA; past president/nominating chair: Mackenzie Farone-Waite, senior manager of corporate communications, Greiner Bio-One; treasurer: Tony Weisenburger, assistant vice president, S&T Bank; administrative secretary and membership officer: Bernard Charles, Treasury Management Assistant for PNC; recording secretary: Danielle Bruno-McDermott, associate attorney, Schnader Harrison Segal & Lewis LLP; public relations adviser: Leanne Watson, recruiting manager, Mutual of Omaha Advisors; technology adviser: Ryan McGregor, developer, Rivers Agile; special projects adviser: Brad Fusco, partner and vice president, Morneau Shepell; social media adviser: Teagan Burkey, talent acquisition specialist, RDX; professional development adviser: Jackie Rodgers, area manager, Dale Carnegie Training; social adviser: Maria Fritzius, management recruiter, BNY Mellon; community outreach adviser: Matthew Horan, vice president, sales and marketing for Evolution E-Cycling; and analytics and research: Bryon Miller, analyst, Highmark.

Health

Marcia E. Klein-Patel, MD, PhD, was named chair of the Allegheny Health Network Women’s Institute. She succeeds Allan Klapper, MD, who is now president of AHN Wexford Hospital. Previously, Dr. Klein-Patel served as the vice chair of operations for the AHN Women’s Institute, as well as the obstetrics and gynecology residency program director at West Penn Hospital.

Information Technology

Apogee said Jake Rogers joined its executive leadership team as vice president of operations and service delivery.

Law

Philip J. Sbrolla joined as a principal in Post & Schell’s Commercial Litigation and Casualty Litigation practices. Mr. Sbrolla defends clients in state and federal court in matters involving general liability, premises liability, products liability, civil RICO, civil rights, catastrophic loss, D&O, class actions and employment.

Sherrard, German & Kelly, P.C. promoted William M. Andersen, Jeremy C. Brown, Jennifer P. Richnafsky and Megan Tymoczko-Korch to shareholders. Mr. Andersen is a member of its corporate and real estate services groups. He counsels companies and individuals in connection with transactional and risk management matters. Mr. Brown is a member of the corporate, real estate, energy and natural resources, cyber security and financial services groups. His practice includes corporate and business matters, commercial and real estate transactions, and technology services. Ms. Richnafsky is a member of its litigation, insurance coverage and employment services groups. She focuses her practice on complex commercial disputes, frequently representing highly-regulated clients throughout all stages of the litigation case cycle. Ms. Tymoczko-Korch is a member of its corporate services group. Her practice includes business and corporate transactions, including entity formations, corporate governance, reorganizations, mergers and acquisitions, real estate acquisition, leasing and financing.

Blank Rome LLP announced that Maria “Masha” Trainor joined its Pittsburgh office as a partner in its corporate, M&A, and securities group. She focuses her practice on international and domestic mergers and acquisitions, corporate governance matters, joint ventures, private debt and equity financings, commercial transactions, and contract management, as well as venture capital and private equity transactions.

Nonprofit

The Pittsburgh Technology Council announced that Dave Passavant, CEO of numo LLC, was elected to its board and Dione Graswick, partner at PricewaterhouseCoopers, was appointed to its executive committee.

Elsewhere

Beachwood, Ohio —  First Catholic Slovak Ladies Association of the United States of America reelected Pittsburgh native Cynthia M. Maleski to her third term as national president (CEO).

Awards and Honors

Phi Theta Kappa Honor Society named Evon Walters, president, CCAC Northwest Region, a 2020 recipient of the Paragon Award for new presidents. He is one of 28 college or campus presidents who will be recognized during PTK Catalyst 2020, the honor society’s annual convention, in April. The award is given to new college or campus presidents who have shown strong support of student success by recognizing academic achievement, leadership and service among high-achieving students. CCAC student chapter members and advisers nominated Mr. Walters, who is the only 2020 award recipient in Pennsylvania.

Susan Fullerton, Bicentennial Board of Visitors Faculty Fellow and assistant professor of chemical engineering at the University of Pittsburgh’s Swanson School of Engineering, was selected as a 2020 Alfred P. Sloan Research Fellow in Chemistry. The two-year, $75,000 fellowship recognizes researchers’ unique potential to make substantial contributions to their field. Ms. Fullerton’s fellowship will further her research on two-dimensional materials for next-generation electronics. The Alfred P. Sloan Foundation, founded in 1934 and named for the former president and CEO of the General Motors Corporation, makes grants to support research and education in science, technology, engineering, mathematics and economics.

Business notes

Franklin Madison Advisors, a new financial planning firm founded by Peter Donisanu, chief financial strategist and president, launched with the help of XY Planning Network the nation’s leading organization of fee-only financial planners serving Generation X and Generation Y clients.

The New Castle Community YMCA has been an anchor in Lawrence County for more than 150 years. With the dedication that the New Castle Community Y has to the residents of Lawrence County and our vision for the future, the Board of Directors has decided to change our name to the Lawrence County YMCA.
