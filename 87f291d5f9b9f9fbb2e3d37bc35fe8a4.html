Gov. Wolf extends stay-at-home order; Pa. reports 92 more COVID-19 deaths

Gov. Tom Wolf on Monday said he was extending Pennsylvania’s stay-at-home order to May 8 as efforts continue to slow the spread of COVID-19.

The governor, though, said the state would soon allow some activities to continue, such as online car sales and notary services. Construction — as long as workers follow social distancing guidelines — could also resume across the state in the near future. 

“Today, I want to announce that we are taking small steps toward regaining a degree of normalcy in Pennsylvania,” he said during a news conference. 

Mr. Wolf said the state will examine the safety of curb-side pickup at the 176 recently reopened wine and spirits stores to determine if the same option is feasible for other businesses. 

“We’ll closely monitor this to see if this can be done safely,” Mr. Wolf said. “While limiting person-to-person contact, not just at the stores but throughout the supply chain. This is going to help us determine if curb-side pickup can be more broadly applied to a much more diverse retail landscape as well as to other industries.”

Senate Bill 841, which Mr. Wolf said he would sign later Monday, will allow for online auto sales as well as online notary services. 

On May 8, the governor said said, the state will allow for limited construction statewide that follow “strict” social distancing guidelines.

Pennsylvania reported 92 more deaths from the COVID-19 pandemic while deaths rose by 13 across Western Pennsylvania, according to the latest release of data on Monday.

Statewide, the Pennsylvania Department of Health has reported a total of 1,204 deaths. An additional 948 cases were reported from Sunday, bringing the total number of cases to 33,232. Yesterday saw an increase of 1,628 cases from Saturday.

Deaths in Allegheny County rose by 5 to 55 total from Sunday, Beaver and Westmoreland counties each reported 4 new deaths.

Allegheny also saw a significant drop in new cases from the previous day. The new figures bring the county’s total to 1,042 cases of COVID-19, an increase of seven cases from the day before. Saturday-to-Sunday saw 26 new cases.

Allegheny’s totals are the highest in Western Pennsylvania. Since the first case was confirmed on March 14, the county has administered more than 12,383 tests.
