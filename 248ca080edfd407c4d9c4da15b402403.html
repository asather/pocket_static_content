Pittsburgh mosque holds drive-thru event to celebrate end of Ramadan

Muslims worldwide celebrated the Eid al-Fitr, the festival holiday marking the end of the fasting month of Ramadan. As with all things lately, measures were taken to celebrate the holiday, while mitigating the spread of the deadly coronavirus.

The Islamic Center of Pittsburgh, closed due to the pandemic, celebrated the Eid holiday with a drive-thru outside the Oakland mosque.

Participants were encouraged to decorate their cars and parade around the block and to other mosques. 

The drive-thru included to-go meals, toys, sweets and refreshments.
