Spring training: JT Riddle shines in leadoff spot as Pirates lose to Tigers

BRADENTON, Fla. — JT Riddle has made just 14 starts in the leadoff spot during his three-year career. You wouldn’t know it by how he performed during a 8-4 Pirates loss to the Tigers on Sunday in a split-squad game at LECOM Park.

The Pirates signed Riddle for $850,000 on Jan. 31 for extra infield depth, and he made a solid early statement in this one by drawing an 11-pitch walk to start, making a heads-up play on the bases, collecting two hits (including a triple) and scoring a run.

“I want to go out and give everything I’ve got and play as hard as I can for however long I’m out there,” said Riddle, a shortstop who started Sunday’s game at second base. “It’s the way I grew up, the way I’ve always played. Go out and play hard.”

On the mound

Derek Holland started and went 1 2/3 innings, striking out five and allowing two earned runs, the first courtesy of a leadoff home run from Detroit center fielder JaCoby Jones.

Holland tinkered with his pitches and kicked himself for not executing his last one — which got hit for a run-scoring double — but all in all came away pleased with the performance.

“I know I’m competing for a job, but I want to make sure I have everything where it needs to be,” Holland said. “If I make this team, I want to be prepared going into the season.”

The game could’ve been framed as an early battle between Holland and Steven Brault for the fifth starter spot, although Brault threw just eight pitches, the result of his slower-than-usual buildup this spring. Those eight pitches were executed well, however, giving Brault and the Pirates a 1-2-3 inning.

Keone Kela was electric in the fourth, striking out the side while hitting 96 mph and mixing in what was a really sharp curveball.

“I definitely wanted to start working on establishing my secondary pitches,” Kela said. “I know I have a fastball, but I also want to get guys off-balance to add more oomph to my fastball.”

At the plate

Some interesting stuff from 6-foot-7 shortstop prospect Oneil Cruz, who laced a double the opposite way to score a run in the second, then flailed at a pitch and struck out looking an inning later. In the second, Cruz was a smart read to score on a squibber to the left side from Riddle.

Gregory Polanco made his spring debut and went 0-for-2 with a walk, while Luke Maile had a pair of hits and threw out a runner at second base.

Will Craig avoided an injury scare during a botched call in the second inning. The pitch hit Craig in the hand, although home-plate umpire Laz Diaz thought it nicked Craig’s bat. After head athletic trainer and manager Derek Shelton made sure Craig was OK, the first baseman struck out looking.

 

The other group

Trevor Williams gave up four earned runs over two innings in a 4-3 loss to the Phillies at Spectrum Field in Clearwater, Fla. First baseman Jose Osuna finished with two hits, including a run-scoring single in the third inning. Kevin Newman went 1-for-3 with a run scored.

Taking the mound for the first time since April 22, 2019, Nick Burdi pitched 2/3 of an inning, giving up a hit and a walk with one strikeout.
