Penguins recall Anthony Angello and Andrew Agozzino

The Penguins on Thursday recalled forwards Anthony Angello and Andrew Agozzino from Wilkes-Barre/Scranton of the American Hockey League.

Angello leads the AHL club with 16 goals, three of them game-winners. The 6-foot-5, 210-pound winger was a fifth-round draft pick in 2014. After playing collegiately at Cornell, he has 32 goals and 22 assists in 112 AHL games.

The 23-year-old winger is looking to make his NHL debut with the Penguins.

Agozzino has recorded one point in nine games with the Penguins this season. The 29-year-old leads Wilkes-Barre/Scranton with 33 points and was named to the AHL’s All-Star game, though he did not play in that game this week.

The Penguins recalled the players on an emergency basis. We may get more clarity on the situation Thursday afternoon, when they practice in Cranberry.

Nick Bjugstad has been sidelined since having surgery in November to repair a core muscle injury but could return to practice soon. Dominik Kahun suffered a concussion Jan. 19 and missed the last game before the All-Star break.

The Penguins opted to recall Angello and Agozzino over other options such as Sam Lafferty and Joseph Blandisi, who were both sent down last week.
