Pittsburgh Public Schools cancels all March board meetings

Pittsburgh Public Schools on Tuesday announced the cancellation of all remaining school board meetings in March as efforts continue to stop the spread of COVID-19.

The city school district joins a growing list of districts calling off or postponing board meetings as the pandemic also known as coronavirus moves though the country.

The canceled Pittsburgh Public Schools board meetings include the agenda review meeting scheduled for Wednesday, the public hearing scheduled for Monday, and the legislative meeting on March 25.

Gov. Tom Wolf on Friday ordered the closure of all K-12 schools in the state for 10 business days in the wake of COVID-19.
