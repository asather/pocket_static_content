Giant Eagle announces $10 million bonus for employees working amid COVID-19 outbreak

Giant Eagle on Saturday announced employees would receive $10 million in bonus pay as they work through the COVID-19 pandemic.

The bonus will go to employees from Giant Eagle stores as well as the company’s Market District, pharmacy and GetGo gas station locations. People working in warehouses and delivering products to stores will also receive bonus pay, according to a release from the company.

The O’Hara-based chain employs around 32,000 people across over 100 grocery stores, plus retail support centers and offices, according to the company’s website.

The bonus pay starts immediately, and will be extended back to March 15. A release from the company notes a May 2 end date is scheduled, but will be “reevaluated in the coming weeks based on the needs of the community.”

Current employees and new hires will both be eligible for the bonus as the company “continues hiring efforts,” the release says.

As layoffs continue due to business shutdowns and economic uncertainty amid the spread of the new, novel coronavirus, Giant Eagle has partnered with the Pittsburgh Penguins and Primanti Bros. to provide temporary work to PPG Paints Arena workers and Primanti’s employees while event cancellations and restrictions on dine-in services continue.

Other chains such as Shop n’ Save, Walmart and Dollar General also announced they were increasing hiring efforts during the pandemic.

“As a company, we must continue to find ways to support our incredibly dedicated Team Members,” Laura Shapira Karet, Giant Eagle’s president and CEO, wrote in a press release. “Across all our communities every day, they are working tirelessly to keep families safe, healthy and fed. We cannot thank them enough. They are our heroes.”

Giant Eagle last week announced it was cutting hours across store locations due to an extraordinary surge in shopping as the COVID-19 pandemic expands across the region.
