Police: Two children found unresponsive at Wilkinsburg home making 'great' improvements

Two children who were found unresponsive and removed from a Wilkinsburg home Wednesday morning — one in critical condition — are now doing better, according to Allegheny County police.

Wilkinsburg police were called to a home on Franklin Avenue at 7:35 a.m. after a 5-year-old girl was not responsive. Once there, officers discovered that another child — a 5-year-old boy — had been taken by ambulance to the hospital around 1:45 a.m. when he was found unresponsive.

One of them was in critical condition, police said.

“Both children have made great improvements,” Allegheny County Police Investigations Cmdr. Andrew Schurman said in an emailed statement Thursday. “It is suspected there was some issue with the food.”

Initial concerns of carbon monoxide were eliminated Wednesday as Pittsburgh firefighters investigated.

The commander said they are waiting for additional details from lab reports.
