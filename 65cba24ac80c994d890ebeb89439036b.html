People on the move: 6/1/2020

Advertising

Em-Media hired Willy James as content partner.

Architectural

The Larson Design Group appointed Rajan Sheth to its board. Mr. Raj has served as chairman of the board of directors of Mead & Hunt Inc. since 1994, and was CEO of the firm from 1994 to 2018.

Associations & Organizations

Andrew C. Maher of Clark Hill was elected president of the Pennsylvania Association of Bond Lawyers. Mr. Maher is senior counsel in Clark Hill’s Pittsburgh office in the public finance and banking and financial services practices. 

The Judicial Conduct Board of Pennsylvania appointed the Rev. James P. McCloskey to its board. Rev. McCloskey serves as senior adviser to the president for strategic initiatives, Duquesne University. Previously, he served in a variety of leadership and teaching positions with Holy Ghost Preparatory School, Bensalem; Congregation of the Holy Spirit, in Pittsburgh and in Rome, Italy; and Duquesne University. 

Banking

First National Bank promoted Colleen O’Neill to senior vice president, manager of commercial underwriting.

Health

David L. Bartlett, MD, was named chair of the Allegheny Health Network Cancer Institute, and has opened his surgical practice at AHN’s Saint Vincent Hospital in Erie. Dr. Bartlett came to the network from the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center. 

Awards and Honors

University of Pittsburgh professor Steven R. Little has been elected to the College of Fellows of the Controlled Release Society, an organization for delivery science and technology. Mr. Little, the William Kepler Whiteford Endowed professor and chair of the department of chemical and petroleum engineering at Pitt’s Swanson School of Engineering, was tapped for outstanding and sustained contributions to the field of delivery science and technology over a minimum of 10 years. His novel drug delivery systems mimic the body’s own mechanisms of healing and resolving inflammation, allowing for dosages millions of times smaller than current treatments. 

The American Chemistry Council honored Covestro LLC with the Responsible Care Initiative of the Year Award. In addition, Covestro also received the Responsible Care Product Safety Award for the fifth year in a row for a new platform developed to modernize a manual chemical prioritization and risk characterization process into an efficient, digital tool. 

Business notes

The Heinz Endowments has announced a new public-private partnership with major funding to help strengthen the region’s child care sector in the wake of the COVID-19 crisis. The endowments has awarded a grant of $200,000 to child advocacy organization, Trying Together, to underwrite critically-needed grants to child care providers affected by the pandemic, and fund a new digital platform that provides families real-time information about child care centers. This combines with funding of $300,000 from the multifoundation-supported Emergency Action Fund created with contributions from Hillman Family Foundations, the Richard King Mellon Foundation, The Pittsburgh Foundation, and the Endowments.
