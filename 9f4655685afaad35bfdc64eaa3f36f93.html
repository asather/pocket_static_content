Peduto names nominating committee to expand PWSA board

Mayor Bill Peduto on Thursday announced the formation of a nominating committee that will be tasked with expanding the Pittsburgh Water and Sewer Authority board.

Mr. Peduto named five individuals to the committee to be chaired by Mark Nordenberg, University of Pittsburgh chancellor emeritus and chair of the Institute of Politics. The other members will include Community College of Allegheny County President Quintin Bullock, former Regional Asset District Executive Director David Donahoe, Women for a Healthy Environment Executive Director Michelle Naccarati-Chapkis and Heinz Endowments President Grant Oliphant.

Plans to expand the PWSA board from seven to nine members arose from a December 2017 recommendation by Mr. Peduto’s PWSA blue ribbon panel, according to the Thursday announcement.

The newly expanded board will “bring more leadership and oversight to the publicly owned authority,” according to the press release.

Mr. Peduto will nominate new board members upon the committee’s recommendation. City Council must grant final approval of nominees.

On Feb. 4, Mr. Peduto nominated City Councilwoman Erika Strassburger to replace Councilwoman Deb Gross on the board.

The current PWSA board meets Friday.
