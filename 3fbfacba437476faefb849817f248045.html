Return of the Maestro: John Williams to conduct the Pittsburgh Symphony in June

John Williams, revered composer of music in film franchises including “Star Wars” and “Harry Potter,” will conduct the Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra on June 13 in a special one-night-only appearance, the symphony announced Wednesday.

Mr. Williams will be joined by acclaimed German violinist Anne-Sophie Mutter to perform selected works from “Across the Stars,” a recent album collaboration between Mr. Williams and Ms. Mutter that includes many of Mr. Williams best known movie arranged specifically for her. Mr. Williams last led the orchestra in 2011.

During the first half of the concert, PSO music director Manfred Honeck will lead the orchestra with Ms. Mutter, who called the PSO her “favorite American orchestra” in a statement, in Beethoven’s Violin Concerto.

Tickets range from $35-$125 and go on sale to the public on Friday Jan. 24 at 10 a.m. For more information, visit pittsburghsymphony.org.
