COVID-19 Update: Pa. reports 426 new cases, 1 more death

The Pennsylvania Department of Health on Monday reported 426 new cases of COVID-19, with Western Pennsylvania accounting for 67 of those latest infections.

In the Pittsburgh region, Allegheny County had the most cases with 29, followed by Westmoreland County at 9.

One new death was reported in the state, and no deaths were reported in the last 24 hours in Western Pennsylvania.

Statewide there have now been a total of 129,474 cases of the coronavirus; and 7,579 related deaths since the pandemic began in mid-March.

Allegheny County has had 9,932 cases overall of the virus. With no new deaths reported, the county death roll remains at 306. The average number of cases per day has decreased from about 82 cases during the week of Aug. 11 to about 61 cases from Aug. 18 to Aug. 24, according to county officials.

The latest infections in Allegheny come from 690 tests that were taken between Aug. 17 and Aug. 23. Those who have recently tested positive for the virus range in age from 18 to 94, with a median age of 45.

Based on the most recent figures from the state, there are currently 86 COVID-19 patients hospitalized, which is down from 101 patients last Monday. In total, Allegheny County has had 926 people hospitalized for the virus since the start of the pandemic.
