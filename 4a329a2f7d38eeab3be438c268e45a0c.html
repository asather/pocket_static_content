Allegheny County reports 127 new cases of COVID-19, 0 new deaths

For the sixth straight day, Allegheny County reported a triple-digit increase in new daily COVID-19 cases with 127 more infections. There were no new deaths reported.

Westmoreland County, which has the region’s second-highest number of COVID-19 cases, reported 27 new positive cases, bringing its total to 801.

Overall, Western Pennsylvania had 230 new cases on Sunday.

Since the Allegheny County reported its first confirmed case of COVID-19 on March 14, it has had a total of 3,557 cases — 3,378 are confirmed and 179 are probable. 

The county is reporting that 187 people have died from COVID-19, with 175 confirmed and 12 probable.

The county reported four new hospitalizations because of the viral disease. Of the latest cases, the ages range from 8 months to 84 years, with 30 being the median.

On Sunday, the state saw an increase of 479 positive cases, with four additional deaths. Pennsylvania now has 89,854 total cases and 6,753 deaths.

The county is continuing to ask residents to limit travel and errands to only those that are necessary. In response to a surge of cases over the last week, county officials on Thursday ordered all bars, restaurants and casinos to close for a week starting Friday, and prohibited gatherings of more than 25 people.
