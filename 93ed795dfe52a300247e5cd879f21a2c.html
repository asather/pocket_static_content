CMU, Penn State students chosen for 'Jeopardy!' college tournament

Emma Farrell, a senior civil and environmental engineering student at Carnegie Mellon University, has been chosen to compete in the next “Jeopardy!” College Tournament.

According to her LinkedIn profile, Ms. Farrell, who is from Telford, Pa., is also the general manager of campus radio station WRCT. She and 14 others, including Kylie Weaver, a Penn State University senior, will be part of the April 6-17 broadcast. Winner takes home $100,00 as well as a berth in the annual Tournament of Champions.

Ms. Weaver is from McLean, Va., and majoring in supply chain management. She is a senior in the Schreyer Honors College and an ROTC member.

Last November, CMU grad Lindsey Shultz reached the Tournament of Champions semifinals. Dr. Shultz, a physician and health care analyst, is a native of Connellsville.
