National Weather Service issues frost advisory for Sunday morning

The National Weather Service issued a frost advisory for Sunday morning in parts of southwest Pennsylvania and West Virginia.

Sundayâ€™s advisory will last from 2 a.m. to 9 a.m. and will include parts of Venango, Clarion, Jefferson, Indiana, Westmoreland and Fayette counties in Pennsylvania as well as Morgan and Tucker counties in West Virginia.

Temperatures in those areas could fall near 36 degrees.

With temperatures in the 30s and 40s around the region, Friday was the coldest night since at least June 1st, according to a post from the National Weather Service.

NWS issued a similar frost advisory Saturday morning for some of the same counties. By Saturday afternoon, temperatures crawled up to the low to mid-60s.

The rest of the week is forecast to have temperatures ranging from high 70s to lows 40s.
