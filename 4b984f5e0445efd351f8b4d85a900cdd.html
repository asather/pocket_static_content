Police investigating shooting death in New Kensington

Police are investigating after a Westmoreland County man was found shot to death Wednesday evening in the front seat of a car outside a housing complex in New Kensington.

New Kensington Police Chief Robert Deringer said 31-year-old Anthone C. Williams, of Arnold, was found just before 5:30 p.m. Wednesday sitting in the driver seat of a BMW in the 200 block of Dent Drive, right outside apartment complex East Ken Manor.

Officers unsuccessfully attempted CPR, but he was pronounced dead at the scene by Deputy Coroner Joshua C. Zappone, according to the Westmoreland County coroner’s office.

“They [medics] ran a strip on his heart, but his heart had no activity,” Chief Deringer said Thursday. The coroner’s office said Williams died from multiple gun shots while sitting in his car.

The portion of the housing complex where Williams was found — which is operated by the Westmoreland Housing Authority — has been vacant for a few years, the police chief said.

“There are no leads in the investigation so far,” he said. “We are interviewing multiple people in the area. The county is processing the vehicle at a local impound and officers are looking into different avenues for suspects.” The BMW is registered to Williams’ girlfriend, who also shared a home with him, the chief said.

Anyone with information regarding this investigation should contact the New Kensington Police Department or Westmoreland police.
