Pittsburgh man indicted on federal gun charge arrested in Ore.

A Pittsburgh man has been indicted on a federal gun charge in connection with a domestic violence allegation.

U.S. marshals arrested Joseph Plasan, 32, of Beechview, on Monday in Portland, Ore.

Mr. Plasan had been indicted in Pittsburgh on July 8. The case was unsealed Tuesday following his arrest.

On May 5, according to the indictment, Mr. Plasan possessed a .45-caliber pistol and ammunition while knowing he was subject to a protection from abuse order. Federal law prohibits anyone subject to a PFA from having a gun.

U.S. Attorney Scott Brady said abusers with guns are five times more likely to kill their victims "so prosecuting those abusers and taking them off the streets is one of our top priorities."

Gun offenses are much more serious in federal court than in the state system, almost always resulting in federal prison time. There is also no parole in the federal system.

The government has requested that Mr. Plasan be detained pending trial.
