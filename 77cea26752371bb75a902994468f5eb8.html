Pittsburgh parks ranked 15th in nation

Pittsburgh’s city parks jumped several spots higher and now rank number 15 in the nation, according to a California-based nonprofit that measures the quality of urban green space and its closeness to residents, amenities, access acreage and investment.

The San Francisco-based Trust for Public Land gave the city’s parks an 88 out of 100 for access based on its calculation that 92% of residents live within a 10-minute walk from a park.

Mayor Bill Peduto has repeatedly cited park access as a goal of his administration.

The organization also awarded the city a 79 for investment.

In November, voters narrowly passed a property tax hike that will be used to fund the city’s parks.

The nonprofit Pittsburgh Parks Conservancy petitioned for the ballot referendum, which Mr. Peduto supported.

City Council has not yet approved enabling legislation to establish the trust fund for the new tax revenue.

Pittsburgh’s parks ranked 22nd on the national list in 2019, and 39th in 2016.
