Competition names Pennsylvania's best wines

The Pennsylvania Winery Association recently released the results of this year’s Pennsylvania Wine Competition, judged by Amenti del Vino (Eastern Connecticut Chapter American Wine Society). 

The “Best Dry Wine in Competition” was pronounced to be the 2017 Gruner Veltliner from Stony Run Winery in Kempton (it also was named Best White Dry Wine). “Best Sweet Wine in Competition” was the non-vintage Red Raspberry from Conneaut Cellars Winery in Conneaut Lake (it also was named Best Fruit Wine and a double gold medal winner). Other bests were: 

Best White Sweet Wine (and a double gold medal): Razzle from Courtyard Winery in North East. 

Best Red Sweet Wine: 2018 Sir Walters Red from Franklin Hill Vineyards in Bangor. 

Best Red Dry Wine: 2017 Cabernet Sauvignon from Yori Wine Cellars in North East. 

For the full list of medal winners, visit https://pennsylvaniawine.com/2020/03/11/2020-pennsylvania-wine-competition. 

While state Fine Wines & Good Spirits stores and online shopping site are closed due to the COVID-19 emergency, consumers can still buy bottles from supermarkets as well as the wineries themselves, most of which will ship to Pennsylvania residents.
