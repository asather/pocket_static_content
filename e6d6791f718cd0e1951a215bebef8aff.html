Joe Starkey: Steelers to be trusted on WRs — but what about J.K. Dobbins?

Before we jump ahead to Steelers-Ravens drama, let’s go back to the very beginning. As it turns out, Round 2 of the NFL’s virtual draft was infinitely more entertaining than Round 1 …

7:02 p.m.: ESPN opens with shots from players’ houses. Not a ton of social distancing happening, but at least it’s not like Thursday, when agent Drew Rosenhaus appeared in a player’s home wearing a mask around his chin.

That’s not the way it works, Drew.

7:08: Roger Goodell, seemingly in the same outfit as the night before (who’s not going back-to-back these days?) mentions Thursday’s record TV rating and says, “That’s a tribute to all of you and your passion for football.”

Actually, that’s tribute to the fact that SPORTS ARE GONE.

Having said that, I like Goodell way better in this minimalist environment. He’s more real. Some people think he looks ridiculous in his basement, but you know what really looks ridiculous? A 61-year-old man doing flying chest bumps with 22-year-old football players.

7:10: The Bengals had an entire day to think about their pick, but they will apparently use their full time allotment.

7:15: They take Tee Higgins, and for some reason we are suddenly watching a women’s basketball game (turns out Higgins’ mom played hoops).

Trey Wingo informs us that Wiggins has the fifth-longest arms of any receiver in the past eight years, or something.

7:21: The Colts take Michael Pittman — a player linked to the Steelers.

But then, which player hasn’t been linked to the Steelers?

7:34: The strangest moment of the draft — even stranger than the kid in the blue chicken suit at Mike Vrabel’s house — just happened: Bill Belichick turned into a dog.

Seriously, a husky is sitting in Belichick’s spot at the kitchen table. The only way this would be better is if the dog were wearing a hoodie with cut-off sleeves.

The Ringer’s Rodger Sherman tweets:

“THE PATRIOTS DYNASTY IS OVER

BRADY IS GONE

GRONK IS GONE

BELICHICK HAS BEEN REPLACED WITH A DOG”

How else to explain them taking a player from Lenoir-Rhyne? And of course we see him playing basketball. I didn’t see this many basketball highlights on the Michael Jordan documentary.

7:46: Steelers-related tension building around running backs J.K. Dobbins, Jonathan Taylor and Cam Akers — all still on the board.

7:48: The Texans take defensive tackle Ross Blacklock, and we are now watching basketball highlights again.

OK, wait, this is legit: His dad, Jimmy, played for the Harlem Globetrotters.

7:48: The Colts trade up for Taylor at No. 41. Steelers eight picks away.

7:55: Jags take receiver Leviska Shenault Jr.

He was linked to the Steelers, too.

8:02: Did Wingo just say the Bears now have 10 tight ends on their roster?

8:09: There’s Bruce Arians on his back porch. The Steelers once were linked to him.

This is beginning to feel anticlimactic. The Steelers are going to take Dobbins or Akers, aren’t they?

8:11: Broncos go with KJ Hamler (linked to you-know-who) and now have so many weapons that Drew Lock could play terribly and still be good.

8:18: Atlanta and Seattle choose defensive linemen at Nos. 47 and 48. The Steelers waste no time. Their pick is in, and if Booger McFarland ever stops talking, we’ll see it.

8:25: Back from break, we get a shot of quarterback Jalen Hurts, who has been linked to … oh, never mind. GM Kevin Colbert appears to be sitting in a furniture store.

With the 49th pick, the Steelers take … Chase Claypool? Of course, the one man in the entire draft who has not been linked to them.

We are suddenly inundated with breathless reviews of the 6-foot-4 Claypool, including Chris Simms saying he’s the best athlete he’s ever seen and ESPN saying he’s the “second fastest receiver ever at 230-plus pounds,” running the 40 in 4.42 seconds. Only Calvin Johnson was faster.

All of which raises a question: Why’d he last until the 49th pick?

We are now watching Claypool’s high school highlights, and he is so much bigger than his opponents that he looks like Andy Reid in the punt-pass-and-kick competition.

“Bruh who was he playing against in high school,” tweets ESPN’s Ryan Clark.

Listen, the Steelers are to be trusted on wide receivers. They draft them better than anyone, and this kid has enticing talent. But I would have gone for Dobbins.

James Conner might be one-and-done here and has trouble staying on the field. Maybe the Steelers are more confident in Benny Snell Football than we think. Maybe they’re in on Leonard Fournette. Who knows?

8:38: There goes Akers, to the Rams.

8:40: There goes Hurts … to the Eagles?

Everyone seems thrilled, except Hurts. Perhaps because he’s thinking, “I just went to a team that has a 27-year-old quarterback with a $128 million contract.”

8:54: The Ravens, of all teams, take Dobbins. They were already the best running team in NFL history.

It’ll be mighty interesting to track his progress vs. Claypool’s.

9:17: The Patriots take edge rusher Josh Uche. The Uche family has two interesting items in the background: a giant Marilyn Monroe picture and a now-maskless Drew Rosenhaus standing too close to people.

Due to time constraints, we move ahead in our coverage …

11:29: Goodell is now making picks from a grandpa-style easy chair. All he needs is a pipe. I’m telling you, this is making him likable.

11:49: The Patriots trade into the 101st spot and choose tight end Dalton Keene, who looks like an Allman Brother.

11:52: The Steelers at No. 102 opt for an edge rusher in Charlotte’s Alex Highsmith. Louis Riddick likes the pick, saying the Clemson linemen were very complimentary of Highsmith.

I’m not sure I knew Charlotte had a college football program.

But then, I didn’t know Lenoir-Rhyne had one, either.
