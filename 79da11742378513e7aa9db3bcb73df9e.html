Labor leaders cancel Labor Day Parade for Pittsburgh

Labor leaders in Allegheny County announced Friday they will forego the traditional Labor Day parade this September and opt for a membership-wide weekend of service.

Citing the unsafe nature of holding such a large gathering in the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic, Darin Kelly, president of the Allegheny-Fayette Central Labor Council, said safety for the workers the council represents and the community at large is what led leaders to make the decision.

Instead of holding the nation's "biggest and most extravagant Labor Day parade," the council's weekend of service will include such actions as cleaning parks and trails and holding a virtual reading session on labor history, and culminate in a "Day of Giving" that will include a blood drive and food drive, Mr. Kelly said.

"This has not been an easy time, but this was the right decision," said Mr. Kelly, flanked by Allegheny County Executive Rich Fitzgerald and dozens of workers.
