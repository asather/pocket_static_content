New food distribution for PPS families

The Pittsburgh Public Schools said Friday it was restarting its partnership with Paragon Foods to distribute food to district families as remote instruction continues.

Paragon will distribute boxes of fresh vegetables, meat and dairy products at one location every weekday in October in parking lots at five schools throughout the city.

Trucks from Paragon will arrive at the locations at 9 a.m., and families can line up for distribution from 10 a.m. to 12 p.m.

The district will remain completely virtual through at least the end of October due to COVID-19 concerns. It is still possible that instruction could remain remote further into the school year. 

From May through August, Paragon, a regional supplier of fresh and specialty foods, distributed bags of fresh vegetables at schools every Thursday.
