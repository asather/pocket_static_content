Dems pushing for creation of civilian police review boards in Pa. counties

A bill that would create independent citizen police review boards in nearly all Pennsylvania counties is expected to be introduced by Pennsylvania Democrats.

Rep. Austin Davis, D-McKeesport, will introduce legislation this week that would establish an independent review board in all second- through eighth-class counties — every county except Philadelphia — that would examine complaints of police misconduct, conduct public hearings, subpoena witnesses, and provide findings and recommendations to a municipality’s governing body.

“What’s been clear over the last couple of weeks and months is that the public wants more transparency as it relates to police officers and accountability,” Mr. Davis said. “And I think this is a measure that will quite frankly help build trust between law enforcement and members of the public.”

Police misconduct has been under a microscope in the U.S. after several incidents of police brutality against Black citizens.

Mr. Davis’ bill has 16 Democratic co-sponsors and is a companion to a similar bill being introduced by Sen. Wayne Fontana, D-Brookline.

Mr. Fontana, who circulated a co-sponsorship memo for the legislation in June, said it has yet to be assigned to a committee. 

Mr. Fontana introduced a similar bill last year that never received a vote.

Both chambers of Pennsylvania’s Legislature are Republican controlled.

Allegheny County’s legislative arm is again considering the adoption of its own countywide citizen police review board.

County Council’s public safety committee will discuss the measure Thursday. Beth Pittinger, executive director of Pittsburgh’s Citizen Police Review Board, is scheduled to speak.

The bill has been in committee since February.

A proposal for a civilian police review board failed to get enough votes in County Council in 2019.
