McKeesport schools hire new business manager

The McKeesport Area School District will have a new business manager this month.

The board, at a Feb. 26 meeting, unanimously approved the hiring of Joan Wehner as business manager. She was given a five-year contract that runs from March 23 to June 30, 2025, at a salary of $95,000 per year.

She also will serve as the school board secretary and the district’s Right-to-Know officer.

Ms. Wehner is a graduate of McKeesport Area High School and Duquesne University, where she earned a degree in business finance.

She most recently worked in the Penn Trafford School District as its assistant manager.

She takes over for previous business manager Matthew Gergley who resigned in January.

The board also unanimously approved the hiring of Brandy Daye as assistant high school principal at a salary per the district’s Act 93 agreement.

In another matter, the board re-approved an agreement with ABM Building Solutions for a districtwide guaranteed energy savings project in the amount of $11,563,981.

Board members Joe Lopretto, Tom Filotei, Steven Kondrosky, Diane Elias and Jim Poston voted in favor of the project. Mindy Sturgess, James Brown and Mr. Donoto dissented.

At a meeting last year, Tyler Nichols, of ABM, told the board the energy savings from the project would offset the cost.

The agreement had been approved in December, however board member Dave Donoto questioned that approval stating that the dollar amount was not included in the motion.

District solicitor Gary Matta said the approval was done correctly in December, but he said he was fine with re-approving it with the amount.

Work has already begun on the project throughout the district, including the installation of LED lighting. District HVAC equipment is also set to be replaced.
