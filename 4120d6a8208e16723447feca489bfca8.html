Beating death of McKees Rocks man, 73, leads to life sentence for killer

An Allegheny County judge Monday sentenced a Stowe man to life without parole in connection with the 2016 beating death of a 73-year-old man.

Police said that on June 1, 2016, Branden A. Frazier, 28, fatally beat Benito Nicoletti in the victim’s McKees Rocks home and then stole his rented Chrysler 300.

On Oct. 7, a jury found Frazier guilty of first-degree murder as well as burglary, receiving stolen property and theft.

Frazier appeared before Allegheny County Common Pleas Judge Kevin G. Sasinoski, who sentenced him to an additional 10 to 21 years in prison in connection with the other charges.

Marvin Leibowitz, Frazier’s lawyer, said he remains hopeful that the outcome will change on appeal.

“I am a great believer in the jury system, but I just believe that the jury got it wrong in convicting him of first-degree murder,” Mr. Leibowitz said.
