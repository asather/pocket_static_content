Beaver reports 3 new nursing home deaths; Allegheny County case count inches near 1,400

This story was updated at 12:40 p.m.

Five new deaths blamed on COVID-19 were reported in Western Pennsylvania, according to data released Wednesday by the state health department.

Beaver County reported three deaths — all at nursing homes — and Allegheny reported two, one of which occurred at a nursing home.

While Westmoreland County did not report any new deaths on Wednesday, the state, which continues to reconcile its data with data received by the counties, listed Westmoreland as having two deaths at nursing homes.

 

With the latest data, Allegheny now has 111 deaths — the highest in Western Pennsylvania — since the first case was reported March 14. It also reported 19 new cases of COVID-19, giving it a total of 1,394. Of Allegheny’s cases, 1,334 are confirmed and 60 are probable. Of the deaths, 101 are confirmed and 10 are probable. Through Wednesday, there have been 247 hospitalizations in the county. Of them, 106 needed to visit the intensive care unit and 62 required mechanical ventilation. So far, the county has performed 19,263 tests.

Beaver County now has 471 cases and 76 deaths. Westmoreland has 409 cases. According to its coroner, Westmoreland has 31 deaths — 17 females and 14 males, all of whom were white.

As of Wednesday, the total number of COVID-19 cases was 51,845 in Pennsylvania, which includes 3,106 deaths. The number of cases rose by 888 and deaths up 94 from Tuesday.

Philadelphia County continues to have the highest totals in the state with 13,742 cases and 625 deaths.
