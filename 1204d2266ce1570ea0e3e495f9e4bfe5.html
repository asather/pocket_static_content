WATCH: Steven Brault teams up in song with Broadway stars — and Josh Bell

Pirates pitcher Steven Brault has been dabbling in music since his days as a vocal performance major at Regis University. In April, he released his debut album “A Pitch to Broadway,” which featured covers of 12 Broadway songs. One of those Brault-ified tunes was “Wait for Me” from “Hadestown,” a musical that won eight awards at the 2019 Tony Awards, including Best Musical.

On Tuesday, Brault leveled up from covering “Hadestown” songs to performing a quarantine-themed version of “Wait for Me” over video chat with the stars of that show and Pirates teammate Josh Bell, who was also featured in a speaking role on Brault’s “A Pitch to Broadway” cover of that song.

The song featured Brault, Bell and “Hadestown” stars Andre De Shields (who won Best Actor in a Musical at the 2019 Tonys), Jewelle Blackman, Kay Trinidad and Yvette Gonzalez-Nacer.

In another Broadway-baseball crossover, Brault and the “Hadestown” cast and creatives also worked together on a heavy-duty version of “Take Me Out to the Ballgame.”

That cover featured everyone who participated in the first song (minus Bell) plus multiple members of the “Hadestown” band; Anais Mitchell, who did the show’s music, lyrics and book; Sharon Robinson, Jackie Robinson’s daughter; MLB Hall of Famer Ozzie Smith; MLB All-Star Nick Swisher; and Yankees great Bernie Williams on guitar.

Both covers were apparently the brainchild of Mitchell, who heard Brault’s cover of “Wait for Me” and decided she wanted to work with him in some capacity. De Shields also seemed thrilled to be working with Brault on these projects.

“Steven Brault made a pitch at Broadway, and I knocked it out of the park,” he said in a statement. “It was a match made in heaven, or rather Hadestown, since we both have ‘Damn Yankees’ on our resumes, he as the young Joe Hardy in a high school production, and I as Mr. Applegate in a regional one. I hope the fans have as much fun watching the videos as we had making them.”

There was a hint of melancholy to both songs, as they highlighted the fact that Broadway has been shut down since March due to the COVID-19 pandemic and just this week announced that it will remain dark until at least Jan. 3, 2021. The tour of “Hadestown” was supposed to grace Pittsburgh this year, but it and the rest of the city’s 2020 Broadway Series was also canceled.

Brault was able to get in one live performance with the Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra at Heinz Hall in February before the pandemic hit. He got some extra time to work on his music during MLB’s hiatus, but with summer camp starting in a few days, he’ll have to focus on making sure his body — especially the left shoulder he injured in March — is ready for a 60-game season.

At least he probably won’t have to worry as much about fellow Pirates pitcher Trevor Williams bugging him about a potential guest appearance.

“Trevor Williams has asked me about being on my album so many times that I refuse to continue hearing about it because I don’t trust him,” Brault told the Post-Gazette in April. “I think that anything he does will be detrimental to the sound of the album.”
