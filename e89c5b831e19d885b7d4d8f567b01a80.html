34-year-old man identified as victim in Larimer homicide

A Pittsburgh man who was shot and killed in the city’s Larimer neighborhood over the Fourth of July weekend has been identified by the Allegheny County Medical Examiner’s Office.

Keith D. Byrd, 34, died just before 6 a.m. Sunday at a hospital from gunshot wounds, the medical examiner’s office said Friday morning. His death was ruled a homicide.

Police had responded around 4 a.m. to the 100 block of Shetland Street for reports that someone had been shot. Authorities said Mr. Byrd was found lying in the street with bullet wounds. He was taken to a hospital in critical condition. 

No arrests have been made and county police continue to investigate.
