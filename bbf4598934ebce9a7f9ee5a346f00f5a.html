Officer grazes man with bullet after incident near Sto-Rox High School

Gunfire near Sto-Rox High School on Monday afternoon led to a foot chase in which a police officer grazed a man with a bullet.

According to a release from Allegheny County police, the incident occurred about 2 p.m. when Stowe and McKees Rocks police officers heard the sound of gunfire in the 1100 block of Dohrman Street — around two blocks from the school — and went to investigate.

Police said responding officers discovered that a black car leaving the area may have been involved in the shooting, and that Stowe police had stopped a vehicle matching that description about three blocks away on Ann Alley. 

The driver of the car on Ann Alley ran from the scene, prompting police to give chase. That man — who police have not named — began running through yards of homes and jumping fences, police said.

At one point, according to police, the man ran at a Stowe officer, leading the officer to fire his gun at the man, who was grazed by the bullet.

The suspect was taken into custody and then treated and released from a hospital. Police said the man was wanted on outstanding warrants and will be placed in the Allegheny County Jail.

Police said the have not received any reports of people who were injured in the initial shooting by the school.

Police, though, identified another vehicle involved in the shooting as a black Chevrolet Spark with Missouri plate VD9-L6S. The vehicle has bullet damage to the front windshield and will likely be missing the driver side window. Anyone who sees the vehicle should call 911 immediately.

Anyone with information regarding the incident should call the Allegheny County Police Tip Line at 1-833-ALL-TIPS. The department can also be reached through social media and its website.
