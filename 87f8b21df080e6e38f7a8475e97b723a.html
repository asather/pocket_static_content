Andean Condor egg stops developing at National Aviary

Despite the efforts of the National Aviary’s Andean condors, the egg that Lianni and Lurch have been incubating since early April has stopped developing, and there will be no chick.

“This is an unfortunate but not uncommon outcome,” said the aviary in a “sad-to-report” statement at 9:22 a.m. Wednesday. “The egg, though it did not successfully hatch, is a promising sign for Lianni and Lurch. We are very hopeful for their future breeding success.”

The mango-sized egg laid by Lianni, 36, was due to hatch in early June. Lurch, 49, her breeding partner since 2017, took turns sitting on the egg in a nest located in an outdoor exhibit.

The 25-pound birds with 10-foot wingspans, and other birds, can be viewed by the public while most of the North Side facility is closed because of the COVID-19 pandemic.

The egg was important because in the wild condors are “critically endangered” in Ecuador and “threatened” in much of their range in other South American countries.

Lianni earlier produced four chicks at the National Aviary with her former partner, Rhodan, and three of them were released in the wild, when grown, in Columbia and Venezuela.

Lurch also has a proven track record. He produced three offspring at other facilities.
