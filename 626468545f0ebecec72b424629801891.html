New Kensington man arrested after suspected child abuse sent 2-year-old child to the PICU

New Kensington police are investigating a suspected child abuse case that sent a 2-year-old to the pediatric intensive care unit.

On March 26, police and EMS responded to reports of an unresponsive child on the 1100 block of 5th Avenue in New Kensington.

Authorities said the child was transported to the Children’s Hospital of Pittsburgh Emergency Room for lifesaving measures. Police on Wednesday said the child is still in the PICU.

Keith Lilly Jr., 31 of New Kensington was arrested April 3 in connection to the incident. Lilly was charged with recklessly endangering another person, endangering the welfare of children and two counts of aggravated assault.

Court documents show Lilly was held in the Westmoreland County Prison on $250,000 bond, and he is scheduled for a preliminary hearing May 7.

New Kensington police and Westmoreland County detectives are investigating the incident and the events preceding it. Officials said the Westmoreland County Children’s Bureau is also investigating.

Officials asked anyone with information about the incident to call the New Kensington Police Department at 724-339-7533 or Westmoreland County detectives at 724-830-3949.
