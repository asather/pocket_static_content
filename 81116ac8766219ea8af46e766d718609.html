Rochester wins rematch for second consecutive Class 1A girls title

History doesn’t always repeat itself, but it’s starting to feel that way in Class 1A.

Top-seeded Rochester (24-1) rallied back from an early deficit against West Greene for the second year in a row to capture its second WPIAL girls basketball championship Friday afternoon at Petersen Events Center with a 59-43 win. The Pioneers (24-1) made it to the title game for the third consecutive year, but all three trips have resulted in silver medals.

Rams sophomore guard Corynne Hauser led all players with 17 points, while senior forward Jasmine Mack finished with 15 points and 10 rebounds. Junior point guard Jersey Wise scored a team-high 16 points for West Greene in the defeat.

Rochester pulled out a 62-56 win against the Pioneers in last year’s title game, and the Rams rumbled through their regular season schedule with only one loss — a two-point defeat vs. Class 4A semifinalist Central Valley. With virtually every key contributor returning from last year’s title-winning team, Rochester’s return to the championship game was no surprise.

West Greene, on the other hand, lost three 1,000-point scorers from last year’s team — yet somehow managed to put together an undefeated regular season on its way back to the championship game rematch.

The Pioneers used a 9-2 run to open up an early six-point lead, then junior point guard Jersey Wise used a timely steal and score after a 3-pointer by sophomore guard Katie Lampe to give West Greene a 15-8 lead.

After another 3-pointer by Wise, Hauser drained a buzzer-beating 3-pointer from the corner at the end of the first quarter to make it an 18-15 game.

Another steal and score by Wise gave the Pioneers a 26-21 lead midway through the second quarter, but that’s when the Rams made their run. First, junior guard Alexis Robison nailed with a 3-pointer in the corner, then a fast-break layup by her sister, Mekenzie, tied the game at 26.

Senior guard Bethany Barto then made a put-back after a missed 3-pointer to give Rochester a 34-28 advantage into the break.

Mack made a layup to open up the second half, then Wise followed with a long-range jumper for West Greene. Back-to-back layups by Alexis Robison and Hauser gave the Rams a 42-32 lead midway through the third quarter, but the Pioneers trimmed it to 42-36 going into the fourth.

Mekenzie Robison opened up the final quarter with a 3-pointer, then a put-back by Mack stretched Rochester’s lead back to double digits at 47-36. A pair of baskets by Wise and sophomore guard Anna Durbin cut it to 47-40, but the Rams responded with four buckets in a row to open up a commanding 55-40 lead on their way to the victory.
