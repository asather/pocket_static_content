Wolf orders restaurants, bars to end dine-in service in Allegheny, 4 other counties

Pennsylvania Gov. Tom Wolf on Sunday ordered all restaurants and bars in Allegheny and four other counties to close their dine-in facilities for 14 days amid efforts to help stop the COVID-19 coronavirus’ spread.

The Wolf administration said in a news release that the governor was using his authority under the state’s COVID-19 disaster declaration order.

Businesses in Allegheny, Bucks, Chester, Delaware and Montgomery counties “that offer carry-out, delivery, and drive-through food and beverage service may continue to do so,” according to the release, “but eating and drinking inside restaurants and bars is temporarily prohibited.”

Mr. Wolf said he understood “this is disruptive to businesses as well as patrons,” but ordering the restaurants and bars to close was in the best interest of the counties’ residents.

Businesses that do not adhere to this order could face enforcement actions, the Wolf administration said.

The governor's order came shortly after Allegheny County officials called on all nonessential businesses in the county to close voluntarily.

“Social distancing is essential as more Pennsylvanians are testing positive for COVID-19,” said Rachel Levine, Pennsylvania’s secretary of health. “By taking these steps now, we can protect public health and slow the spread of this virus.”

 

Correction: This story’s original headline and lead mischaracterized the Wolf administration’s directive. The Pennsylvania governor ordered all restaurants and bars in Allegheny, Bucks, Chester, Delaware and Montgomery counties to end dine-in facilities, not close entirely.
