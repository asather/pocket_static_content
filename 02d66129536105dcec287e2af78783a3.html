COVID-19 Update: 141 new cases in Western Pa.; Allegheny County reports 61

Pennsylvania on Thursday reported 620 new cases of COVID-19 for Thursday, with Western Pennsylvania accounting for 141 of the newest infections.

Allegheny County had the highest increase in the region with 61 new cases, followed by Westmoreland County with 15. 

Washington County saw an additional seven cases, making it the fifth county in the region to have over 1,000 cases since the beginning of the pandemic.

Statewide, a total of 131,156 cases have been reported.

For Allegheny County, Thursday marked the seventh straight day that new infections have fallen below 100.

The latest cases come from 1,084 tests administered between June 22 and Aug. 26, and bring the total to 10,065 cases since the pandemic began in March.

“With the exception of one outlier, all results were from specimens taken since Aug. 17,”according to a statement by the Allegheny County Health Department.

Those who have recently tested positive range in age from 16 to 91, with a median age of 59. The county has had a total of 10,065 cases of the virus since mid-March when the pandemic began.

County health officials also are reporting one new death, bringing the county death toll to 318. The latest death was a person in their 80s and occurred on Aug. 22 at a long-term care facility.

In Pennsylvania, there were 11 additional deaths reported as a result of COVID-19. Three of them occurred in Western Pennsylvania, one each in Allegheny, Cambria and Washington counties. 

The county health department announced on Thursday that the data used to update the COVID-19 dashboard will now be available to the public through the Western Pennsylvania Regional Data Center website.

Test and case information will be updated every day at 1:30 p.m., and information including death demographics and statistics and geography counts will be updated every Monday at 1:30 p.m.

The data will reflect each person who is tested, not each test that is given. If more information becomes available, that person’s information will be updated.

If an individual previously had a negative test and then a positive test, their data will be updated.

The most recent data available on the WPRDC website is from Aug. 25.

Wednesday evening the county health department released data from the contact tracers, outlining trends in recent positive cases.

For the week of Aug. 9 through Aug. 15, the number of people who tested positive and reported going to bars or restaurants decreased to 27 from 40 the week of Aug. 2 to Aug. 8. This is the third week in a row this category has seen a decrease.

Over the same time period, the number of people who reported going to parties increased from 18 to 26. Attendance at weddings and funerals also increased from four to 14.

Positive cases of individuals who reported traveling to Florida, South Carolina, Tennessee, West Virginia, Georgia, Maryland and Virginia all decreased, while reports of travel to Ohio and North Carolina increased.
