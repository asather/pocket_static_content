State's COVID-19 death toll passes 6,000 mark; 1 death reported in Allegheny County

There were 61 new COVID-19 deaths reported Tuesday in Pennsylvania, pushing its total above the 6,000 mark, according to the latest data released by the state Department of Health.

One death, in Allegheny County, was reported in Western Pennsylvania, which also reported 63 new cases of the infectious disease. 

Allegheny County had 19 new cases. Since the first case of COVID-19 was reported in the county on March 14, it has had 2,027 cases — 1,899 are confirmed and 128 are probable.

With the new death, Allegheny’s total is 169, the highest in the region. Of that total, 157 are confirmed and 12 are probable.

Pennsylvania on Monday reported 493 new cases of COVID-19. The state has gone 30 consecutive days of reporting less than 1,000 cases. Its total number of cases is 76,436, and the total number of deaths is 6,014.
