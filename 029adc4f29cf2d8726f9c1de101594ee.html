Allegheny County now reporting 48 total cases of COVID-19

The Allegheny County Health Department reported eight new COVID-19 cases on Monday, bringing the county’s total to 48 total cases, the fourth highest total in the state after Montgomery, Philadelphia and Delaware counties.

The county said that six of the 48 cases are hospitalized.

The state health department reported that as of 12 noon. Monday, there  were 165 additional cases statewide total cases in Pennsylvania, for a total of 644 cases across the state. There was also one new death in Montgomery County, for three total COVID-19 deaths statewide - one of them in Allegheny County on Saturday.

 

The cases are the latest in a widening trend in southwestern Pennsylvania, where more counties have recently reported cases of COVID-19, including the first case Monday in Cambria County.

In addition to Allegheny, these counties also are reporting cases: Washington (7), Westmoreland (6, with two new ones reported Monday), Beaver (3), Butler (1), Fayette (1), and Mercer (1). Fayette and Mercer counties reported their first cases over the weekend.

Thirty-four of Pennsylvania’s 67 counties now have at least one COVID-19 case.
