Man pleads guilty to beating death of woman in East Pittsburgh

A Cambria County inmate pleaded guilty Wednesday to the beating death of a woman in East Pittsburgh in June.

Delrico McClinton, 28, of Penn Hills, pleaded guilty to third degree murder, theft and access device fraud before Allegheny County Common Pleas Judge Jill Rangos.

He will receive 25 to 50 years in prison in exchange for the plea, according to Mike Manko, spokesman for the district attorney’s office.

Investigators found the decomposing body of Whitney Maleeca Lyn Boyer, 25, in an East Pittsburgh home after McClinton, an inmate at the Cambria County Prison, confessed to law enforcement on June 17 that he had killed a woman and directed state troopers to the home at 18 Maple St.

Pennsylvania State Police assumed jurisdiction in East Pittsburgh after the borough disbanded its police department in December 2018, roughly six months after the police-involved fatal shooting of an unarmed black teenager.

McClinton told investigators he met Ms. Boyer in the early morning of June 1 and that they went to his ex-girlfriend’s residence on Maple Street and had consensual sex. The ex-girlfriend had not been living at the house for more than a month, investigators said. She told police McClinton was not welcome at the home.

McClinton said he killed Ms. Boyer after they had sex, beating her, throwing her down a set of steps and then strangling her, according to a criminal complaint. He said he stole her credit cards and phone, according to a criminal complaint. He told investigators he used her phone throughout the next day and used her card to obtain about $200 in cash from Walmart.

Ms. Boyer was estranged from most of her family in Eastern Pennsylvania, except for a foster father.
