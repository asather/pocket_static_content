Public Theater announces virtual lineup of commissioned works

The O’Reilly remains darkened, but Pittsburgh Public Theater plays on.

Its “Classic N’at” online series kicks off in September as an extension of its benefit PlayTime series, which has been watched by more than 20,000 audience members from 22 countries.

“What the Heart Wants,” which was to open the Public’s 2020 season at the O’Reilly, has been moved to fall 2021.

Joining it will be “Murder on the Orient Express,” “She Loves Me,” “Two Trains Running,” “The Belle of Amherst,” “Yoga Play” and a special season opener soon to be announced.

Subscribers will have their 2020 tickets honored when the 2021 season commences.

“Classics N’at” features table read commissions of a wide variety of works, including “Much Ado About Nothing,” adapted by Steven Wilson (Sept. 17); “A Tell Tale Heart” adapted by Alec Silberblatt (Oct. 22); “Redux,” a new play by “The Chief” playwright Rob Zellers (Nov. 19); “The Gift of the Magi” adapted by Brian Pope (Decd. 17); a “The Three Musketeers” story titled “The Inseparables,” by T.J. Young (Jan. 21); “Romeo and Juliet” adapted by Public Theater artistic director Marya Sea Kaminski (Feb. 18); “North of Forbes,” adapted by Tammy Ryan (March 18), “Cyrano de Bergerac” as “The Bergerac Simulation” adapted by Mora Harris (April 15); “Black Ballerina,” a new play by Stephen Fedo and Tim Rhoze (May 20). On June 17, there will be a reading of the work selected as winner of the upcoming new play contest open to writers from Pennsylvania, Ohio and West Virginia.

For details, go to PPT.org/PlayTime. A portion of the suggested donation starting at $10 benefits the artists involved.

“While we look forward to gathering in person again soon, we are thrilled to use these next few months as an opportunity to deeply invest in the future of the Pittsburgh arts community by showcasing the astonishing playwrights in our midst,” Kaminski said.
