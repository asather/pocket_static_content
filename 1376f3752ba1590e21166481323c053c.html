Downed trees knock out power to more than 2,000 in Cranberry and Ligonier area

About 2,300 households and businesses were without power in Cranberry early Wednesday after a tree fell on power lines.

West Penn Power said the tree fell on a line along Powell Road and knocked out power at 12:45 a.m. Three utility poles were damaged and had to be repaired. All but around 70 customers had power restored by 2:15 a.m. and the rest by 6 a.m., the company said.

Strong winds swept through southwestern Pennsylvania Tuesday and into the overnight hours.

Wires also went down in Wilpen north of Ligonier in Westmoreland County, knocking out power to about 440 customers in the area shortly before 3 a.m., West Penn Power said.

The power lines fell in the area of Route 259. Utility crews made repairs and power was fully restored by 7 a.m. Wednesday.
