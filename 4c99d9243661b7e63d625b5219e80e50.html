Sixteen residents at Kane Glen Hazel have died due to COVID-19

COVID-19 has claimed 16 lives of residents at Kane Living Center in Glen Hazel, according the Allegheny County's official website.

The county said as of Monday, 76 residents at the facility have now tested positive for COVID-19. Eight of those patients are hospitalized; 47 patients have recovered from the virus.

Out of 79 staff members at Glen Hazel who tested for COVID-19, 42 have tested positive.

In Kane’s Ross center, there are no positive resident tests, but one positive staff test. One staff test is pending.

In Kane’s Scott center, there are no confirmed cases within the residents, but one positive staff test. One staff test is pending.

In Kane’s McKeesport center, one resident and one staff member have tested positive for the virus. One staff test is pending.
