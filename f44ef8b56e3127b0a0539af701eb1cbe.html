Duquesne man found guilty after police found $22k worth of crack

A federal jury found a former Duquesne resident guilty Friday after he was caught with $22,000 worth of crack while on parole.

Donte Taylor, 37, was found guilty of breaking one count of federal narcotics law, according to a release from the Department of Justice.

Taylor was at his girlfriend’s house when he was visited by parole agents on May 10, 2018. The agents smelled marijuana at the home and administered a drug test.

Taylor tested positive for marijuana and cocaine, and agents notified local police.

After the police arrived, Taylor’s girlfriend admitted there was a small amount of marijuana inside, and gave the officers written and verbal consent to search the house, the released reads. 

When police entered, they found much more: 279 grams of crack cocaine worth between $22,000 to $28,000. They also found cocaine, heroin and fentanyl, plus scales and drug packaging materials.

Under federal law, Taylor could face 10 years to life in prison and a fine of up to $8,000,000.

Taylor’s sentencing hearing is scheduled for June 5, 2020.
