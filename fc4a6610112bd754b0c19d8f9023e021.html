City council extends Pittsburgh’s emergency declaration for another 7 days

Pittsburgh City Council on Wednesday again extended the city’s emergency disaster declaration for seven more days.

The body must approve the declaration every week to keep it in effect.

This is the fifth time council voted to extend the designation since Mayor Bill Peduto declared the emergency on March 13. In early April, members approved the extension for a 26-day stretch.

The declaration allows the city to cancel or limit large gatherings; limit city permits; cancel or limit some city services; and cancel or limit public and private activities.

City council also gave tentative approval on accepting just over $138,000 in federal grant funding to assist the city’s Emergency Medical Services in “preventing, preparing for, and responding to the coronavirus.”

The money will be used to pay for personnel and overtime costs, existing agreements or contracts, and health care-related expenses attributable to the COVID-19 pandemic, according to the legislation summary.

Council is expected to approve the grant Tuesday.
