With eviction moratorium expiring, local activists push for more time

Allegheny County will not extend an eviction moratorium for nonpayment of rent, according to an order issued Monday by President Judge Kim Berkeley Clark. However, the order allows for district judges to provide additional time for tenants and landlords to apply for and receive assistance through a statewide aid program.

A statewide eviction moratorium was set to expire Monday, and it was unclear if it might be extended by the Gov. Tom Wolf.

Mr. Wolf, a Democrat, last week said he would not extend the moratorium further and asked state legislators to step in. On Monday he hinted he might take additional action, but later said he could not.

The statewide program to distribute federal funds to renters and landlords has been slow to get money to needy households; as of Friday, it had only paid out 34 of more than 5,000 applications in Allegheny County.

Activists in Pittsburgh on Monday called on the governor, state legislators, and local court officials to take action to extend the moratorium.

“Folks are just in dire straits,” said Celeste Scott, housing justice organizer for Pittsburgh United.

Advocates have argued that with high unemployment and a pandemic still raging, the moratorium is needed to keep people housed.

“Home is the foundation. When you take away someone’s home, you take away everything,” said Ms. Scott. 

To apply for rental assistance, visit http://phfa.org/pacares/rent.aspx or call 1-855-U-Are-Home (827-3466). Callers should listen for the prompt mentioning CARES assistance.

To apply for rental assistance in Allegheny County, visit https://covidrentrelief.alleghenycounty.us/ or call 412-248-0021.
