Two men arrested after police find heroin and children in Braddock home

Allegheny County police arrested two men in after finding over a thousand stamp bags of heroin in a Braddock home with children in it.

James Wise, 28, and Brandon Balthrop, 31, were arrested after narcotics detectives executed a search warrant at 613 Corey Avenue. Authorities seized 1,650 individual stamp bags of heroin and fentanyl, a stolen .380 caliber handgun, a small quantity of marijuana and $3,175 in cash, according to a release from police.

Two children, ages four and seven, were in the home while the warrant was served, the release says. 

Mr. Balthrop and Wise were charged with felony possession with intent to deliver, receiving stolen property and endangering the welfare of children.

Under a state statute, Wise was not allowed to possess a firearm due to a prior felony drug arrest and conviction, police said. He was out of jail on bond after he was arrested at the same house in December.

During that arrest, detectives found heroin and fentanyl, a stolen 9mm handgun, and $2,700 in cash, according to county police. 

Mr. Balthrop and Wise on Friday were placed in the Allegheny County Jail awaiting preliminary arraignments.

Their preliminary hearing is set for March 24.
