Youngstown prison contractor tests positive for COVID-19

A contractor for the company that operates the Northeast Ohio Correctional Center, which houses some inmates for the U.S. District Court in Pittsburgh, has tested positive for COVID-19.

According to a letter from Warden Michael Phillips sent to inmates on Tuesday, he received notice that a contractor for CoreCivic tested positive, and had been inside the Youngstown facility on April 2.

A spokesman for CoreCivic said the contractor worked for TransCor, which provides transportation for prisoners.

Neither the spokesman nor letter said how many people may have been exposed to that employee.

In his letter, Warden Phillips said the company had enacted its emergency response plan which identifies staff and inmates who may have had close contact with the contractor, and require isolation.

“This response enables the facility to take the steps necessary to ensure the facility is properly disinfected for your health and safety,” the letter said.

The warden noted, too, that local public health officials had also been notified.

All social visitation at the facility was canceled as of March 12.

The facility is housing about 1,800 inmates and has a capacity of about 1,900, said employee Carey Thake.
