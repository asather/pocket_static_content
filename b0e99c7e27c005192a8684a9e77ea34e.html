Protesters call for complete investigation into May 26 death of Aaliyah Johnson

Protesters marched through McKeesport Friday to call for a complete investigation into the death of a 32-year-old trans woman found dead after a fall from her apartment in the city.

Although the Allegheny County Medical Examiner’s office has ruled the May 26 death of Aaliyah Johnson a suicide, Post-Gazette news partner KDKA-TV has reported that an Allegheny County police investigation is continuing.

“I feel as though it’s my duty to make sure there’s a fair investigation and we can lay rest to all speculation,” Terrance McGeorge, Aaliyah’s friend and organizer of Friday’s protest told KDKA.

“She was very spiritual. She was talented. She could do hair, she could dance, she could sing,” Mr. McGeorge added.

And people who knew Ms. Johnson say they’re not so sure she took her own life, telling KDKA  that she spent some of her final days living in fear.

“This lady was receiving death threats,” said Chauntey Porter, executive director of TransYOUniting. “This lady was trying to get out of the city.”

“Even if she would have done something like that, there would have been a note. There was nothing there,” said Dena Stanley, founder of TransYOUniting.

County police told KDKA while there’s no indication of foul play at this time, they continue to comb through video as the investigation continues.
