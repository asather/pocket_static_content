Wesco's "Hail Mary" bid for Anixter deemed superior for the first time

Wesco International Inc. has again increased its bid to acquire an Illinois-based supplier of electrical equipment and, for the first time since the bidding war began, its proposal was deemed the better one.

Station Square-based Wesco has been pursuing Anixter International for months. It is battling private equity firm Clayton, Dubilier & Rice, LLC which is also trying to acquire Anixter and, in the middle of this proxy battle, offered to buy Wesco as well.

A large distributor of products for utilities, industrial and construction clients, Wesco has been on the prowl for a large acquisition that would consolidate the electrical distribution space.

In its latest proposal, Wesco offered Anixter shareholders $100 in Wesco stock (both common stock and a new category of preferred stock) for each of their Anixter shares. This is Wesco’s eighth proposal and the first one that Anixter’s board deemed to be the “superior company proposal.”

This kicks off a period of five business days where CD&R, which already has a merger agreement in place with Anixter, has the right to respond and up the ante. If, at the end, Anixter breaks its agreement with CD&R, Wesco has agreed to pay the $100 million termination fee.

“Basically this is a Hail Mary for the (Pittsburgh) company,” said Hamzah Mazari, a managing director with Jefferies who covers Wesco.

Not only is Anixter the only large, publicly-traded electric distribution company up for sale, satisfying Wesco’s declared thirst for a “transformational deal,” Mr. Mazari said, but CD&R’s $68 per share offer for the Pittsburgh firm is now menacing Wesco’s current existence.

“If they don’t win this asset, they’re probably subject to a takeover from CD&R,” he said.

Mr. Mazari said it is likely that CD&R will counter the latest Wesco proposal, but once the offer price per share gets above $103, the deal would be hard to justify financially.
