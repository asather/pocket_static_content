Portion of Nadine Road closed for construction reopens in Penn Hills

A portion of Nadine Road in Penn Hills that had been closed for more than a month has reopened, Allegheny County officials said Friday.

 

Nadine Road had been closed between Lincoln Road and Allegheny River Boulevard/Route 130 since Aug. 10 for construction of two support walls and installation of a drainage pipe.

However, paving work will continue through October and will cause traffic stoppages. Flaggers will provide traffic control.

The overall work, which began Feb. 28, is being done by Northeast Paving in Pittsburgh as part of a $10.7 million capital roads contract.
