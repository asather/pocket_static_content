GNC shares fall below $1

GNC shares closed Thursday at 99 cents, a level that can draw the attention and possible delisting warning from the New York Stock Exchange if shares remain below $1 for 30 days.

Even given Wall Street’s overall poor performance this week due to the COVID-19 virus fallout, the Pittsburgh-based health and wellness retailer’s 20% drop from Wednesday’s $1.24 share price close is notable, even if the reasons for it are unclear.

GNC officials did not respond to a request for comment late Thursday afternoon. The company has not yet reported its fourth quarter and full year 2019 financials.

Now eight years after its initial public offering in 2012, GNC shares in late November 2013 hit $60 but have steadily declined since the summer of 2015.

The Dow closed Thursday down 10% at 21,200.62, its worst day since 1987.
