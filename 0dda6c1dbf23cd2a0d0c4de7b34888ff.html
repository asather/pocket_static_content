Shrines parishes to reopen Monday after COVID-19 scare

The parishes in the Shrines of Pittsburgh will hold Mass on Monday after an employee tested negative for COVID-19 following potential exposure to the virus.

The churches on Friday canceled weekend Masses after the employeeâ€™s spouse tested positive for the novel coronavirus, and the Catholic Diocese of Pittsburgh said the parishes would undergo cleaning before reopening.

The parishes in the Shrines grouping are Immaculate Heart of Mary in Polish Hill, Most Holy Name of Jesus and St. Anthony Chapel in Troy Hill, St. Nicholas in Millvale, and St. Patrick and St. Stanislaus Kostka in the Strip District.

This week marked the first time since March that the diocese has held in-person Masses following restrictions put in place to mitigate the spread of COVID-19.
