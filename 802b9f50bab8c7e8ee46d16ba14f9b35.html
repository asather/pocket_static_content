Pa. identifies six more possible cases of COVID-19 in southeastern counties

Six more cases presumed to be COVID-19 virus infections have been confirmed, Pennsylvania Department of Health officials said Friday, bringing the statewide total to 28.

Four of the new cases involve Montgomery County residents and the other two reside in Delaware County.

In addition, state officials say the federal Centers for Disease Control and Prevention has confirmed that six earlier presumptive cases have been verified: One each in Delaware and Wayne counties and four in Montgomery County.

The Allegheny County Health Department is expected to release updated recommendations for the public Friday afternoon "due to developments over the past 24 hours,” according to spokesman Ryan Scarpino.

He also emphasized that there are still no positive COVID-19 cases in Allegheny County.

On Wednesday, county health officials issued guidance urging people to avoid large events and mass gatherings, in addition to recommending frequent hand washing and staying home if you’re feeling ill.

In the meantime, the University of Pittsburgh, Carnegie Mellon University and other colleges have moved spring classes online and, on Thursday, major professional, collegiate and high school sports authorities canceled or postponed major championships including March Madness and the Masters golf tournament.
