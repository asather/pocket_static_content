Pitt announces its 2020 Hall of Fame class

Men's basketball star Brandin Knight, prolific women's scorer Jennifer Bruce and Pro Football Hall of Fame running back Curtis Martin highlight the 2020 Pitt Athletics Hall of Fame class, a 13-member group that was announced Tuesday night.

Knight, a point guard from 1999-2003, led Pitt to consecutive Big East regular-season titles, two Sweet 16 appearances and the program's first Big East tournament title. The two-time All-American still holds Pitt records for career assists (785), career steals (298), season minutes played (1,284 in 2001-02) and more. Knight, who also served on Pitt's coaching staff from 2006-16, had his No. 20 jersey retired in 2009.

Bruce, like Knight, is considered one of the most decorated players in Pitt basketball history. She is the second all-time leading scorer in Panthers history — men's or women's — with 2,295 points, behind Lorri Johnson, who was inducted in last year's class. A three-time All-Big East honoree, Bruce still ranks in the top 10 in blocks, rebounds and steals in Pitt history.

And Martin, an Allderdice graduate, rushed for 2,643 yards at Pitt despite missing most of his final year due to injury. He was a third-round draft choice in 1995 and went on to a Pro Football Hall of Fame career, rushing for 14,101 yards total with the New England Patriots and New York Jets.
