Upcoming 'COPS' episodes will feature Pittsburgh police

Whatchu gonna do when they come for you?

That’s a question yinzers will be able to get some answers to in several new episodes of the television series “COPS” that will feature Pittsburgh police.

Last week, the docu-style drama, which has been running for over three decades, tweeted out a photo of a city police cruiser heading across the Roberto Clemente Bridge.

“Pittsburgh, we have arrived,” the tweet read.

Three different production crews filmed throughout the city for about eight weeks last summer, gathering enough footage for five episodes, said Pittsburgh Public Safety spokeswoman Cara Cruz.

The first episode will air at 10 p.m. Monday, Feb. 10 on the Paramount Network.

Crews taped in zones 2, 3 and 5, as well as with narcotics and vice detectives, Ms. Cruz said.

Currently in its 32nd season, COPS will air two more Pittsburgh-based episodes in March. The show begins a new season this summer when the remaining two episodes will air.

COPS has filmed in Pittsburgh twice before; once in the early 1990s and again in the mid-2000s.
