Allegheny County Health Department issues two consumer alerts

Two establishments were hit with consumer alerts last week by the Allegheny County Health Department. 

The Safari Club on Grant Street in Duquesne was issued a consumer alert on Feb. 26 for violations that included unsound food source/condition; lack of sufficient and commercial refrigeration; improper storage of raw and ready-to-eat food; inadequate cleaning and sanitization; blocked handwashing facilities; inadequate pest management; and improper storage of toxic items. The phone line listed for the establishment had been disconnected.

Olive Oil's Pizzeria on Brookline Boulevard in Brookline was issued a consumer alert on Feb. 28 for recurring violations including improper cold holding, inadequate facilities to maintain temperature, and lack of a certified food protection manager. A manager at the restaurant declined to comment.
