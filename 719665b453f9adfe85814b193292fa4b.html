People on the move: 5/25/2020

Associations & Organizations

Pittsburgh lawyer Rodney R. Akers, deputy general counsel in the Governor’s Office of General Counsel, will serve a three-year term as a minority governor on the Pennsylvania Bar Association Board of Governors. Mr. Akers’ principal areas of legal expertise are in information technology, intellectual property, cyberspace issues, regulatory compliance and contracts. He is an adjunct professor of law at the University of Pittsburgh Law School, teaching courses that are part of the law school’s Innovation Practice Institute.

Banking

First National Bank promoted Karen Homrock to senior vice president, manager of residential mortgage servicing. 

Professional Services

Newton Talent appointed Jason Rellick vice president of client service and delivery.

Tourism

VisitPittsburgh announced its 2020 officers, as well as the election of six new board members. Officers are: chairman Timothy Q. Hudak, CEO, Eckert Seamans; vice chairman Thomas E. Martini, general manager, The Westin Pittsburgh; treasurer Tommy Johnson, vice president, external relations and corporate responsibility, CNX; secretary Michele Meloy Burchfield, co-founder and CEO, Blume Honey Water; and immediate past chairman Bryan N. Iams, vice president, corporate communications, marketing, and government and community affairs, PPG Industries. Board members are: Gino Caliendo, general manager, Omni William Penn Hotel; Scott Frenz, senior vice president, sales and marketing, Fenner Dunlop Americas; Dan Griffin, president, Griffin-Spence; Kevin Kilkeary, president and CEO, Prospera Hospitality; Meredith Meyer-Grelli, co-founder/co-owner, Wigle Whiskey and Threadbare Cider & Mead; and Troy Schooley, CEO, P3R.

Awards and Honors

Three California University of Pennsylvania faculty members have received Presidential Distinguished Merit Awards for excellence in service, teaching and scholarship. They are: Sheri Boyle, Louise Nicholson and Kimberly Vanderlaan. Ms. Boyle, associate professor, chair of the department of social work and master of social work program director, was honored for excellence in service. Ms. Nicholson, an associate professor and chair of the Department of Biological and Environmental Sciences, was honored for excellence in teaching. Ms. Vanderlaan, an associate professor in the Department of English, was honored for excellence in scholarship in her area of expertise: late 19th- and 20th-century American literature.
