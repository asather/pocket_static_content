Pirates sign catcher John Ryan Murphy to minors deal

Ahead of a busy Friday of arbitration decisions, the Pirates made a move to add to their catching depth by signing John Ryan Murphy to a minor league contract. Murphy's deal comes with a spring training invite.

The 29-year-old started the 2019 season with the Arizona Diamondbacks, where he hit .177 in 25 games with 11 hits in 62 at-bats. The Diamondbacks designated him for assignment and the Atlanta Braves claimed him off waivers.

Murphy is a .219 career hitter in his seven big league seasons. His best batting averages came in 2014 (.284) and 2015 (.277) for the New York Yankees.

Murphy has a reputation as a good pitch framer and being a sound defender. He will compete with Jacob Stallings and the recently signed Luke Maile for playing time during spring training.

Murphy should have some familiarity with the Pirate City facility and LECOM Park as a native of Bradenton, Fla.

The Pirates also gave infielder Jake Elmore a non-roster invite to spring training. Elmore played in 20 games for the Pirates in 2019, hitting .213 in 47 at-bats.
