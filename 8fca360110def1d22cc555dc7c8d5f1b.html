Man acquitted in Wilkinsburg massacre appears in federal court by video on new charge

The man acquitted earlier this year in the killings of five people at a Wilkinsburg cookout appeared by video before a federal judge on Thursday on a weapons charge and is set for a U.S. detention hearing next week.

Cheron Shelton is under indictment on a count of illegally possessing a gun and ammunition as a felon and is in U.S. custody.

A federal grand jury handed up the charge on Feb. 25, about a week after an Allegheny County Common Pleas jury acquitted him in the 2016 mass shooting that left five dead and three injured.

Shelton, 33, had an initial appearance by video conference before U.S. Magistrate Judge Cynthia Reed Eddy, who appointed a lawyer and set a detention hearing for Monday.

That hearing will also be held by video conference because of the coronavirus.

Shelton is accused of possession of a .22-caliber rifle and ammunition for it. As a felon, he can't have either. During the investigation of the March 9, 2016 mass killing, police searched Shelton's mother's house in Homewood and said they found the gun and bullets.

The weapon is not the gun used in the killings, which was an assault rifle.

If Shelton is convicted on the new charge, he is likely to get at least five years in federal prison.
