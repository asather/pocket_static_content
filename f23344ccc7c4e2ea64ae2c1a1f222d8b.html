Epps brothers lead South Allegheny to 77-59 win vs. Steel Valley, claim section title

Steel Valley’s Cam Polak is having a season to remember, and he continued his torrid scoring pace Friday night with a 36-point performance at South Allegheny

The Gladiators, though, are having a season their fans might never forget.

WPIAL Class 3A No. 4-ranked South Allegheny (15-1, 9-0) wrapped up only the second section title in school history — and its first since 1997 — with a 77-59 win against the Ironmen (10-6, 6-3).

“This is the most success we’ve had in a really, really long time,” Gladiators coach Tony DiCenzo said. “All the hard work and the dedication that these kids have been putting in, we’re seeing the results of that now. We’re a young team. We don’t have a senior, so this could be a sign of things to come here.”

South Allegheny’s hot-shooting tandem of brothers Bryce and Antonio Epps led the way with 30 and 29 points, respectively. Bryce, a freshman, scored 20 of his career-high 30 in the second half, while Antonio, a junior, put up 17 after the break to help close out the win.

Not only that, Bryce registered four steals, including three while guarding Polak.

“It feels great, because this is the first time in a while we’ve had a gym full of people supporting us,” Bryce Epps said. “I’m just at a loss for words right now. It’s unbelievable.

“To go out and play the way we did, it feels great.”

The Gladiators went 6-15 a year ago and won just 28 games combined over the past five seasons. They are only four years removed from a 1-20 season, and the year before DiCenzo arrived in 2011-12 they went 0-21.

Now, they’re tied for the second-most wins in the WPIAL, and they’re beginning to set their sights on loftier goals than just winning a section title.

“Kudos to our kids for showing up in a big moment,” DiCenzo said. “It’s the first time they’ve ever had a packed gym to capacity, and with what was at stake and the environment here, it didn’t rattle us, and that tells me we’re capable of bigger and better things.”

The skinny freshman point guard with a silky-smooth shooting stroke and poise beyond his years is certainly a big reason why, but he’s quick to deflect the credit to the rest of his teammates.

“It’s all a team effort,” Bryce Epps said. “We have great chemistry. We all support each other. It doesn’t matter if I’m the leading scorer or my brother’s the leading scorer.

“We’re a family. We all support each other no matter what.”

Bryce Epps scored 10 points in the first quarter, showing no fear in the biggest game of his young high school career while helping South Allegheny jump to a 17-5 lead. Steel Valley couldn’t get any type of rhythm going on offense, and the Gladiators soon built a 25-9 lead and threatened to turn the game into a blowout.

That’s when shots started to fall for the Ironmen, who went on a 14-4 run to close out the half and cut South Allegheny’s lead to 29-23 at the break.

The Gladiators began to regain momentum on back-to-back 3-pointers by Bryce Epps early in the third quarter, but Polak started heating up with back-to-back 3s of his own. A 3-pointer by Shane Reagan cut South Allegheny’s lead to four, but Bryce Epps made a veteran play with a steal and score after an inbounds pass to put the Gladiators up, 49-41, late in the third.

Bryce Epps closed the third quarter with a 3-pointer, then drained another 3 to start the fourth, giving South Allegheny a 55-44 lead. His older brother then helped seal the win with clutch free-throw shooting and timely 3-pointers late in the final quarter.

Antonio Epps, who came into the game averaging a team-high 19.1 points per game, was as proud as could be of his younger brother — but he wasn’t surprised.

“I honestly feel like he’s made for it,” he said.

“He puts in a lot of work, getting up early in the morning, doing a lot of drills and getting shots up.

“I feel like he’s definitely built for these moments, and today, he proved it.”
