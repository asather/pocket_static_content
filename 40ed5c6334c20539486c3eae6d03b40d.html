For second straight day, Allegheny County's cases increase by more than 100

Allegheny County on Wednesday topped its highest single-day increase of positive COVID-19 cases with an additional 110 new cases, according to the county health department.

This is the second day in a row that the county has seen a daily increase of more than 100 new cases, and happens just a day after the county imposed a ban of alcohol sales for on-site consumption at bars and restaurants.

With the additional cases, the new total for the county is 2,870.

The statewide total for COVID-19 cases is now 87,242 with an additional 636 cases being reported by the state health department on Wednesday. Western Pennsylvania accounts for 194 of those new cases — Allegheny County had the highest increase in cases, followed by Westmoreland County with 29 new cases of the virus.

In the last seven days, Allegheny County has seen an increase of 586 cases. County officials are attributing the recent spike in cases to people visiting bars and restaurants, specifically in the Oakland and South Side areas.

On Monday, the state health department said that, despite the rise, it had no plans to move Allegheny County back into the yellow phase.

Due to the increase in cases being seen around the state, the state health department released a new early warning dashboard on its website.

The Early Warning Monitoring System Dashboard allows users to pick a county and see a comparison of the last two weeks in categories such as new case counts, the incident rate per 100,000 residents and the average number of COVID-19 hospitalizations, among others.

Hospitalizations in the county on Wednesday increased by four, making the total 397 past and present hospitalizations.

Statewide, there was an increase of 38 new deaths, three of which were in Western Pennsylvania — Allegheny County reported one and Erie County reported two additional deaths.

The death toll for Allegheny County is now 187, reported by both state and county health departments.
