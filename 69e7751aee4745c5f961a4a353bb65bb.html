If it's not toilet paper, don't flush it, plumbers say

Jordan Echard, general manager of the Pittsburgh Division for Roto-Rooter, recalled the early rush at the stores when folks began stocking up on supplies in March.

“I was at Sam’s Club and watching people buying up wet wipes, paper towels, napkins. When you start flushing that stuff, you’re going to get in trouble real fast.”

With more people staying home during the COVID-19 pandemic, plumbers are staying busy, even as elective services like that bathroom renovation are down.

Turns out, just because that pack of wet wipes claims to be flushable doesn’t mean they really are.

Baby wipes and the like don’t break down the way toilet paper does and can get stuck in the pipes. And who wants that headache when you’re already self-isolating?

“They’re not made to break down,” Mr. Echard said. “And depending on how your house is situated, if there’s not a lot of incline on your pipes, they’ll get lodged and then nothing goes through. It backs up into the house.”

Ernie Fagnelli, owner, of Fagnelli Plumbing, Heating, & Cooling in Oakland, said calls for clogged drains have been coming in steadily.

“The key thing is, even if it says it’s flushable — none of that biodegrades quickly,” he said.

While plumbing businesses are considered essential during the shutdown due to the novel coronavirus outbreak, the companies are taking precautions when they enter someone’s home.

Roto-Rooter is sanitizing vehicles and social distancing while wearing protective gear.

Mr. Fagnelli said the precautions help everyone involved.

“We’re wearing gloves, masks coveralls,” Mr. Fagnelli said. “We’re protecting them and our own families.”
