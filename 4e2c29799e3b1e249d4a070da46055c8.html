City launches housing resource website

If you are a renter seeking affordable housing or facing homelessness, a homeowner looking for a first house, or a landlord seeking guidance, the city has a new website to direct you to the program or person who can help.

The Housing Assistance Resource Portal, which launched Thursday, screens website users based on need, age, income and ability to direct them to an appropriate online resource or phone number.

The site is a joint project between the city, the Urban Redevelopment Authority and the Housing Authority, and includes resources from Allegheny County, Neighborhood Allies, the Federal Home Loan Bank and the Pittsburgh Housing Finance Agency.

Mayor Bill Peduto described the resource as “a one-stop shop for housing.”

“It’s  something that I’ve asked for, for about 5 years,” he said by phone Wednesday. “During the pandemic, our team across multiple departments and authorities worked together to be able to help, no matter what you’re situation is.” 

The administration is awaiting city council approval for the allocation of roughly $13 million in federal funds for community development and emergency housing.

About $2 million will be dedicated to the URA’s mortgage and rental programs for those facing economic hardship from COVID-19. Other housing services programs are also slated to receive funds, including $50,000 for NeighborWorks Foreclosure Prevention, $50,000 for the Women’s Center & Shelter of Greater Pittsburgh, $40,000 for the Pittsburgh Commission on Human Relations pre-court landlord-tenant/anti-eviction mediation, and $40,000 for Action Housing assistance to youths aging out of place.

The city faces a deficit of 17,000 affordable housing units, according to a 2016 city-commissioned affordable housing task force report.

Majestic Lane, the mayor’s deputy chief of staff and chief equity officer, said the resource is “critical, especially in the time of this pandemic,” according to a Thursday press release.

Those outside of the city can contact the county’s Allegheny Link service at 1-866-730-2368.
