City paving to resume May 11

The City of Pittsburgh will resume asphalt street resurfacing on May 11.

The city plans to pave 27 miles of street this year and has budgeted $15.7 million for asphalt resurfacing, ADA curb ramps, and concrete, brick and block stone streets.

Residents can find a weekly paving schedule at pittsburghpa.gov/domi/paving-schedule.

Yard debris collection has been rescheduled for May 16.

Leaves, brush and grass must be in paper bags weighing less than 35 pounds, according to the Department of Public Works. Branches must be less than 4 inches in diameter and bundled with string or twine.
