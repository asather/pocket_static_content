WEATHER: Hot and humid day on tap for Father's Day with summer's arrival

The official start of summer will usher in a hot and humid Father’s Day throughout the Pittsburgh region.

The summer solstice officially arrives Saturday at 5:43 p.m., heralding the longest day and shortest night of the year.

The high temperature on Sunday should reach into the high 80s with a “calm wind,” according to the National Weather Service in Pittsburgh.

But with humidity in the 60% to 70% range, it’ll feel more “like 91 to 93 degrees,” said weather service meteorologist David Shallenberger.

Pittsburghers will wake up to temps in the low-60s. It should stay mostly sunny during the daylight hours, then some precipitation is expected to move in.

“We have a really good chance of showers and thunderstorms,” Shallenberger said. “From 5 to 8 p.m. you’re looking at a 50% chance.”

Today’s high was expected to reach 83 degrees.
