Pitt names new health sciences chief

A physician at Indiana University who is a leading educator and researcher is poised to become the University of Pittsburgh's next senior vice chancellor for health sciences and dean of its school of medicine, officials said Tuesday.

Dr. Anantha Shekhar now works for IU's medical school in Indianapolis, the nation's largest, where holds various leadership roles. He is due to begin his duties in June, subject to a vote by Pitt’s board of trustees.

The trustees’ vote, expected at their winter meeting in February, is the last step in the hiring process and it would make Dr. Shekhar an officer of the university, officials announced.

Dr. Shekhar succeeds Dr. Arthur Levine, who has announced his plans to transition to a new research role within Pitt.

According to Pitt officials, Dr. Shekhar's multiple titles at the IU medical school include: Executive associate dean for research affairs, distinguished professor, associate vice president for university clinical affairs and research, executive vice president of academic affairs for clinical research at IU Health, and founding director of the Indiana Clinical and Translational Sciences Institute, the sole statewide institute of its kind.

“This is a critical hire for the University of Pittsburgh,” said Chancellor Patrick Gallagher. 

“Anantha’s capacity to envision solutions, galvanize partnerships and produce results is second to none, and his record of propelling both people and institutions to success is unparalleled. I could not be more excited to welcome Anantha into our community, and I am already looking forward to his arrival in June,” he added.

Born in India, Dr. Shekhar holds a medical degree from St. John’s Medical College and PhD in neuroscience at IU, Pitt officials said. He and his wife, Dr. Gina Laite, have two children.

Once he arrives, he will oversee all six health sciences schools at Pitt, more than 6,000 faculty and staff within them, and be dean of the John and Gertrude Petersen Dean of the School of Medicine at Pitt. He also will work with the university's medical partner, UPMC.

Pitt Provost Ann Cudd said: “Anantha’s exceptional and deep experience in basic research leading to novel, patentable therapeutics and in directing large, multi-institutional research initiatives, as well as being an award-winning teacher, will provide transformative contributions to our efforts going forward.”

"I am honored and inspired to be joining one of the most respected medical and research communities in the world, Dr. Shekhar said in a statement that accompanied the announcement.

“Building on the University of Pittsburgh’s exceptional record of health sciences education, innovation, clinical excellence and research preeminence is my top priority, and I look forward to advancing this goal in partnership with UPMC — for the greater good of society — in the months to come.” he added.

Pitt officials said Dr. Shekhar has been recipient of continuous funding from the National Institutes of Health since 1989 for basic, clinical and translational research. He has written 200-plus articles in peer-reviewed publications.
