COVID-19 Update: Western Pa. reports 263 new cases; Allegheny has highest single-day hospitalizations

Allegheny County health officials Thursday reported its highest single-day increase in COVID-19 hospitalizations with 23 new patients, as 132 more cases of the virus were recorded. 

Thursday also marks the eighth day in a row that case counts have reached over 100 new cases a day. The newest 132 county cases come from 1,800 tests that were taken between July 15 and July 29. Those diagnosed range in age from 9 months to 94 years, with a median age of 39.

Statewide, there have been 860 new cases, bringing the state total to 111,078. Of those cases, approximately 8,165 of them are among health care workers, according to state figures.

Western Pennsylvania accounted for 263 of the state’s new infections for Thursday. Allegheny County had the highest increase for the day, followed by Westmoreland County with 31 new cases, which brings its total to 1,382.

Allegheny has now had a total of 7,850 cases of the virus since the first recorded case in mid-March.

With the county’s 23 new hospitalizations reported for Thursday, the county has now had 636 people who have required hospitalization since the pandemic began in March, 100 of which occurred in the last eight days. The second highest hospitalization increase that the county saw was on July 24, with 22 additional hospitalizations.

The Pennsylvania health department is reporting 14 new deaths for the state, which brings the new death toll to 7,176. Of the newly reported deaths, five of them occurred in Western Pennsylvania: one in Allegheny, three in Beaver and one in Washington. 

Allegheny’s additional death was of a person in their 60s. A specific death date is not given, but the county health department says it is “from earlier this week.” The county’s deaths now stand at 235.
