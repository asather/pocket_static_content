New $88 million engineering building up for vote by Penn State board

Plans for a new $88 million research and teaching building for Penn State University's College of Engineering will be up for approval Friday afternoon as the board of trustees meets.

The development dubbed West 2, slated for a site near the western edge of Penn State’s main University Park campus, was endorsed on Thursday by the board’s finance, business and capital planning committee. It is part of a decade-long initiative to enhance the Penn State College of Engineering and its facilities

Payette, an architecture firm from Boston, was hired last year to design two new engineering buildings.

The building project going before trustees will include 105,000 square feet for flexible classrooms, multi-use design studios, “cornerstone to capstone” maker spaces, a high-bay research lab, faculty offices and research spaces, Penn State officials said in a project summary.

It will be built adjacent to the West Campus Parking Deck currently under construction.

The board also is expected to act on Penn State’s appropriation request for the 2021-22 fiscal year and discuss topics from COVID-19 to the resumption of Big Ten football this fall, backed by President Eric Barron.
