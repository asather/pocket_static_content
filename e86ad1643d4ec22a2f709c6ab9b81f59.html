DAILY HOT LIST: Dramatic readings, local drag show, movies, books and Jimmy Fallon

The Post-Gazette daily Hot List highlights news about virtual entertainment and performers from the worlds of music, dance, movies, television, gaming and all things online. And we may throw in some of what PG staffers are enjoying from home, too.

For any time of day or night, check the livestream events list, Going Live, at www.post-gazette.com/ae, which is being updated as news happens.

Starting Friday, March 20

THEATER: ‘Chronicles’ of assistance

On May 11, 2015, Mark Clayton Southers, founder and leader of Pittsburgh Playwrights Theater company, was seriously injured in a car accident. Over the next year he wrote 99 essays documenting his recovery.

With theaters closed nationwide, Southers, a writer, director and photographer, established an Artist Assistance Fund to distribute donations to Pittsburgh theater artists in need. He reached out to 99 actors to support this mission, and they have begun readings of “The 99 Chronicles,” available at www.pghplaywrights.org. First up on Friday was “Chronicles #20: Pills, pills, pills!,” read by David Conrad.

The videos at pghplaywrights.org are free, but the nonprofit Pittsburgh Playwrights is asking for contributions to its assistance fund.

Saturday, March 21

THEATER: Drive-in stage

As Pittsburgh theaters find ways to reach audiences, the Plaza Theatre Co. of Cleburne, Texas (near Fort Worth), is finding a way to work and maintain social distance. After having to shut down its production of "Little House on the Prairie," the theater regrouped to create drive-in live theater.

The actors will perform a free "drive up" theater production called "The Nifty Fifties." Patrons can park in the theater's parking lot and watch and listen (via radio set up) from their cars. The show will feature fifties songs by a small group of five performers, singing mostly solos and dud

The cabaret style show begins at 7:30 p.m. and will stream live on The Plaza Theatre Facebook page.

WEEKNIGHTS

TELEVISION: ‘The Tonight Show: At Home Edition’

If you want to get a sense of what it’s like for celebrities to work and parent 24/7, plus see stars such as Lin-Manuel Miranda and Jennifer Garner, check out “The Tonight Show: At Home Edition.”

While other late-night talk show hosts have gone to strictly repeats, JImmy Fallon and his family have been recording from home. His wife, Nancy, is his camera operator, and his three daughters create the “graphics” for the NBC show, airing at 11:35 p.m.

On Thursday, Miranda was his first guest, via Zoom, and performed “Dear Theodosia” from his at-home keyboard, while Garner played “Happy Birthday” on the clarinet. Both artists pitched for charities, including #SaveWithStories, launched by actresses Garner and Amy Adams as a fundraiser for Save The Children and No Kid Hungry (more at www.savethechildren.org).

LISTEN/READ: Top fiction books of 2019

•  Pulitzer Prize: "The Overstory" by Richard Powers (W.W. Norton) — The fictional story of nine strangers brought together by an unfolding natural catastrophe.

• Audio Book of the Year: "Children of Blood and Bone" by Tomi Adeyemi, narrated by Bahni Turpin (Macmillan Audio) — A world of dark magic and danger in the author's West African-inspired fantasy debut.  **

• H​ugo Award and Nebula Award (science fiction): ​"The Calculating Stars​: A Lady Astronauts Novel" by Mary Robinette Kowal​ (Tor Books) — ​​ ​“A fine balance of integrating historical accuracy ― including mid-​20th-century sexism, racism, and technology ― with speculative storytelling.” (According to Booklist.)

• Booker Prize winners (tie): "The Testaments" by Margaret Atwood (Vintage, Chatto & Windus​)​, a sequel to "The Handmaid's Tale" by Margaret Atwood and "Girl, Woman, Other" by Bernardine Evaristo​ (Hamish Hamilton)​​, about the lives and struggles of ​12 characters​, mostly women, black and British​.

​• Will Eisner Awards: ​"Umami" by Ken Niimura (Panel Syndicate), best digital comic, at panelsyndicate.com/comics/umami, and  "The Contradictions" by Sophie Yanow, best web comic, at thecontradictions.com.
