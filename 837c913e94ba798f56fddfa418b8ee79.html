Central Valley's Isiah Warfield named to all-state team at second different school

It’s fairly unusual for a Pennsylvania high school basketball player to make all-state at two different schools. But it has happened for the second consecutive year for a WPIAL player.

The Pennsylvania Sports Writers All-State Team for Class 4A was released Thursday, and Central Valley senior guard Isiah Warfield was a second-team pick. Warfield’s selection comes after he made the Class 2A teams the past two years when he played at Sewickley Academy. Warfield was a first-team selection in 2018 and 2019 before transferring to Central Valley.

It was only a year ago that Donovan “Puff” Johnson made first-team Class 5A at Moon High School. In 2018, Johnson made second-team in Class 2A when he played at Our Lady of the Sacred Heart before transferring to Moon.

As for Warfield, he is a 6-foot-5 point guard who also made the Post-Gazette Fabulous 5. Warfield averaged 21.2 points a game and shot 49% from the field. He did a little of everything for Central Valley, also averaging 8.5 rebounds and 4 assists. He finished his career with 1,679 points. Warfield has signed with Liberty University, which qualified for the NCAA tournament this season and in 2019.

Warfield is one of only two WPIAL players that made the Class 4A team. The other was Highlands senior forward Johnny Crise, who was a third-team selection. Crise is a 6-6 forward who plans to play football at Penn State as a walk-on. But Crise sure did have plenty of highlights from his final basketball season.

Crise was on a team that won the WPIAL Class 4A championship and he was selected the Post-Gazette Class 4A Player of the Year. He averaged 14.7 points, 8.8 rebounds, 1.8 blocks and shot 69% from the field. Crise was a player who didn’t mind contact, evidenced by him taking 61 charges during his career.

The Class 4A player of the year on the all-state team is Jordan Longino of Germantown Academy. The coach of the year is Brendan Stanton of Pope John Paul II in eastern Pa.
