Second Port Authority driver tests positive for COVID-19

Port Authority on Saturday announced a bus driver tested positive for COVID-19, with the operator becoming the second employee at the authority to test positive for the novel coronavirus.

The employee works out of the West Mifflin garage and has not been to work since March 21. The driver has been in “medically-directed” quarantine since Wednesday, according to a release from Port Authority.

No other information about the person was made available.

The authority, after learning of the diagnosis, conducted a “deep cleaning” of the West Mifflin garage and the last bus the operator drove.

“On behalf of our board and the entire management team, I want to extend our thoughts and best wishes to the employee to make a full and speedy recovery,” Port Authority CEO Katharine Kelleman wrote in a statement.

Another bus driver tested positive in late March.

Port Authority reportedly has 2,600 employees made up of some 1,300 vehicle operators and 800 mechanics.
