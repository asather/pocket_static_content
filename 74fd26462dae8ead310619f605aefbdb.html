Philadelphia Eagles running back makes tribute to Antwon Rose II

Miles Sanders, a former standout running back at Woodland Hills High School and Penn State, planned to honor Antwon Rose II during Sunday’s game between the Philadelphia Eagles and the Washington Football Team.

Sanders, an Eagles tailback, posted onto his Instagram account a photo of his helmet with Rose’s name written across the back, incorrectly writing Antwon Rose Jr. However, Sanders will miss his team’s opener due to a hamstring injury, according to NFL.com.

Rose, 17, who was a graduate of Woodland Hills, was killed on June 19, 2018, when he was shot three times while running from former East Pittsburgh police officer Michael Rosfeld during a traffic stop.

Rosfeld has since been acquitted.

Last season as a rookie, Sanders led the Eagles with 819 yards rushing. He was a second-round pick in 2019.
