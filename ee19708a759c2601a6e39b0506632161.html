Penguins re-sign Juuso Riikola, despite playing him sparingly in second half of 2019-20

Despite playing him sparingly in the second half of last season, the Penguins announced Saturday that they’ve signed Juuso Riikola to a two-year deal.

The left-shot defenseman’s contract, which runs through the 2021-22 season, will have an average annual value of $1.15 million — a slight raise for the Finn.

“When he is on his game, Juuso has shown the ability to be a solid contributor for our team,” general manager Jim Rutherford said of the restricted free agent. “We feel he is going to take the next step forward in his development.”

How often is Riikola on his game, though? It’s a question worth exploring.

The 26-year-old played 36 games last season. He was asked to be a winger in four of those games due to injuries and a tight salary cap situation. The Penguins played Zach Trotman, a righty, over him down the stretch. Riikola did not suit up in the playoffs even though the third pair struggled against Montreal.

Advanced statistics that are publicly available suggest Riikola made a positive impact last season. The Penguins generated 59.1% of the high-danger chances with Riikola on the ice at 5-on-5. That led all Pittsburgh defensemen.

But the coaching staff didn’t show much faith in him, perhaps because of major mental lapses like the one he had Feb. 20 when he drifted into no-man’s land in the neutral zone in a loss to Toronto. Out of position, Riikola let Kaspari Kapanen, his new Penguins teammate, sneak behind him for a breakaway goal.

Someone wanted Riikola and his big shot and plus skating back in Pittsburgh. He will enter training camp as a candidate to play on the left side in the third pair. For now, Jack Johnson and his $3.25 million salary remain on the squad. Prospect P.O. Joseph will also be given an opportunity to compete for a spot.

Riikola had a goal, six assists and a plus-3 rating last season. In two seasons with Pittsburgh, he has dressed in 73 games, tallying three goals, 12 points and a plus-6 rating. The Penguins had a 43-21-9 record in those games, though he wasn’t among the primary reasons for the team success.
