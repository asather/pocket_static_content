Pittsburgh Public Schools plan Light Up the Night for class of 2020

Pittsburgh Public Schools is planning to light up the city on Friday in honor of its 2020 graduates.

As part of an event dubbed Light Up the Night, the school system is asking the city to turn on their lights and make noise to celebrate the graduating class.

Locations participating in the event include Cupples Stadium in the South Side, PNC Park, the City-County Building, the Allegheny County Courthouse, Gulf Tower, Koppers Tower, and the PPS Administration Building.

“As seniors finalize their plans for life after high school, we want the class of 2020 to know that we care despite the unforeseen changes to their end of the year celebrations,” Superintendent Anthony Hamlet wrote in a release. “Friday is the perfect opportunity for the city to band together and turn on our lights to show the class of 2020 how proud we are of their recent accomplishments.”

The effects of the COVID-19 pandemic have caused high schools across the country to forgo typical end-of-year events like prom and graduation ceremonies. More than 1,500 students are preparing to graduate from PPS this semester.
