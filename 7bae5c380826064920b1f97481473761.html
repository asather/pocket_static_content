Pittsburgh finishes work on landslides in Spring Hill, South Oakland

The lengthy work of correcting landslides that occurred in 2018 finally is bearing fruit for Pittsburgh officials.

The city announced Monday that landslides have been cleared and remedial work completed on Swinburne Street between Greenfield and South Oakland and List Street in Spring Hill.

On Swinburne, crews completed a $630,000 project that involved stabilizing the slope and building a retaining wall. The street has been closed for construction since November and before that traffic had been restricted to one lane at a time.

In Spring Hill, crews finished a five-month, $880,000 project to stabilize the slope and build a retaining wall to support List Street after a landslide reduced traffic to one lane at a time in 2018.   

Both streets are open to traffic in both directions now but they will be restricted for resurfacing during the spring.
