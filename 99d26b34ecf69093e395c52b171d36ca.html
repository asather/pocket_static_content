Pittsburgh police officer tests positive for COVID-19

A Pittsburgh police officer has tested positive for COVID-19.

The officer, an instructor at the police academy who was in recent contact with recruits, was sent home April 1 after displaying flu-like symptoms, the Pittsburgh Bureau of Police announced Monday.

A test was performed the next day, and a positive result was returned Saturday.

Officials did not release the officer’s name, age, gender or years on the force.

Pittsburgh police are in the process of contact tracing.

“Police officials are in the process of identifying and notifying everyone who had contact with the officer and placing them in self-quarantine,” according to a news release.

It was not clear how the officer might have contracted the highly contagious infection. The bureau did not say how many people have been told to quarantine themselves.

Police said that the officer’s equipment has been thoroughly cleaned.

Officials are in daily contact with the officer.
