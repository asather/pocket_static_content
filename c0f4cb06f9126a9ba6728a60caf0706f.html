Thornburg man charged with armed robbery at Robinson market

Authorities have arrested a Thornburg man in connection with an armed robbery of a convenience store in Robinson last week.

Allegheny County sheriff’s deputies arrested Tyler Rutherford, 33, Tuesday afternoon in the April 28 robbery at Rossi’s Market on Forest Grove Road.

Store employees told police that a man, wearing a hoodie and mask, entered the market armed with a rifle and demanded cash.

Earlier this week, sheriff’s deputies received a tip that Rutherford was in Pittsburgh’s North Side neighborhood and arrested him without incident on Cedar Avenue.

He was arraigned Tuesday night on charges of robbery and reckless endangerment, and is being held at the Allegheny County Jail. A preliminary hearing is scheduled for July 14, according to court records.

Rutherford was also wanted on two bench warrants for violation of probation stemming from a theft and an escape, both in 2014. The warrants were issued on April 30 due to the alleged robbery, authorities said.
