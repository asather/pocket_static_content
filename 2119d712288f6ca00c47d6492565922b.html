County reports 85 new COVID-19 cases, state counts more than 1,100

Allegheny County reported 85 new COVID-19 cases Saturday and three new deaths, including one of a person in their 40s.

The other two deaths were of people in their 70s and 90s. The deaths occurred on Sept. 14 and 15 and two were associated with a long-term care facility. 

The state of Pennsylvania reported more than 1,162 new cases of COVID-19 Saturday, with 22 additional deaths. 

Western Pennsylvania accounted for 170 of the state’s new cases and nine of the new deaths. Allegheny County had the largest increases in new cases, with 85, followed by Beaver and Westmoreland, with 14 each. Beaver County had the highest amount of new deaths, at 4, followed by Allegheny County, with 3.

Since March, the state has reported 149,845 cases and 7,956 total deaths. The county has seen 11,663 cases and 373 deaths.

Allegheny County has had a total of 1,157 people hospitalized for COVID-19. As of Saturday, there were 35 people currently hospitalized, six of which require mechanical ventilators.
