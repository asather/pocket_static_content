Bishop suspends use of communion wine in precaution over virus

The Roman Catholic Diocese of Pittsburgh on Monday temporarily suspended the distribution of communion wine and instituted a no-contact policy for the ritual sign of peace during Masses in an effort to prevent the spread of the COVID-19 coronavirus.

Bishop David Zubik also ordered that fonts of holy water be cleaned and drained weekly, and he said that anyone feeling ill is not obligated to attend Sunday Mass.

“It’s important to point out that there are no confirmed cases of the coronavirus illness in Pennsylvania,” Bishop Zubik said in a statement. “However, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, as well as local healthcare agencies, are encouraging preparation and precaution as the situation continues to be monitored.”

He said these are “preventative steps to help our faith community, and the region as a whole, stay healthy.”

The diocese is the largest religious body in the region, covering Allegheny, Beaver, Butler, Greene, Lawrence and Washington counties. 

Its churches hold scores of Masses each week.

Typically communion wine is given from a shared cup.

During the sign of the peace at Mass, worshipers typically greet other parishioners nearby with handshakes or other gestures such as hugs.
