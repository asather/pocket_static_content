No new COVID-19 deaths in Western Pa., according to state

Western Pennsylvania had no deaths linked to COVID-19 in the past 24 hours and reported 22 new cases, according to data released Sunday by the state health department.

Allegheny County said it has eight new cases, which was the highest number reported in the region. There have been a total of 1,603 COVID-19 cases reported — 1,517 confirmed and 86 probable cases — since the first county case was reported March 14. A case is probable “when the person displays symptoms of COVID-19 and has had close contact with a confirmed positive case but has not had a positive test themselves,” according to the county.

Allegheny County’s death toll from the coronavirus stands at 143 people — 132 confirmed and 11 probable. 

Additionally in the county, as of Sunday, there were 292 past or present hospitalizations related to COVID-19. Of those, 120 have required admission to the intensive care unit and 67 needed mechanical ventilation.

Allegheny County has administered 23,747 tests, which is 151 more than the total reported Saturday. County health department Director Dr. Debra Bogen said Friday that the county has the capacity to administer roughly 2,000 tests per day.

The number of those infected with COVID-19 in Pennsylvania jumped by 623 cases on Saturday, bringing the statewide total to 62,234, according to the state Department of Health.

The health department also reported 15 new deaths, bringing the statewide total to 4,418.
