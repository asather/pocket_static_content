TSA confiscates handgun at Pittsburgh International security checkpoint

Boarding pass, check. Facemask, check. Handgun, er, no.

A Penn Hills woman was arrested Friday at Pittsburgh International Airport after an unloaded .22 revolver was found in her carry-on bag. A U.S. Transportation Security Administration officer spotted the handgun on an X-ray machine monitor, the TSA stated in a release.

Allegheny County police confiscated the revolver and arrested the woman after questioning her, according to the TSA. The traveler's name was not released. No other information was available.

It marked the seventh firearm caught at the airport checkpoint so far in 2020. Last year, 35 firearms were confiscated at Pittsburgh International.
