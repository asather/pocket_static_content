JCPenney to close several Pittsburgh-area stores as part of bankruptcy

Four Pittsburgh-area JCPenney stores are on the list of more than 150 stores that the bankrupt department store chain plans to close.

“Following a comprehensive review of our retail footprint, JCPenney made the difficult decision to close 154 stores,” according to a statement on the Texas-based retailer’s website. “We will continue to operate the majority of our stores and our flagship store, jcp.com,

The list of locations slated to close in Pennsylvania includes stores at Monroeville Mall, Beaver Valley Mall, Clearview Mall in Butler and the Galleria at Pittsburgh Mills mall in Frazer.

Penney’s filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy reorganization protection in May, as the already struggling retailer took an additional hit from closures forced by the pandemic. The company is more than a century old and operates more than 850 stores.

At the time of the bankruptcy filing, it employed nearly 90,000 workers, according to the Associated Press.

The company said it had reached an agreement on a financial restructuring plan. “Implementing the financial restructuring plan, with the support of our first lien lenders, is the best path to ensure JCPenney will emerge stronger,” according to its website.
