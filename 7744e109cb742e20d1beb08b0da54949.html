Here’s what you need to know about testing for COVID-19:

A doctor will order the coronavirus test based on a medical evaluation and history of recent travel.

The test can be done at a doctor’s office, hospital or clinic.

The physician will swab the back of the nose and throat for specimens.

By Monday, two big commercial diagnostic labs operating in Western Pennsylvania — LabCorp and Quest Diagnostics Inc. — will be doing coronavirus testing.

UPMC Health Plan does not cover testing by LabCorp. Check your health plan or contact your insurance carrier to confirm coverage details.

Highmark Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna Corp. and CVS Health’s Aetna plans are waiving co-pays and deductibles for coronavirus testing. Other plans may be considering similar offers.

Test results are sent to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention for confirmation.

If you test positive, you may be asked to stay at home, away from others, for 14 days. The quarantine is voluntary.

Most COVID-19 cases are mild; some cases are fatal, especially among the elderly and people with underlying medical conditions.
