With 14 new cases of COVID-19, Allegheny County eclipses 2,000 mark

Pennsylvania reported 566 new cases of COVID-19 and 12 new deaths, the state Department of Health announced Sunday.

None of the deaths occurred in Western Pennsylvania.

The state has gone 28 straight days of reporting less than 1,000 cases. It’s total is 75,592. With the new deaths, the state has a total of 5,943.

Allegheny County reported no new deaths from COVID-19 and 14 new cases of the infectious disease, according to data released Sunday by the county health department.

With the new figures, Allegheny County has reported a total of 2,003 cases — 1,875 are confirmed and 128 are probable. Of the 168 total deaths, 156 are confirmed and 12 are probable.

Allegheny has the highest figures in Western Pennsylvania and is followed by Beaver and Westmoreland counties. Based on the state’s data through Sunday, Beaver has had 603 cases and 74 deaths and Westmoreland 459 cases and 38 deaths.

Overall, 31 new cases were reported in Western Pennsylvania
