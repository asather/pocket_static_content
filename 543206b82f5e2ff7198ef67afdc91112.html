Country singer Chase Rice to play the Starlight Drive-In in August

Chase Rice, the North Carolina country singer who went from “Survivor: Nicaragua” to national touring airtist, will perform at the Starlight Drive-In in Butler on Thursday, Aug. 16.

With The Clarks set to play the first show at the drive-in on Saturday, this is the second concert announced for the venue at 1985 North Main Street Extension.

Gates will open at 6 p.m., with the music starting at 8 p.m. General admission tickets, which go on sale at noon Friday at DruskyEnt.com or Eventbrite.com, are $135 per car (up to four occupants per vehicle). The VIP Car ticket for $225 allows access in the first five rows in front of the stage. Parking is general admission, and on the day of show, there will be a charge of $30 per person over the 4-person limit. Only eTickets will be available.

Rice, who co-wrote the Florida Georgia Line smash “Cruise, is known for such solo hits as "Ready Set Roll," “Gonna Wanna Tonight” and “Eyes on You.” His current tour is a mix of drive-ins, civic centers and casinos. His previous Pittsburgh show was at Stage AE in October.

Audio will be transmitted via PA System and FM transmitter that you can listen to in your car.

Patrons will be subject to a mandatory trunk check upon entry.

For the safety of fans, tailgating will not be permitted,

Patrons are allowed to watch the show from outside of their vehicle, but are only permitted to in front of their car, between the car and the stage.
