People on the move: 9/21/2020

Banking

KeyBank named Kim Cantalamessa vice president and relationship manager for Key Private Bank’s Western Pennsylvania market.

Casino

Live! Casino named Brent Colston vice president of gaming operations.

Consulting

Aspirant promoted Michele Petruccelli to vice president. 

Education

Dawn R. Webb-Turner joined the faculty of educators at Pittsburgh Westinghouse Academy 6-12. Ms. Webb-Turner will teach sixth grade history and science.

Equipment rental and sales

Admar hired Mark Armstrong as service manager.

Health

Allegheny Health Network appointed of Dr. Margaret Larkins-Pettigrew as the health system’s first chief clinical diversity an inclusion officer, effective Dec. 1. Dr. Larkins-Pettigrew joins AHN from University Hospitals/Case Western Reserve University School of Medicine in Cleveland, where she serves as a professor of obstetrics and gynecology, the chair of clinical diversity and inclusion, and the assistant dean of students.

Investment Services

Greycourt & Co. Inc. promoted Matthew Litwin to president.

Law

Tucker Arensberg PC elected Irving R. Firman managing shareholder.

Nonprofit

Diane Bowman Neely joined the board of Functional Literacy Ministry of Haiti.

Real Estate

Howard Hanna Real Estate Services hired Matt Lachut as chief marketing officer.

Technology

Matthews International Corp. appointed Lillian Etzkorn to its board. Ms. Etzkorn is senior vice president and chief financial officer for Shiloh Industries Inc.

Business notes

TRAK Machine Tools’ Pittsburgh showroom is open at New Century Careers Anselmo Innovation Training Center, 305 E. Carson St., South Side. 

Clearview Federal Credit Union hosted its 34th annual golf outing featuring the Tee It Up for Charity contest, which benefits Pittsburgh-area nonprofits. The two-day golf outing was held at Beaver Valley Golf Club and netted a total of $10,000; of that, $5,000 each will support of Hair Peace Charities, the winner of the Tee It Up for Charity contest, and the Greater Pittsburgh Community Food Bank, one of Clearview’s charitable partners. Hair Peace Charities, founded by Bonny Diver, helps Western Pennsylvania women and girls with financial assistance to purchase a wig needed due to cancer treatments. 

Pittsburgh Steelers wide receiver JuJu Smith-Schuster announced the launch of a limited-edition cereal, Jumpin’ JuJu’s Crunch. The cereal is available at Pittsburgh-area Giant Eagle and Market District locations. All profit will benefit the JuJu Foundation, a nonprofit that supports youth initiatives.

Never Fear Being Different founder and CEO Jordon Rooney announced the launch of Built Different, a creative agency that will work with companies to amplify their impact on social media with the aim of transforming companies into digital brands.
