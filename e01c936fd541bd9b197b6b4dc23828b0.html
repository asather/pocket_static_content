Lorri Johnson, Pitt's all-time leading basketball scorer, dies

Pitt has lost one of its basketball legends. 

WTAE-TV reported Wednesday morning that Lorri Johnson, the school’s all-time leading scorer, has died. Pitt later confirmed the news to the Post-Gazette. 

Johnson scored 2,312 points for the women’s team during her career from 1987-92. That mark still stands as the highest career total for any Panther, man or woman. She also holds numerous other Pitt records, including single-season field goals and single-game points. 

The university retired Johnson’s number early last year, becoming the first female athlete to receive that honor. She was also a member of the Pitt Athletics Hall of Fame’s 2019 class. 

Before her college days, Johnson was also a standout at New Castle, earning two selections to the Post-Gazette’s Fabulous Five team in 1985-86 and leading the WPIAL in scoring at 29.9 points per game as a senior.

Her 40 points in a playoff game in 1985 still stands as a record in Class 4A. She also had a 53-point performance, which was the second-best in WPIAL history at the time.
