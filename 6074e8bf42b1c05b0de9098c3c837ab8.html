North Catholic moves into Class 1A hockey semifinals with 3-2 victory over South Park

North Catholic hasnâ€™t been in the Penguins Cup playoffs for more than a decade, but if they learned nothing else in their absence, the Trojans showed they certainly picked up a flair for the dramatic.

Quinten Shaffer scored the game-tying goal with 3:23 remaining in regulation and Dominic Dezort gave North Catholic (18-2-1) its only lead of the game 29 seconds into overtime as the Trojans moved into the Class 1A semifinals with a 3-2 victory over South Park (14-6-1) at the Lemieux Sports Complex in Cranberry.

North Catholic has not won a championship since 1995.

Ryan Malacki scored both goals for South Park as he opened the scoring at 9:29 of the first period and staked the Eagles to a 2-1 advantage at 6:37 of the second.

Other Class 1A

Thomas Jefferson 5, Blackhawk 0: The Jaguars (20-1-0) had the best record in the classification in the regular season and showed why in their easy first round win over the Cougars (7-13-1). Colby Bilski led the way for Thomas Jefferson with two goals and a pair of assists and Riley Rolzer had the game-winning goal at 3:47 of the first period and chipped in a helper as well. The Jaguars will face North Catholic in the semifinals on Tuesday at 9 p.m. at the Robert Morris University Island Sport Center.

Kiski Area 3, Norwin 2: Aiden Sites scored his fifth goal of the season 33 seconds into the second period to give the Cavaliers the lead and Johnathan Ayres netted an insurance power-play tally at 6:19 of the frame to lead Kiski Area (13-7-1) to the win over the Knights (15-5-1). The Cavaliers will play division rival Indiana in the semifinals on Tuesday at 7 p.m.

Class 3A

Peters Township 3, Cathedral Prep 2: The Indians (15-6-0) jumped out to an early lead with a three-goal outburst during a span of 2:45 in the first period, then held on throughout the game as the Ramblers (12-7-2) fought back in a desperate attempt to tie the game. Cathedral Prep scored both of its goals in a 1:56 apart in the first period after Peters Township opened up a 3-0 advantage. All five goals in the game were scored in the first period in a span of 5:34. Indians goaltender Alex Wilbert stopped 12 shots for the win.

North Allegheny 4, Bethel Park 3: Connor Chi and Tyler Putnam each scored two goals and the Tigers (16-4-1) held off a late Black Hawks (8-11-2) rally as top-seeded North Allegheny began its title defense with a home win against No. 8 Bethel Park. Chi and Putnam scored third period goals to give the Tigers a 4-1 lead, but Joey Wandrisco scored a power play goal to get the Black Hawks back within two and Ryan McLinden scored with one second left to close it out.

Pine-Richland 4, Canon-McMillan 1: Charles Mill scored a pair of goals to help the reigning Class 2A champions move into the Class 3A semifinals with the win over the Big Macs (12-7-2). The Rams (13-6-2) will now play North Allegheny in the semifinals on March 11 at 7 p.m.

Seneca Valley 5, Upper St. Clair 2: Ryaan Russell and Chris Belt each scored two goals and goaltender Robbie Halyama made it stand up with 22 saves as the seventh-seeded Raiders (12-7-2) upset the regular season runner-up Panthers (14-5-2). Seneca Valley will now play Peters Township in the semifinals on March 12 at 9 p.m.
