Sincere Carry lifts Duquesne to overtime win at St. Bonaventure

Sincere Carry made two free throws with six seconds remaining in overtime to seal the game as Duquesne beat St. Bonaventure, 81-77, Wednesday night in Olean, N.Y.

Carry had 18 points and 11 assists and Michael Hughes added 18 points for Duquesne (19-8, 9-6 Atlantic 10). Lamar Norman Jr. chipped in with 14 points. It’s the first time since 1971-72 that the Dukes have notched 19 or more wins in consecutive seasons.

Osun Osunniyi scored a career-high 23 points and also contributed 13 rebounds and three blocks for the Bonnies (18-10, 10-5).

Carry and Kyle Lofton of St. Bonaventure were the only players to score in the final minute of regulation, Carry making two free throws to tie it with 13 seconds remaining.

This was the 124th series meeting with St. Bonaventure — Duquesne’s most-frequent all-time opponent.  It’s also the first time Duquesne has produced back-to-back seasons with nine or more A-10 wins.

The Dukes host George Mason on Saturday at UPMC Events Center in Moon.
