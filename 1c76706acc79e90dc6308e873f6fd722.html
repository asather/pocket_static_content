Pennsylvania reports over 1,000 new COVID-19 cases; new cases in Allegheny County hit triple digits again

Allegheny County reported 106 cases of COVID-19 Saturday, pushing the county’s total cases during the pandemic over 12,000. This is the first time since Sept. 11 that the county reported more than 100 cases on a single day.

Across Pennsylvania, there were 1,029 additional cases reported Saturday, bringing the statewide total to 155,232. Of the latest cases 215 were in Western Pennsylvania. Allegheny had the most new cases with 106, followed by Westmoreland with 19.

The state reported 22 new deaths for a total of 8,103 since the pandemic began. Nine of those deaths were from Western Pennsylvania residents, though none were from Allegheny County. 

Of the newly-reported cases in Allegheny County, 101 are confirmed and five are probable. The date of the positive tests ranges from Sept. 17 through Sept. 25. The ages of those testing positive range from 5 to 94 years old, with a median age of 31.

Since March 14, Allegheny County has seen 12,101 cases of COVID-19 and 380 deaths.

Currently there are 444 people hospitalized across the state for the virus, 48 of which are in Allegheny County. 

“We know that congregation, especially in college and university settings, yields increased case counts. The mitigation efforts in place now are essential to flattening the curve and saving lives,” Pennsylvania secretary of health Rachel Levine said.

“Wearing a mask, practicing social distancing and following the requirements set forth in the orders for bars and restaurants, gatherings and telework will help keep our cases low,” Dr. Levine said.
