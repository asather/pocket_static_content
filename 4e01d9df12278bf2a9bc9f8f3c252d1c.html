Mattress Factory receives grant to digitize Greer Lankton archive

The Mattress Factory museum on the North Side has received a nearly $210,000 grant to digitize the archive of the late artist Greer Lankton.

Born a boy, Lankton had sexual reassignment surgery at the age of 21. The artist worked in New York City’s East Village in the 1980s, creating lifelike, sewn dolls posed in elaborate, theatrical settings. The dolls, modeled on friends or celebrities, could be glamorous and grotesque. Lankton was 38 when she died in 1996.

The Arlington, Va.-based Council on Library and Information Resources gave the Mattress Factory $209,527 to digitize more than 14,000 items, including artworks, dolls, objects, papers and the artist’s press clippings.

The contemporary art museum, which has a Lankton installation on the third floor, has hired a second archivist, Sinead Bligh, to help digitize the archive. Museum spokeswoman Mandy Wilson said the goal is to complete digitization by the end of 2021 and make it available online.
