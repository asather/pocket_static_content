Four calves killed when trailer full of cows crashes on turnpike in Irwin

A pick-up truck towing a trailer full of cows jack-knifed and struck a concrete median Tuesday on the Pennsylvania Turnpike in Irwin, killing four of the 135 calves aboard, officials said.

The crash happened around 5:30 a.m. at the westbound Irwin interchange, turnpike officials said.

The pick-up truck and trailer were using the exit ramp.

Authorities said some of the surviving calves may have sustained injuries in the crash.

No people were harmed.

Turnpike workers built a temporary corral for the surviving calves in a grassy area behind the interchange, according to turnpike spokeswoman Rosanne Placey. The works were assisted on scene by a team of three veterinarians, Ms. Placey said.

Ms. Placey said a replacement trailer was sent to pick up the livestock.
