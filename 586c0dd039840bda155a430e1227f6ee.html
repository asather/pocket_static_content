Mt. Lebanon boys basketball takes down Upper St. Clair despite short blackout in gym

Just over two minutes into the third quarter Tuesday, the lights went out in Mt. Lebanon’s gym.

“Somebody, a little kid probably, got their fingers on [an outlet] and was pushing on the buttons,” Mt. Lebanon coach Joe David said.

Soon after, the lights went out on Upper St. Clair’s 10-game winning streak.

Jake Hoffman scored 21 points to help No. 3 Mt. Lebanon defeat No. 5 Upper St. Clair, 63-56, in a showdown of arch rivals and two of the top teams in Class 6A.

With the win, its fifth in a row, Mt. Lebanon (11-3, 5-0) took over sole possession of first place in Section 2. Upper St. Clair (12-2, 4-1) lost for the first time since falling to Pine-Richland on Dec. 10.

It was a payback game for Mt. Lebanon, which was thumped by Upper St. Clair, 71-45, at home on senior night last season. It was the only section loss for a team that went on to win the WPIAL championship.

The seniors on that team have since graduated, but Hoffman and Blaine Gartley both started in that game. Hoffman said the group had not forgotten about it.

“Going in, we had the guys rally. We knew this would be a huge win for us. We knew we had to come out with a lot of energy,” Hoffman said.

Gartley scored 11 points Tuesday and fellow senior Joey King had 14.

Hoffman, a senior guard, has been terrific this season, averaging 22 points a game. He made two 3-pointers and scored 10 points in the first quarter to vault Mt. Lebanon to a 21-10 lead. The Blue Devils were 8 of 13 from the field.

But Upper St. Clair came out with a vengeance in the second quarter, going on a 10-0 run to pull within 21-20. Luke Gensler and Andrew Casey buried 3-pointers and Zack Kingseed scored a pair of baskets.

David then called a timeout to try to stop Upper St. Clair’s momentum.

“We really wanted to find Gensler and we let him free for some 3s,” David said. “It was a 10-0 run and we just weren’t executing. I think we did a better job on him in the second half. He’s a very nice player and you have to find him.”

Mt. Lebanon went on to outscore Upper St. Clair, 10-4, the rest of the half to take a 31-24 halftime lead.

There was 5:37 left in the third quarter when the lights went out and eventually started flickering. A delay of three or four minutes took place before David got them turned back on fully.

“I didn’t know I was the only one who knew how to control the lights in Mt. Lebanon,” David said, laughing.

Landon Rauch then scored to pull Upper St. Clair within five at 33-28, but that’s as close as the Panthers would get.

Gensler finished with five 3-pointers and 22 points. Rauch added 13 points, Kingseed 10 and Casey 9.

“We missed too many layups,” said Upper St. Clair coach Danny Holzer. “In the first half, we missed seven layups. The main part is they have some length and we had a hard time finishing inside, but if we make a couple of those early, we wouldn’t have gotten into that hole.”
