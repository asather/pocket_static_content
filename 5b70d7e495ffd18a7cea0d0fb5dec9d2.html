Late night fire in Homewood injures three firefighters

Three firefighters required medical treatment after battling a three-alarm fire late Friday night in Homewood.

At 11:25 p.m., Pittsburgh firefighters responded to a call of a structure fire on the 7900 block of Madiera St. They found heavy flames coming from the second floor of an unoccupied building.

One firefighter fell 10 feet off of a ladder and was taken to a hospital with a laceration to his head. He was conscious and alert while medics treated him at the scene. Another firefighter was also transported to a hospital after twisting his back falling through a hole in the floor. A third firefighter was treated at the scene for extreme heat exposure.

The fire was determined to be under control by 12:25 a.m. Saturday. The cause of the fire is under investigation.
