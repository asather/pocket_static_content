Two malls, JC Penney to temporarily close due to COVID-19

Ross Park Mall, South Hills Village and Grove City Outlets are the latest to announce they are shutting their doors temporarily during the COVID-19 pandemic. 

"The health and safety of our shoppers, retailers and employees is of paramount importance and we are taking this step to help reduce the spread of COVID-19 in our communities," said David Simon, chairman, CEO and president of Simon Property Group, the malls’ parent company, in the company’s announcement.

Indianapolis-based Simon will close all of its retail properties, including malls and outlet centers in the U.S. The measure will take effect at 7 p.m. Wednesday and is expected to end March 29.

J. C. Penney Co.  also said it will temporarily close its stores and business offices starting Wednesday, with plans to reopen on April 2.

“With the effects of the outbreak being felt more each day, our primary concern and area of focus is and has been on the health and safety of our associates, our customers, and our communities,” said Jill Soltau, chief executive officer of JCPenney, in the announcement. “We know this is a critical, unprecedented time and our thoughts are with those who have been impacted.”

The retailer will continue to monitor the situation and follow guidance from the Centers for Disease Control and local, state, and federal health officials and will reassess or adjust its policies accordingly as the situation unfolds. Customers can continue to shop online or through its app.
