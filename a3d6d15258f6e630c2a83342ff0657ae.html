Mt. Lebanon school board member uses profanity prior to meeting

Mt. Lebanon school board member Michael Riemer, who was participating in a school board discussion meeting via Zoom on Monday, was unmuted prior to the official start of the meeting and could be heard by the public spouting a number of profanities.

At the beginning of the meeting, during which some board members attended in-person and some via Zoom, Mr. Riemer could be heard on the Zoom broadcast saying several profanities.

A few minutes after his comments, Mr. Riemer apologized for what he called a “hot mic.”

“I apologize for any crudeness that may have slipped out,” Mr. Riemer said, and added he was going through some things with his family.

Board President Sarah Olbrich responded to Mr. Riemer’s remarks with this statement: “To the community, I apologize for the behavior of Mr. Riemer tonight. His language was unacceptable and unbecoming his position. It will not happen again.”

The board later made a motion to censure Michael Riemer at its next meeting voting meeting on Sept. 22. 

Board member Elaine Cappucci also condemned Mr. Riemer’s remarks.

“Whether that language was directed at the school community or, as he said, his family — either way it’s offensive,” she said.

Additionally, she pointed out that the board discussed a policy related to Title IX.

“This is particularly difficult for me as a female to hear words that were used from someone who may be voting on issues dealing with sexual harassment,” Ms. Cappucci said.

Later in the meeting, Mr. Riemer apologized again.

“I want to apologize for the extremely inappropriate language that I used this evening before the board meeting. There is no excuse for this egregious lapse of judgment. I deeply and sincerely apologize to our families, community members, District staff and my fellow board members for my behavior,” he said.

Parent Cindy Janay said of the incident during public comment, “I understand everyone has a bad day” and that she has been guilty of using profanity, “but I don’t do it in public.”

Mr. Riemer is a retired Mt. Lebanon police officer and worked as a school resource officer in the Mt. Lebanon district for several years prior to serving on the board.
