Death of woman paralyzed in 2005 Brighton Heights shooting ruled a homicide

A Glen Hazel woman's death in January has been ruled a homicide and linked to her being shot and partially paralyzed as a teen in 2005.

Tionna Highsmith died Jan. 27 in her apartment on Roselle Court. She was 29.

The Allegheny County Medical Examiner’s Office determined that she died as a result of complications from paraplegia associated with a suspected gunshot wound.

Ms. Highsmith lost the use of her legs at the age of 14 after being wounded during a drive-by shooting at a Brighton Heights church festival in August 2005.

Police believed she was an innocent who got caught in a crossfire between rival gangs when she wandered over to see a commotion involving two women fighting in the street.

Someone dangling out a car window fired a 9mm handgun numerous times in Ms. Highsmith’s direction.

Her shooting remains unsolved.

Pittsburgh police said Monday they are continuing to investigate.
