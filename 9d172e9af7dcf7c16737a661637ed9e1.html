Pittsburgh-area groups deliver list of demands on police reform to local officials

Several Pittsburgh-area groups delivered a dozen demands for police reform to city and Allegheny County officials on Monday.

Among their demands: that Pittsburgh police union president Robert Swartzwelder step down.

Activists from the groups — which include 1Hood, Take Action Mon Valley, the Alliance for Police Accountability and Okra Ethics — also said they want government funds reallocated from public safety and invested instead into communities.

"The safest communities are not the ones with the most police but the ones with the most resources," said Jasiri X, of the group 1Hood.

"Reform and training [of police] aren't going to make our communities safer," said Miracle Jones, also of 1Hood.

The full list of demands include:

1. Defund the police and fund black communities

2. Demilitarize police

3. End the criminalization of black people

4. Remove police from schools

5. Make all collective bargaining with police public

6. Terminate FOP president Robert Swartzwelder

7. Disband all private police departments

8. End no-knock warrants

9. Cease partnership with ICE

10. End cash bail

11. Release all vulnerable individuals from jail

12. Create an independent, fully funded police review board

Brandi Fisher, head of the Alliance for Police Accountability, said budgets are a municipality's "moral document."

Allocating the majority of funds for public safety "shows us that your priority is to lock people up," she said.
