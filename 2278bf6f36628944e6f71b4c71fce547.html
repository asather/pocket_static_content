PIAA sets sites for second-round basketball playoff games

Butler and Upper St. Clair played a memorable WPIAL boys basketball semifinal game almost two weeks ago that went to overtime before Butler pulled out a win. The rematch will take place in a few days.

Butler will play Upper St. Clair in a PIAA Class 6A second-round game Wednesday, 7 p.m. at North Allegheny High School. On Sunday, the PIAA announced sites and starting times for all second-round games and Butler vs. Upper St. Clair is one of the most anticipated contests. It will be the only game played at North Allegheny Wednesday.

When Butler beat Upper St. Clair, 83-78, in the WPIAL semifinals, it was the second game of a Class 6A doubleheader at Robert Morris University’s UPMC Events Center. The two games drew 3,500 fans. For whatever reason, the PIAA is holding the Butler-Upper St. Clair rematch at a high school.

North Allegheny has one of the bigger gyms in the WPIAL, holding a little more than 2,200 fans. The Butler-Upper St. Clair game figures to be a hot ticket. Tickets (adults and students) will be sold Monday, starting at 10 a.m. at Upper St. Clair, and 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. at Butler. Prices are $8 for adults and $4 for students.

North Allegheny athletic director Bob Bozzuto said tickets will not be available at North Allegheny until the night of the game and ticket windows will not open until 6 p.m. Student tickets will not be sold the night of the game. No one will be allowed to enter the gym until 6 p.m. Butler fans should enter the building at the gym entrance while Upper St. Clair fans should enter at the front of the school.
