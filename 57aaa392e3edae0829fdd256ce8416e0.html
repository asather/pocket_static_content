Allegheny Health Network partnering with Morgantown health system

A Morgantown, W.Va. health system is joining a clinically integrated network run by Allegheny Health Network, extending the reach of the Pittsburgh-based provider into West Virginia.

Mon Health System is joining AHN’s Physician Partners of Western PA LLC, an organization founded in 2017 that coordinates care for nearly a quarter million patients through claims-based analytics and predictive modeling. In addition, Mon Health will join AHN’s bulk purchasing group, giving the health system access to competitive prices for an array of medical supplies.

Mon Health, which was formed in 1982, operates three hospitals, an ambulance service, retirement community in West Virginia.

AHN’s bulk purchasing group represents more than 90 hospital members in Pennsylvania, West Virginia and Delaware.

As part of the agreement, AHN will open a heart failure program at Mon Health headed by cardiologist Christopher Link, who came to Allegheny Health Network from Maine Medical Partners in 2019. Heart failure is a chronic, costly disease with a high rate of mortality.

“We are honored to call AHN a partner in providing higher level services to our patients,” Mon Health President and CEO David Goldberg said in a prepared statement. Mr. Goldberg was previously a executive vice president at AHN.

Cynthia Hundorfean, president and CEO of AHN, said she was pleased by the partnership.

“We look forward to working with the leadership of Mon Health System to help support their mission of service to the people of north central West Virginia,” she said in a prepared statement.
