PPG to buy Chicago coatings maker

PPG is adding a Chicago powder coatings maker to its portfolio.

The Downtown-based paints giant Wednesday said it has a deal to acquire Alpha Coating Technologies, which manufactures powder coatings for machinery, household items and light industrial applications.

Financial terms were not disclosed; the deal is expected to close by March.

Alpha was founded in 2000 and employs 35 people.

The deal is part of PPGâ€™s ongoing strategy to grow through acquisitions.

Earlier this week, it said it closed on the purchase of Italian coatings producer Industria Chimica Reggiana which makes automotive refinish and light industrial paints.

ICC has about 180 employees.

Last year, PPG spent $700 million on acquisitions.

On an earnings call with Wall Street analysts last month, company Chairman Michael McGarry said he hoped to complete at least four or five deals in 2020.

The company said recent acquisitions accounted for $500 million of total sales of $15.1 billion in 2019.
