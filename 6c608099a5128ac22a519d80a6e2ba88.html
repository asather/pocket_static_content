In a hopeful move, Pittsburgh Playhouse announces new reading series for 2020-21 season

With the hope that we will be gathering in theaters and classrooms come fall, Point Park University’s Pittsburgh Playhouse has announced a new Play Reading Series for the 2020-21 season.

The Playhouse is taking submissions of original plays and musicals, not previously produced, and will select six. Each work will be assigned a professional director and a cast of students from Point Park’s Conservatory of Performing Arts. Rehearsals of up to two weeks will culminate in a public reading/discussion in the Playhouse’s Rauh Theatre.

Steven Breese, artistic director and dean of COPA, noted that the COVID-19 pandemic began as the series was being planned. It is meant to extend the Playhouse’s commitment to presenting new works — the April 3-12 premiere of a musical adaptation of “Pump Up the Volume” was canceled due to the coronavirus outbreak. 

The reading series adds to programming at the Downtown campus venue, which dropped the Playhouse Rep and Playhouse Jr. series that were staples of the former Playhouse in Oakland.

“We wanted to recognize the ongoing impact of the pandemic on our lives and will be actively seeking one new play to interpret this time, in a meaningful way,” said Breese.

Other suggested criteria include, multiethnic casts, strong female protagonists and contemporary social and political issues. The process is electronic submissions only, accepted June 1-July 1, 2020 at ArtisticDirector@pointpark.edu.

Selected plays are expected to be announced by Sept. 1, 2020. For details about formats and more, visit pittsburghplayhouse.com.
