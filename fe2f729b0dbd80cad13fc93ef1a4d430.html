People on the move: 3/22/2020

People on the Move

Architecture

PWWG Architects promoted Anthony Pitassi to managing principal.

Education

The Watson Institute appointed Alexandra Salcido to program director of Friendship Academy, on South Negley in Friendship, and director of psychological services at the institute’s Sewickley and South Fayette locations.

Paul Spradley joined the Consortium for Public Education to become co-manager of The Future Readiness Lab, a six-week experiential learning program for rising high school seniors launching in June in collaboration with The Heinz Endowments. 

Law

Blank Rome LLP said Frank Spadafino joined as chief information officer.

Robert J. Waine joined Rothman Gordon. Mr. Waine represents businesses in a broad spectrum of matters including commercial transactions, corporate formation, real estate, employment issues and litigation. 

Burns White LLC said D. Matthew Jameson III, Kurt F. Fernsler and Richard W. Saxe Jr. joined as members.

Manufacturing

HarbisonWalker International said Michael D. Van Hill joined as vice president, supply chain. 

Center Rock Inc. hired Dan Reed as research and development manager.

Nonprofit

Advanced Robotics for Manufacturing Institute appointed Ira Moskowitz CEO.

Pharmaceutical

Mylan N.V. and Pfizer Inc. announced the remaining appointees to the inaugural 13-member board of directors for Viatris, the new company that will result from the combination of Mylan and Upjohn, a division of Pfizer. Pfizer has appointed current Pfizer board member W. Don Cornwell, who will resign from the board to serve as a director of Viatris upon the close of the transaction. Additionally, Mylan has appointed eight of its own directors: JoEllen Lyons Dillon, Neil Dimick, Melina Higgins, Harry A. Korman, Rajiv Malik, Richard A. Mark, Mark W. Parrish and Pauline van der Meer Mohr. 

Business notes

Youngjae Chun, Ph.D., an associate professor of industrial engineering and bioengineering at the University of Pittsburgh, is developing a new type of metallic frame for pediatric heart valves that could not only be placed by a minimally invasive catheter-based procedure but would also grow with the child, eliminating the need for follow-up surgeries. The project recently received an award of $120,000 from the Children’s Heart Foundation’s Liam Ward Fund.

Awards and Honors

VisitPittsburgh received the Ragan’s PR Daily Awards top prize in the Hospitality, Travel or Tourism Campaign category for its “Pull Up a Chair. You are Welcomed Here.” national marketing campaign.

The Master Builders’ Association of Western Pennsylvania, Inc. announced the winners for the 2019 MBA Building Excellence Awards competition. They are:

• New construction over $25 million — Project: Carnegie Mellon University David A. Tepper Quadrangle. Contractor: PJ Dick Inc.

• New construction between $10 million and $25 million — Project: Bobby Rahal South Hills Jaguar Land Rover Dealership. Contractor: Dick Building Co. 

• New construction under $10 million — Project: Carnegie Library of Pittsburgh, Carrick branch. Contractor: Massaro Corp. 

• Renovation construction over $10 million — Project: West Virginia University Medicine Rockefeller Neuroscience Institute Center for Innovation. Contractor: Landau Building Co. 

• Renovation construction between $5 million and $10 million — Project: UPMC Hillman Cancer Center, third-floor renovation. Contractor: Mascaro Construction Co. LP. 

• Renovation construction under $5 Million — Project: Cathedral of Learning 22nd- and 23rd-floor renovations. Contractor: AIMS Construction. 

• Excellence in craftsmanship, exterior construction — Project: Allegheny County Courthouse, phase one. Contractor: Cuddy Roofing Co. Inc. 

• Excellence in craftsmanship, interior construction — Project: Penn State School of Music and Recital Hall. Contractor: Giffin Interior & Fixture Inc.
