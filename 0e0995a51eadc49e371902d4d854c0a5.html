COVID-19 Update: 354 new cases reported in Western Pa.

Pennsylvania health officials on Friday reported 1,009 new cases of COVID-19, bringing the state total to 93,876. The last time the state saw an increase of over 1,000 new cases in a single day was May 10, when the majority of the state was still in the red phase. 

Of the 1,009 new cases, 354 of them are in Western Pennsylvania, with 180 coming from Allegheny County. Washington County had the second highest increase in the region with 44 new cases.

Allegheny County Health Department on Friday reported 180 new cases of COVID-19 and seven new hospitalizations as the surge in infections continues in the Pittsburgh region. It marks the 11th consecutive day that the county has seen a triple-digit increase in new infections.

With these additional cases, the county total is now 4,547 cases. Health officials said these new infections come from 1,743 tests that were administered between June 24 and July 9, health officials said. The ages of the new positive cases range from 2 to 91 years old, with the median age of 38.

The new hospitalizations as a result of COVID-19 bring the total of past and present hospitalizations in the county to 450. In the last week, the county saw an increase of 44 new hospitalizations, health officials said.

There was one additional death reported in the county, bringing the total to 197.

UPMC physicians on Thursday, however, said that the recent spike in cases has not resulted in a notable increase in levels of hospitalizations or deaths related to the virus. UPMC noted that only 2% of recent infections are severe enough to result in hospitalizations and 0.2% of the hospitalizations result in death.

The state health department is also reporting 32 new deaths, bringing the state death toll to 6,880 deaths since the beginning of the pandemic. Only one of the deaths is from Western Pennsylvania, which was in Allegheny County.
