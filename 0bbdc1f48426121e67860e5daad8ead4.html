PennDOT begins work on long-term projects Tuesday in Squirrel Hill, Plum

Road projects that begin Tuesday will disrupt traffic for several months in Squirrel Hill and Plum.

In Squirrel Hill, A. Folino Construction Inc. will work on parts of Beeler Street, Wilkins Avenue and South Dallas Avenue from 7 a.m. to 7 p.m. daily. PennDOT said the work will result in single-lane alternating traffic trough late October.

The work includes milling and paving, concrete patching, base repairs, drainage improvements, manhole and utility box work, and pavement marking installation. The project area includes Beeler between Forbes and Wilkins avenues; Wilkins between Beeler and South Dallas; and South Dallas between Wilkins and Penn avenues.

The project is one of several jobs the contractor is doing under a group contract worth $4.15 million contract.

Reconstruction work in Plum will be done overnight to improve Route 286.

Crews from A. Liberoni Inc. will do milling and paving, concrete patching, base repairs, drainage improvements, guide rail updates and pavement marking installation between Old Frankstown Road and Hileah Drive. Flaggers will control single-lane alternating traffic until early December.

Work will be done six days a week under the following schedule: 8 p.m. to 5 a.m. Monday through Thursday nights; 8 p.m. to 10 a.m. Fridays; and 6 p.m. to 10 a.m. Saturdays.

The work is part of a group contract worth $4.83 million.
