‘Disillusioned’ Dems should abandon party and back Trump, Parnell urges in RNC address

Last October, before he had come to his final decision on whether to run for Congress in Western Pennsylvania, Sean Parnell garnered a public endorsement from the Republican Party's ultimate standard-bearer: President Donald Trump.

On Monday night, Mr. Parnell got to officially return the favor from the main stage of the Republican National Convention, urging "disillusioned" Democrats — during a livestreamed address — to abandon their party and back the president's bid for four more years in the White House.

Mr. Parnell, who is running to unseat incumbent U.S. Rep. Conor Lamb, D-Mt. Lebanon, framed the modern-day Democratic Party as one that's "bloated with contempt for Middle America" and desires "conformity" instead of freedom for its voters.

Republicans, Mr. Parnell insisted, will gladly take those voters under their tent.

"It doesn’t matter what you look like, who you love, how you worship, your gender or your job,” Mr. Parnell said. “If you are a traditional Democrat who has become disillusioned with how radical your party has become, then stand with us. You are most welcome.”

Warning that Democrats have become too radical has been a staple of Mr. Parnell's campaign, though he's often sought to link his opponent, Mr. Lamb, to the idea.

But he did not mention Mr. Lamb by name on Monday, and instead used the platform to criticize the Democratic Party at large — including its presidential nominee, former Vice President Joe Biden — for "[turning] against the very people it professed to represent."

Mr. Parnell also spoke about his service as an Army Ranger during the war in Afghanistan, recounting a specific incident in which he said his platoon was attacked by "hundreds of Taliban."

"I was wounded three times in the fighting," he said in his speech. "Nearly all of my platoon was wounded within the first minute. But the enemy kept coming."

It was when he returned to the U.S., he said, that he saw the Democratic Party change.

Though he only spoke for about five minutes, Mr. Parnell cited just one specific example of the Democrats' perceived radical shift: when Mr. Biden "spit venom at an auto worker who dared to question [his] intent to dismantle the Second Amendment and take your guns."

Mr. Parnell was referencing a March exchange between Mr. Biden and a Michigan man — captured on video — in which the Democrat told the worker he was "full of [expletive]" and said he would only take away his AR-15s. Mr. Biden's platform on guns, according to a policy paper, includes “banning] the manufacture and sale of assault weapons and high-capacity magazines” and buying back those that are already in the community.

President Donald Trump, meanwhile, has "advanced freedom — despite savage political attacks — to overcome the agenda of the radical left," Mr. Parnell said in his remarks.

With his speech, Mr. Parnell became the first — and last — Republican from Pennsylvania to speak during the RNC’s primetime slate this week.
