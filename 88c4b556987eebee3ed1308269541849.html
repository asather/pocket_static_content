Changes to I-579, Elizabeth Bridge work to start next week

PennDOT announced Friday that changes will occur on several major projects next week.

Northbound I-579, Uptown, will close for beam setting between the Centre Avenue Exit and the Bigelow Boulevard overpass from 7 p.m. to 6 a.m. nightly from Tuesday until May 30.

A single-lane restriction will simultaneously occur on southbound I-579 on each night of the closures.

The work is part of the $29.34 million “Cap” project sponsored by the city of Pittsburgh and the Sports & Exhibition Authority, to create a structure spanning I-579 to link Downtown with the Hill District.

Also on Tuesday, a traffic switch will occur on the Elizabeth Bridge.

A long-term single-lane restriction will be implemented northbound for crews to conduct cable replacements on the bridge.

The work is part of the $31.1 million Elizabeth Bridge Rehabilitation Project, which also includes steel repairs, concrete deck and barrier replacement, expansion dam replacement, bearing repairs, and more.

Wednesday through next Friday, crews will do milling and paving operations on West Carson Street. 

Single-lane restrictions on Route 837 will occur between the Smithfield Street Bridge and the West End Circle from 6 a.m. to 6 p.m. each day. Only minor work with short-term restrictions will occur during the overnight hours.

The work is part of a $6.06 million improvement project.
