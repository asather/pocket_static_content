Buying Here: Turned around Crafton house is priced at $159,900

Jeffrey and Sarah Taylor came to Pittsburgh for work. When they decided to purchase a home, Crafton was an easy choice.

“We loved being close to the airport and friends,” she said. “The day we moved in, everyone came to say ‘Hi.’ There was a real sense of community.”

They chose 15 Harrison St., a three-bedroom, 1½-bath frame house built in 1878. Now, after 2½ years, they are moving again and have put the house on the market for $159,900 (MLS No. 1431950) with Lu Ryan of Howard Hanna Real Estate Services (412-708-2300 or www.howardhanna.com). The house is under agreement.

The previous owner, a carpenter, lived in the home for more than 50 years, Ms. Taylor said. It was originally one of several houses built on a back lot of a house on Johnston Street. Homeowners successfully petitioned the borough to make Harrison a street. After the change, the owner made the back of the house the front.

The exterior is covered in vinyl siding and the front porch, the former back porch, has lots of gingerbread trim.

Inside, the entry features crown molding and trime that was reclaimed from an old area steel mill. The wood floors are parquet.

“There was not much to do there,” Ms. Taylor said. “The neighbors helped us spackle and repaint several rooms.”

When the couple realized the fireplace in the 13-by-15-foot living room had been covered up, they decided to restore it. They found an old mantel on Craigslist and restored it.

“It was covered by four coats of paint,” she said. “We removed the paint and scraped the dirt off of the mirror and saw it was dated 1838.”

The 12-by-15-foot dining room is located in the former entry, The original carved entry door gives access to the back porch, which is screened..

The 12-by-6-foot kitchen was updated by the previous owners with wood cabinets, solid surface counter tops and white appliances, including a gas range.

The largest of the three bedrooms is the 13-by-16-foot master. Painted a deep navy blue with crisp white trim, it has natural pine floors and a ceiling fan. An 11-by-15-foot bedroom and full bathroom are also on the second floor.

The third bedroom on the third floor measures 12 by 15 feet and has a powder room that could be turned into a full bathroom. The floor is natural pine and dormers add visual interest to the beamed ceiling.

There are two off-street parking spaces and a level, fenced-in backyard. The walk-out basement contains the laundry and a bathroom with a shower.

“We installed new windows on the first floor,” Ms. Taylor said. “We use window air-conditioners.”

The property’s assessed value is $113,100. Over the last four years, five houses have sold on Harrison Street, including this one, for prices ranging from $100,000 in October 2018 to $165,000 in July 2016 (www2.county.allegheny.pa.us/RealEstate/Sales.aspx). The home is in the Carlynton School District.

“It was hard to leave our neighbors,” Ms. Taylor said. “The community was such a nice surprise for us. We hope the new owners will love it as much we did.”
