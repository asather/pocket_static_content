Pittsburgh will receive $1.5 million state grant for police overtime during Rosfeld trial, Tree of Life shooting

The city is poised to receive $1.5 million in state funds to cover public safety overtime costs incurred during the high-profile trial for an officer who killed an unarmed black teenager and during the worst anti-Semitic attack in U.S. history.

The overtime costs related to the four-day trial for the ex-East Pittsburgh police officer who killed 17-year-old Antwon Rose II and for a mass shooting at the Tree of Life synagogue in Squirrel Hill totaled nearly $1.6 million, according to the Department of Public Safety.

The shooting death of Antwon by then-Officer Michael Rosfeld in June 2018 sparked protests and vigils; law enforcement closed roads surrounding the Allegheny County Courthouse during the March 2019 trial. Protesters and mourners demonstrated outside of the courthouse the night of Mr. Rosfeld’s acquittal and in the days following.

In October 2018, a gunman entered the Tree of Life synagogue, killing 11 worshipers from three congregations and injuring several, including responding officers. Days of vigils followed the shooting and a large public memorial site formed outside of the building.

The grant from the Pennsylvania Commission on Crime and Delinquency to cover the police overtime costs received an affirmative recommendation from council’s standing committee Wednesday. The body is expected to approve the grant in a final vote on Tuesday.
