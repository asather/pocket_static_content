State reports 776 new COVID-19 cases, as Westmoreland County exceeds the 2,000 mark

The Pennsylvania Health Department reported an increase of 776 COVID-19 cases and 28 new deaths on Wednesday.

Of the newest cases,164 were in Western Pennsylvania. Allegheny County had the highest daily increase with 73, followed by Indiana County with 15. Westmoreland County surpassed 2,000 total cases with 10 reported on Wednesday. At 2,003 cases. Westmoreland County has Western Pennsylvania’s second-highest number of cases, behind Allegheny County’s 11,431.

Statewide, 146,990 people have tested positive for the virus since the pandemic began, and 7,903 people have died as a result of the respiratory illness.

Five of the state’s 28 deaths occurred in Western Pennsylvania. Armstrong County reported two new deaths, while Butler, Indiana and Lawrence counties reported one each. 

In Allegheny County, the 73 new cases come from 1,210 tests that range from Aug. 27 to Sept. 15. All but three of the tests were taken between Sept. 7 and Sept. 15, according to the health department.

Those who tested positive range in age from seven to 71 years old, with a median age of 28. 

Allegheny County reported no new deaths on Wednesday, keeping its death toll at 363.

Across the state, there are currently 483 people hospitalized due to COVID-19, with 61 of them requiring mechanical ventilation. Allegheny County has 51 of the state’s hospitalized cases with eight patients requiring the use of a mechanical ventilator, based on figures from the state.

Since the pandemic began, a total of 1,096 people in Allegheny have required hospitalization due to COVID-19-related complications.
