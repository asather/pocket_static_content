Peters Township safety Donovan McMillon commits to Florida

One of the most heavily recruited players in the WPIAL revealed his college decision Saturday evening, as Peters Township safety Donovan McMillon announced his verbal commitment to Florida.

McMillon, a 6-foot-2, 193-pounder who helped guide the Indians to an appearance in the WPIAL Class 5A championship last season, received scholarship offers from more than a dozen Power Five programs after the end of his junior year. Considered a three-star prospect by Rivals and a four-star prospect by 247 Sports, he narrowed his final list to five schools before making his decision, choosing Florida over Virginia Tech, Texas A&M, Oklahoma and Oregon.

“Having all these offers come within such a short period of time these past six months has just blown my mind,” McMillon said in a video posted on his Twitter account announcing his decision. “I’m pretty speechless about it.”

McMillon didn’t spend much time on offense last season, though he did catch 12 passes for 102 yards. The defensive side of the ball is where he made a name for himself. Going into last year’s WPIAL championship, McMillon had been credited with a team-high 78 tackles — including seven tackles for loss — along with two sacks, two interceptions, four forced fumbles and two fumble recoveries.

At the end of his junior season, McMillon had offers from Pitt and a few mid-major Football Bowl Subdivision programs. But soon after the first of the national powerhouses extended an offer, the rest just kept flowing in.

“I think what went into making my final decision was finding a home from home,” McMillon said in the video. His father, Peters Township co-defensive coordinator Darrin McMillon, is from Florida.

Along with being a blue-chip recruit on the football field, McMillon is also one of the best wrestlers in the WPIAL. He finished second in the 182-pound weight class at the WPIAL and PIAA Class 3A individual championships earlier this year, losing only to Canon-McMillan’s Gerrit Nijenhuis both times.

McMillon will now attempt to lead the Indians to a return trip to the WPIAL title game in Class 5A before heading down south to Gainesville in 2021.
