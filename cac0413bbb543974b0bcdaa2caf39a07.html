Police probe Snapchat video showing Westmoreland County child vaping while being babysat

Charges are pending against two teenage girls accused of letting a 2-year-old boy whom one of them was babysitting use a vaping device containing nicotine.

Video of the incident was posted to social media. Pennsylvania State Police in Indiana County said they learned of it Sunday through the state attorney general’s Safe2SaySomething tipline.

Police got the video and launched an investigation.

The incident occurred Jan. 9 at a home in St. Clair, Westmoreland County, between 5 p.m. and 8 p.m., police said.

A 17-year-old girl from Seward was babysitting on Jan-L Street when the 2-year-old “took the vaping device from a nightstand and began to use it,” police said.

The babysitter and an 18-year-old woman from Armagh, Indiana County, who was also in the house, let the child inhale, police said. He coughed but “did not display any other visible effects from inhaling,” police said.

One or both of the teenagers took video and posted it to Snapchat.

The child is from New Florence. It was unclear whose house they were in when the vaping incident occurred.

Police said the teenagers and the child are not related.

The vaping device is believed to have contained 3% nicotine but no THC, the compound that gives pot its high, police said.

Police contacted the child’s parent, who were not aware of what happened.

The incident was reported by Children and Youth Services.

Police said charges of endangering the welfare of children are pending against both teenagers.
