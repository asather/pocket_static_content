Fishing Report: Heavy rains cloud waters as trout season opens

Authorities advise anglers to stay at least the length of one fishing rod apart as a COVID-19 precaution.

Lake Erie and tributaries (Erie County) Creeks emptied recent rain overflows into the bay, leaving fishing sporadic. Yellow perch took live minnows under floats. Catches of a few small Northern pike and smallmouth bass were reported.

Pymatuning Reservoir (Crawford County) Prior to recent rains, anglers reported a slow crappie bite.

Sandy Creek (Venango County) Fish and Boat reported that 95% of Venango County stocked trout waters had received pre-season stockings, and 85% had their in-season stockings as well.

Lake Arthur (Butler County) Waters were still too cool for the crappie spawn. Males were caught using bright jigs above rocks in 10-15 feet of water.

The Glades (Butler County) Bass and panfish were taken from shore in the waters of State Game Land 95.

North Park Lake (Allegheny County) Anglers showed up but a thunderstorm put fish down. As mud clears, heavily stocked trout were expected to resume feeding.

Keystone Lake (Westmoreland County) Lots of trout took live bait before Tuesday’s rain.

Twin Lakes (Westmoreland County) Throughout the weekend, trout were caught using worms or meal worms. 

Loyalhanna Creek (Westmoreland County) Despite recent stockings, fishing was hampered by high, cloudy water.

Killbuck Run (Cambria County) Running clear Wednesday despite Tuesday’s rains.

Monongahela River (Washington, Greene counties) Waters were high and muddy throughout the region due to heavy storms early in the week.
