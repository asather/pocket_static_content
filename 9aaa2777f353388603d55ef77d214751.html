Aviary opens with timed entry

The National Aviary re-opened on July 1, so bird lovers can go to shop.aviary.org to purchase timed-entry tickets online. Staff and visitors are required to wear masks and practice social distancing, staying at least 6 feet away from people. All CDC guidelines for Covid-19 precautions are being followed. 

Available ticket slots are being posted two weeks at a time. Entry times to the North Side facility are staggered so that staff can control the movement and safety of people inside. 

Go to the aviary.org website to see the schedule of daily events.

Tickets are $17 adults, $16 children and seniors; free for children under 2.

Hot yoga with goats

“NamastHay is rolling out something a li’l different ... summer on the farm,” says Jen Stratakis. “We usually skip July and August for home field events because it can be very warm for us and the goaties,” but they will try a goat yoga session on Saturday, July 11, 10:30 a.m. to noon.

The $20 tickets are available at: https://www.eventbrite.com/e/pop-up-namasthay-goat-yoga-of-pittsburghtm-to-benefit-animal-friends-tickets-110529063404?aff=ebdssbonlinesearch

“It might be hot yoga in a field. It might be absolutely delightful,” Ms. Stratakis said. “One thing is for sure. It's to benefit a great cause, one I volunteer for: Animal Friends!”

The goats will be doing yoga in the field where they live: Potential Farm, 101 Boyds Run Road, Presto 15142.

Masks are required until people get to the yoga mats, which will be placed at safe social distances in the field.
