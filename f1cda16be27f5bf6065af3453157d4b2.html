Oakmont nursing center evacuated

A nursing center in Oakmont was evacuated late Thursday afternoon after a drain cleaner caused fumes, according to Allegheny County Emergency Services.

The Oakmont Center for Nursing & Rehabilitation on Ann Street evacuated 76 residents and staff after maintenance employeees “poured a drain cleaner into a clogged slop sink which had a reaction and started fuming,” according to ACES Chief Matt Brown in a statement. “Until it could be determine what it is, the facility chose to evacuate its residents out.”

In the statement, Chief Brown said, “there are no unusual meter readings, they are cleaning it up now and ventilating.”

To expedite the evacuation and care for residents during the process, Chief Brown said Emergency Services implemented its mass casualty incident protocol

Port Authority assisted by providing two buses for temporary sheltering until ventilation of the building is complete.
