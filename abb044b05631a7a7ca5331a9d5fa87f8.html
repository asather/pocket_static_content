Analysts, betting odds favor Louis Riddick, Pat McAfee for ESPN's new 'Monday Night Football' booth

If football analysts and sports gambling sites are to be believed, there’s a legitimate chance ESPN’s “Monday Night Football” booth next season might be manned by one, maybe two ex-NFL players with strong Pittsburgh ties.

On May 9, The Athletic’s Richard Deitsch reported that last season’s crew of Joe Tessitore and Booger McFarland will not be coming back for the 2020-21 NFL season (whenever that will be) and whoever replaces them will come from within ESPN.

With the candidates for the job being narrowed down to folks who already work for ESPN, two names have consistently been proposed to take over commentating duties: Pitt football alum Louis Riddick and Plum native Pat McAfee.

Riddick has been an ESPN analyst since May 2013 who has since become a huge part of ESPN’s yearly NFL draft coverage. He’s currently the co-favorite for one of the “Monday Night Football” positions with ex-NFL journeyman quarterback Dan Orlovsky, according to odds put out by betting site Bovada.

Not far behind Riddick in the odds is McAfee, who made his ESPN booth debut during a UCLA-Cincinnati college football game in August of last year, though he has plenty of other broadcast experience elsewhere. He and veteran “SportsCenter” host Steve Levy are tied for the next-highest Bovada odds right behind Riddick and Orlovsky.

Former NFL quarterbacks Matt Hasselbeck, Kurt Warner and Peyton Manning all have odds high enough to indicate that they could be highly sought-after prospects for this job, as well. Two fun long shots for the gigs according to Bovada are current Colts quarterback Philip Rivers and legendary coach and broadcaster John Madden.

Fellow sports gambling operation BetOnline also released its odds Tuesday for the “Monday Night Football” jobs.

Leading this pack is Brian Griese, a former NFL quarterback and current color commentator for “ESPN College Football.” The rest of their odds are about the same as Bovada, though they give McAfee less of a shot at the coveted role. It’s also worth mentioning that an expanded version of BetOnline’s odds apparently also includes Antonio Brown at 100-1, according to Sports Illustrated. Moving on.

If either Riddick or McAfee land a job in the “Monday Night Football” booth, it wouldn’t be the first time someone with Pittsburgh ties called those nationally televised games. Dennis Miller, a former “Saturday Night Live” cast member and Castle Shannon native, held that position from June 2000 until February 2002. Fun fact: He apparently beat out conservative radio host Rush Limbaugh, who also auditioned for the job.

Whoever ends up landing this job, one thing’s for sure: Plenty of folks in the football media sphere and beyond would love to see Riddick given that prestigious opportunity.

McAfee seems to be confident he would do well in the job, though he’s not quite sure it’s his time yet.
