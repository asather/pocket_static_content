Police say New Castle man argued with woman over cigarettes, then killed her husband

The 26-year-old man who was gunned down last week in New Castle was killed after a dispute over cigarettes between his wife and another man, according to a criminal complaint.

Police said Juan Aviles Suarez, 33, shot Eiram Alfonso Santiago-Hernandez last Thursday evening during an argument near the accused shooter’s apartment in the 1100 block of Pin Oak Drive in the Oak Leaf Gardens housing complex.

Mr. Suarez turned himself in and was arraigned Monday afternoon on one count of homicide, according to New Castle police, who had obtained a warrant for his arrest on Friday, the day after the homicide.

Police responded to the complex in response to calls for multiple shots fired around 7:48 p.m. Thursday.

They passed a car speeding in the opposite direction, as the driver flashed the lights and honked the horn. Police figured that a shooting victim might be in the car, and they were right. They gave the car an escort to UPMC Jameson, where Mr. Santiago-Hernandez died.

Police and security at the housing complex reviewed video, which showed an argument between two men outside the victim’s apartment and then the shooting, police said. The video showed a muzzle blast and a plume of smoke, according to the complaint.

A security officer told police that he recognized the shooter as Mr. Suarez.

The victim’s wife, Franchaly Shymko, said problems started when she was attending a birthday party near Mr. Suarez’s house. She started to argue about cigarettes with Mr. Suarez, whom she knew only as “Finito,” and she called her husband, who was at work, the complaint said.

When Mr. Santiago-Hernandez got home, he got into an argument with Mr. Suarez outside 1105 Pin Oak Drive, just down from Mr. Suarez’s apartment, police said.

Ms. Shymko told police she tried to separate the men, but Mr. Suarez pulled a gun and shot her husband, according to the complaint. He then ran away.

Two men helped Ms. Shymko get her husband into a car and drove to the hospital.

Before leaving the scene, though, one of the men — Ramon Luis Ramos Vasquez — grabbed the victim’s handgun and chased Mr. Suarez, firing several times, according to police.
