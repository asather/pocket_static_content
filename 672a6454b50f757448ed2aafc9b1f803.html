Steelers hire ex-Pitt assistant Matt Canada as quarterbacks coach

The Steelers have hired former Pitt offensive coordinator Matt Canada to be their quarterbacks coach, giving them a full-time assistant at the most important position on the team.

Randy Fichtner has served as quarterback coach since 2010, but it became a dual role when he was promoted to offensive coordinator before the 2018 season.

The job became more important this season when the Steelers had to use two quarterbacks who had never appeared in an NFL game — Mason Rudolph and Devlin Hodges — after Ben Roethlisberger was injured.

Canada was Pitt’s offensive coordinator in 2016 when the Panthers averaged 42 points per game. He left the following year to become offensive coordinator/quarterbacks coach at LSU. Canada also served as interim coach at Maryland in 2018.
