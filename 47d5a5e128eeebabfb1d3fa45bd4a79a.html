Seven sworn in Thursday as City of Pittsburgh paramedics

“Fearless” would describe the seven paramedics sworn in Thursday morning at the Fallen Officers Memorial on the North Shore.

Despite the lingering threat of the Covid-19 pandemic, several of the newly-minted paramedics said they didn’t fear the coronavirus and were ready to do their jobs serving the city.

“That’s what I signed up for,” said Zachary Saylor, of Shadyside. He is originally from Asbury, N.J., and started as a firefighter for the Franklin Township Fire Department in New Jersey at age 17.

Mr. Saylor graduated from the University of Pittsburgh with a degree in emergency medicine and will serve as a “roving” paramedic in the city until he is assigned to a specific medical station.

“We’re going to help the people the best we can,” he said.

His fellow graduate, Emon Ismael, originally of Toms River, N.J., noted that the medical world is always changing and paramedics like him have to be ready to adapt.

“This is the job I signed up for,” Mr. Ismael said. “There’s going to be an ever-revolving door of ‘what’s next’ in medicine.”

Also sworn in Wednesday were Benjamin Bowman, Matthew Lederman, Emily Migdal, Nissa Thomsen and Joseph Mahoney. Mr. Mahoney previously served as an EMT for the city and was promoted to a paramedic. They all swore the oath to the city at 11:14 a.m.

“It’s much easier to promote a paramedic from an EMT because they already know the system,” said Public Safety Director Wendell Hissrich.

Dr. Ronald Roth, medical director of the City of Pittsburgh EMS, offered words of confidence, encouragement and caution for the newly-minted paramedics in a brief speech.

“This is a unique time,” Dr. Roth said. “We are in the midst of two plagues - an acute plague and a plague that has been around for hundreds of years,” referring to recent civil unrest and the demand for equal rights.

“I hope you will accept this challenge,” he said.

The ceremony is normally held in the Pittsburgh City Council chambers, but due to the need to social distance the swearing-in was held outdoors at the North Shore.
