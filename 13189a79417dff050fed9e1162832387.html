State reports no new deaths in Western Pa. from COVID-19

The state Department of Health on Sunday reported 18 new COVID-19 deaths and 511 new positive cases of the disease.

It was the 21st straight the state has reported few than 1,000 new cases.

Of the deaths, none occurred in Western Pennsylvania, according to the state data.

With the new figures, the state has reported 71,926 positive cases of COVID-19 and 5,555 deaths since the state’s first recorded death from COVID-19 on March 18. The death toll is the fourth most of any state in the country. Fifty-five of the state’s 67 counties have recorded at least one death.

The cases count is the sixth most of any state in the country. All 67 counties in the state have had at least two positive cases.

Allegheny County’s health department said it had 14 new cases of COVID-19 on Sunday. That pushes the county total to 1,911 cases — 1,789 confirmed and 122 probable — since the first confirmed case on March 14. Allegheny County’s total is the highest in Western Pennsylvania. Beaver County ranks second in the region with  590 cases. Westermoreland County is third with 449 cases.

There have been a total of 151 deaths in the county — 140 confirmed and 11 probable — but only one since May 21, according to the county’s coronavirus dashboard.
