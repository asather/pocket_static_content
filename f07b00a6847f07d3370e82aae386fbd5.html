Alcoa Corp. is selling its Gum Springs, Arkansas waste treatment facility to Illinois-based waste management firm Veolia ES Technical Solutions in a $200 million cash deal that is expected to close in the first quarter.

The Pittsburgh-based aluminum company will receive an additional $50 million “if certain post-closing conditions are satisfied,” according to an Alcoa release. The sale is also subject to regulatory approval.

Elemental Environmental Solutions, a wholly owned Alcoa subsidiary, is a 1,300-acre hazardous waste treatment business in Gum Springs with about 70 employees.

The sale is part of an initiative announced in October to sell non-core assets over the next 12-18 months with a goal of generating $500 million to $1 billion in cash.

In mid-December, Alcoa announced it would permanently close its alumina refinery in Port Comfort, Texas, in a cost-cutting move. The plant had been idle since June 2016, and only about 45 employees remain. The company said that closure would result in an after-tax charge of about $175 million, or 94 cents per share, in the fourth quarter.
