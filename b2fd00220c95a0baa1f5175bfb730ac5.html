Ex-controller at Marco Contractors sentenced to over 5 years in prison for embezzling over $8 million, filing false tax returns

A Sewickley woman was sentenced to five years and four months in prison after she was convicted of embezzling approximately $8.7 million over nine years while employed as the controller for Marco Contractor Inc., the U.S. Attorney’s office announced Thursday. 

According to information presented in court, Sue O’Neill, 55, and another individual, identified only as R.G., started a company in 2014 called Bulldog Contractors LLC. R.G. has not been charged or further identified, however online records indicate that Bulldog is owned by Robin L. Gabel.

The case represents one of the largest embezzlement schemes uncovered in Western Pennsylvania.

O’Neill used $6.7 million of the stolen funds to support her new business venture, according to the U.S. Attorney’s office. O’Neill kept the other $2 million of embezzled funds for her personal bank account. 

In November, O’Neill admitted in federal court that she stole the money and didn’t report the extra income on her taxes. 

According to the U.S. Attorney’s office, the court was advised that O’Neill and her partner R.G. purchased “two 1969 Ford Shelby GT500s for approximately $160,000 each and spent approximately $163,000 renovating a 1969 Ford Mustang.”

O’Neill also spent hundreds of thousands of dollars on jewelry and a country club membership, according to the U.S. Attorney’s office. The vehicles, real estate, bank accounts, tractors and other assets were forfeited to the federal government as part of Ms. O'Neill's plea agreement. 

In addition to the embezzled funds, the court also was advised that O’Neill filed false income tax returns because she did not declare the stolen funds as a taxable income. 

Starting in 2009, O'Neill would issue payroll checks drawn on the Marco account at Citizens Bank and deposit the checks directly into Bulldog's account at Key Bank, according to a criminal complaint.

The total amount of that scheme was $2 million, with O’Neill manipulating Marshall-based Marco’s payroll system to conceal the fraud. O’Neill also made checks totaling to a sum of $215,000 payable to Bulldog from Rockerz Inc., another company controlled by Marco, and deposited those in the Key Bank account.

She also made checks totaling another $1 million payable to Bulldog from Marco and wired funds totaling $5.5 million from Marco to the Bulldog account. 

Following her time in prison, O’Neill will be held on two years of supervised release, according to the U.S. Attorney’s office. U.S. District Judge Donetta W. Ambrose imposed the sentence.
