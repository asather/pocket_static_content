Downtown's Law and Finance Building hits the market

The Law and Finance Building, a fixture Downtown for nearly a century, is up for sale.

Built in 1928 — a year before the start of the Great Depression — the 21-story office tower at 429 Fourth Avenue is being marketed for sale by SVN Three Rivers Commercial Advisors.

It is seeking $12.6 million for the property, which is 82% occupied. The building, at the corner of Fourth and Cherry Way, is living up to its name with current tenants that include law firms and finance-related businesses.

Law and Finance is one of three buildings Downtown being put up for sale by their owner, the Ewing family. The others are 4 Smithfield, at the corner of Fort Pitt Boulevard and Smithfield Street, and 100 Ross, which is already under contract to be sold.

Jason Campagna, managing director of SVN Three Rivers Commercial Advisors, said the Ewings are looking to move into industrial real estate.

“They are shifting their portfolio to other asset types, other investment opportunities,” he said.

In addition to the office and finance tenants, the Law and Finance Building is home to Bruegger’s Bagels and a Central Blood Bank office.

The Ewing family bought the property for $3.2 million in 2006.

Mr. Campagna said the asking price of $12.6 million is in line on a square footage basis with other recent sales Downtown, including Convention Tower at Penn Avenue and 10th Street, which sold for $7.7 million.

Despite the slowdowns and construction stops caused by the COVID-19 pandemic, he is optimistic that the Law and Finance Building and 4 Smithfield will attract attention.

“There are a lot of people looking at these buildings equal to or greater than there were three months ago,” Mr. Campagna said.

The asking price for the 12-story 4 Smithfield building, with a 73% occupancy rate, is $5.85 million. It was purchased for $450,000 in 1996. It was built in 1920.
