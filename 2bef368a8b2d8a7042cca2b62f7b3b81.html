More than 300 turn out for Freedom Corner prayer and protest

About 300 Pittsburgh clergy members and others are gathered Monday at the historic Freedom Corner in the Hill District for a "prayer and protest" in the wake of George Floyd’s death while in police custody and then marched to the City-County Building in Downtown.

The event, meant to show solidarity against racial injustice, started at noon and included nine speakers to signify the nearly nine minutes a white Minneapolis police officer had his knee on Floyd's neck. The African American man was pleading with police that he could not breathe during the Memorial Day incident.

"We are living between the tension of risking our individual health ...and the seeking of national justice," said the Rev. B. De Neice Welch, of Bidwell Presbyterian Church in Manchester, referring also to the coronavirus pandemic.

The participants then began marching down Centre Avenue toward Downtown.

A Pittsburgh police SUV escorted the marchers while police motorcycles blocked traffic at different intersections.

As they marched, they chanted, “Say his name,” “We can’t breathe,” and “Say her name.”

At least one man, wearing a pro-Donald Trump “Make America Great Again” hat, got out of his car to denounce the group and its message.
