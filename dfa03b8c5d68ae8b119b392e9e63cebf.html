WATCH: Steelers' JuJu Smith-Schuster goes cliff diving in Hawaii

Remember last July when Steelers receiver JuJu Smith-Schuster considered skydiving into the first day of training camp? That ultimately didn’t happen, probably because the 23-year-old realized putting himself in a position to get hurt wasn’t the greatest idea for his livelihood.

Fast forward to Wednesday, and Smith-Schuster seems to be taking a slightly more laissez-faire attitude toward his physical well-being.

Smith-Schuster — who is up for a contract extension either this offseason or in 2021 — posted a video of himself cliff diving into a lagoon in Hawaii.

Here’s the full video from his vacation that he uploaded to YouTube.

Right before he jettisoned himself off the cliff, you can hear Smith-Schuster say, “It’s my contract year, send it!” Clearly, he’s more than aware of his financial situation.

Through the first two years of his career, it looked like Smith-Schuster was going to be the next great Steelers receiver. That’s still entirely possible, though he had a disappointing 2019-20 season in just about every statistical way.

His down year was partially due to the carousel of quarterbacks throwing to him following Ben Roethlisberger’s early season-ending injury and the knee ailment that plagued him in the back half of the 2019 campaign.

Steelers nation is probably hoping that Smith-Schuster’s Hawaii excursion has left him rested, ready to get back to his previous form and, most importantly, injury-free.
