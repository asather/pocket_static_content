People on the move: 2/3/2020

Accounting

Goff Backa Alfera & Co. LLC hired Tao He as a manager in its tax department.

Architecture

AE Works appointed Dawn Cindric, manager, architecture, and Richard Witt Jr., chief client officer, and promoted Mary Margaret McCarty to client development advisor.

Banking

Key Private Bank named Victor Laurenza senior vice president and team leader for its Western Pennsylvania market. 

Andrew (Andy) W. Hasley, president of Standard Bank, was elected to the board of Federal Home Loan Bank of Pittsburgh.

Financial

Chris Blass was named vice president and senior wealth advisor with First Commonwealth Advisors.

Law

Michael P. Oliverio was named an equity partner at The Lynch Law Group.

Leech Tishman said partners Kaylyn Boca and Patrick W. Carothers were elected to serve additional two-year terms on its management committee, where they will oversee firm governance, strategic planning and daily operational issues. Ms. Boca is chair of Leech Tishman’s real estate practice group; Mr. Carothers is a member of its bankruptcy and creditors’ rights and corporate practice groups. 

Marshall Dennehey elevated Brett Shear to shareholder in its health care department. Mr. Shear represents physicians, medical professionals and hospitals in a wide variety of health care liability and medical malpractice actions. 

Nick Godfrey was elected to partnership at Dinsmore & Shohl. He works with its litigation, tort and mass tort groups, and he maintains an active NCAA practice, defending coaches against allegations of violating NCAA bylaws.

Professional Services

Solutions 21 promoted Albert Ciuksza to vice president of growth and development. 

Business notes

Cornerstone Consulting Organization named Marc USA its agency of record as part of its growth strategy. 

Rimkus Consulting Group Inc., a worldwide provider of forensic engineering and consulting services, announced the strategic acquisition of Pittsburgh-based Brashear Construction Consulting Inc.

The Heinz Endowments announced grant recipients for its new Just Arts program, an initiative that supports artists, organizations and communities who harness the power of the arts to respond to social issues. They are: Diversity Art Project Billboards (Westmoreland Diversity Coalition, $150,000), lead artist Sheila Cuellar-Shaffer; Sibyl’s Shrine (Greater Pittsburgh Arts Council, $150,000), lead artists Alisha B. Wormsley, Jessica Gaynelle Moss and Naomi Chambers; Visualizing Inequality (Pittsburgh Filmmakers, $50,000), lead artist Brian Cohen; Something Old, Something New (Jewish Family and Children’s Services, $30,250), lead artist Blaine Siegel; Bridges Home (Union Project, $28,500), lead artists Liz Foster-Shaner and Michelle King; and Latino Americans in Pittsburgh: Fighting Hate and Discrimination Project (Casa San Jose, $26,000), lead artist Andres Tapia-Urzua.
