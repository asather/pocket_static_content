Allegheny County reports 66 new COVID-19 cases, one additional death

The number of new COVID-19 cases in Allegheny County dropped to 66 on Saturday, with 12 additional hospitalizations and one new death. The person died on July 29 and was in their 80s, said the county.

Those positive COVID-19 results came from 1,459 tests, a positivity rate below 5 percent. It is the fewest number of cases reported by the county on any one day since June 26, when the county reported 61 cases. The positive tests spanned from July 7 to July 31 and those who tested positive ranged in age between 1 and 90, with a median age of 37.

Since March 14, Allegheny County has seen 8,160 cases of COVID-19, with 681 hospitalizations and 240 deaths.

The state of Pennsylvania reported an additional 888 postive COVID-19 cases Saturday, with 15 additional deaths. Four of those deaths occurred in Western Pennsylvania, with Greene County reporting its first death on Saturday. Total cases in Pennsylvania are now at 112,936, with 7,204 deaths statewide.
