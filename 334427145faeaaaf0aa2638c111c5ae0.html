Pirates unaffected by COVID-19 spike in Florida

While novel coronavirus cases have spiked in Florida and have impacted other major league teams’ spring training facilities, the Pirates have not experienced anything of the sort.

According to a team spokesman, they have not had any player, coach or staff member test positive for COVID-19.

The Pirates had already ceased all baseball operations at Pirate City in Bradenton, Fla. for “sometime now” and have had “limited” operations with less than a handful of major league players utilizing the facilities.

“All follow appropriate entry protocols and social distancing guidelines, and the facility is cleaned multiple times throughout the day,” Pirates vice president of communications and broadcasting Brian Warecki said.

About an hour away, however, the Phillies announced Friday that five players have tested positive for COVID-19 at the team’s spring camp in Clearwater, Fla., prompting the club to indefinitely close the complex. The team also said that three staff members at the camp have tested positive. The club didn’t identify any of those affected.

The announcement came while Major League Baseball owners and players try to negotiate a deal to begin the season amid the coronavirus pandemic, including health protocols. Some players had been recently been working out at spring training sites while practicing social distancing.

The Pirates do have 10 minor league players who have remained in Bradenton, unable to return home because of travel restrictions. Their focus, Warecki said, has shifted toward preparing for spring training 2.0 in Pittsburgh.

The Toronto Blue Jays have also closed their spring training facilities amid the COVID-19 scare.
