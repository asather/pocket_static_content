Downtown bomb suspect indicted by federal grand jury

A Duquesne Heights man has been indicted by a federal grand jury for allegedly placing a bag of bombs in some bushes near 2 PNC Plaza on May 31.

The grand jury charged Matthew Michanowicz, 52, with possession of an unregistered destructive device.

He had previously been charged by complaint, after which prosecutors have 30 days to seek an indictment.

The U.S. attorney's office said Mr. Michanowicz planted a backpack filled with homemade Molotov cocktails during protests of the killing of George Floyd at the hands of Minneapolis police.

At the time of the complaint, U.S. Attorney Scott Brady characterized Mr. Michanowicz as an agitator.

"He wasn't there to protest," he said. "He was there to engage in violent attacks."

Pittsburgh police on June 1 were called to PNC Plaza for a report of a suspicious military-style green backpack near a bike rack under some trees. Police discovered three explosive devices and detected a foul odor and called in the bomb squad.

The devices were "spent OC vapor grenades," prosecutors said, and had wicks attached to held in place by spray foam insulation.

Security footage from PNC showed an older man possessing the bag where it was found who was riding a blue bike with a bright red pouch on the handlebars. On June 3, police on patrol saw a man with a bike matching that description at the place where the bag had been found on June 1 and took Mr. Michanowicz into custody.

Mr. Michanowicz faces a detention hearing in federal court on July 7.
