Police believe Indiana County man, 21, shot own baby by accident

A 21-year-old father shot his own baby by accident last week inside an Indiana County home, police said Monday.

“Troopers do not have any reason to believe that this was an intentional discharge of the firearm,” a Pennsylvania State Police news release said.

The shooting, which wounded a 4-month-old boy, happened around 6:16 p.m. Saturday inside an apartment on College Lodge Road in White Township.

Police said the only people home at the time were the child and his parents.

“Troopers believe that the father, while inside the apartment, fired a single round from a 9mm handgun. The round struck the child, resulting in significant injuries to the child,” police said.

The boy was shot in the upper body. He was in stable condition at UPMC Children’s Hospital of Pittsburgh.

Police said when the investigation is done, the results will be turned over to the Indiana County District Attorney’s Office.

“It’s possible that some criminal charges might be filed,” Trooper Cliff Greenfield said, declining to elaborate.

The trooper said that as far as he was aware, the gun was legally possessed.

When the shooting occurred, the child’s parents immediately alerted a neighbor, who called 911.

“We have a good idea of how it happened,” Trooper Greenfield said, but he declined to provide details, saying there was more to investigate.
