Tristan Jarry and Kris Letang make NHL All-Star game

Tristan Jarry and Kris Letang have been invited to the All-Star game after all.

The NHL on Tuesday announced that both Penguins have been selected as injury replacements for All-Star weekend, which takes place Jan. 24-25 in St. Louis. Jarry will replace Columbus Blue Jackets goalie Joonas Korpisalo and Letang will go in place of teammate Jake Guentzel, who underwent shoulder surgery last week.

Guentzel was initially the Penguins’ lone All-Star when teams were announced last week. Letang was Pittsburgh’s candidate for the “Last Men In” fan vote to select the final player for the nine-skater, two-goalie Metropolitan Division team.

Jarry will be making his All-Star game debut. Entering Tuesday, he has played in 20 games this season, posting a record of 13-6-1. The 24-year-old goalie leads the league with a 1.99 goals-against average and a .935 save percentage.

This is the sixth All-Star game selection for Letang, the most among defensemen in Penguins history. While he has been up and down with partner Brian Dumoulin sidelined, his 26 points in 34 games rank fourth among Metro blue-liners.
