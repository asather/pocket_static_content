COVID-19 Update: Pa. reports more than 900 new cases, 10 deaths

The Pennsylvania Health Department on Thursday reported 933 new cases of COVID-19 and 10 additional deaths due to the virus.

Western Pennsylvania accounted for 204 of the state’s new cases, with Allegheny County showing the largest daily increase with 79 new cases, followed by Indiana County with 28 new cases from the previous day.

Statewide, there have now been 147,923 total cases of the virus since the beginning of the pandemic.

With the addition of Thursday’s diagnoses, Allegheny County’s total number of cases stands at 11,510 since March.

The 79 latest cases in the county come from 1,226 tests performed between Aug. 30 and Sept. 16, although county health officials said the “vast majority” of the tests were taken between Sept. 10 and Sept. 16. The people who have recently tested positive range in age from 2 to 100, with a median age of 38.

Of the 10 new deaths statewide, the Pittsburgh region reported five — three in Allegheny County and one each in Beaver and Westmoreland counties. 

The deaths in Allegheny County occurred between Aug. 25 and Sept. 15 and include one person in their 60s and two people in their 70s. One of the deaths has been associated with a long-term care facility, according to health officials.

Since mid-March, a total of 366 people in the county and 7,913 people across the state have died as a result of the illness.

Currently, 459 people in Pennsylvania are hospitalized for COVID-19 — 54 of them requiring mechanical ventilation. Forty-one of those hospitalizations are in Allegheny County, with eight COVID-19 patients needing a ventilator, based on the most recent data from the state

The state and county health departments have both that they will no longer update case counts on Sundays. The Monday reports will include a break down of the cases for the two days.
