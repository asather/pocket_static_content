Pirates roll out new ticket program for families

The Pirates are making a kid-friendly change to get more people inside PNC Park during the first two months of the baseball season.

New president Travis Williams announced an initiative Friday that allows parents to bring fans ages 14 and under to April or May games for free with the purchase of a $20 adult ticket. Each adult ticket nets one free kids ticket.

Those $20 tickets will be in special sections throughout the upper grandstand level.

The Pirates also announced an expansion of the kids’ concession offerings. Bucaroos, the family-friendly concession stand located just inside the left-field gate, will have two new locations — in sections 147 and 307.

There, hot dogs, popcorn, pretzels and Coke products will be available for $2.50 each, while nachos and chicken nuggets will be sold for $3 each.

More information on the program can be found at pirates.com/kidsfree.

“Baseball brings generations of families together and creates lasting memories, and there is no better place to do that than PNC Park,” Williams said in a statement. “Our focus is to make Pirates baseball as accessible to as many families as possible and, as part of their overall ballpark experience, families should be able to enjoy a game at PNC Park as often as possible at a reasonable cost.”
