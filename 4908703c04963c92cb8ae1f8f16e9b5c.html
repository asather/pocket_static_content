WPIAL 2A-1A roundup: Fort Cherry surprises No. 4 Rochester

Home hasn’t been sweet to Fort Cherry in recent years. But the host Rangers (2-3, 2-3) rallied late to stun WPIAL Class A No. 4-ranked Rochester on Friday night, 21-19, thus sending shock waves through the Big Seven Conference standings.

Maddox Truschel’s 10-yard touchdown run with 47 seconds remaining proved to be the winning points for Fort Cherry. Truschel finished with 95 yards rushing and two touchdowns on 17 carries. The junior quarterback also completed 10 of 16 passes for 152 yards.

The Rangers won their first home game since defeating Carlynton August 31, 2018. It snapped a home losing streak of nine games while sending the Rams (4-1, 4-1) to their first defeat of the season. Rochester’s loss means No. 3-ranked Shenango has sole possession of first place in the Big Seven Conference.

More Class 1A

Shenango 59, Northgate 12: Reis Watkins rushed for 299 yards and scored five touchdowns to lead the Wildcats (5-0, 5-0) past the host Flames (0-5, 0-5) in the Big Seven Conference. Watkins went over the 1,000-yard mark for the season. He leads the WPIAL in rushing with 1,093 yards. He also leads the district in scoring with 19 touchdowns. Shenango outgained Northgate on the ground, 326-0.

Jeannette 36, Springdale 28: The No. 2-ranked Jayhawks (4-1, 4-1) handed the host Dynamos (4-1, 4-1) a setback in the Eastern Conference. Roberto Smith led Jeannette with 61 yards rushing and two touchdowns, including a late three-yard scoring run that clinched the game with the Jayhawks sitting on a two-point lead. Legend Ausk led Springdale with 245 yards passing.

Our Lady of the Sacred Heart 28, Union 18: Jaymar Pearson led the Chargers (4-1, 4-1) to a win against Union (2-3, 2-3) in a Big Seven Conference contest played at Moon Stadium. Pearson scored on touchdown runs of 11, 24 and 47 yards to offset the performance of Union’s Anthony Nealy who had nine catches for 120 yards receiving, including a 50-yard touchdown reception from quarterback Tyler Staub. Pearson finished with 108 yards rushing on 15 carries.

Avella 13, Mapletown 7: Noah Markle’s 4-yard touchdown run in the fourth quarter provided the Eagles (4-1, 4-1) with a victory at Mapletown (1-4, 1-4) in the Tri-County South Conference. KJ Rush led Avella with 77 yards rushing and 117 yards passing. Markle also scored Avella’s first touchdown in the second quarter via a 1-yard run. Mapletown’s Landan Stevenson tied the score with a 1-yard touchdown run with 36 second left before halftime.

Burgettstown 29, Cornell 14: The Blue Devils (2-3, 2-3) snapped a three-game losing streak with a road victory over the Raiders (1-4, 1-4) in the Big Seven Conference. Jackson LaRocka completed 5 of 7 passes for 120 yards and two touchdowns for Burgettstown. Shame Kemper led the Blue Devils with 190 yards rushing and a touchdown.

Class 2A

McGuffey 54, Waynesburg 8: A Century Conference victory gave the Highlanders’ Ed Dalton the 200th win of his coaching career. Coming up short by a touchdown the previous week at home in a non-conference loss to Linsly, W.Va., No. 2-ranked McGuffey (4-0, 4-1) wasted no time in securing a victory against the Raiders (0-5, 0-5). McGuffey led, 41-0, after the first quarter. Kyle Brookman scored the first two McGuffey touchdowns on runs of 28 and 10 yards. Jeremiah Johnson also scored two Highlanders touchdowns, including one via a 100-yard interception return. McGuffey outrushed Waynesburg, 287-95.

Apollo-Ridge 42, Shady Side Academy 7: Logan Harmon scored four touchdowns and rushed for 119 yards on 15 carries as the Vikings (4-0, 2-0) remained unbeaten in the Allegheny Conference. Shady Side Academy fell to 0-3 overall, 0-2 in conference. Apollo-Ridge led, 28-0, at halftime.

Carlynton 44, Carrick 8: The host Cougars (2-3) routed Carrick (0-4) of the City League in a non-conference game. Bryan Lee led Carlynton with 83 yards rushing and two touchdowns. He also scored a touchdown on an interception return covering 20 yards.
