How much does it cost to remove a bus from a sinkhole? About $88,000 in Pittsburgh

The bill for the removal of a Port Authority bus from a large Downtown sinkhole in October is in, and it’s going to cost $88,150.

Pittsburgh City Council introduced legislation Tuesday to pay Allegheny Crane Rental for its emergency services that involved two cranes and a 12-member crew after the rear end of the bus fell into a 20-foot-deep sinkhole the morning of Oct. 28 as it stopped for a red light on 10th Street at Penn Avenue.

The additional costs for street repairs remain unknown at this point, said Councilman Daniel Lavelle, who chairs the Finance and Law Committee.

“This [cost] is just to remove the bus. So we’re probably talking hundreds of thousands,” Mr. Lavelle said, adding that he is unsure of how the total cost will be split.

Utility companies have been fixing damaged underground infrastructure since the sinkhole incident occurred.

Pittsburgh Allegheny County Thermal is finishing repairs to tunnels “deep below the hole,” said Timothy McNulty, mayoral spokesman. “When that's done we will determine whether we can repave and reopen the street.”

The company is expected to finish repairs to the Downtown steam heat system by early February.

The cause of the sinkhole has not yet been determined, as the focus has been “completely on fixing the utility issues and getting the street reopened,” Mr. McNulty said.
