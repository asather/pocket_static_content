People on the move: 9/14/2020

Banking

First National Bank, the largest subsidiary of FNB Corp., promoted Jason Dobronz to director of corporate resilience and physical security.

Education

The Robert Morris University School of Business named Jacob C. Peng its first Richard J. Harshman Professor of Accounting.

Health

Excela Health named Jeffrey A. Tiesi executive vice president and chief operating officer.

BlueSphere Bio appointed James Onuffer as vice president of product development.

Law

Eckert Seamans said Renée Martin-Nagle joined as special counsel.

Manufacturing

Kelley Skoloda and Michael Golden were named to the board at Circadiance.

Nonprofit

The Westmoreland-Fayette Workforce Investment Board selected Courtney Venick to serve as youth services manager. 

Dress for Success Pittsburgh named new board members: Jennifer Bibb Hisdorf, assistant vice resident/banking center manager, WesBanco; Becca Carl, general manager, retail market trainer, Ulta Beauty Pittsburgh; Jodi Fowler, manager of special projects, corporate and foundation relations, University of Pittsburgh; Alexander Heit, vice president partnership and strategy, Visimo; Amber McGee, Victims of Crime Act attorney, Neighborhood Legal Services; Jennifer Mondi, senior director, divisional chief financial officer, Evoqua Water Technologies; Kim Moses, senior associate legal counsel for health sciences and research, Office of University Counsel, University of Pittsburgh; Cindy Oliver, owner, The Social Source Marketing and Resource Advantage Consulting; Arthur Pang, manager, marketing and communications, The Federal Home Loan Bank of Pittsburgh; Soo Yi Thompson, director, cybersecurity operations, Highmark Health; and Becky Trimpey, senior global training manager, MSA.

Awards and Honors

Prabhjot Singh Bedi, a hospitalist at UPMC East Hospital in Monroeville, was named a Top Physician Under 40 by the Pennsylvania Medical Society.

Business notes

CertaSite said Advanced Fire Co. Approved Fire Protection and Advanced Fire & Security have merged to form Advanced Fire, a CertaSite company. CertaSite is a new commercial fire protection and life safety company that provides the highest levels of customer service, responsiveness, building safety and code compliance to customers across the country. 

Advanced Controls and Distribution acquired Adcon Engineering Co. Together, the two companies offer a very comprehensive product portfolio from manufacturers, such as Banner Engineering, Turck, Siemens, Sick, Maple Systems, IDEC, Nachi Robotics and many others.

Cardiothoracic surgeons at Allegheny General Hospital, part of Allegheny Health Network, are joining biomedical engineers from Carnegie Mellon University to develop a new heart monitoring device to serve as an alternative for cardiac patients who have undergone open-heart surgery. The team was awarded a Trailblazer grant for $400,000 from the National Institutes of Health’s National Institute of Biomedical Imaging and Bioengineering. AGH cardiac surgeon Dr. Scott Halbreiner will serve as principal investigator of the three-year project alongside Christopher J. Bettinger, professor of material sciences and biomedical engineering at CMU.
