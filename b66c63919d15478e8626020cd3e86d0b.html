Two more southwestern Pa. nursing homes confirm residents tested positive for COVID-19

Two nursing homes in southwestern Pennsylvania confirmed Wednesday night that a resident tested positive for COVID-19.

Rochester Manor and Villa in Beaver County confirmed a resident tested positive and is now quarantined in a “secure, isolated unit.”

Southminster Place in Washington also confirmed Wednesday that a resident had tested positive. The home, a part of Presbyterian SeniorCare Network, announced on Saturday that an employee had tested positive.

The resident at Rochester Manor and Villa has been placed in a “re-opened personal care wing” that was prepared in anticipation of a case of COVID-19 in the home.

“We treasure every single one of our residents and have taken extra precautions since the outbreak to protect their welfare, safety, and health,” executive director Kristin Oeder wrote in a statement. “It’s critical for us to be transparent and let our community know what precautions we have been and are taking.

“We are actively monitoring all residents for signs and symptoms and coordinating closely with the Pennsylvania Department of Health, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention and local health departments.”

The resident at Southminster Place was one of 47 who were tested Monday for the virus; the other 46 of those came back negative. Eight of the home’s staff members who were in contact with the affected employee also tested negative.

“Since the start of this pandemic, we have been aggressive about implementing network-wide precautionary measures and restrictions in accordance with federal, state and local guidance,” Presbyterian SeniorCare’s senior director of clinical services Kristin Henderson wrote in a release. “And we have been constantly updating and adjusting our protocols and procedures as the guidance and learnings have continued to evolve.”

The nursing homes both said the measures they’ve taken to mitigate spread include following government protocols on mask usage and social distancing.
