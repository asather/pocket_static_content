Allegheny County reports 1 new death, 139 cases of COVID-19

For the second time in the past week, Pennsylvania has logged over 1,000 new cases of COVID-19 in a single day. The increase of 1,027 new cases for Tuesday brings the statewide total to 102,765, according to the state health department.

The state also saw an increase of over 1,000 cases on Friday, July 17. 

Western Pennsylvania accounted for 319 of the newest cases in the state. 

In Allegheny County there are 139 new COVID-19 cases, eight new hospitalizations and one new death from the virus, the health department reported Tuesday.

The positive cases range in age from 1 to 92 years old, with the median age being 38. 

The newest batch of cases comes from 1,501 tests that were administered between June 29 and July 20, with five positive cases from June 29, 22 from July 5, 105 from July 12 and 12 from July 19. They are part of Tuesday’s single-day total because they were only recently reported to the county health department.

Beaver County saw an increase of 43 new cases, bringing it’s county total to 1,011. It is the third county in the region to have over 1,000 cases, along with Allegheny County with 6,574 cases and Westmoreland County with 1,193 total cases. 

With the eight new hospitalizations, the Allegheny County has had 520 past or present hospitalizations.

According to state health department data, there are currently 736 people hospitalized as a result of COVID-19.

The county health department said the single fatality reported Tuesday was a 63-year-old who died on July 19. The county death toll is now 209 virus related deaths, according to figures from the county. The state is reporting 211 deaths for Allegheny. 

Statewide, there were 20 additional COVID-19 related deaths, bringing the state death toll to 7,038.
