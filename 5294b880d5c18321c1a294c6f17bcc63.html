COVID-19 Update: Allegheny County reports 63 new cases, 7 more deaths

The Allegheny County Health Department on Friday reported 63 new cases of COVID-19 and seven related deaths.

The county has now gone eight straight days with case counts below 100. The latest infections bring the county’s case total to 10,128 since the beginning of the pandemic.

The newest cases come from 1,605 tests taken between Aug. 17 and Aug. 27. Those who have recently tested positive range in age from 7 months to 97, with the median age of 38.

For the week of Aug. 13 to Aug. 20, Allegheny County had a positivity rate of 3.9%, according to the most recent data from the state health department. 

Pennsylvania reported an increase of 835 new cases for Friday, with Western Pennsylvania accounting for 186 of them. Allegheny County saw the largest increase in the region for Friday, followed by Beaver County with 31 additional cases. 

Statewide there have been 131,991 cases of the virus, according to data from the state.

County health officials said the seven new deaths occurred between Aug. 13 and Aug. 27; four of them are related to long-term care facilities. The deaths were among two people in their 60s, three in their 70s, one in their 80s and one in their 90s.

In the county, there have been 27 deaths as a result of the virus since Aug. 21. The death toll overall is now 325.

The state death toll has climbed by 20 new deaths on Friday, bringing the total number of COVID-19 related deaths to 7,655. Of the newest deaths, eight occurred in the region. Allegheny County reported seven and Washington County reported one new death.

Since mid-March, 962 people have been hospitalized for COVID-19. Currently there are 75 patients hospitalized in the county for the virus, 18 of whom are currently requiring mechanical ventilation, according to the most recent data from the state.
