Pitt cornerback Damarri Mathis out for the 2020 season

This is a developing story. It will be updated.

Pitt’s vaunted defense has lost another starter.

Senior cornerback Damarri Mathis will miss the 2020 season “due to a non-football related injury,” the program announced Tuesday afternoon. Mathis, a 10-game starter last year, was expected to lead a thin cornerback group alongside Jason Pinnock in 2020.

“We are certainly disappointed for Damarri,” Pitt coach Pat Narduzzi said in a statement. “Fortunately, he still has a redshirt season available in addition to the eligibility relief the NCAA will provide this year.”

Mathis, who has played in 36 games over the last three years, logged two interceptions and 11 pass breakups in 2019, splitting time with Pinnock opposite Dane Jackson.

Now, redshirt sophomore Marquis Williams — who was projected to be Pitt’s No. 3 corner — is in a prime position to step in for Mathis. Narduzzi also praised sophomore A.J. Woods, who played 10 games on special teams as a freshman and has impressed during training camp.

In addition, the Panthers have current freshmen Jahvante Royal and Rashad Battle as depth options. Both were four-star prospects, per Rivals.

The Mathis news comes two weeks after returning All-ACC defensive tackle Jaylen Twyman opted out of the 2020 season. The Pitt defense — which lost starting defensive linemen Rashad Weaver and Keyshon Camp last August for the 2019 season — is forced to replace a pair of first-teamers in consecutive campaigns.
