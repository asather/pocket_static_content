Woman accused of passing along drugs with a kiss during prison visit

A kiss between a prison inmate and his visitor wasn’t what it appeared to be, authorities said.

Somerset police allege that 21-year-old Jasmine Lanell Livingston of Manchester didn’t have romance on her mind on Jan. 23 when she was arrested for delivering — via a kiss — a tootsie-roll shaped package filled with drugs while visiting her boyfriend at SCI Somerset prison.

According to police, Ms. Livingston held the package in her mouth while visiting Anthony Jackson, 26. During a kiss goodbye, police said she transferred the package from her mouth to his.

Ms. Livingston said she received the package from a friend and was not prescribed the drugs, according to police.

She was charged with possession with intent to deliver and other offenses.

Ms. Livingston was held at Somerset County Jail until she posted a $10,000 bail on Jan. 27. A preliminary trial was originally scheduled for Jan. 30 and has yet to be rescheduled, according to court documents.

According to the Pennsylvania Department of Corrections, inmates and their visitors are allowed a “brief kiss and embrace” at the beginning and end of the visitation.
