Billy Porter uses the ‘LGBTQ State of the Union’ to take on Trump and decry anti-trans crimes

Actor Billy Porter, providing the “LGBTQ State of the Union” address ahead of President Donald Trump’s speech to Congress on Tuesday night, took the commander-in-chief to task for his administration’s treatment of the transgender community but said “our union is far from broken.”

Mr. Porter called the rate of violent crimes against transgender people “nothing short of an epidemic" in the speech, delivered from a clear lectern in a video published at noon by Logo TV. The award-winning performer \u2015 who also spoke on the state of the LGBTQ union a year ago \u2015 listed a range of other groups he said are under assault from the White House, including women and immigrants.

“The fate of the entire country is in the balance,” Mr. Porter said. “I know it sounds dramatic. But if now is not the time for drama, child, when is?”

While Mr. Porter’s roughly seven-minute commentary lacked the endless applause and myriad tributes of the marathon State of the Union in Washington, the 50-year-old Pittsburgh native still hit numerous serious notes.

“Donald J. Trump has painted himself as a friend of the LGBTQ community while revealing his true colors at every malicious turn,” Mr. Porter said. “For some of us, each day under this administration has been a matter of life and death.”
