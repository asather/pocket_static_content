North Catholic's Isaac and Dave DeGregorio earn all-state honors

North Catholic’s Isaac DeGregorio found his name on the all-state team when the Pennsylvania Sports Writers All-State Team for Class 3A was released Wednesday.

Like father, like son.

Isaac, a senior at North Catholic, and his dad, coach Dave DeGregorio, were both honored. Isaac was a second-team selection, while Dave was named coach of the year.

The DeGregorios formed a WPIAL championship father-son combo this season, leading North Catholic to the WPIAL Class 3A title and PIAA quarterfinals before the season was stopped because of the COVID-19 pandemic. Isaac and Dave are just the second father-son tandem from the WPIAL to earn all-state recognition in the same season in the last 10 years. In 2016, Mars’ Rob Carmody was Class 3A coach of the year and Robby Carmody made the first team.

Isaac DeGregorio, a 5-foot-11 guard, averaged 17.9 points per game this season while helping the Trojans go 26-2. He made 86 3-pointers and shot 87% from the free-throw line. A third-team all-state pick his junior season, DeGregorio finished his career with 1,563 points, 283 3-pointers and 440 assists. He will continue his career as a preferred walk-on at Kentucky. DeGregorio’s grandfather, Joe, coached Kentucky coach John Calipari in college.

This year’s WPIAL title was Dave DeGregorio’s first as a head coach. He’s 47-8 in two seasons at North Catholic. He previously led Pine-Richland and North Allegheny to WPIAL runner-up finishes. Another of Dave’s sons, Owen, was a junior guard who saw time off the bench.

Two other WPIAL players were selected to Class 2A all-state teams — Steel Valley senior guard Cam Polak and Lincoln Park senior guard Isaiah Smith were third-team picks. Polak was second in the WPIAL in scoring with 30.6 points a game. Smith averaged 14.2 points a game and led Lincoln Park to the WPIAL final and PIAA quarterfinals. Smith finds himself on the all-state team for the third time. He was a second-team pick in 2019 and made the third team as a sophomore when he played at Sewickley Academy. Both Polak and Smith will play at Division II schools — Polak at West Chester and Smith at Caldwell.

Camp Hill Trinity sophomore point guard Chance Westry was named player of the year. Westry averaged 24.4 points a game and already has offers from the likes of Michigan, Syracuse and Georgetown. Like Westry, Westtown School senior forward Noah Collier was a first-team honoree. Collier is a Pitt recruit.
