Cold weather, winter school breaks bump up region's December unemployment to 4.5%

Winter break at schools and universities and cold weather that slowed construction combined to push up the December unemployment rate in the seven-county Pittsburgh area by two-tenths of a percentage point to 4.5%, according to the state Department of Labor & Industry.

In the Pittsburgh Metropolitan Statistical Area, Allegheny County had the lowest unemployment rate at 4.5% while Fayette County had the highest rate at 6.9%. Among the counties in the region, only Allegheny did not see an increase in the unemployment rate from November.

The biggest declines from November in the number of jobs were in the education and health services sector, down less than 1% or 1,800, and construction, down 2.6% or 1,600, which helped nudge up the regionâ€™s jobless rate. But December was also the height of the shopping and shipping season, which accounted for a 2,300 job increase to 216,200 in the sector. However, the figures were not seasonally adjusted.

The total number of seasonally adjusted nonfarm jobs in the seven-county region rose by 2,800 or 0.24% to 1.19 million from November, according to the report, but was unchanged over the year, while the number of jobs statewide bumped up by 0.5%. Unemployment rates statewide ranged between 3.3% in Centre County to 8.1% in Cameron County.
