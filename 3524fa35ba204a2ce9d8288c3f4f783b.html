Pennsylvania closing some Fine Wine & Good Spirits stores

The Pennsylvania Liquor Control Board has announced some “phased closure” of Fine Wine & Good Spirits stores and service centers in Eastern Pennsylvania. as part of COVID-19 mitigation. 

Scores of stores are to temporarily close in Bucks, Chester, Delaware and Montgomery counties, but several stores are to remain open in each for a few days longer. 

There are 88 stores in those counties and those that are open will remain so for normal hours on Sunday.

But on Monday, opening as normal and closing at 9 p.m., are just one licensee service center and six stores in Bucks County,  the same in Chester and Delaware counties, and one licensee service center and eight stores in Montgomery County. 

And on Tuesday, all licensee service centers and stores in those four counties will close and remain closed until further notice, according to the state Liquor Control Board.

According to a release, “At this time the PLCB plans to continue to operate its e-commerce fulfillment center, and Pennsylvania residents can shop for wine and spirits at www.FineWineAndGoodSpirits.com, having purchases shipped directly to their homes.”

Earlier this week, Fine Wine & Good Spirits stores indefinitely suspended all product tastings, bottle signings, educational seminars and other group events at all 598 stores across Pennsylvania.

The PLCB also reinforced with store employees enhanced cleaning and disinfecting procedures.

Toward the end of the month, the PLCB says, it will re-evaluate its operations, guided by the strategy developed by the Governor’s office and the Pennsylvania Department of Health.

Asked if store closing of any sort were going to happen in Western Pennsylvania, a spokesperson replied, “We’re following guidance from the Department of Health and the Governor’s Office.”

The PLCB says it will update store closings on the store locator page of its website. 

The PLCB’s “Coronavirus (COVID-19) Information for Consumers“ update is at https://www.lcb.pa.gov/Consumers/Pages/Coronavirus.aspx.
