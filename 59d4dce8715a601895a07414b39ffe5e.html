Biden campaign names two Philadelphia-based operatives to top positions

The effort to secure Pennsylvania for presumptive Democratic presidential nominee Joe Biden will be led — at least in part — by two Philadelphia-based political operatives.

Leading the campaign as state director will be Brendan McPhillips, a longtime campaign guru who most recently managed Pete Buttigieg’s momentous primary win in Iowa in February. Sinceré Harris, who has directed the state Democratic Party through the period following Donald Trump’s election, will serve as senior adviser to Mr. Biden’s Pennsylvania effort.

The Biden campaign announced the hires on Tuesday, touting the pair’s history of helping Democratic candidates win in Pennsylvania and — in Mr. McPhillips’ case — elsewhere in the U.S.

Mr. McPhillips, a veteran of previous Democratic presidential campaigns here, managed former Tallahassee Mayor Andrew Gillum’s winning Democratic primary bid in 2018, then — according to a report in Politico — split ways with the candidate before Mr. Gillum lost in the general election to now Florida Gov. Ron DeSantis.

Before that, Mr. McPhillips served as campaign manager for former Braddock Mayor John Fetterman’s U.S. Senate bid, which ended in a third-place finish in the primary.

Ms. Harris, as executive director of the state Democrats, has overseen administration of the party while it has picked up Congressional seats, made gains in the state Legislature and re-elected two of its most prominent members, Gov. Tom Wolf and U.S. Sen. Bob Casey — a record that the Biden campaign praised in its hiring announcement.

Ms. Harris, a veteran of Mr. Wolf’s first winning campaign, also worked on President Barack Obama’s re-election effort in 2012.
