3 Port Authority workers test positive for COVID-19

Three West Mifflin Port Authority bus garage workers reportedly tested positive for COVID-19 this past week.

According to Port Authority’s weekly COVID-19 case update, the three cases bring the total up to 51 — a tally starting in March. All three employees, who are operators working at the Port Authority bus garage in West Mifflin, reported they had family members who recently tested positive, according to agency spokesperson Adam Brandolph. 

Mr. Brandolph states one of the employees reported testing positive Sept. 11 and last worked Sept. 7. 

Another employee reported testing positive Sept. 14 and last worked Sept. 9. The third employee reported testing positive Sept. 15 and last worked Sept. 10. 

All three employees have been directed to self-quarantine since the time they last worked, according to Mr. Brandolph. 

Of the 51 Port Authority employees who tested positive for COVID-19 since March, 44 employees have recovered and been cleared to work, according to Mr. Brandolph. Seven employees are currently at home recovering. 

“Port Authority continues to follow all state and county health department guidelines and as with all previous cases, confidentially reported these cases to the Allegheny County Health Department,” Mr. Brandolph said in a statement.
