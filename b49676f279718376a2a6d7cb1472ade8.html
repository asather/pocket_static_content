None hurt in Hazelwood house fire

No one was hurt and no one was home Monday morning as Pittsburgh firefighters put out a blaze in Hazelwood.

The fire started on the second floor of a home that is part of a unit of three rowhomes, Battalion Chief Ron Dunlap said. It was first reported to authorities just before 7:30 a.m., dispatch officials said.

While the fire spread to the third floor, Chief Dunlap said it was contained to just that home.

“The crews did a good job and knocked (the fire) down pretty quick," he said. “We kept it out of the other houses."

Gas and electric utilities were shut off during the blaze.
