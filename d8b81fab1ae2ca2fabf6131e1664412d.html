Unemployment rate jumps to 16.8% in Pittsburgh metro area

The seasonally adjusted unemployment rate in the seven-county Pittsburgh area jumped to 16.8% in April, up 10.9 percentage points from March as total non-farm jobs shrunk 17% to 985,100, the lowest number since 1990, according to new data from the state Bureau of Labor & Industry.

The jobless rate in the Pittsburgh metropolitan statistical area was the highest seen in the region since 1983 during the collapse of the steel industry.

Job losses in the Pittsburgh metro area totaled 207,000 in April compared to the same month a year ago.

Statewide, unemployment rate was 15.1% for April, up 9.3 percentage points from a month ago, while the national rate rose 10.3 percentage points to 14.7%.

The report reflects the shutdown of most forms of commerce to try to contain the spread of the new coronavirus, which causes COVID-19, a highly infectious respiratory disease.

Fayette County led the Pittsburgh metro region in unemployment at 20.5% followed by Beaver County at 18.7% and Armstrong County at 15.9%, according to the state Bureau of Labor & Industry.

Unemployment in Allegheny County, which transitions to green Friday from the yellow classification as state-imposed business and travel restrictions ease, was 15.9%.
