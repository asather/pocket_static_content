'He has a fresh start': Gov. Wolf signs pardon for McKeesport man denied council seat

Gov. Tom Wolf has pardoned McKeesport barber Corry Sanders, who was elected to the local council five years ago but was denied his seat due to decades-old drug charges.

The Pennsylvania Board of Pardons voted unanimously in September 2019 to recommend that Mr. Sanders be pardoned.

According to Lt. Gov. John Fetterman, Mr. Sanders, 50, was “over the moon” after receiving his pardon in the mail earlier this week.

“He’s exactly the kind of person who could help a community like McKeesport,” Mr. Fetterman said during a phone interview Wednesday morning. “In 2019, he was discouraged because he was voted down. But I found out yesterday that the governor had finally signed it, and Corry got it in the mail yesterday.

“He has a fresh start, and a second chance after a drug conviction that dates back 30 years. I couldn’t be happier.” 

Mr. Sanders could not be immediately reached for comment.

​State law prohibits anyone convicted of a felony from serving in public office.​

Allegheny County Common Pleas Court Senior Judge Joseph M. James ruled on Feb. 24, 2016, that Mr. Sanders’ 1993 no-contest plea to felony drug charges made him ineligible to serve on council, despite him winning a November 2015 election.

Court documents showed that police suspected that Mr. Sanders dealt cocaine out of his house and, on four occasions, bought a total of $11,400 worth of the drug from an undercover officer.

Mr. Sanders served prison time and was paroled in 1998.

Mr. Sanders has said he has since turned his life around and now talks to kids about making good choices. He has owned a barbershop in McKeesport since 1999 and was a board member of the McKeesport Business Authority.

The Board of Pardons processes and signs off on all pardons before they are sent to the governor’s desk. Mr. Fetterman, chairman of the five-member board, had called the decision “appropriate.” The other members of the pardons board are state Attorney General Josh Shapiro, a psychiatrist, a corrections expert and a victims advocate.
