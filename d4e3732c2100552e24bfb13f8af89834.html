Here's how the Arconic breakup will work for shareholders

Pittsburgh-based aluminum parts producer Arconic Inc. announced Thursday how its stock would be distributed to shareholders when it separates into two publicly traded companies on April 1.

At the time of the split, stockholders will keep their shares of Arconic Inc., which will change its name to Howmet Aerospace Inc. and assume the ticker symbol HWM.

In addition, they will receive one share of the spin-off company — Arconic Corp. — for every four Arconic Inc. shares they hold. Arconic Corp. will retain the Arconic Inc. ticker symbol ARNC.

No action is required by Arconic Inc. stockholders to receive the distributions, which are expected to be paid April 1 to shareholders of record March 19.

More information about the distributions is available at www.arconic.com.

The breakup of Arconic was announced in February 2019 in the wake of the company’s failed sale to a private equity firm.

Under the separation, Howmet Aerospace will include the engineered products and forgings segment, while Arconic Corp. will consist of the rolled products segment. Arconic Inc. itself was formed from the breakup of Alcoa in 2016.
