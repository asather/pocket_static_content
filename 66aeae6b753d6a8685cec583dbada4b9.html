Walmart to expand hours after limiting them due to COVID-19

Walmart is expanding its hours this week after limiting them in March due to the pandemic.

The world’s largest retailer announced Monday that more than 4,000 of its 4,700 Walmart stores will expand their closing time from 8:30 p.m. to 10 p.m.

Walmart stores will host a senior shopping hour — starting one hour before the stores usually open — on Tuesdays for customers age 60 and older who may be more vulnerable to COVID-19. Pharmacies and vision centers will also be open during this time.

“We’ll continue assessing our remaining stores to determine the right time to expand their closing hours,” the Bentonville, Arkansas-based company said. “With this change, we will continue the health and safety measures currently in place, such as social distancing, health screenings, plastic barriers at the registers and masks.”

Many major grocers had adjusted their hours in response to high demand to allow time to restock shelves and sanitize stores, while following social-distancing guidelines handed down by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention.

Locally, Walmart stores that have expanded hours include locations in Frazer, the Natrona Heights section of Harrison, West Mifflin, Carnegie, Bethel Park, Cranberry, and Butler.
