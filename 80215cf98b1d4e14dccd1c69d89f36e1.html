City employees suspended from business travel

While the city continues to grapple with how to contain the likely spread of COVID-19, leaders took another preventative step on Wednesday by announcing that all business travel for city employees has been suspended effective immediately, according to mayoral spokesman Timothy McNulty.

The emphasis will be on cancelling all business trips that include air travel, he said. The ban does not affect personal vacations.

"I was actually supposed to go to a conference next month, and I won't be doing that," said Councilman R. Daniel Lavelle after council's standing committee meeting.

Mayor Bill Peduto was scheduled to land in Pittsburgh at 3 p.m. Wednesday after traveling from Washington, D.C. where he attended a conference with other U.S. mayors.

Mr. McNulty said the office has no immediate concerns about the mayor's travel.

Earlier in the day, the city also canceled the St. Patrick’s Day parade, city concerns about COVID-19.
