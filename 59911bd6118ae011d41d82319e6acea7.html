COVID-19 cases in Allegheny County exceed 850

Twenty-one new cases of COVID-19 were reported Sunday in Allegheny County, while the number of deaths remained at 19, according to the county’s latest data.

In Pennsylvania, the number of positive cases rose by 1,178 to 22,833 and deaths rose by 13 to 507.

It’s not yet clear if the Allegheny County’s death toll includes three residents who died at Kane Community Living Center in Glen Hazel. The assisted living facility has suffered a rash of infections, with 25 residents and 15 staff testing positive for the new coronavirus as of Friday. Another 19 tests are still pending.

Kane’s other three centers, in Scott, McKeesport and Ross, have had no confirmed cases although about half a dozen tests are still pending.

Countywide, since the first case was confirmed on March 11, 857 people have tested positive and 130 have been hospitalized. Of those, 48 required intensive care and 25 needed to be put on a ventilator.

Around the rest of Western Pennsylvania, the case count is: Armstrong, 27; Beaver, 145; Butler, 133; Cambria, 13; Clarion, 15; Clearfield, 9; Crawford, 15; Erie, 39; Fayette, 54; Forest, 5; Greene, 23; Indiana, 40; Jefferson, 2; Lawrence, 49; Mercer, 40; Somerset, 12; Venango, 6; Washington, 68; Westmoreland, 223.

Here are the number of deaths in those counties: Beaver has 13 deaths; Westmoreland, 11 (although the state is reporting only six); Butler and Lawrence have four; Fayette, three; and Armstrong and Cambria have one. 

There is at least one confirmed case of COVID-19 in each of Pennsylvania’s 67 counties.
