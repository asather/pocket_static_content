Former Penn-Trafford standout pitcher killed in car crash

A former WPIAL baseball standout was killed Saturday in a car crash in Westmoreland County, his high school baseball coach confirmed Sunday.

MacLean Maund, who was a star pitcher for Penn-Trafford High School and enrolled as a freshman at Seton Hill University, was killed in a head-on car crash on Route 130 between Jeannette and Harrison City.

Maund made the Pittsburgh Post-Gazette All-Area team in 2019, which is made up of the top 10 players in the WPIAL. As a senior pitcher, Maund helped Penn-Trafford get to the WPIAL championship game last year for the first time in school history. He finished with a 6-1 record and 61 strikeouts in 44 1/3 innings. Opponents hit only .188 against him.

Maund was a freshman at Seton Hill and a member of the baseball team.

“People that pass away when they’re 80 and people say they lived a good life,” Penn-Trafford coach Dan Miller said. “Maclean lived a good life, but it was taken away way too soon.”

In a statement to its student and faculty community, Seton Hill said Maund was a business administration major “looking forward to his first baseball season as a pitcher and first baseman with Seton Hill this spring.”
