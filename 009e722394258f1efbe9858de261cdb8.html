Tree of Life synagogue says phishing scam spreading across country

The Tree of Life / Or L’Simcha congregation is once again warning the public of a reactivated phishing scam circulating from an email account posing as “Rabbi Hazzan Meyers.”

In January, the congregation posted an alert to its social media pages concerning people in Squirrel Hill, where the synagogue resides, receiving an inauthentic email that appears to come from Rabbi Meyers asking for a “favor.” The congregation said Wednesday morning that the scam has begun spreading nationwide.

“Unfortunately it has happened again and seems to be happening around the country,” a message posted to The Tree of Life / Or L’Simcha’s Twitter page read.

The alert asked anyone who receives the email to not click any links, respond, or take action, but to instead contact the congregation’s office.

The scam is looking to capitalize on those willing to donate to Tree of Life after an Oct. 27, 2018, anti-Semitic attack by a gunman who killed 11 worshipers from three congregations meeting at the Tree of Life synagogue building.

Anyone who may have received the email can also call the Tree of Life office at 412-521-6788.
