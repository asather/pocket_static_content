Teacher resigns after kindergarten student burned at North Side school

A Pittsburgh Public Schools teacher resigned Saturday after a kindergarten student was burned during a science project.

The incident happened in early January at Pittsburgh King PreK-8 on the North Side, where the 5-year-old girl was injured after spilling a liquid while participating in some sort of science project.

Pittsburgh Public Schools spokeswoman Ebony Pugh confirmed the teacher’s resignation.

The teacher initially had been placed on administrative leave pending an investigation by the school district. The name of the teacher has not been disclosed. 

Photos of the injuries show the student visibly blistered.

The girl has “severe trauma” and will likely have lasting injuries from the incident, Todd Hollis, the attorney for the girl’s family, told the Post-Gazette earlier this month.

Ms. Pugh said school officials could not comment further because it is a personnel matter.
