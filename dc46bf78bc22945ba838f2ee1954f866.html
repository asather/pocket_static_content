Annual PPS back-to-school distribution draws parents to the North Shore

Hundreds of cars lined up on the North Shore Friday as Pittsburghers waited to pick up some needed back-to-school essentials courtesy of the city’s sports teams and public school district.

Stuffed backpacks, fresh produce and prepared lunches, preK to grade 5 books, masks and hand sanitizer were being handed out at the event outside Heinz Field. The distribution was taking place until 3 p.m. on a first-come, first-serve basis to Pittsburgh Public School families, school district officials said.

The Greater Pittsburgh Community Food Bank and 412 Food Rescue provided the food items, and the United Way and Eat n’ Park put together boxed lunches.

Others helping the cause were the Pittsburgh Steelers, Pirates and Penguins as well as Reebok, Pittsburgh Police, Global Links and Reading is Fundamental Storymobile.
