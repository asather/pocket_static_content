Police charge Fox Chapel man in second homicide

Police have charged a Fox Chapel man with killing a man who went missing in December and whose remains were found near the alleged killer’s property in March.

Adam Rosenberg, who was already in the Allegheny County Jail in a separate homicide case, will be served Tuesday with an arrest warrant in the death of Christian Moore-Rouse, 22, of Verona.

He is charged with homicide, robbery, abuse of a corpse, tampering with evidence and firearms violations.

Mr. Rosenberg, 21, had previously been charged Feb. 19 with killing Jeremy Dentel, 28, inside Mr. Dentel’s home in Baldwin Borough several days earlier.

Mr. Moore-Rouse was reported missing on Dec. 25. He was last seen alive four days earlier.

“Over the course of several months, investigative leads were developed in both the homicide and missing person investigations implicating Adam Rosenberg in the disappearance of Christian Moore-Rouse,” Allegheny County police said Tuesday in a statement.

On March 3, county detectives found human remains in a wooded area in the 900 block of Settlers Ridge Road in Fox Chapel near the Rosenberg family property. Police said Tuesday there was evidence of a shooting at the scene, and the remains were identified as those of Mr. Moore-Rouse.

“We are in the process of gathering information to have a better understanding of the allegations. Once all of the facts are known to the defense team, we will proceed accordingly,” said Casey White, one of the lawyers on Mr. Rosenberg’s defense team.

Randall McKinney, the lawyer for Mr. Moore-Rouse’s family, declined comment.
