Death of victim in Penn Hills home invasion is ruled homicide

The death of a woman who was fatally beaten last fall during a home invasion in Penn Hills has been ruled a homicide.

Tiffany Williams, 46, died Nov. 2 from bilateral pulmonary emboli, or blood clots affecting the lungs, the Allegheny County Medical Examiner’s Office said Monday.

On the night of Oct. 30, two men wearing ski masks entered Ms. Williams’ home in the 900 block of Cimarron Drive in search of money, police said.

Witnesses reported seeing two men arguing at or near the residence and hearing gunshots. Police found a badly beaten Ms. Williams who was taken to Forbes Regional Hospital in Monroeville where she died three days later.

At the time, Penn Hills police chief Howard Burton said he did not believe the attack was random.

Chief Burton could not immediately be reached for comment Monday afternoon.
