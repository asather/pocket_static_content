Sources: Pirates to pay minor leaguers through June

The Pirates will pay their minor league players at least through the end of June, sources told the Pittsburgh Post-Gazette on Saturday.

This includes every player in their minor league system, more than 300 in all, though the sources said roster moves are still being contemplated.

Saturday’s move does not involve players who are on the club’s 40-man roster.

If the Pirates do release a player, he would be paid through the end of June while receiving full medical benefits.

This sort of thing has been a hot-button topic in baseball, as a previous agreement that pays minor leaguers $400/week expires on Sunday.

The Athletics aren’t paying minor leaguers through May 31, while several others have offered to pay those players for the entire season, even if it’s canceled.

The largest group of teams is mirroring the Pirates — paying through June, with the plan to make decisions or revaluate at a future date.

Unlike many teams, who have released minor leaguers en masse recently, the Pirates have not released any minor league players since spring training.
