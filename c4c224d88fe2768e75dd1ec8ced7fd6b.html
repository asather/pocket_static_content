Current, former Pittsburgh athletes give dads unforgettable Father's Day gifts

Break out the tissue boxes, because Father’s Day weekend brought about a new batch of current and former Pittsburgh athletes doing extremely heartwarming things for their dads.

First up, we have Steelers rookie Chase Claypool, who surprised his dad with a new truck for Father’s Day. The video is a bit NSFW, but it’s highly worth checking out for the reaction of the Steelers receiver’s father as the giant truck with the bright-red bow pulls up behind him.

Claypool is at least the fifth professional athlete with Pittsburgh ties to gift a parent with a new car since August 2019, when former Pitt receiver and current Bengal Tyler Boyd bought his mother a 2019 Range Rover. Less than a week later, Steelers receiver JuJu Smith-Schuster got his stepfather a new car.

The unofficial tradition continued with Pirates pitcher Chris Archer getting his parents two cars for Christmas and, most recently, Steelers running back James Conner giving his dad a new truck as well.

But wait, there’s more! Qadree Ollison, a former Pitt running back who now plays for the Falcons, went one step further than Claypool by purchasing a house for his dad as a Father’s Day present.

While you have that tissue box handy, check out the messages Steelers quarterback Ben Roethlisberger and linebacker T.J. Watt put out for their fathers yesterday.

Dino Tomlin — Mike Tomlin’s son and a rising sophomore receiver at the University of Maryland — provided a little Father’s Day levity by pushing back at his dad’s assertion in an NFL video that the key to being a great father was “supporting your kids unconditionally.”

At this point, we might as well start taking bets on which Pittsburgh athlete goes viral making a video of themselves doing something adorable for their parents. Pro tip: The smart money is on the Pirates’ most recent first-round draft picks, Nick Gonzales and Carmen Mlodzinski.
