Ways to observe Juneteenth in Pittsburgh

The 2020 Western Pennsylvania Juneteenth Freedom Day Walk, presented online by Stop the Violence PGH, will guide us through historic black spaces on June 19, the date in 1865 when the message of emancipation was delivered in Texas, the last of the states to release its enslaved people.

The Facebook livestream event begins at 1 p.m. Friday, at https://www.facebook.com/events/588557801637171. Historic sites will include: Fort Pitt (1758), Bethel AME Church (1808), Monongahela House (1840), Avery College on East Ohio Street (1849), John C. Peck Oyster House, (1838), John Vashon Bathhouse & Barbershop (1832), Martin Delany Print Office (1846), Little Hayti/Arthurville/Freedom Corner (1835) and Benjamin/Dave Richards Butchery/Negro Tavern (1790).

Speakers are Lawrence Glasco (University of Pittsburgh), Sarah J. Martin (Underground Railroad Historian), Dr. Robert Hill (Pitt), Rev. Dr. Johnnie Monroe (Grace Memorial Church), Samuel W. Black (Heinz History Center), Charlene Foggie-Barnett (Carnegie Museum of Art historian) and Wali A. Jamal (actor/historian).

• Honoring the memory of Antwon Rose II. June 19 is the anniversary of the death of Antwon Rose II, who at 17 was killed by police during a traffic stop. An event in Antwon’s honor will be held at 10 a.m. at 827 Linden Ave. in East Pittsburgh.

• Liberty Magic takeover. Magician Eric Jones is taking over as host of Liberty Magic @ Home for a Black Lives Matter episode, featuring the magicians Ran’D Shine and The Evasons for conversation and magic tricks. Discussion topics include cultural appropriation in the history of the performance of magic and social injustice in the world of entertainment.

• “Refresh, Relax, & Rejoice” picnic. Pittsburgh Parks Conservancy will host an all-ages, BYO food and drink Juneteenth picnic, 5-8 p.m. The “Refresh, Relax, & Rejoice” event is “a celebration of sovereignty, black lives and community.” Weather permitting, registrants are asked to wear a mask when entering the Frick Environmental Center area and exiting the park, and adhere to one family of five or fewer people per blanket or seating area. Registration required at www.showclix.com.

• WQED will live stream a Juneteenth event by BLKFREEDOM.org, a digital commemoration from six Black museums and institutions. The noon event can be accessed at WQED.org/juneteenth.
