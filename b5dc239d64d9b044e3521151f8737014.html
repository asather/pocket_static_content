Asthma blamed in August death of youth football player in Windgap

The Allegheny County Medical Examiner’s Office said in a recent report that a 13-year-old boy who collapsed and died in August while playing football in Windgap suffered a severe asthma attack.

Jordan C. Duckett — a member of a youth football team at the Westside Youth Athletic Association — was playing football and collapsed while running off the field just before 8 p.m. on Aug. 14, officials said. The medical examiner’s office reported Sunday he died of natural causes an hour later at Children’s Hospital of Pittsburgh of UPMC.

He stood 6 feet tall, weighed 185 pounds and wore a size 13 shoe. During a memorial held in August, he was remembered as a “gentle giant” who was careful to avoid hurting others despite his obvious physical abilities.
