Kennedy’s Ashley Marina makes most of a second chance to advance on ‘America’s Got Talent’

Ashley Marina proved she is more than a mere talent, she also has world-class poise.

Her audition on Tuesday night's broadcast of "America's Got Talent" was a roller coaster of emotions. Ashley, 12, a singer/songwriter from Kennedy Township, will be advancing to the next round of the NBC reality competition, but it wasn't easy.

Midway through her audition, judge Simon Cowell stopped her, saying the backing track was drowning out her vocals. At his request, she sang something a cappella. But even that seemed to leave him unimpressed.

"I think you brought the wrong songs with you today," he said, triggering a cascade of boos from the audience. Calling the song choices — "Anyway" and "Opportunity" — "a bit karaoke," he asked if she would take a break, work on a new one and return that afternoon.

The audition for Ashley, who attends Lincoln Park Performing Arts Charter School, had gotten off to a pleasant start. When she mentioned she was from Pittsburgh, judge Sofia Vergara — who is married to Mt. Lebanon native Joe Manganiello — replied "Oooh, that's where my husband is from!"

But what followed was, at best, confusing. Ashley was not thrown, and went down to a rehearsal room to regroup. Under pressure, she didn't seem to be feeling any of the other suggested song choices.

When she returned to the stage, she explained that she was going to do something else: "I know I can get into this, 'cause I wrote it."

She proceeded to earn a standing ovation from the judges with a song she wrote about her father, Mark, "You'll Always Be My Hero."

"Well, I think we finally met Ashley. I can't tell you how important that is," said Cowell.

There were some moist eyes in the house, including her father's.

"He's crying," she said, as her family stood off to the side of the stage.

"Well, he should be crying," judge Howie Mandel said. "That's the most wonderful, the best gift you can give a father."

"You now have three well-deserved 'yeses.'" Cowell said.
