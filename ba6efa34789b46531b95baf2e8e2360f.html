WPIAL announces 2021 Hall of Fame class

The WPIAL has another class for its Hall of Fame. Those selected will just have to wait a year to be inducted.

The WPIAL released its newest HOF class Tuesday morning. This would’ve been the class of 2020, but a banquet for the inductees in May was canceled because of the COVID-19 pandemic. The WPIAL then decided to call this the class of 2021, and a banquet to honor the inductees is set for May 28, 2021.

The new class includes an all-time great NFL player, a two-time NCAA champion, two female athletes from Latrobe High School and the first WPIAL team to win a state softball championship, a feat accomplished 40 years ago.

A committee makes the Hall of Fame selections, considering nominations from schools. Selections are made in seven different categories — athlete, coach, official, contributor, team, Heritage Award, Courage Award.

Here is the class of 2021:

ATHLETE

NATALIE BOWER, LATROBE: The first female to win four WPIAL cross country championships (2005-08). Also won five individual championships in WPIAL track and field and one PIAA track championship before going on to run at Penn State. She qualified for the NCAA cross country championship three times and was a second-team All-American in the steeplechase.

MICHAELA KISSELL, LATROBE: A tennis star who won three WPIAL and PIAA championships. Kissell missed one season because of a knee injury but came back in the spring of her senior year and made it to the quarterfinals of the WPIAL boys tournament. Played two years at the University of Miami before transferring to Marshall, where she qualified for the NCAA tournament as a junior and senior.

ROB GAGLIANI, AVELLA: In 2009, Gagliani became the first to win three WPIAL rifle championships. He also won one state championship during his career before going on to compete at Morehead State, where he eventually earned second-team all-conference honors.

JIMMY GULIBON, DERRY: In 2012, Gulibon won his fourth WPIAL wrestling championship and then became only the 10th wrestler in PIAA history to win four state championships. Gulibon finished his career with a record of 136-4. At Penn State, he qualified for the NCAA tournament four times and placed fifth as a 133-pound sophomore.

ED JOSEFOSKI, PENN HILLS: Josefoski led Penn Hills to WPIAL and PIAA volleyball titles in 1990. He followed that up with a terrific career at Penn State and led the Nittany Lions to an NCAA championship. Josefoski was a second-team All-American at Penn State and was selected to play for the U.S. in the World University Games in 1995. He also was an all-section basketball player at Penn Hills.

KATIE MILLER, HEMPFIELD: In 2002, Miller became only the second golfer to win three PIAA championships. She also was runner-up one season. Made all-ACC twice at the University of North Carolina and played professionally on the LPGA Futures Tour in 2009-10.

SAMANTHA PICKENS, FRANKLIN REGIONAL: Pickens never knew what it was like to not win a state championship as a diver. She won four PIAA titles at Franklin Regional and also three WPIAL championships. She continued her success at the University of Arizona, where she won two NCAA championships.

DARRELLE REVIS, ALIQUIPPA: Considered one of the best defensive backs in NFL history. Revis was a standout in football and basketball at Aliquippa. He was a RB-WR-DB in football and won WPIAL and PIAA titles. He also led WPIAL basketball in scoring in the 2002-03 season at 26.2 points a game and was a two-time Post-Gazette Fabulous 5 selection. He played football at Pitt and made the Pro Bowl seven times and won a Super Bowl in the NFL.

COACH

GENE KLEIN, SOCCER: One of the most successful boys soccer coaches in Pennsylvania history, Klein won six PIAA titles, seven WPIAL championships and had 505 wins in 29 seasons as Quaker Valley’s coach. His 1996 team finished 26-0 and was ranked No. 1 in the country. He had two different stints coaching Quaker Valley and coached the Pittsburgh Riverhounds in between.

JOE MAIZE, BASEBALL: Maize coached Peters Township’s baseball team for 35 years before retiring after the 2019 season. He won 422 games and two WPIAL titles. His teams also finished second in the PIAA twice. Maize also was Peters Township’s athletic director.

OFFICIAL

TOM EVANS: Evans was a longtime baseball and softball official in the high schools ranks who worked 15 WPIAL championships and three PIAA title games. He is now the athletic director at Yough High School and a member of the WPIAL board of directors.

CONTRIBUTOR

AMY CAPRINO: Caprino has been the chairman of the WPIAL girls gymnastics committee for more than two decades. She also has been the gymnastics coach at Moon for 40 years and has six WPIAL titles.

DAVID HAYS: Hays is a retired principal from Moon High School who served the WPIAL in various administrative roles. He was a member of the WPIAL board of directors and spent 21 years on the league’s student-athlete scholarship committee.

TEAM

NORTH HILLS 1980 SOFTBALL: North Hills, under coach Dennis Papalia, was the first softball team from the WPIAL to win a PIAA title. The Indians captured the title by winning five games in five days in a double-elimination tournament in Shippensburg. The team featured a number of outstanding athletes, including three who went on to play Division I basketball — Leigh Curl (University of Connecticut), Mary Maloney (St. Bonaventure) and Melanie Lees (Kansas State). All three, along with softball players Linda Massucci and Rhonda Caye, are in the North Hills Hall of Fame.

UPPER ST. CLAIR 2004 BOYS SOCCER: Under coach Uwe Schneider, the Panthers finished ranked No. 1 in the country by the National Soccer Coaches Association of America. Upper St. Clair finished 27-0 and won WPIAL and PIAA championships. The team never gave up more than one goal in a game and allowed only nine goals all season. Four of the Upper St. Clair players went on to play Division I college soccer.

HERITAGE AWARD

JOE TRANCHINI, CLAIRTON: This award goes to an impactful athlete who graduated from high school before 1960. Tranchini graduated Clairton in 1955 and went on to become the starting quarterback at the Naval Academy. During Tranchini’s time at Navy, he played alongside Heisman Trophy running back Joe Bellino.

COURAGE AWARD

ANTHONY CHICCITT, BETHEL PARK: This award was started in 2009 in honor of former Freedom athlete John Challis, whose battle with cancer became a national story. Chiccitt is a three-sport athlete (football, basketball, baseball) who played baseball last season and hit better than .300 while going through treatments for cancer. He came back to be Bethel Park’s starting quarterback in football this past season, as well as a starter on the basketball team.
