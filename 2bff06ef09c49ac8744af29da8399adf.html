PennDOT license centers to offer limited services in yellow counties

PennDOT will begin a staggered reopening of its driver’s and photo license centers in counties that are moving into the yellow phase of COVID-19 restrictions.

The Pittsburgh driver’s and photo license center on Smithfield Street will open Monday. The Downtown location will operate Monday through Friday from 8:30 a.m. to 4:15 p.m. Designated service times for people who are over 60 years old are set for Wednesday and Thursday mornings between 8:30 and 10:30.

On Friday, license centers in Altoona, Bridgeville, Greensburg, New Kensington, Penn Hills and Uniontown will open. Centers in Allison Park (McCandless), Butler, Johnstown, Somerset and Washington will open Saturday.

After reopening, those facilities will operate Tuesdays through Saturdays from 8:30 a.m. to 4:15 p.m. 

All of these centers have designated service times for people who are over 60 years old on Wednesday and Thursday mornings between 8:30 and 10:30.

Services available at the centers will include commercial driver's license transactions, out-of-state transfers and driver's license restoration services.

Commercial road testing will not be available during the initial reopening, nor will any services that can be done online.

The McConnellsburg photo license center will open Friday, and will be operating Fridays and Saturdays from 8:30 a.m. to 4:15 p.m. Photo license centers in Clairton, East Liberty and Monroeville will open Tuesday. 

PennDOT officials said customers seeking renewal of noncommercial licenses or IDs should renew them online or by mail to avoid unnecessary visits and because wait times will be longer due to increased demands following the monthslong closures.

A full list of driver's and photo license facilities that are reopening, and their hours of operation, is on the PennDOT website.
