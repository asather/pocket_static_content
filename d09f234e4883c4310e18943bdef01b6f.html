Pittsburgh author Damon Young wins Barnes & Noble award

North Side resident Damon Young, the author of the bestselling collection of essays “What Doesn’t Kill You Makes You Blacker,” was honored with other winners of Barnes & Noble’s Discover Great New Writers award at a ceremony in New York on Thursday.

Mr. Young’s book, which examines race, masculinity, culture, sex and growing up black in Pittsburgh, took home the best nonfiction prize. The other titles nominated in the non-fiction category included Saeed Jones’ “How We Fight for Our Lives,” Jaquira Diaz’s “Ordinary Girls” and Jia Tolentino’s “Trick Mirror.”

Claire Adam won in the fiction category for “The Golden Child,” a novel set in Trinidad. The other nominated novels include “On Earth We’re Briefly Gorgeous” by Ocean Vuong, “The Travelers” by Regina Porter” and “Lights All Night Long” by Lydia Fitzpatrick. 

According to Publisher’s Weekly, the eight books were chosen by two panels of noted authors from among 52 titles picked by Barnes & Noble booksellers from a selection of more than 1,000 books. The judges are Christopher Bollen, Janet Fitch, Matthew Goodman, Veronica Chambers and Beth (Bich Minh) Nguyen. Barnes & Noble announced that the authors of the winning books will receive a cash prize and marketing support.
