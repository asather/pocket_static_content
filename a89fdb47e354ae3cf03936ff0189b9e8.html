City Council unanimously passes housing protections for immigration status, language

Pittsburgh City Council on Tuesday unanimously approved housing protections for renters no matter their immigration status or native language.

The ordinance expands the city’s anti-discrimination code to include "perceived or actual” citizenship status and language — spoken or written -— as protected classes for housing and public accommodation.

The city’s Commission on Human Relations enforces the anti-discrimination code.

“By and large these are cases we hear about that complainants are far too scared to report because they didn't have protections,” said Megan Stanley, the commission’s executive director. “... It won't change overnight, but we hope it will be one step in the right direction to let them know we care, [and] we can be a constant, trustworthy presence.”

Ms. Stanley said that cases include landlords charging higher security deposits or rent; some situations reportedly escalate to sexual harassment, physical assault or blackmail.

Councilwoman Erika Strassburger introduced the bill on April 28 with support from Mayor Bill Peduto. Council members Corey O’Connor, Bruce Kraus, Daniel Lavelle, Ricky Burgess and Bobby Wilson joined as co-sponsors.
