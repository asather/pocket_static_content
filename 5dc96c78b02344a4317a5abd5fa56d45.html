BNY Mellon imposing 40-hour workweek, raising minimum wage

Bank of New York Mellon is imposing a standard 40-hour workweek on employees across the U.S., raising the number of hours many of its employees will work.

The new standard, announced in an email to employees Thursday, takes effect Sept. 6.

The change will ensure “more consistent treatment of employees,” the email stated.

Many of the company’s employees currently are required to work from 35 to 37.5 hours per week.

“We recognize that there may be some concerns about this change,” the email said.

For example, it said, employees who worked less than 40 hours will now be required to work additional hours.

The adjustment will not affect workers’ take-home pay.

“Because you will be receiving the same pay, you will receive a slightly lower regular hourly rate,” the email said.

At the same time, BNY Mellon said it was bumping up its minimum wage from $15 to $16.50 per hour. The hike in the minimum wage takes effect Sept. 1.

“BNY Mellon periodically reviews its policies and practices,” spokeswoman Jennifer Hendricks Sullivan said Thursday. “This change will result in a more consistent treatment of employees with regard to expected hours of work.”

BNY Mellon employs 47,900 globally. The company does not disclose how many work in the U.S., Ms. Sullivan said.
