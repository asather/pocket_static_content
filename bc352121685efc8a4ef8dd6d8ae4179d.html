Here's where you can purchase homemade masks in the Pittsburgh area

As the state continues to implement stricter guidelines on protective gear amid the pandemic for both essential employees and average consumers, the demand for face masks continues to soar.

Several local businesses of all sizes have pitched in to help make, provide and donate masks to health care workers and first responders on the front lines battling COVID-19. But after Pennsylvania Health Secretary Rachel Levine signed an order requiring both critical workers and customers to wear masks while on the premises of a businesses, citizens’ need for mask vendors could reach an all time high.

The Post-Gazette has put together a list of local organizations, businesses and individual vendors that are selling homemade face masks to the general public that can be purchased to protect yourself and the members of your household.

If you are a vendor and would like to be added to this list, please send an email with detailed information to ajohnson@post-gazette.com, as we will continue to update this article as needed.

Here is where you can find face masks throughout the Pittsburgh area (listed alphabetically):

AudinoMasks

AundinoMasks can be found on Etsy, an online marketplace. Brooke Audino created the shop with the help of her mom who runs Grand Champ Bows, a Cranberry in-home business that makes and sells custom cheerleading bows. Ms. Audino and her mom have taken the bow and scrunchie fabric and made mask materials. AundinoMasks is offering dozens of custom design masks ranging from $10-$26. Masks can be ordered here.

CinnamonSewCrafty

When Cristi Parks began working from home as a paraprofessional in the Peters Township School District, she started sewing masks for family and friends. She eventually began selling them on her Etsy shop CinnamonSewCrafty, where she also sells shoes and other clothing items. Ms. Parks is selling custom design masks for $15 with free shipping. Masks can be ordered here.

David Allen Clothing 

David Allen, owner of David Allen Clothing in Mount Washington, has turned his luxury suit business into a face mask operation. Mr. Allen has already made and given away thousands of masks to businesses and health care workers, and he is also offering them for purchase to the general public. Mr. Allen is selling family packs, as well as personal packs of up to 50 masks. A portion of his mask sales will go towards the Coronavirus Relief Fund. Masks can be ordered at davidalanmasks.com.

Floss Boss Designs

New Kensington native Rachel Sloane McPherson is selling homemade masks on her website, flossbossdesigns.com. While she currently lives in Tennessee, Ms. McPherson is making custom homemade masks that include Steelers designs, among others. Floss Boss Designs masks start at $9, and Ms. McPherson also offers kid sizes.

Free Store Wilkinsburg

Wilkinsburg Mayor Marita Garrett, co-founder of Free Store Wilkinsburg, is using the shop to give away free reusable face masks. Ms. Garrett is partnering with local mask makers who are donating masks for Free Store to give to the public. You can drop by the store on Thursdays and Fridays between the hours of 10 a.m.-1 p.m. and Saturdays from 11 a.m.-1 p.m. to get a free mask. If you have an urgent need and want to contact the store after its business hours, Ms. Garrett asks that you email freestorewilkinsburg@gmail.com.

Gerbe Glass

Bernadette Gerbe has transformed her Lawrencville glass studio into a mask-making shop. Ms. Gerbe has been donating masks to first responders, while also taking orders from the general public. Ms. Gerbe did not set a price for her masks, as she said she is instead accepting $1 donations that will go towards purchasing fabric. To place an order, you can contact Ms. Gerbe at gerbeglass@gmail.com or message her Facebook page.

Gloria Horn Sewing Studio

Gloria Horn Sewing Studio in Mt. Lebanon is selling face mask kits to help customers make reusable masks at home. The kit comes with the fabric cut-to-size as well as elastic. Each kit holds enough material to sew together 12 masks. Owner Gloria Horn is also accepting donated homemade masks that she giving out to health care workers and first responders. The kits are priced at $15, and can be ordered here.

The Iron Horse Atelier 

Amanda Vereb began making face masks after her bridal alterations business, The Iron Horse Atelier, came to a halt during the pandemic. Ms. Vereb’s masks are made of 100% cotton or linen, and she’s offering adult, children and infant sizes. The cost is $20 per mask. To place an order, email TheIronHorsePGH.masks@gmail.com.

Kin of Duncan

Thirteen-year-old Verona native Jazmiere Bates turned her Kin of Duncan t-shirt brand for kids and pets into a mask making business. The cotton reusable masks cost $10 for toddler sizes and $13 for adults, plus a $2 shipping fee. Jazmiere makes her reusable masks out of 100% cotton. To order, you can message her Facebook page or email Jazmiere at Kinofduncan@gmail.com.

Mazetoons

Professional cartoonist Joe Wos is selling face masks that feature his custom drawings and designs through his Mazetoons brand. Masks are available in small, medium or large and are handmade to order. The cost is $20 which includes shipping, and Mr. Wos is also accepting requests for different custom designs. Mazetoon reusable masks can be ordered here.

412PPE

Designer Diana Misetic of Little Black Dress in Shadyside, ophthalmologist Deval “Reshma” Paranjpe and seamstress Constance Paras recently came together to launch 412PPE, a reference to Pittsburgh and the acronym for personal protective equipment. 412PPE is currently accepting preorders for homemade face masks that will eventually sell for $14.95. For each mask purchased, 412PPE promises to donate one to a local health care professional. You can place a preorder here.

Individual vendors:

Darlene Gilliam, Garfield: Selling homemade reusable masks for $10. She is offering pickup and mail delivery. Ms. Gilliam can be reached on Facebook or by phone at 412-304-5403.

Christina Jones, Monroeville: Selling homemade reusable masks for $5. Ms. Jones can be reached on Facebook or by email at christinajones2@outlook.com.

Karlissia Council, Garfield: Selling homemade reusable masks for $10. She is offering delivery and pickup. Ms. Council can be reached on Facebook or by email at Karlissiac@gmail.com.
