Pitt basketball lands transfer guard Nike Sibande

It's rare for a college basketball program to add a player in June, only a handful of months before the start of the next season, but Pitt just did it.

Nike Sibande, a transfer from Miami (Ohio), has signed an aid agreement with the Panthers and will join them for the 2020-21 season, the program announced Saturday.

As a junior last season, Sibande, a 6-foot-4 guard, averaged 13.9 points and 5.9 rebounds per game. Over his three-year career with the RedHawks, Sibande averaged 15.1 points, 5.1 rebounds and 1.4 assists per game while shooting 41.4% from the field and 33.6% from 3-point range.

An Indianapolis native, Sibande earned third-team all-Mid-American Conference as a sophomore after averaging 16.1 points and 5.1 rebounds per game.

In May, Sibande narrowed his college choices to a list of five that did not include Pitt -- he was considering Alabama, Arkansas, Georgia, Georgetown and Xavier -- making his commitment to Pitt something of a surprise, even beyond the timing of it.

Sibande is not immediately eligible as a traditional transfer and would need a waiver to compete next season, an avenue Pitt's coaching staff may very well pursue. Should he be eligible, he would bolster a pool of guards that is relatively thin beyond Xavier Johnson and Delaware transfer Ithiel Horton.

With the addition of Sibande, coach Jeff Capel and the Panthers have no more available scholarships for next season.

“Nike is an experienced, skilled and productive player who will fit in well with the culture we are building here at Pitt,” Capel said in a statement. "He is an outstanding athlete who loves to compete and has a scorers’ mentality. We look forward to having him in the program and working with him as he continues to develop on and off the court.”
