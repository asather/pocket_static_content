60 new deaths in Pa., as Allegheny County's COVID-19 case total nears 900-mark

This was updated at 12:25 p.m.

The state reported 60 new deaths and 1,146 new positive cases of COVID-19, bringing its new totals to 584 and 25,346, according to data released Tuesday.

Washington County reported its first death, while Beaver and Butler each reported new deaths, putting their totals at 14 and five, respectively. Allegheny and Westmoreland counties each reported three new deaths.

In Allegheny, 15 new cases of COVID-19 were reported. It has had 893 positive cases, highest in Western Pennsylvania, according to the state.

All of Allegheny’s deaths were individuals who were over the age of 65. Since the first case was confirmed on March 11, there have been 142 hospitalizations due to COVID-19. Of that number, 53 were admitted to the Intensive Care Unit, and 28 required mechanical ventilation. The county said it has administered 10,479 tests.

Statewide, the number of positive cases reported Tuesday rose to 25,345 from 24,199 on Monday. There is at least one case in each of Pennsylvania’s 67 counties. 

On Monday, state Health Secretary Dr. Rachel Levine said that social distancing measures are working to “flatten the curve.”

“Our case count numbers continue to grow, but we aren’t seeing the doubling of cases, that exponential growth, like other countries and states have seen,” Dr. Levine said, describing the trend in the state’s data as a “plateau.” She added later in the press conference that “Social distancing works. The closures are saving lives.”

As for Pennsylvania’s economy, Gov. Tom Wolf announced Monday that Pennsylvania will be part of a coalition of six northeastern states that would work together to plan the eventual reopening of the region’s economy.
